## Hacker News

### EFF is leaving X
### 电子前沿基金会 (EFF) 宣布退出 X 平台

The digital rights non-profit has announced it will no longer post on X, citing a sharp decline in reach and engagement over the past several years. The organization noted that its impressions dropped from millions per month to a fraction of that, making the platform no longer a viable channel for their advocacy.

电子前沿基金会 (EFF) 宣布将停止在 X 平台发布内容，主要原因是该平台近年来流量和互动率出现断崖式下跌。该组织指出，其月度曝光量已从巅峰时期的数千万降至极低水平，导致该平台不再是其进行数字权利倡导的有效渠道。

## TechCrunch

### Florida AG to probe OpenAI, alleging possible connection to FSU shooting
### 佛罗里达州总检察长调查 OpenAI，指控其与校园枪击案有关

Florida Attorney General James Uthmeier is launching an investigation into OpenAI, citing concerns over national security and the potential misuse of AI. The probe follows reports that ChatGPT was allegedly used to plan a shooting at Florida State University last year.

佛罗里达州总检察长 James Uthmeier 宣布对 OpenAI 展开调查，理由涉及国家安全风险及 AI 技术被滥用的可能性。此前有报道称，ChatGPT 曾被用于策划去年发生在佛罗里达州立大学的一起枪击事件。

[Read more →](https://techcrunch.com/2026/04/09/florida-ag-to-probe-openai-alleging-possible-connection-to-fsu-shooting/)

### Volkswagen drops all-electric ID.4 in the US in pivot back to gas SUVs
### 大众汽车在美国停产 ID.4，战略重心回归燃油 SUV

Volkswagen is ending production of the all-electric ID.4 at its U.S. factory to reallocate resources toward the upcoming Atlas SUV. This move reflects a broader industry trend of automakers scaling back EV ambitions in the American market to prioritize high-demand gas vehicles.

大众汽车宣布在美国工厂停止生产 ID.4 电动汽车，以便将资源集中于即将推出的 Atlas SUV。此举反映了汽车行业在美国市场的一大趋势：多家车企正在缩减电动化目标，转而优先满足对燃油车的高需求。

### John Deere will pay farmers $99 million over right-to-repair lawsuit
### 约翰迪尔因“维修权”诉讼向农民赔偿 9900 万美元

Agricultural giant John Deere has agreed to a $99 million settlement to resolve a class-action lawsuit regarding its restrictive repair policies. The company will now provide farmers and independent repair shops with access to necessary diagnostic tools and software for the next decade.

农业巨头约翰迪尔已同意支付 9900 万美元，以和解关于其限制性维修政策的集体诉讼。根据协议，该公司将在未来十年内向农民和独立维修店提供必要的诊断工具和软件访问权限。
