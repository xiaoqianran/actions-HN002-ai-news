### Defining Monotonicity and Stability
### 定义单调性与稳定性

Monotonicity refers to the analysis of the risk direction of a pre-selected variable. For a continuous variable, it answers the following question: when the value of the variable increases or decreases, does the credit risk increase or decrease accordingly? For example, in a corporate context, we expect that when a company’s revenue increases, its financial situation improves. Conversely, when its revenue decreases, its financial situation deteriorates. This is the risk direction.
单调性是指对预选变量的风险方向进行分析。对于连续变量，它回答了以下问题：当变量的值增加或减少时，信用风险是否会相应地增加或减少？例如，在企业背景下，我们预期当公司收入增加时，其财务状况会改善；反之，当收入减少时，财务状况会恶化。这就是风险方向。

Stability goes one step further. It answers the question: is this risk direction consistently respected across multiple years, or do we observe risk inversions? A risk inversion occurs when, despite an increase in revenue, the financial situation deteriorates — or vice versa. Stability gives a long-term view of the variable’s behavior and supports informed decision-making. In credit scoring, we study both the monotonicity of variables and their stability over time. We also study the stability of variable distributions between consecutive years and between the train, test, and out-of-time datasets.
稳定性则更进一步。它回答了这个问题：这种风险方向在多年内是否始终保持一致，还是我们观察到了风险反转？风险反转是指尽管收入增加，财务状况却恶化了（反之亦然）。稳定性提供了变量行为的长期视角，并支持明智的决策。在信用评分中，我们既研究变量的单调性，也研究它们随时间的稳定性。我们还研究变量分布在连续年份之间以及训练集、测试集和跨期数据集之间的稳定性。

### 变量的单调性与稳定性

This analysis acts as a pre-selection step. If a variable shows a risk inversion over time, we must either treat it or remove it from the model. For continuous variables, treatment typically consists of discretizing the variable and aggregating its bins. For categorical variables, we can directly combine certain categories.
此分析作为预筛选步骤。如果一个变量随时间表现出风险反转，我们必须对其进行处理或将其从模型中剔除。对于连续变量，处理通常包括对变量进行离散化并合并其分箱。对于类别型变量，我们可以直接合并某些类别。

### Defining the Risk Direction
### 定义风险方向

The first step is to assign a risk direction to each variable. For a continuous variable, we assign a “+” sign if we expect that an increase in the variable leads to an increase in credit risk. We assign a “−” sign if we expect that an increase leads to a decrease in credit risk. For a binary categorical variable, we assign a “+” sign if moving from the least risky to the most risky category increases the risk. We assign a “−” sign if it decreases the risk.
第一步是为每个变量分配一个风险方向。对于连续变量，如果我们预期变量增加会导致信用风险增加，则分配“+”号；如果我们预期变量增加会导致信用风险降低，则分配“−”号。对于二元类别变量，如果从风险最低类别变为风险最高类别会增加风险，我们分配“+”号；如果降低风险，则分配“−”号。

For a multi-category variable, we do not assign a binary sign. Instead, we rank the categories from least risky to most risky based on their empirical default rate. The category with the lowest default rate is the least risky; the one with the highest is the most risky. We then validate this ranking with business experts.
对于多类别变量，我们不分配二元符号。相反，我们根据经验违约率将类别从风险最低到风险最高进行排序。违约率最低的类别风险最低，违约率最高的类别风险最高。然后，我们与业务专家一起验证此排序。

I make two specific comments here. For person_age, age is a sensitive variable that may discriminate counterparties. We expect both very young and very old counterparties to carry higher risk, which makes it difficult to assign a single direction. We therefore let the data reveal the risk pattern. For person_home_ownership, the variable has multiple categories, making it equally difficult to assign a binary direction a priori. We expect the RENT category to carry the highest risk, followed by MORTGAGE, then OWN, with the OTHER category capturing counterparties in more ambiguous housing situations. We let the data confirm this ordering.
在此我特别说明两点。对于 person_age，年龄是一个敏感变量，可能会对交易对手产生歧视。我们预期非常年轻和非常年长的交易对手都具有较高风险，这使得难以分配单一方向。因此，我们让数据来揭示风险模式。对于 person_home_ownership，该变量有多个类别，同样难以先验地分配二元方向。我们预期 RENT 类别风险最高，其次是 MORTGAGE，然后是 OWN，而 OTHER 类别则涵盖了住房情况更模糊的交易对手。我们让数据来确认这种排序。
