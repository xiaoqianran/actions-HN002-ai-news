# Ubuntu services hit by outages after DDoS attack
# Ubuntu 服务因 DDoS 攻击陷入瘫痪

Hacktivists have claimed responsibility for taking down the public-facing infrastructure of popular Linux operating system distribution Ubuntu, as well as Canonical, the company that develops and maintains the software. The attack began on Thursday, and affected services that Ubuntu users rely on. “Canonical’s web infrastructure is under a sustained, cross-border attack and we are working to address it. We will provide more information in our official channels as soon as we are able to,” the company said on its website.

黑客活动分子声称对瘫痪流行 Linux 操作系统发行版 Ubuntu 以及负责开发和维护该软件的 Canonical 公司的公共基础设施负责。此次攻击始于周四，影响了 Ubuntu 用户所依赖的服务。Canonical 公司在其网站上表示：“Canonical 的网络基础设施正遭受持续的跨境攻击，我们正在努力解决。我们将尽快通过官方渠道提供更多信息。”

The hacktivists are believed to have launched a distributed denial-of-service, or DDoS, a crude but often effective attack that consists of flooding a target with junk traffic until it overloads or crashes. Ubuntu developers have been discussing the attack on an unofficial Ubuntu community forum, claiming that the attack affects Ubuntu’s security API, and several Ubuntu and Canonical websites. According to a post on a threat intelligence forum, the DDoS attack has also made it impossible for users to update and install Ubuntu. TechCrunch verified that updates failed to install on a test device running Ubuntu. As of this writing, the outage has been ongoing for around 20 hours. When contacted, Canonical spokesperson Lelanie de Roubaix reiterated what the company said on its website.

据信，这些黑客活动分子发动了分布式拒绝服务（DDoS）攻击。这是一种简单粗暴但往往行之有效的攻击方式，通过向目标发送海量垃圾流量，直至其过载或崩溃。Ubuntu 开发人员一直在一个非官方的 Ubuntu 社区论坛上讨论此次攻击，称攻击影响了 Ubuntu 的安全 API 以及多个 Ubuntu 和 Canonical 的网站。根据威胁情报论坛上的一篇帖子，此次 DDoS 攻击还导致用户无法更新和安装 Ubuntu。TechCrunch 经核实发现，在一台运行 Ubuntu 的测试设备上，更新确实无法安装。截至本文撰写时，中断已持续约 20 小时。在联系 Canonical 发言人 Lelanie de Roubaix 时，她重申了公司在网站上的声明。

Hacktivists calling themselves The Islamic Cyber Resistance in Iraq 313 Team claimed on its Telegram channel that it was to blame for the DDoS attack.

自称为“伊拉克伊斯兰网络抵抗 313 团队”（The Islamic Cyber Resistance in Iraq 313 Team）的黑客活动分子在其 Telegram 频道上声称，他们应对此次 DDoS 攻击负责。

The hackers claimed to be using Beamed, a DDoS-for-hire service. These types of services, also called booters or stressers, allow anyone to pay to launch DDoS attacks, even if they have no technical skills nor the necessary infrastructure to flood targets with bogus traffic. The DDoS-for-hire service in this case claims to power attacks in excess of 3.5 Tbps, which is about half of the bandwidth of a cyberattack that Cloudflare last year called the “largest DDoS attack ever recorded.” For years, authorities such as the FBI and Europol have played a game of whack-a-mole against these services, taking down and seizing domains, and sometimes arresting the people behind them.

这些黑客声称使用了名为“Beamed”的 DDoS 雇佣服务。这类服务（也称为“启动器”或“压力测试器”）允许任何人付费发起 DDoS 攻击，即使他们没有任何技术技能，也没有向目标发送虚假流量所需的基础设施。在此次事件中，该 DDoS 雇佣服务声称能够支持超过 3.5 Tbps 的攻击流量，这大约是 Cloudflare 去年所称的“有史以来记录的最大 DDoS 攻击”带宽的一半。多年来，FBI 和欧洲刑警组织等机构一直在与这些服务进行“打地鼠”式的博弈，通过查封域名，有时甚至逮捕幕后黑手来打击此类活动。
