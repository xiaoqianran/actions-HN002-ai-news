# A Gentle Introduction to Stochastic Programming
# 随机规划入门指南

**How to make decisions when your spreadsheet is lying about the future**
**当你的电子表格对未来撒谎时，该如何做决策**

In my first TDS post, I wrote about how to translate a real-world problem into an integer linear program. In my second, I wrote about how to make that program robust against uncertainty. Both were variations on the same idea: take a fuzzy real-world question, squeeze it into an LP, and let a solver do the rest.
在我发表的第一篇 TDS 文章中，我写了如何将现实世界的问题转化为整数线性规划。在第二篇中，我写了如何使该程序能够应对不确定性。两者都是同一个想法的变体：选取一个模糊的现实问题，将其压缩进线性规划（LP）模型，然后让求解器完成剩下的工作。

There is a moment in every optimizer’s life, though, when the LP starts to feel a bit too neat. Demand is a number. Travel time is a number. Wind speed is a number. The model accepts the input, returns an optimal solution, and goes on its way. The reality those numbers were supposed to describe (messy, jittery, and occasionally surprising) doesn’t really show up anywhere.
然而，每个优化从业者都会经历这样一个时刻：线性规划开始显得过于“整洁”了。需求是一个数字，行程时间是一个数字，风速也是一个数字。模型接收输入，返回最优解，然后就结束了。而这些数字本应描述的现实（混乱、波动且偶尔令人惊讶）却在模型中无处体现。

Stochastic programming is the field that takes that discomfort seriously. Instead of pretending the data is exact, it builds the uncertainty directly into the model. The price you pay is a bit more notation; the payoff is decisions that hold up when the world doesn’t cooperate.
随机规划正是认真对待这种不适感的领域。它不再假装数据是精确的，而是将不确定性直接构建到模型中。你付出的代价是需要更多的数学符号，但回报是当世界不按常理出牌时，你的决策依然稳健。

This post is a gentle tour of the basics. We’ll see why the obvious approach doesn’t work, walk through the four standard ways to handle uncertainty in a linear program, and finish with a quick sanity check on whether any of this is worth the effort. There’s some math, but it’s the same math you already know from LP, with one extra symbol attached.
这篇文章是对基础知识的温和导览。我们将了解为什么显而易见的方法行不通，逐步探讨在线性规划中处理不确定性的四种标准方法，最后进行一次快速的理性评估，看看这一切是否值得投入精力。文中会有一些数学内容，但它们与你在线性规划中已知的数学知识相同，只是多了一个额外的符号。

### Starting point: a fashion company with a bad crystal ball
### 起点：一家拥有“劣质水晶球”的时尚公司

To make this concrete, we’ll use the running example from dr. Ruben van Beesten’s lectures. It goes like this. You run a fashion company that sells winter clothing in Germany. Production happens in Bangladesh, which is cheap but slow: the goods take a few weeks to arrive. So in the fall, you have to decide how much to produce for the upcoming winter season. Two ways this can go wrong: produce too little, and you lose sales; produce too much, and you’re stuck with stock you can’t sell.
为了具体化，我们将使用 Ruben van Beesten 博士讲座中的案例。情况是这样的：你经营着一家在德国销售冬装的时尚公司。生产在孟加拉国进行，成本低但速度慢：货物需要几周才能运达。因此，在秋季，你必须决定为即将到来的冬季生产多少产品。这可能会出两个问题：生产太少，你会损失销售额；生产太多，你会积压卖不出去的库存。

And here we hit a wall. What does it mean for x to satisfy a constraint that depends on a random variable? Is x = 100 feasible if demand might be 80, might be 120, and might be anywhere in between? The problem isn’t hard to solve: it’s ill-defined. The solver doesn’t even know which problem you’re asking it to solve. 在这里我们碰壁了。让 x 满足一个依赖于随机变量的约束意味着什么？如果需求可能是 80，可能是 120，也可能是两者之间的任何值，那么 x = 100 是否可行？这个问题不是难解，而是定义不清。求解器甚至不知道你要求它解决的是什么问题。随机规划本质上是对该问题的一系列原则性回答。我们将探讨其中最常见的四种。

### Four ways to handle the uncertainty
### 处理不确定性的四种方法

Each of the four approaches takes the ill-defined LP above and turns it into a well-defined optimization problem. They differ in what they assume you know about the uncertainty, and in how cautious they are about bad outcomes.
这四种方法中的每一种都将上述定义不清的线性规划转化为定义明确的优化问题。它们的区别在于对不确定性的假设不同，以及对不良结果的谨慎程度不同。

#### 1. Robust optimization: prepare for the worst
#### 1. 鲁棒优化：为最坏情况做准备

The most cautious approach. You don’t need to know the full probability distribution of ξ, but only its support, i.e., the set of values it could possibly take. We call this set the uncertainty set, written U. Then you ask: what is the best decision that stays feasible no matter which ξ ∈ U actually shows up?
这是最谨慎的方法。你不需要知道 ξ 的完整概率分布，只需要知道它的支撑集，即它可能取值的集合。我们将此集合称为不确定性集合，记为 U。然后你问：无论实际上出现哪个 ξ ∈ U，都能保持可行的最佳决策是什么？

The constraint now has to hold for every ξ in the uncertainty set. In our fashion example with U = [0, 10], you’d be planning for demand of 10, the worst case, every time. That’s the strength and the weakness of robust optimization in one sentence. The solution is bulletproof, but it’s also conservative: you’ll often be sitting on inventory you didn’t need, because you planned as if the unlikely worst case were guaranteed.
现在，约束条件必须对不确定性集合中的每一个 ξ 都成立。在我们的时尚案例中，如果 U = [0, 10]，你每次都要按需求量 10（最坏情况）进行规划。这就是鲁棒优化的优缺点所在。该方案固若金汤，但也过于保守：你经常会积压不需要的库存，因为你的规划假设了那个极不可能发生的最坏情况一定会发生。

#### 2. Chance constraints: relax the worst case
#### 2. 机会约束：放宽最坏情况

Robust optimization plans for any possible outcome. Chance constraints relax that to: plan for most of them. You pick a probability level α, say 95%, and require the constraint to hold with at least that probability.
鲁棒优化为任何可能的结果做规划。机会约束将其放宽为：为大多数情况做规划。你选择一个概率水平 α（例如 95%），并要求约束条件至少以该概率成立。

This is called a joint chance constraint: all the entries of the constraint vector have to be satisfied simultaneously, with joint probability ≥ α. A weaker variant treats each row separately.
这被称为联合机会约束：约束向量的所有项必须同时满足，且联合概率 ≥ α。一种较弱的变体是分别处理每一行。

These are individual chance constraints: each constraint i must hold with probability at least αᵢ, but you don’t care about the joint event. Quick exercise: if you set every αᵢ equal to the joint α, which formulation is more conservative? Answer: the joint version. Satisfying all constraints simultaneously is a stricter requirement than satisfying each one in isolation, so the joint formulation has a smaller feasible region and a worse (higher) optimal cost.
这些是个体机会约束：每个约束 i 必须以至少 αᵢ 的概率成立，但你不在乎联合事件。快速练习：如果你将每个 αᵢ 都设为联合概率 α，哪种公式更保守？答案：联合版本。同时满足所有约束比单独满足每一个约束要求更严格，因此联合公式的可行域更小，最优成本也更差（更高）。

Either way, chance constraints give you a knob, α, to dial how cautious you want to be. Crank it to 1, and you’re back to (almost) robust. Drop it to 0.5, and you’re basically flipping a coin on feasibility. Most real applications live somewhere in the 0.9–0.99 range.
无论哪种方式，机会约束都为你提供了一个旋钮 α，用来调节你想要的谨慎程度。将其调至 1，你就回到了（几乎）鲁棒的状态。将其降至 0.5，你基本上就是在掷硬币决定可行性。大多数实际应用都在 0.9 到 0.99 的范围内。

There’s a catch worth flagging: chance constraints are hard in general. The probability term inside the constraint is a non-linear, often non-convex function of x, so you usually can’t hand the formulation directly to a standard LP solver. There are tractable special cases, but the general problem is harder than it looks at first glance.
值得注意的是：机会约束通常很难处理。约束内部的概率项是 x 的非线性且通常是非凸的函数，因此你通常不能直接将其交给标准的线性规划求解器。虽然存在一些可处理的特殊情况，但总体问题比乍看起来要难得多。
