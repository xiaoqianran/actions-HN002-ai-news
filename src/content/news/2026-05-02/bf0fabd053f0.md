# Why I Don’t Vibe Code
# 为什么我不玩“氛围编程”（Vibe Coding）

March 05, 2026 A "brief" accounting of various reasons why vibe coding has just never clicked for me personally as a developer. There has been a lot of discussion online lately about vibe coding and and how Large Language Models (LLMs) will revolutionize the field of software development. Every new model will launch us into realms of pure productivity, shipping software at the speed of thought and removing all the friction and overhead of product development. Or something like that. Maybe. I’ll have to take your word for it. I don’t vibe code. If it’s working for you, great! I’m not really here to argue the merits or flaws of LLMs at depth here in this piece, but it’s just never clicked for me personally. This page is a “brief” accounting of various reasons why.

2026年3月5日。这是一份关于为什么“氛围编程”（vibe coding）作为一名开发者从未让我产生共鸣的“简要”说明。最近网上关于氛围编程以及大语言模型（LLM）将如何彻底改变软件开发领域的讨论非常多。每一个新模型的发布都宣称将把我们带入纯粹生产力的领域，让我们以思维的速度交付软件，并消除产品开发中所有的摩擦和开销。大概是这样吧。也许吧。我只能姑且听之。我不玩氛围编程。如果它对你有用，那很好！我写这篇文章并不是为了深入探讨LLM的优缺点，只是它从未让我产生共鸣。这一页是对其中原因的“简要”说明。

### I’m a Cheapskate
### 我是个吝啬鬼

I’m not a purist. I’ve tried using LLMs that are integrated into an IDE. They have been useful for some tasks that are simple enough to be easily describable but annoying enough to not just do them myself. For instance, resizing a grid of square images to be smaller. I could go look at the command-line arguments for ImageMagick, but that was a perfect thing to ask the AI to do. I then tried using one of the AI tools to analyze my code in a project and a few other small tasks before it all came to an awkward halt. The system informed me that I had just run out of credits and I would need to provide a credit card to purchase more tokens I wanted to keep going.

我不是个纯粹主义者。我尝试过使用集成在IDE中的LLM。对于一些简单到容易描述、但又繁琐到不想亲自动手的任务，它们确实很有用。例如，将一组方形图片调整为更小的尺寸。我本可以去查ImageMagick的命令行参数，但让AI来做这件事再完美不过了。随后，我尝试使用其中一个AI工具来分析项目中的代码以及处理其他一些小任务，但一切都在尴尬中戛然而止。系统通知我，我的额度用完了，如果想继续使用，就必须提供信用卡来购买更多的Token。

Now, you must understand that I come from a long line of cheapskates from both sides of my family tree. We’ve been pinching pennies and hunting bargains for centuries both here and on the other side of the Atlantic. As an example, one of my distant ancestors died during the King Philip’s War because he left the safety of the fort to retrieve some cheese he had left behind when evacuating his house. So you must believe me that the idea of paying a service in perpetuity so I could think just seemed so laughably absurd and horrific that I didn’t even bother giving them my card. I closed the laptop. I uninstalled the IDE and went back to using Emacs even. And I realized that I just didn’t even notice the lack anymore.

你必须明白，我的家族血统两边都有着悠久的“吝啬”传统。几个世纪以来，无论是在大西洋这边还是那边，我们一直在精打细算、搜寻廉价商品。举个例子，我的一位远祖在菲利普国王战争期间丧生，原因是他为了取回撤离家园时落下的一块奶酪，竟然离开了堡垒的安全区。所以你必须相信我，为了“思考”而永久性地向某项服务付费，这种想法在我看来简直荒谬且可怕到可笑，我甚至懒得给他们我的信用卡信息。我合上了笔记本电脑，卸载了IDE，甚至重新用回了Emacs。我意识到，我根本没觉得少了什么。

### I’m Old
### 我老了

It does help that I’m old. I’ve been writing code for a long time, especially in an industry that calls a developer with 5 years of experience a “senior engineer.” Experience is a welcome antidote to anxiety sometimes (as long as it’s not anxiety about ageism in an industry that calls a developer senior with only 5 years of experience), and the AI hype does remind me of earlier breakthroughs in low and no-code tooling. I don’t doubt that AI can be a useful tool for developers. I know there are tasks it can help with as better tooling. But these arguments always leave me thinking about the accidental and essential complexity again.

我老了，这确实有帮助。我写代码已经很久了，尤其是在这样一个将拥有5年经验的开发者称为“高级工程师”的行业里。经验有时是缓解焦虑的良药（只要不是因为身处一个5年经验就称“高级”的行业而产生的年龄歧视焦虑），而AI的炒作确实让我想起了早期低代码和无代码工具的突破。我不怀疑AI可以成为开发者的有用工具。我知道作为更好的工具，它可以在某些任务上提供帮助。但这些论点总是让我再次思考起“偶然复杂性”和“本质复杂性”。

Fred Brooks was old even when I was a young coder myself. As the project manager for IBM’s System 360 line of mainframes (and accompanying operating system) he had a front row seat to when all the now common ways software projects go wrong were novel and new. He collected these observations in a book The Mythical Man-Month which should still be required reading for software engineering courses today. My edition was a newer reprint that included a later essay titled “No Silver Bullet” where Brooks looked at the effect that new tools can have on developer productivity.

即使在我还是个年轻程序员的时候，弗雷德·布鲁克斯（Fred Brooks）就已经是一位老前辈了。作为IBM System 360大型机（及其配套操作系统）的项目经理，他亲眼见证了所有如今常见的软件项目失败方式在当时是如何作为新鲜事物出现的。他将这些观察记录在《人月神话》（The Mythical Man-Month）一书中，这本书至今仍应是软件工程课程的必读书目。我手头的是一个较新的重印版，其中收录了一篇名为《没有银弹》（No Silver Bullet）的后续文章，布鲁克斯在文中探讨了新工具对开发者生产力的影响。

To think like a programmer, you must understand that the real world is complex. Programming can be best thought of as imposing simplified representations – we call them abstractions – on top of our messy reality to make it understandable by reducing complexity. This lets us generalize specific situations into layers that can be built on top of each other. For instance the specific action of putting peanut butter onto a piece of bread could be generalized into a spread(substance) method that could take peanut butter or cream cheese as an argument. And we could use these spread methods to create higher-level functions like create_pbj() and so on. Coding in a modern high-level programming language is like standing on top of a ziggurat of abstractions, where a single line of code could trigger millions of operations on multiple systems. It’s very exciting!

要像程序员一样思考，你必须明白现实世界是复杂的。编程可以被最好地理解为：在混乱的现实之上施加简化的表示——我们称之为“抽象”——通过降低复杂性使其变得可理解。这使我们能够将具体情况概括为可以相互叠加的层级。例如，将花生酱涂在面包上的具体动作，可以概括为一个 `spread(substance)` 方法，该方法可以接受花生酱或奶油奶酪作为参数。然后，我们可以使用这些 `spread` 方法来创建更高级的函数，如 `create_pbj()` 等等。使用现代高级编程语言编码，就像站在一座抽象的通天塔顶端，一行代码就能触发多个系统上的数百万次操作。这非常令人兴奋！

Now, what if we could keep going and abstract away the act of programming itself? This is the dream of agentic AI, where swarms of agents can be given tasks to implement on their own without supervision. Sounds great! But this is addressing what Brooks calls accidental complexity, the things that are complicated about writing code itself. In the time since the essay was written, software development has made great strides against this type of complexity. Instead of writing in low-level machine code, we can use modern dynamically interpreted languages which are compiled to assembly. Instead of remembering how to write a quick sort (trust me, you’re going to want to click that link) from scratch, I just need to call a sort method in a standard library. Instead of having to build a whole web application from scratch, I can use an existing framework. If I want to rename or restructure some code, my editor can help do that for me. AI seems like the latest iteration and some editors have already replaced their predictable old tooling for renaming and refactoring code with unpredictable AI agents. Sure, it might seem like rolling the dice, but how common is a critical failure anyway?

那么，如果我们能继续前进，把“编程”这一行为本身也抽象掉呢？这就是智能体AI（agentic AI）的梦想，即让成群的智能体在无人监督的情况下自行完成任务。听起来很棒！但这解决的是布鲁克斯所说的“偶然复杂性”，即编写代码本身所带来的复杂性。自那篇文章写成以来，软件开发在对抗这种复杂性方面取得了巨大进步。我们不再需要编写低级机器码，而是可以使用编译为汇编的现代动态解释语言。不再需要记住如何从零开始编写快速排序（相信我，你会想点开那个链接的），我只需要调用标准库中的 `sort` 方法。不再需要从头构建整个Web应用程序，我可以使用现有的框架。如果我想重命名或重构代码，我的编辑器可以帮我完成。AI似乎是这种演进的最新迭代，一些编辑器已经用不可预测的AI智能体取代了它们原本可预测的旧式重命名和重构工具。当然，这看起来像是在掷骰子，但关键性故障发生的频率到底有多高呢？

However, even as the better tooling has diminished accidental complexity, essential complexity still remains. There still is the complicated work of designing our abstractions and systems the right way, one that is elegant, clear and maintainable. And that complexity isn’t going anywhere. This type of work takes skill and experience and wisdom hard-won from system failures past. And, I’m not sure if LLM’s fancy autocomplete approach works so well with this type of complexity, which often isn’t so straightforward to solve. Maybe...

然而，即使更好的工具减少了偶然复杂性，本质复杂性依然存在。以正确的方式设计我们的抽象和系统——即优雅、清晰且可维护的方式——仍然是一项复杂的工作。这种复杂性不会消失。这类工作需要技能、经验以及从过去的系统故障中艰难获得的智慧。而且，我不确定LLM那种花哨的自动补全方法是否能很好地应对这种复杂性，因为这类问题往往并不那么容易解决。也许……
