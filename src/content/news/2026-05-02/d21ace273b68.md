# Useless but Safe? Benchmarking Utility Recovery with User Intent Clarification in Multi-Turn Conversations
# 无用但安全？在多轮对话中通过用户意图澄清来基准测试效用恢复

**Abstract:** Current LLM safety alignment techniques improve model robustness against adversarial attacks, but overlook whether and how LLMs can recover helpfulness when benign users clarify their intent. We introduce CarryOnBench, the first interactive benchmark that measures whether LLMs can revise their interpretation of user intent and recover utility, while remaining safe through multi-turn conversations.

**摘要：** 当前的大语言模型（LLM）安全对齐技术提高了模型抵御对抗性攻击的鲁棒性，但却忽略了当良性用户澄清其意图时，模型是否以及如何恢复有用性。我们引入了 CarryOnBench，这是首个交互式基准测试，旨在衡量大语言模型在多轮对话中能否在保持安全的同时，修正对用户意图的解读并恢复效用。

Starting from 398 seemingly harmful queries with benign underlying intents, we simulate 5,970 conversations by varying user follow-up sequences, evaluating 14 models on both intent-aligned utility and safety. CarryOnBench yields 1,866 different conversation flows of 4--12 turns, totaling 23,880 model responses. We design Ben-Util, a checklist-based metric that evaluates how well each model response fulfills the user's benign information need using atomic items.

我们从 398 个表面上具有危害性但底层意图为良性的查询出发，通过改变用户的后续追问序列，模拟了 5,970 场对话，并从意图对齐的效用和安全性两个维度对 14 个模型进行了评估。CarryOnBench 产生了 1,866 种不同的对话流程（每场 4 到 12 轮），总计 23,880 条模型回复。我们设计了 Ben-Util，这是一种基于检查表的指标，通过原子项来评估模型回复在多大程度上满足了用户的良性信息需求。

At turn one, models fulfill only 10.5--37.6% of the user's benign information need. When the same query includes the benign intent upfront, models fulfill 25.1--72.1%, confirming that models withhold information due to intent misinterpretation, not limited knowledge. With benign clarifications in multi-turn conversations, 13 of 14 models approach or exceed this single-turn baseline, yet recovery cost varies across models.

在第一轮对话中，模型仅能满足用户 10.5% 到 37.6% 的良性信息需求。当同样的查询在开头就明确了良性意图时，模型能满足 25.1% 到 72.1% 的需求，这证实了模型拒绝提供信息是由于对意图的误解，而非知识储备不足。通过多轮对话中的良性澄清，14 个模型中有 13 个达到了或超过了这一单轮基准，但不同模型的恢复成本各不相同。

We identify three failure modes invisible to single-turn evaluations: utility lock-in, where a model rarely updates despite clarification; unsafe recovery, where a model updates at disproportionate safety cost; and repetitive recovery, where a model recycles prior responses rather than providing new information. Moreover, conversations converge to similar harmfulness levels regardless of how conservative the model starts. These findings expose a gap that single-turn evaluations miss -- whether a model is appropriately cautious or simply unresponsive to clarified user intent.

我们识别出了三种在单轮评估中无法发现的失败模式：效用锁定（utility lock-in），即模型尽管收到澄清却很少更新回复；不安全恢复（unsafe recovery），即模型以不成比例的安全代价进行更新；以及重复恢复（repetitive recovery），即模型循环使用之前的回复而非提供新信息。此外，无论模型初始设置多么保守，对话最终都会收敛到相似的危害水平。这些发现揭示了单轮评估所忽略的差距——模型究竟是表现得适度谨慎，还是仅仅对用户澄清的意图反应迟钝。
