# From Euler to Dormand-Prince: ODE Solvers for Flow Matching Generative Models
# 从欧拉法到 Dormand-Prince：流匹配生成模型的常微分方程求解器

**Abstract:** Sampling from Flow Matching generative models requires solving an ordinary differential equation (ODE) whose computational cost is dominated by neural network forward passes. We derive four classical ODE solvers -- Euler, Explicit Midpoint, Classical Runge-Kutta (RK4), and Dormand-Prince 5(4) -- from first principles via Taylor expansion, implement them from scratch in PyTorch, and systematically benchmark their efficiency on Conditional Flow Matching tasks ranging from 2D toy distributions to MNIST digits.

**摘要：** 从流匹配（Flow Matching）生成模型中进行采样需要求解常微分方程（ODE），其计算成本主要由神经网络的前向传播决定。我们通过泰勒展开从第一性原理出发，推导了四种经典的 ODE 求解器——欧拉法（Euler）、显式中点法（Explicit Midpoint）、经典龙格-库塔法（RK4）以及 Dormand-Prince 5(4) 法，并在 PyTorch 中从零实现了这些算法。此外，我们还在从 2D 玩具分布到 MNIST 手写数字等条件流匹配任务上，系统地评估了它们的效率。

On the quantitative side, we use sliced Wasserstein distance to construct NFE-quality Pareto frontiers, finding that RK4 at 80 function evaluations achieves sample quality comparable to Euler at 200. Beyond reproducing known convergence rates, we report two empirical observations: (1) the Jacobian eigenvalue spectrum of the learned velocity field stiffens sharply near t=1, explaining why the adaptive Dormand-Prince solver automatically concentrates its step budget at the end of the trajectory; (2) the quality gap between low-order and high-order solvers widens for undertrained and smaller models, indicating that solver choice matters most when the model is imperfect. Code and all experiment scripts are publicly available.

在量化分析方面，我们利用切片 Wasserstein 距离（sliced Wasserstein distance）构建了 NFE（函数评估次数）与质量的帕累托前沿（Pareto frontiers），发现 RK4 在 80 次函数评估下的采样质量与欧拉法在 200 次评估下的质量相当。除了复现已知的收敛速率外，我们还报告了两个实证观察结果：（1）学习到的速度场的雅可比矩阵特征值谱在 t=1 附近急剧变硬（stiffens），这解释了为什么自适应的 Dormand-Prince 求解器会自动将步长预算集中在轨迹的末端；（2）对于训练不足或较小的模型，低阶求解器与高阶求解器之间的质量差距会拉大，这表明当模型本身不完美时，求解器的选择尤为重要。代码及所有实验脚本均已公开。
