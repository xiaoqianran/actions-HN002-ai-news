### Inexpensive seafloor-hopping submersibles could stoke deep-sea science—and mining
### 低成本海底跳跃潜水器或将推动深海科学——以及采矿业

Last week, two oblong neon submersibles started to descend nearly 6,000 meters into the Pacific Ocean. Throughout the rest of May, they will map the seafloor in search of critical mineral deposits. If all goes well, the vehicles, built by Orpheus Ocean, could help scientists probe the vastly understudied deep sea—and the resources it holds—at a fraction of the cost of existing systems. But the same submersibles are also attracting deep-sea mining companies, raising concerns about environmental impacts. Find out why they’re drawing so much attention. —Hannah Richter
上周，两台长方形的霓虹色潜水器开始下潜至太平洋近 6000 米深处。在五月余下的时间里，它们将绘制海底地图，寻找关键矿藏。如果一切顺利，这些由 Orpheus Ocean 制造的潜水器将能以现有系统一小部分的成本，帮助科学家探测研究极少的深海及其蕴藏的资源。但这些潜水器也吸引了深海采矿公司的注意，引发了对环境影响的担忧。了解它们为何备受关注。——Hannah Richter

### The new war room: 10 Things That Matter in AI Right Now
### 新的作战室：当下 AI 领域最重要的 10 件事

A new kind of system has entered the war room: conversational AI tools that commanders turn to not just for analysis, but for advice. One US defense official told MIT Technology Review that personnel might give these advice engines a list of potential targets to help decide which to strike first. China is commissioning similar tools too. But as the systems gain traction, they’re also sparking concerns about AI-generated errors, a lack of transparency, and Big Tech gaining undue influence over what information gets seen. Here’s how these AI advice engines could impact the battlefield. —James O'Donnell
一种新型系统已进入作战室：指挥官不仅将其用于分析，还用于寻求建议的对话式 AI 工具。一位美国国防官员告诉《麻省理工科技评论》，人员可能会向这些建议引擎提供一份潜在目标清单，以帮助决定优先打击哪个目标。中国也在委托开发类似的工具。但随着这些系统逐渐普及，它们也引发了对 AI 生成错误、缺乏透明度以及大型科技公司对信息获取产生不当影响的担忧。了解这些 AI 建议引擎将如何影响战场。——James O'Donnell

### MIT Technology Review Narrated: is fake grass a bad idea?
### 《麻省理工科技评论》播客：人造草坪是个坏主意吗？

The AstroTurf wars are far from over. In 2001, Americans installed just over 7 million square meters of synthetic turf. By 2024, that number was 79 million square meters—enough to carpet all of Manhattan and then some. The increase worries folks who study microplastics and environmental pollution. While the plastic-making industry insists that synthetic fields are safe if properly installed, lots of researchers think that isn’t so. —Douglas Main
人造草坪之争远未结束。2001 年，美国人安装了约 700 万平方米的人造草坪。到 2024 年，这一数字达到了 7900 万平方米——足以铺满整个曼哈顿还有余。这一增长令研究微塑料和环境污染的人士感到担忧。尽管塑料制造业坚称只要安装得当，人造草坪就是安全的，但许多研究人员认为事实并非如此。——Douglas Main
