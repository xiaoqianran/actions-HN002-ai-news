# Deconstruct Any Metric with a Few Simple ‘What’ Questions
# 用几个简单的“什么”问题拆解任何指标

Data Science: What you see is rarely what you get with flashy dashboards and data storytelling.
数据科学：在华丽的仪表板和数据叙事中，你所看到的往往并非真相。

When you see a bold claim like “The improvement is 5x” [FULL STOP], it’s designed to make you think, “That’s impressive; it must be worth my time and money.” However, a standalone statement like that is a red flag, and knowing how to interrogate vague metrics is a foundational skill for anyone who wants to separate real value from clever marketing. That’s why I invite you to put on your data analyst goggles and uncover the context needed to get insights you can act on.
当你看到诸如“提升了 5 倍”这样大胆的断言时，它旨在让你觉得：“这太令人印象深刻了，一定值得我的时间和金钱。”然而，这种孤立的陈述是一个危险信号。对于任何想要从精明的营销中区分出真实价值的人来说，学会如何审视模糊的指标是一项基本技能。因此，我邀请你戴上数据分析师的眼镜，去挖掘那些获取可执行洞察所需的背景信息。

The popularisation of analytics followed a similar path to psychology, moving from specialist guidance to self-help books and eventually to blog posts, YouTube videos, and inspirational Instagram quotes stamped on T-shirts. The whole idea was to pour our pain into words we could proudly wear on the best piece of clothing out there — the hoodie — and finally explain to the world what an analyst (me!) does, thinking it explains the analytics field, too.
分析学的普及过程与心理学类似，从专家指导转向自助书籍，最终演变为博客文章、YouTube 视频以及印在 T 恤上的励志 Instagram 名言。其核心理念是将我们的痛苦转化为文字，自豪地穿在最好的服装——连帽衫上，并最终向世界解释分析师（我！）的工作，以为这样也能解释分析领域本身。

Of course, for the folks who caught me on a laundry day, I had a simple explanation of analytics ready to go, and shared that analytics is “What you see is rarely what you get,” while statistics, on the other hand, is “The truth is out there” (as stated in The X-Files). Then I added that I’m always enthusiastic about searching for the “truth,” referencing my six years of Ivory Tower experience, but I prefer to swim in analytical waters now.
当然，对于那些在我洗衣服时遇到我的人，我准备了一个简单的解释：分析学是“你所看到的往往并非真相”，而统计学则是“真相就在那里”（正如《X 档案》中所说）。接着我补充道，我一直热衷于寻找“真相”，这参考了我六年的象牙塔经验，但我现在更喜欢在分析的海洋中遨游。

That’s why I stand by the statement: Flashy dashboards backed with data storytelling often aim to obfuscate the untrained eye by presenting cherry-picked insights. Meanwhile, the trained eye knows something more is hiding behind the metrics whenever a statement like this shows up: “The improvement is 5x” Followed by the full stop.
这就是我坚持这一观点的原因：由数据叙事支撑的华丽仪表板，往往通过呈现精心挑选的洞察来迷惑未经训练的眼睛。与此同时，受过训练的眼睛知道，每当出现“提升了 5 倍”这样后面跟着句号的陈述时，指标背后一定隐藏着更多信息。

I don’t know about you, but it hurts when I look at that sentence knowing someone dared to call it a success metric. For the sake of the common sense I hope still exists in the current AI limbo, let’s rewrite the above statement the way a metric should be presented by adding…
我不知道你怎么想，但当我看到有人竟敢称其为成功指标时，我感到很痛心。为了在当前人工智能的混沌状态中保留一点常识，让我们通过添加以下内容，将上述陈述重写为指标应有的呈现方式……

### #1: The dimensions
### #1：维度

I know a few of my friends who are still Simon Sinek followers would say, “Start with why.” 🙂 Nope. I started with “what,” and asked, “The improvement of what?” I dared because the first thing that went through my mind after seeing the word “improvement” hanging like that was: print(“The improvement is 5x.”) And that’s no way to get insights anyone can act on, is it?
我知道我的一些仍然追随西蒙·斯涅克（Simon Sinek）的朋友会说：“从‘为什么’开始。” 🙂 不。我从“什么”开始，并问道：“什么提升了？”我之所以敢这么问，是因为看到“提升”这个词孤零零地放在那里，我脑海中浮现的第一个念头就是：print(“提升了 5 倍。”) 这根本无法获得任何可执行的洞察，不是吗？

But imagine for a second the statement was formulated as “The model accuracy improvement is 5x.” If that had been the case, I would have pictured something different and envisioned a small table with specific performance dimensions, such as model accuracy, and their exact logged measurements. However, to be sure the “5x” wasn’t pulled out of thin air, the second dimension missing from this imaginary table of mine is the date/datetime. Which would make our statement sound like: “The monthly model accuracy improvement is 5x.”
但试想一下，如果该陈述表述为“模型准确率提升了 5 倍”。如果是这样，我会想象出不同的画面，并构思出一个包含特定性能维度（如模型准确率）及其精确记录测量值的小表格。然而，为了确保“5 倍”不是凭空捏造的，我这个虚构表格中缺失的第二个维度是日期/时间。这会让我们的陈述听起来像：“月度模型准确率提升了 5 倍。”

Now, that’s better, or at least it feels this way, because we can claim we derived the improvement by comparing model accuracy logs across monthly runs. But, to understand this “5x”, or any improvement whatsoever, another important missing piece of information is…
现在，这好多了，或者至少感觉好多了，因为我们可以声称是通过比较每月运行的模型准确率日志得出的提升。但是，要理解这个“5 倍”或任何形式的提升，另一个重要的缺失信息是……

### #2: The baseline
### #2：基准

Which is why I will continue with my “what” questions by asking, “The improvement from what?” A “5x improvement” really does sound amazing until we realise the baseline model accuracy for predicting the exact right outcome out of a hundred possibilities was 1% last month, and now it’s 5%. If we saw the raw value of 5%, we would know it means the model’s predictions are still wrong 95% of the time, and wouldn’t consider it a kind of improvement that should drive our actions.
这就是为什么我将继续我的“什么”提问，问道：“从什么基准开始提升的？”“提升 5 倍”听起来确实很棒，直到我们意识到，在一百种可能性中预测出正确结果的基准模型准确率上个月是 1%，而现在是 5%。如果我们看到 5% 的原始值，我们就会知道这意味着模型在 95% 的情况下仍然是错误的，也就不会认为这是一种应该驱动我们行动的提升。

That’s why we often won’t get presented with the raw numbers, but simply a “5x” because it looks great on a dashboard. Having this information, we can rewrite our statement again: “The monthly model accuracy improvement is 5x, growing from a baseline of 1% to 5%.” That looks better again. Still, seeing a specific time period attached brings me to the next missing piece…
这就是为什么我们通常不会看到原始数字，而只是看到一个“5 倍”，因为它在仪表板上看起来很棒。有了这些信息，我们可以再次重写我们的陈述：“月度模型准确率提升了 5 倍，从 1% 的基准增长到 5%。”这看起来又好了一些。然而，看到附带的特定时间段，让我引出了下一个缺失的部分……

### #3: The comparison period
### #3：比较周期

Which leads me to my last “what” question: “The improvement compared to what period?”
这引出了我的最后一个“什么”问题：“与哪个周期相比的提升？”

Our statement still doesn’t tell us what kind of comparison it’s making — an evolution over time, a fixed-cadence comparison (month-over-month, quarter-over-quarter, year-over-year), or an arbitrary period comparison? To reframe: Is it a direct comparison to the previous month, or across several consecutive months? Maybe a year-over-year comparison, where this month was compared to the same month last year? Or is it just an arbitrary comparison between two hand-picked months?
我们的陈述仍然没有告诉我们它在进行什么样的比较——是随时间的演变、固定周期的比较（环比、季比、同比），还是任意周期的比较？换句话说：是与上个月的直接比较，还是跨越几个连续月份的比较？也许是同比比较，即本月与去年同月相比？还是仅仅是两个精心挑选月份之间的任意比较？

Assuming we got an answer, we can tweak the previous version once more: “Comparing May 2026 to April 2026 results, the monthly model accuracy improvement is 5x, growing from a baseline of 1% to 5%.” Much better. The sentence finally tells us what improved, from what baseline, and over which period. And now, to the delight of some of my friends, I will ask…
假设我们得到了答案，我们可以再次微调之前的版本：“将 2026 年 5 月的结果与 2026 年 4 月进行比较，月度模型准确率提升了 5 倍，从 1% 的基准增长到 5%。”好多了。这句话终于告诉了我们什么提升了、从什么基准提升的，以及在哪个周期内提升的。现在，为了让我的一些朋友高兴，我将问……

### The “why” questions
### “为什么”问题

To wrap up, the two “why” questions I would ask when served with a statement like “The improvement is 5x” are:
最后，当面对“提升了 5 倍”这样的陈述时，我会问的两个“为什么”问题是：

**#1: Why should this metric concern me?**
**#1：为什么这个指标与我有关？**
The metrics that drive the presenter’s decisions aren’t always the ones that drive yours, and the same goes for the level of detail. What’s enough for them to decide often isn’t enough for you to act. So the next time someone presents a statement similar to the one above, ask them who it is for and which actions it’s supposed to drive.
驱动演示者决策的指标并不总是驱动你决策的指标，细节程度也是如此。对他们来说足以做出决定的信息，往往不足以让你采取行动。所以下次当有人提出类似上述的陈述时，问问他们这是为谁准备的，以及它应该驱动哪些行动。

**#2：为什么忽略了“什么”问题？**
