# Query2Uncertainty: Robust Uncertainty Quantification and Calibration for 3D Object Detection under Distribution Shift
# Query2Uncertainty：分布偏移下 3D 物体检测的鲁棒不确定性量化与校准

**Abstract:** Reliable uncertainty estimation for 3D object detection is critical for deploying safe autonomous systems, yet modern detectors remain poorly calibrated, especially under distribution shifts.
**摘要：** 可靠的不确定性估计对于部署安全的自动驾驶系统至关重要，然而现代检测器的校准效果仍然不佳，尤其是在面临分布偏移（distribution shifts）时。

Although post-hoc calibration methods address this issue and provide improved calibration for in-distribution tests, they fail to adapt in distribution-shifted scenarios.
尽管事后校准（post-hoc calibration）方法可以解决这一问题，并为分布内（in-distribution）测试提供更好的校准效果，但它们在分布偏移场景下往往无法适应。

In this work, we address this issue and introduce a density-aware calibration method that couples post-hoc calibrators with the feature density of latent object queries from DETR-style 3D object detectors.
在这项工作中，我们针对这一问题提出了一种密度感知校准方法，将事后校准器与 DETR 风格 3D 物体检测器中潜在物体查询（latent object queries）的特征密度相结合。

These queries form a compact, location and class-aware feature, ideal for density estimation, allowing our approach to adjust model confidences in distribution-shift scenarios.
这些查询构成了一种紧凑的、具备位置和类别感知能力的特征，非常适合进行密度估计，从而使我们的方法能够在分布偏移场景下调整模型置信度。

By fitting a density estimator on these query features, our approach jointly recalibrates both classification and bounding box regression uncertainties.
通过在这些查询特征上拟合密度估计器，我们的方法能够联合重新校准分类和边界框回归的不确定性。

On both a multi-view camera and LiDAR-based detector, our approach consistently outperforms standard post-hoc methods in both in-distribution and distribution-shifted scenarios.
在多视角相机和基于激光雷达（LiDAR）的检测器上，我们的方法在分布内和分布偏移场景中均持续优于标准的事后校准方法。
