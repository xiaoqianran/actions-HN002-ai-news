### SAT: Sequential Agent Tuning for Coordinator Free Plug and Play Multi-LLM Training with Monotonic Improvement Guarantees
### SAT：用于无需协调器的即插即用多大模型训练的序列智能体微调，具备单调改进保证

**Abstract:** Large language models (LLMs) with a large number of parameters achieve strong performance but are often prohibitively expensive to deploy. Recent work explores using teams of smaller, more efficient LLMs that collectively match or even outperform a single large model. However, jointly updating multiple agents introduces compounding distribution shifts, making coordination and stability during training difficult.

**摘要：** 拥有海量参数的大语言模型（LLM）虽然性能强大，但部署成本往往高得令人望而却步。近期的研究探索了使用由更小、更高效的 LLM 组成的团队，通过协作来达到甚至超越单个大模型的性能。然而，联合更新多个智能体会引入复合分布偏移，使得训练过程中的协调与稳定性变得困难。

We address this by introducing Sequential Agent Tuning (SAT), a coordinator-free training paradigm. SAT represents the team as a factorized policy and employs block-coordinate updates over agents, enabling scalable, decentralized training without a central controller. Specifically, we develop a sequence-aware, on-policy advantage estimator that conditions on the evolving team policy, coupled with per-agent KL trust regions that isolate occupancy drift.

为了解决这一问题，我们引入了序列智能体微调（Sequential Agent Tuning, SAT），这是一种无需协调器的训练范式。SAT 将团队表示为一种因子化策略，并对智能体采用块坐标更新，从而实现了无需中央控制器的可扩展、去中心化训练。具体而言，我们开发了一种序列感知、同策略（on-policy）的优势估计器，该估计器以不断演进的团队策略为条件，并结合了针对每个智能体的 KL 信任域，以隔离占用漂移（occupancy drift）。

Theoretically, this framework provides two critical guarantees. First, it ensures monotonic improvement, stabilizing the training process. Second, it establishes provable plug-and-play invariance: any agent can be upgraded to a stronger model without retraining the rest of the team, with a formal guarantee that the performance bound improves.

从理论上讲，该框架提供了两个关键保证。首先，它确保了单调改进，从而稳定了训练过程。其次，它建立了可证明的即插即用不变性：任何智能体都可以升级为更强的模型，而无需重新训练团队中的其他成员，并从形式上保证了性能边界的提升。

Empirically, a team of three 4B agents (12B total) trained with SAT surpasses the much larger Qwen3-32B on AIME24/25 benchmarks by 3.9% on average. We validate our plug-and-play theory by swapping in two 8B agents, which boosts the composite score by 10.4%. We provide code and appendix of proof at this https URL.

实验结果表明，由三个 4B 智能体（总计 12B）组成的团队在经过 SAT 训练后，在 AIME24/25 基准测试中的表现平均超过了规模大得多的 Qwen3-32B 模型 3.9%。我们通过替换两个 8B 智能体验证了我们的即插即用理论，结果显示综合得分提升了 10.4%。我们已在指定网址提供了代码及证明附录。
