# Are Flat Minima an Illusion?
# 平坦极小值（Flat Minima）是一个错觉吗？

Neural networks that land in flat regions of the loss landscape tend to generalise better than those in sharp regions. Sharpness-Aware Minimisation exploits this to improve generalisation. But function-preserving reparameterisation can inflate the Hessian of any minimum by two orders of magnitude without changing a single prediction. If the geometry of weight space can be manufactured from nothing, it cannot be the cause of anything. In other words, flat is simple and simplicity depends on encoding.

神经网络如果收敛于损失函数地形中的平坦区域，通常比收敛于尖锐区域具有更好的泛化能力。“锐度感知最小化”（Sharpness-Aware Minimisation）正是利用这一点来提升泛化性能。然而，通过保持函数不变的重参数化（reparameterisation），可以在不改变任何预测结果的情况下，将任何极小值的海森矩阵（Hessian）放大两个数量级。如果权重空间的几何结构可以凭空捏造，那么它就不可能是任何现象的根本原因。换句话说，“平坦”意味着“简单”，而“简单”取决于编码方式。

Here I show that the actual driver is weakness, the volume of completions compatible with the learned function in the learner's embodied language. Weakness is reparameterisation-invariant because it is defined over what the network does, not how it is parameterised. I prove weakness is minimax-optimal under exchangeable demands, and that PAC-Bayes bounds work because they correlate with it.

在此，我证明了真正的驱动因素是“弱度”（weakness），即在学习者的具体语言中，与所学函数兼容的补全空间体积。“弱度”具有重参数化不变性，因为它定义于网络的“行为”而非其“参数化方式”。我证明了在可交换需求下，“弱度”是极小极大最优的（minimax-optimal），并且 PAC-Bayes 界之所以有效，是因为它们与“弱度”相关。

On MNIST, the large-batch generalisation advantage vanishes as training data grows, from $+1.6\%$ at $n = 2{,}000$ to $+0.02\%$ at $n = 60{,}000$. A quantity whose predictive power depends on how much data you have is not a cause but a confounder. I run head-to-heads on 100 networks with identical architecture and training. For MNIST weakness predicts generalisation ($\rho = +0.374$, $p = 0.00012$), sharpness anticorrelates ($\rho = -0.226$) and simplicity predicts nothing ($p = 0.848$). For Fashion-MNIST ($\rho = +0.384$, $p = 8.15 \times 10^{-5}$), though simplicity is at least somewhat predictive there. Simplicity is dataset dependent, whereas weakness is invariant. Flat minima were never the answer.

在 MNIST 数据集上，随着训练数据量的增加，大批量训练带来的泛化优势会逐渐消失，从 $n = 2{,}000$ 时的 $+1.6\%$ 降至 $n = 60{,}000$ 时的 $+0.02\%$。一个预测能力取决于数据量多少的量，不是原因，而是混杂因素。我针对 100 个具有相同架构和训练过程的网络进行了对比测试。结果显示，对于 MNIST，弱度可以预测泛化能力（$\rho = +0.374$, $p = 0.00012$），锐度呈负相关（$\rho = -0.226$），而简单性则毫无预测力（$p = 0.848$）。对于 Fashion-MNIST，弱度同样有效（$\rho = +0.384$, $p = 8.15 \times 10^{-5}$），尽管简单性在那里至少具有一定的预测性。简单性依赖于数据集，而弱度是不变的。平坦极小值从来都不是问题的答案。
