# From Canopy to Collision: A Hybrid Predictive Framework for Identifying Risk Factors in Tree-Involved Traffic Crashes
# 从树冠到碰撞：识别树木相关交通事故风险因素的混合预测框架

**Abstract:** Tree-involved crashes represent a critical subset of run-off-road (ROR) collisions, often resulting in fatal or severe injuries due to high-energy impacts. This study develops a comprehensive analytical framework to identify and quantify risk factors contributing to crash severity in tree-involved collisions using the Crash Report Sampling System (CRSS) database spanning 2020-2023.

**摘要：** 树木相关碰撞是偏离车道（ROR）事故中的一个关键子集，由于高能量冲击，往往会导致致命或严重的伤害。本研究利用 2020 年至 2023 年的事故报告抽样系统（CRSS）数据库，开发了一个综合分析框架，旨在识别并量化导致树木相关碰撞事故严重程度的风险因素。

The modeling framework follows a multi-step process. First, a machine learning based classification model (CatBoost) identifies key factors associated with binary crash injury severity (KA: fatal or incapacitating injury versus BC: non-incapacitating or possible injury). Second, SHapley Additive exPlanations (SHAP) tool is used to quantify and visualize the marginal effects of top influential factors on crash severity. Third, a binary logistic regression model estimates factor effects and validates SHAP-derived importance measures. Finally, SHAP interaction plots examine the combined effects of key contributing factors.

该建模框架遵循多步骤流程。首先，使用基于机器学习的分类模型（CatBoost）识别与二元事故伤害严重程度相关的关键因素（KA：致命或丧失行为能力的伤害，对比 BC：非丧失行为能力或可能的伤害）。其次，利用 SHapley 加性解释（SHAP）工具量化并可视化各主要影响因素对事故严重程度的边际效应。第三，通过二元逻辑回归模型评估因素影响，并验证 SHAP 得出的重要性指标。最后，利用 SHAP 交互图分析关键影响因素的综合效应。

Results reveal restraint non-use as the most influential predictor, with unrestrained occupants nearly three times more likely to experience severe outcomes due to ejection risk. Vehicle age, speeding violations, and driver impairment demonstrate substantial effects, reflecting reduced crashworthiness, increased impact forces, and reduced control capabilities.

研究结果显示，未使用安全约束装置是最具影响力的预测指标；由于存在被甩出车外的风险，未系安全带的乘员发生严重后果的可能性几乎是其他人的三倍。车辆使用年限、超速违规和驾驶员受损（如酒驾或药驾）表现出显著影响，反映了车辆耐撞性降低、冲击力增加以及驾驶控制能力下降等问题。

Critical interactions emerge between lighting conditions and vehicle age, speeding and lighting conditions, restraint use and vehicle age, and road surface and speeding, demonstrating additive risk effects with specific interactions. These findings provide critical insights for targeted safe system-based interventions, including enhanced seat belt enforcement, speed management in reduced visibility conditions, and vehicle fleet modernization.

研究还发现光照条件与车辆年限、超速与光照条件、安全约束使用与车辆年限、以及路面状况与超速之间存在关键的交互作用，证明了特定交互下风险的叠加效应。这些发现为基于安全系统的针对性干预措施提供了关键见解，包括加强安全带执法、在低能见度条件下进行速度管理以及推进车辆更新换代。
