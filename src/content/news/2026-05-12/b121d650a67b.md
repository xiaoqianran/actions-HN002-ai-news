### What problem does the paper solve?
### 论文解决了什么问题？

Traditional Bag of Words models are useful for classification, but they do not learn meaningful relationships between words. For example, the words *wonderful* and *amazing* should be close because they express similar meaning and similar sentiment. On the other hand, *wonderful* and *terrible* may appear in similar movie review contexts, but they express opposite sentiments. The goal of the paper is to learn word vectors that capture both semantic similarity and sentiment orientation.
传统的词袋模型（Bag of Words）虽然对分类有用，但无法学习词与词之间有意义的关系。例如，“wonderful”（精彩的）和“amazing”（极好的）应该在向量空间中很接近，因为它们表达了相似的含义和情感。另一方面，“wonderful”和“terrible”（糟糕的）可能出现在相似的影评语境中，但它们表达的情感却截然相反。本文的目标是学习能够同时捕捉语义相似性和情感倾向的词向量。

### Data structure
### 数据结构

The dataset contains: 25,000 labeled training reviews or documents, 50,000 unlabeled training reviews, and 25,000 labeled test reviews. The labeled reviews are polarized: Negative reviews have ratings from 1 to 4; Positive reviews have ratings from 7 to 10. The ratings are linearly mapped to the interval [0, 1], which allows the model to treat sentiment as a continuous probability of positive polarity.
该数据集包含：25,000 条带标签的训练影评、50,000 条无标签的训练影评，以及 25,000 条带标签的测试影评。带标签的影评具有极性：负面评价评分为 1 到 4 分；正面评价评分为 7 到 10 分。评分被线性映射到 [0, 1] 区间，这使得模型可以将情感视为正面极性的连续概率。

```text
aclImdb/
├── train/
│   ├── pos/ "0_10.txt" -> review #0, 10 stars, very positive
│   │    "1_7.txt" -> review #1, 7 stars, positive
│   ├── neg/ "10_2.txt" -> review #10, 2 stars, very negative
│   │    "25_4.txt" -> review #25, 4 stars, negative
│   └── unsup/ "938_0.txt" -> review #938, 0 stars, unlabeled
└── test/
    ├── pos/ positive reviews, never seen during training
    └── neg/ negative reviews, never seen during training
```

We can therefore store each document in a Review class with the following attributes: text, stars, label, and bucket. Of course, it does not have to be a class specifically named Review. Any object can be used as long as it provides at least these attributes.
因此，我们可以将每篇文档存储在一个 `Review` 类中，该类包含以下属性：`text`（文本）、`stars`（星级）、`label`（标签）和 `bucket`（桶/类别）。当然，不必非要命名为 `Review` 类，只要对象至少包含这些属性即可。

### Vocabulary construction
### 词表构建

The paper builds a fixed vocabulary by first ignoring the 50 most frequent terms, then keeping the next 5,000 most frequent tokens. No stemming is applied. No standard stopword removal is used. This is important because some stopwords, especially negations, can carry sentiment information.
论文通过以下方式构建固定词表：首先忽略出现频率最高的 50 个词，然后保留接下来的 5,000 个高频词。不进行词干提取，也不使用标准的停用词过滤。这一点很重要，因为某些停用词（尤其是否定词）可能携带情感信息。

Before building this vocabulary, we first need to look at the raw data. We noticed that the reviews are not fully cleaned. Some documents contain HTML tags, so we remove them during the data loading step. We also remove punctuation attached to words, such as ".", ",", "!", or "?". This is a slight difference from the original paper. The authors keep some non-word tokens because they may help capture sentiment. For example, "!" or ":-)" can carry emotional information. In our implementation, we choose to remove this punctuation and later evaluate how much this decision affects the final model performance.
在构建词表之前，我们需要先查看原始数据。我们注意到影评并未完全清洗。有些文档包含 HTML 标签，因此我们在数据加载阶段将其移除。我们还移除了附着在单词上的标点符号，如 "."、","、"!" 或 "?"。这与原论文略有不同。原作者保留了一些非单词标记，因为它们可能有助于捕捉情感。例如，"!" 或 ":-)" 可能携带情感信息。在我们的实现中，我们选择移除这些标点，并稍后评估这一决定对最终模型性能的影响。

When working with text data, the next question is always the same: How should we represent documents and words numerically? The authors start by collecting all tokens from the training set, including both labeled and unlabeled reviews. We can think of this as putting all words from the training documents into one large basket. Then, to represent words in a space where we can train a model, they build a set of words called the vocabulary.
处理文本数据时，下一个问题总是相同的：我们应该如何用数字表示文档和单词？作者首先收集训练集中的所有标记，包括带标签和无标签的影评。我们可以将其想象成把训练文档中的所有单词放入一个大篮子里。然后，为了在可训练模型的空间中表示单词，他们构建了一个称为“词表”的单词集合。

The authors build a dictionary that maps each token, which we will loosely call a word, to its frequency. This frequency is simply the number of times the token appears in the full training set, including both labeled and unlabeled reviews. Then they select the 5,000 most frequent words, after removing the 50 most frequent terms. These 5,000 words form the vocabulary V. Each word in V will correspond to one column of the representation matrix R.
作者构建了一个字典，将每个标记（我们姑且称之为单词）映射到其频率。该频率即为该标记在整个训练集（包括带标签和无标签影评）中出现的次数。然后，在剔除出现频率最高的 50 个词后，他们选择了频率最高的 5,000 个词。这 5,000 个词构成了词表 V。V 中的每个单词对应表示矩阵 R 的一列。

The authors choose to represent each word in a 50-dimensional space. Therefore, the matrix R has the following shape:
作者选择在 50 维空间中表示每个单词。因此，矩阵 R 的形状如下：

$$R \in \mathbb{R}^{\beta = 50 \times |V| = 5000}$$

Each column of R is the vector representation of one word: $\phi_w = R_w$
R 的每一列都是一个单词的向量表示：$\phi_w = R_w$
