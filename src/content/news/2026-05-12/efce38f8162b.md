# A Wasserstein GAN-based climate scenario generator for risk management and insurance: the case of soil subsidence
# 基于 Wasserstein GAN 的气候情景生成器在风险管理与保险中的应用：以土壤沉降为例

According to the United Nations Office for Disaster Risk Reduction (2025), the average annual cost of natural catastrophes increased from 70--80 billion USD between 1970 and 2000 to 180--200 billion USD between 2001 and 2020.
根据联合国减少灾害风险办公室（2025年）的数据，自然灾害的年均损失成本已从1970年至2000年间的700亿至800亿美元，上升至2001年至2020年间的1800亿至2000亿美元。

Reports from organizations such as the IFOA and the WWF highlight the need for the insurance sector to adapt to this rapidly evolving context by developing medium- to long-term strategies that go beyond the one-year horizon of prudential regulations such as Solvency II.
英国精算师协会（IFOA）和世界自然基金会（WWF）等机构的报告强调，保险业必须通过制定超越“偿付能力II”（Solvency II）等审慎监管一年期视野的中长期战略，来适应这一快速变化的环境。

This paper introduces an artificial intelligence framework based on Conditional Generative Adversarial Networks (Conditional GANs) to generate future spatio-temporal trajectories of climatic indices.
本文引入了一种基于条件生成对抗网络（Conditional GANs）的人工智能框架，用于生成气候指标的未来时空轨迹。

The approach focuses on the Soil Wetness Index (SWI), a key indicator used in France to assess drought severity. Drought accounts for approximately 30% of the indemnities paid under the French natural catastrophe insurance scheme.
该方法聚焦于土壤湿度指数（SWI），这是法国评估干旱严重程度的关键指标。在法国自然灾害保险计划的赔付中，干旱约占赔付总额的30%。

The proposed model, SwiGAN, simulates plausible drought propagation patterns up to 2050 for a region of France particularly exposed to this hazard. By generating realistic sequences of SWI maps, SwiGAN provides insights into drought dynamics under climate change scenarios and supports the design of adaptive risk management and insurance strategies.
本文提出的模型 SwiGAN，模拟了法国某易受干旱影响地区直至2050年的干旱演变模式。通过生成逼真的 SWI 地图序列，SwiGAN 为理解气候变化情景下的干旱动态提供了洞察，并支持适应性风险管理和保险策略的设计。

The methodology is also generalizable to other climate-related perils and actuarial applications such as economic scenario generation.
该方法还可推广至其他与气候相关的灾害，以及经济情景生成等精算应用领域。
