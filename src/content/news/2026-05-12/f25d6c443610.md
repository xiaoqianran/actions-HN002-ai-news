# HumanNet: Scaling Human-centric Video Learning to One Million Hours
# HumanNet：将以人为中心的视频学习扩展至一百万小时

**Abstract:** Progress in embodied intelligence increasingly depends on scalable data infrastructure. While vision and language have scaled with internet corpora, learning physical interaction remains constrained by the lack of large, diverse, and richly annotated human activity data. We present HumanNet, a one-million-hour human-centric video corpus that captures how humans interact with the physical world at scale.

**摘要：** 具身智能的进步日益依赖于可扩展的数据基础设施。尽管视觉和语言模型已通过互联网语料库实现了规模化，但物理交互的学习仍受限于缺乏大规模、多样化且标注丰富的人类活动数据。我们提出了 HumanNet，这是一个包含一百万小时以人为中心视频的语料库，旨在捕捉人类如何与物理世界进行大规模交互。

HumanNet spans both first-person and third-person perspectives and covers fine-grained activities, human-object interactions, tool use, and long-horizon behaviors across diverse real-world environments. Beyond raw video, the dataset provides interaction-centric annotations, including captions, motion descriptions, and hand and body-related signals, enabling motion-aware and interaction-aware learning.

HumanNet 涵盖了第一人称和第三人称视角，包含了多样化现实环境中的细粒度活动、人机交互、工具使用以及长周期行为。除了原始视频外，该数据集还提供了以交互为中心的标注，包括字幕、动作描述以及手部和身体相关信号，从而支持动作感知和交互感知的学习。

Beyond scale, HumanNet introduces a systematic data curation paradigm for embodied learning, where human-centric filtering, temporal structuring, viewpoint diversity, and annotation enrichment are treated as first-class design principles. This design transforms unstructured internet video into a scalable substrate for representation learning, activity understanding, motion generation, and human-to-robot transfer.

除了规模之外，HumanNet 还引入了一种用于具身学习的系统化数据整理范式，将以人为中心的过滤、时间结构化、视角多样性和标注丰富化视为核心设计原则。这种设计将非结构化的互联网视频转化为可扩展的基底，用于表征学习、活动理解、动作生成以及人机迁移学习。

We conduct a first-step validation on the value of this design through controlled vision-language-action ablation: under a fixed set of validation data, continued training from the Qwen VLM model with 1000 hours of egocentric video drawn from HumanNet surpasses the continued training with 100 hours of real-robot data from Magic Cobot, indicating that egocentric human video could be a scalable and cost-effective substitute for robot data. By building this project, we aim to explore the opportunity to scale embodied foundation models using human-centric videos, rather than relying solely on robot-specific data.

我们通过受控的视觉-语言-动作消融实验对该设计的价值进行了初步验证：在固定的验证数据集下，使用来自 HumanNet 的 1000 小时第一人称视角视频对 Qwen VLM 模型进行持续训练，其效果超过了使用 Magic Cobot 的 100 小时真实机器人数据进行持续训练的效果。这表明，第一人称人类视频可以作为机器人数据的一种可扩展且具有成本效益的替代方案。通过构建该项目，我们旨在探索利用以人为中心的视频来扩展具身基础模型，而非仅仅依赖于机器人专用数据。
