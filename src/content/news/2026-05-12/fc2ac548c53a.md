# Using Transformers to Forecast Incredibly Rare Solar Flares
# 利用 Transformer 预测极罕见的太阳耀斑

**Introduction (X-45)**
Machine Learning forecasting fundamentally changes whenever we try to predict a very rare event. We must fundamentally shift what we are modelling to focus on tail events. From model performance metrics and target definition to the tail model and the transformer output heads, rare-event forecasting is difficult. Difficult yet worth it.

**引言 (X-45)**
每当我们试图预测极罕见事件时，机器学习预测的本质就会发生改变。我们必须从根本上转变建模对象，将重点放在“尾部事件”（tail events）上。从模型性能指标和目标定义，到尾部模型和 Transformer 输出头，罕见事件预测极具挑战性。虽然困难，但却意义重大。

The Halloween storms of 2003 began as a disturbance on the Sun, a single dark spot that created one of the strongest space weather events of the satellite era. Through late October to early November, a series of enormous active regions churned across the solar disk. This released powerful flares and clouds of magnetized plasma towards Earth. This event presented a uniquely aesthetic flair-up with radio-wave implications. Satellites malfunctioned, GPS and radio were disrupted, and airlines rerouted polar flights. According to NOAA, power grids worldwide were affected, with some currents exceeding 100 amps, leading to the Malmö Blackout in Sweden. At 20:07 UT, a power outage hit the region, leaving approximately 50,000 customers without electricity for 20 to 50 minutes.

2003 年的“万圣节风暴”始于太阳上的一次扰动，一个单一的黑子引发了卫星时代最强烈的空间天气事件之一。从 10 月下旬到 11 月初，一系列巨大的活动区在太阳圆盘上翻腾，向地球释放了强大的耀斑和磁化等离子体云。这次事件呈现出一种独特的视觉爆发，并对无线电波产生了影响。卫星发生故障，GPS 和无线电通信中断，航空公司不得不更改极地航线。据美国国家海洋和大气管理局（NOAA）称，全球电网受到影响，部分电流超过 100 安培，导致瑞典马尔默发生停电。世界时 20:07，该地区发生停电，约 5 万名用户断电 20 至 50 分钟。

**The Prediction Problem**
A paradoxical problem with catastrophes is that the more catastrophic they are, the rarer they tend to be. Think floods, snow-storms and avalanches. Every 50-year story happens once in fifty years. This is usually a good thing, but because of their rarity, they become incredibly hard to predict. There are several things that make predicting rare-events a particularly interesting challenge in machine learning:
* Our metrics for model evaluation must change
* Features need to be engineered from magnetism data
* Make a tail model to specifically capture rare events
* Combine the tail model with the full distribution model using a transformer

**预测难题**
灾难性事件存在一个悖论：灾难越严重，发生的频率往往越低。想想洪水、暴风雪和雪崩。所谓的“50 年一遇”确实每 50 年才发生一次。这通常是件好事，但由于其稀有性，它们变得极难预测。在机器学习中，预测罕见事件之所以成为一个特别有趣的挑战，原因有几点：
* 模型评估指标必须改变
* 需要从磁场数据中进行特征工程
* 构建专门捕捉罕见事件的“尾部模型”
* 使用 Transformer 将尾部模型与全分布模型相结合

A note on accuracy, which is typically a good metric for binary classification. We could achieve 99% accuracy by missing every single solar flare in 10,000 forecasts if we had only 100 major flares. We could simply guess. It won’t happen every single time. Accuracy = (10,000-100)/10,000 = 9900/10,000 = 0.99 = 99% True Positives = 0

关于准确率（Accuracy），它通常是二分类任务中一个很好的指标。但如果我们有 10,000 次预测，其中只有 100 次是重大耀斑，那么即使我们漏掉每一个耀斑，也能达到 99% 的准确率。我们完全可以瞎猜，虽然不会每次都对。准确率 = (10,000-100)/10,000 = 9900/10,000 = 0.99 = 99%，而真正预测正确的（True Positives）为 0。

**The Data**
If you’re interested in where this data comes from, all the data we have on solar flares comes from an altogether different layer of the sun than where the flare occurs. The data we have on solar flares comes from the Photosphere, the sun’s first visible layer. Flares occur in the Corona and Chromosphere. The data is collected by the Solar Dynamics Observatory (SDO), a NASA spacecraft that continuously observes the Sun to monitor its activity. Using the Helioseismic and Magnetic Imager (HMI).

**数据来源**
如果你对这些数据的来源感兴趣，我们拥有的所有太阳耀斑数据都来自太阳的一个与耀斑发生位置完全不同的层。我们拥有的太阳耀斑数据来自光球层（Photosphere），即太阳的第一可见层。而耀斑发生在日冕（Corona）和色球层（Chromosphere）。这些数据由 NASA 的太阳动力学天文台（SDO）收集，该航天器持续观测太阳以监测其活动，使用的是日震与磁场成像仪（HMI）。

Solar flare forecasting measures the magnetic field most directly at the photosphere, the Sun’s visible surface, while flare energy release occurs higher in the corona. Photospheric sunspot and magnetic-field data are therefore used to infer the buildup of coronal magnetic stress that can lead to reconnection and flares.

太阳耀斑预测最直接地测量光球层（太阳可见表面）的磁场，而耀斑能量的释放发生在更高的日冕层。因此，光球层的黑子和磁场数据被用来推断日冕磁应力的积累，这种应力可能导致磁重联和耀斑爆发。
