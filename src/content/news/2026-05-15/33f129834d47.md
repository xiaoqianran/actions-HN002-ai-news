# Runtime Monitoring of Perception-Based Autonomous Systems via Embedding Temporal Logic
# 通过嵌入时序逻辑对基于感知的自主系统进行运行时监控

**Abstract:** Runtime monitoring of autonomous systems traditionally relies on mapping continuous sensor observations to discrete logical propositions defined over low-dimensional state variables. This abstraction breaks down in perception-driven settings, where such mappings require additional learned modules that are often computationally expensive, brittle, and semantically misaligned.

**摘要：** 自主系统的运行时监控传统上依赖于将连续的传感器观测值映射到定义在低维状态变量上的离散逻辑命题。这种抽象在感知驱动的环境中会失效，因为此类映射需要额外的学习模块，而这些模块往往计算成本高昂、脆弱且语义不匹配。

In this work, we propose Embedding Temporal Logic (ETL), a temporal logic that performs monitoring directly in learned embedding spaces. ETL defines predicates through distances between observed embeddings and target embeddings derived from reference observations. This formulation allows specifications to capture high-level perceptual concepts, such as similarity to visual goals or avoidance of semantic regions, that are difficult or impossible to express using traditional predicates.

在这项工作中，我们提出了嵌入时序逻辑（Embedding Temporal Logic, ETL），这是一种直接在学习到的嵌入空间中执行监控的时序逻辑。ETL 通过观测到的嵌入与从参考观测中导出的目标嵌入之间的距离来定义谓词。这种表述方式使得规范能够捕捉到高层感知概念，例如与视觉目标的相似性或对语义区域的规避，而这些概念在使用传统谓词时是难以甚至无法表达的。

By composing these predicates with temporal operators, ETL naturally expresses temporally extended and sequential perceptual behaviors. We introduce ETL monitors for evaluating specifications over bounded embedding traces, along with a conformal calibration procedure that provides reliable and safety-oriented predicate evaluation. We evaluate our approach across multiple manipulation environments to show that ETL achieves strong empirical agreement with ground-truth semantics, including accurate monitoring of temporally composed behaviors.

通过将这些谓词与时序算子组合，ETL 可以自然地表达在时间上延伸的序列感知行为。我们引入了 ETL 监控器，用于评估有界嵌入轨迹上的规范，并结合了一套共形校准程序，以提供可靠且面向安全的谓词评估。我们在多个操作环境中评估了我们的方法，结果表明 ETL 与真实语义之间达到了极强的一致性，包括对时间组合行为的精确监控。
