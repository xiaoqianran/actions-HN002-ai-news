# The Counterintuitive Networking Decisions Behind OpenAI’s 131,000-GPU Training Fabric
# OpenAI 131,000 GPU 训练集群背后的反直觉网络决策

Artificial Intelligence. The Counterintuitive Networking Decisions Behind OpenAI’s 131,000-GPU Training Fabric. Static routes. Lossy Ethernet. No routing protocols. Inside the architectural decisions that make OpenAI's 131,000-GPU training fabric work. Five design decisions, each individually counterintuitive, that together explain how synchronous training at this scale is even possible.
人工智能。OpenAI 131,000 GPU 训练集群背后的反直觉网络决策。静态路由。有损以太网。无路由协议。深入探讨使 OpenAI 131,000 GPU 训练集群得以运行的架构决策。五个设计决策，每一个单独来看都违背直觉，但它们共同解释了为何在如此规模下进行同步训练成为可能。

Disable all routing protocols. Accept packet loss on purpose. Spray each transfer across hundreds of random paths. If someone handed you this list of design decisions for a network connecting 131,000 GPUs, you would assume it was written by someone who had never operated a production network. A consortium of OpenAI, AMD, Broadcom, Intel, Microsoft, and NVIDIA built exactly this — and quietly inverted three decades of consensus about how high-performance data center networks should work.
禁用所有路由协议。有意接受丢包。将每次传输分散到数百条随机路径上。如果有人把这份连接 131,000 个 GPU 的网络设计决策清单交给你，你肯定会认为这是出自一个从未运营过生产网络的人之手。然而，由 OpenAI、AMD、博通 (Broadcom)、英特尔、微软和英伟达组成的联盟恰恰构建了这样的系统，并悄然颠覆了过去三十年来关于高性能数据中心网络应如何运作的共识。

The protocol is called MRC, short for Multipath Reliable Connection. It was released on May 5, 2026 through the Open Compute Project. The accompanying research paper (Araujo et al., 2026) details its deployment across OpenAI’s largest NVIDIA GB200 supercomputers, including the Stargate site with Oracle Cloud Infrastructure in Abilene, Texas, and Microsoft’s Fairwater supercomputers. MRC has been used to train the latest frontier models behind ChatGPT and Codex.
该协议被称为 MRC，即“多路径可靠连接”（Multipath Reliable Connection）的缩写。它于 2026 年 5 月 5 日通过开放计算项目 (Open Compute Project) 发布。随附的研究论文 (Araujo et al., 2026) 详细介绍了其在 OpenAI 最大规模 NVIDIA GB200 超级计算机上的部署情况，包括位于德克萨斯州阿比林的 Oracle 云基础设施 Stargate 站点，以及微软的 Fairwater 超级计算机。MRC 已被用于训练 ChatGPT 和 Codex 背后的最新前沿模型。

What is most striking on close reading of the paper is something the press coverage has not surfaced: MRC effectively eliminates the entire Layer 3 control plane from the data center fabric. No OSPF. No BGP. No IS-IS. No FIB. The switches in the deployment maintain zero dynamic forwarding state. To the author’s knowledge, this is the most aggressive elimination of dynamic routing in any production AI training fabric publicly documented to date.
仔细阅读论文后最令人震惊的一点是媒体报道尚未揭示的内容：MRC 有效地从数据中心架构中彻底消除了整个三层 (Layer 3) 控制平面。没有 OSPF，没有 BGP，没有 IS-IS，也没有 FIB（转发信息库）。部署中的交换机不维护任何动态转发状态。据作者所知，这是迄今为止公开记录的所有生产级 AI 训练架构中，对动态路由最激进的消除。

The paper’s core argument is that at 100,000+ GPU scale, tail latency from network congestion and failures dominates training performance, and the conventional networking stack cannot solve this without fundamental changes to how packets move between GPUs. MRC is those fundamental changes, implemented in 800 Gb/s NICs from three different silicon vendors and deployed in production.
该论文的核心论点是：在 10 万个 GPU 以上的规模下，由网络拥塞和故障引起的尾部延迟主导了训练性能，而传统的网络协议栈如果不从根本上改变数据包在 GPU 之间的传输方式，就无法解决这一问题。MRC 正是这些根本性的变革，它已在来自三家不同芯片供应商的 800 Gb/s 网卡中实现并投入生产。

What makes MRC worth studying carefully is not that it is fast. It is that the design decisions behind it contradict several principles that the networking community has treated as settled for decades. Understanding why those decisions work at this scale, and where they might not, matters for anyone building or operating AI infrastructure.
MRC 值得仔细研究的原因不在于它有多快，而在于其背后的设计决策与网络社区几十年来视为定论的几项原则相矛盾。对于任何构建或运营 AI 基础设施的人来说，理解这些决策为何能在如此规模下奏效，以及它们在何处可能失效，至关重要。

The problem: one straggler blocks 100,000 GPUs. Synchronous pretraining runs in lock-step. Every training step involves millions of data transfers across thousands of GPUs performing a combination of tensor parallelism, pipeline parallelism, data parallelism, and expert parallelism. The step cannot advance until the slowest transfer completes. At 100,000 GPUs, the duration of each communication round is determined by the tail of the transfer latency distribution, not the mean.
问题所在：一个“掉队者”会阻塞 10 万个 GPU。同步预训练以步调一致的方式运行。每个训练步骤都涉及跨越数千个 GPU 的数百万次数据传输，执行张量并行、流水线并行、数据并行和专家并行的组合。在最慢的传输完成之前，步骤无法推进。在 10 万个 GPU 的规模下，每一轮通信的持续时间由传输延迟分布的尾部决定，而不是平均值。

The paper frames this precisely: “As computations scale, communication becomes increasingly outlier-dominated.” A single congested link, a single flow collision, a single switch buffer overflow can stall thousands of GPUs for milliseconds. At the hourly cost of 100,000 H100-class GPUs (roughly $300,000 per hour at cloud rates), a 10-millisecond stall that occurs once per training step and repeats across thousands of steps is not a rounding error. It is a line item.
论文对此进行了精确的描述：“随着计算规模的扩大，通信越来越受到异常值的影响。”单个拥塞的链路、单次流冲突、单个交换机缓冲区溢出都可能导致数千个 GPU 停顿数毫秒。以 10 万个 H100 级 GPU 的每小时成本（按云服务价格计算约为每小时 30 万美元）来看，每个训练步骤发生一次并重复数千次的 10 毫秒停顿，绝非四舍五入的误差，而是一笔巨大的开支。

Network failures compound the problem. At this scale, link flaps, optic failures, and switch reboots are not rare events. They are statistical certainties that occur multiple times per day across a fabric with hundreds of thousands of links. The paper reports a production incident where an optical transceiver on a T0 switch “suffered a glitch, and flapped all its four links in rapid succession,” affecting three active training nodes simultaneously. In a conventional network, this would have crashed the training job.
网络故障加剧了这一问题。在如此规模下，链路抖动、光模块故障和交换机重启并非罕见事件。在拥有数十万条链路的架构中，这些是每天都会发生多次的统计学必然。论文报告了一起生产事故：T0 交换机上的一个光收发器“出现故障，并迅速连续抖动了其所有四条链路”，同时影响了三个正在运行的训练节点。在传统网络中，这会导致训练任务崩溃。

MRC’s design goal was not just higher bandwidth. It was predictable bandwidth, even in the presence of failures, with a control plane simple enough that a small team can manage multiple supercomputers simultaneously.
MRC 的设计目标不仅仅是更高的带宽。它追求的是可预测的带宽，即使在出现故障时也能保持，并且其控制平面足够简单，使得一个小团队就能同时管理多台超级计算机。

Instead of treating an 800 Gb/s NIC as one fat pipe, MRC splits it into eight 100 Gb/s links, each connecting to a different switch. This creates eight parallel network planes, each operating independently. Consider a conventional approach.
