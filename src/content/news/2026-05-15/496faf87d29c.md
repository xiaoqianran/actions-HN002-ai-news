# OceanCBM: A Concept Bottleneck Model for Mechanistic Interpretability in Ocean Forecasting

**Title:** OceanCBM: A Concept Bottleneck Model for Mechanistic Interpretability in Ocean Forecasting
**标题：** OceanCBM：用于海洋预报机制可解释性的概念瓶颈模型

**Abstract:** Extreme ocean phenomena are challenging not only to predict but to diagnose, as accurate forecasts alone do not reveal the underlying physical drivers. While recent machine learning approaches achieve strong predictive skill, they remain largely opaque and provide limited guarantees of fidelity to ground-truth physics.
**摘要：** 极端海洋现象不仅难以预测，而且难以诊断，因为单纯的准确预报无法揭示其背后的物理驱动因素。尽管近期的机器学习方法取得了强大的预测能力，但它们在很大程度上仍是不透明的，且在符合真实物理规律方面提供的保证有限。

We introduce OceanCBM, the first concept bottleneck model (CBM) for spatiotemporal prediction and mechanistic interrogation of ocean dynamics. OceanCBM uses mixed supervision to predict mixed layer heat content, a key precursor of marine heatwaves, while routing information through an intermediate layer of prescribed concepts derived from geophysical fluid dynamics and a 'free' concept.
我们引入了 OceanCBM，这是首个用于海洋动力学时空预测和机制探究的概念瓶颈模型（CBM）。OceanCBM 利用混合监督来预测混合层热含量（海洋热浪的关键先兆），同时通过一个由地球物理流体动力学导出的预设概念中间层以及一个“自由”概念来传递信息。

This design imposes soft physical structure without over-constraining the model, and the free concept both regularizes concept predictions and captures residual physical processes. Across ensemble initializations, we show that mixed supervision yields consistent mechanistic representations, whereas prediction-only and prescription-only baselines learn highly variable latent structures despite similar predictive performance.
这种设计在不过度约束模型的前提下施加了软物理结构，而“自由”概念既规范了概念预测，又捕捉到了残余的物理过程。通过集成初始化实验，我们证明了混合监督能够产生一致的机制表征，而仅基于预测或仅基于预设的基准模型，尽管预测性能相似，却学习到了高度可变的潜在结构。

OceanCBM achieves interpretable, physically grounded representations without sacrificing skill, explicitly characterizing the interpretability-performance trade-off.
OceanCBM 在不牺牲预测能力的情况下实现了可解释且具有物理基础的表征，明确刻画了可解释性与性能之间的权衡。
