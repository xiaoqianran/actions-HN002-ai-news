# MorphOPC: Advancing Mask Optimization with Multi-scale Hierarchical Morphological Learning
# MorphOPC：利用多尺度分层形态学学习推进掩模优化

**Abstract:** As feature sizes shrink to the nanometer scale, accurately transferring circuit patterns from photomasks to silicon wafers becomes increasingly challenging. Optical proximity correction (OPC) is widely used to ensure pattern fidelity and manufacturability. Recent generative mask optimization models based on encoder-decoder architecture can synthesize near-optimal masks, serving as fast machine learning (ML) surrogates for traditional OPC.

**摘要：** 随着特征尺寸缩小至纳米级，将电路图案从光掩模精确转移到硅片上变得愈发困难。光学邻近校正（OPC）被广泛用于确保图案的保真度和可制造性。近期基于编码器-解码器架构的生成式掩模优化模型能够合成近乎最优的掩模，作为传统 OPC 的快速机器学习（ML）替代方案。

However, these models often fail to capture the geometric transformations from target layouts to mask patterns, leading to suboptimal quality. In this work, we formulate mask generation as a sequence of morphological operations on local layout features and propose \textit{MorphOPC}, a multi-scale hierarchical model with neural morphological modules to learn these transformations.

然而，这些模型往往难以捕捉从目标布局到掩模图案的几何变换，导致优化质量不佳。在这项工作中，我们将掩模生成表述为对局部布局特征的一系列形态学操作，并提出了 *MorphOPC*——一种具有神经形态学模块的多尺度分层模型，用于学习这些变换。

Experiments on edge-based OPC and ILT benchmarks across metal and via layers show that \textit{MorphOPC} consistently outperforms state-of-the-art methods, achieving higher printing fidelity and lower manufacturing cost, demonstrating strong potential for scalable mask optimization.

在金属层和通孔层的基于边缘的 OPC 和 ILT 基准测试上的实验表明，*MorphOPC* 始终优于现有最先进的方法，实现了更高的打印保真度和更低的制造成本，展示了其在可扩展掩模优化方面的巨大潜力。
