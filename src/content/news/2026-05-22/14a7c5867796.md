# Benders’ Decomposition 101: How to Crack Open a Stochastic Program That’s Too Big to Swallow Whole

**Benders’ Decomposition 101：如何拆解规模庞大、难以一次性求解的随机规划问题**

Whenever you can rewrite a (stochastic) optimization problem so that fixing some variables makes the rest separable, you could try Benders.

每当你能够重写一个（随机）优化问题，使得固定某些变量后剩余部分变得可分离时，你就可以尝试使用 Benders 分解法。

In my first TDS post, I wrote about translating a real-world problem into an integer linear program. In my second, I made that program robust against uncertainty. In my third, I introduced stochastic programming: four principled ways to put uncertainty into the model rather than hand-waving it away. The third post ended with a promise. I said two-stage recourse models are easy to write down and tidy in theory, but in practice they have an embarrassing problem: as you add more scenarios, the so-called “deterministic equivalent” explodes in size and the solver starts to complain. I name-dropped Benders’ decomposition as the standard fix and then disappeared without showing how it actually works. This is the post that makes good on that promise.

在我的第一篇 TDS 文章中，我写了如何将现实世界的问题转化为整数线性规划。在第二篇中，我使该程序能够抵御不确定性。在第三篇中，我介绍了随机规划：即四种将不确定性纳入模型的原则性方法，而不是对其避而不谈。第三篇文章结尾时我许下了一个承诺。我说两阶段追索模型（two-stage recourse models）在理论上易于书写且整洁，但在实践中却有一个令人尴尬的问题：随着场景数量的增加，所谓的“确定性等价模型”（deterministic equivalent）规模会爆炸式增长，求解器也会开始报错。我提到了 Benders 分解法作为标准解决方案，但当时并未展示其具体工作原理。这篇文章就是为了兑现那个承诺。

We’ll start with where the previous post left off, show why the obvious approach falls apart at scale, work out the one observation that saves us, and then walk slowly through the algorithm itself. There’s a bit more math than last time, but nothing exotic; the most advanced tool we’ll use is LP duality, and even that comes packaged as “we can write the same problem in a different way.”

我们将从上一篇文章结束的地方开始，展示为什么显而易见的方法在规模扩大时会失效，推导出那个拯救我们的关键观察点，然后逐步深入讲解算法本身。这次的数学内容比上次稍微多一点，但并不深奥；我们将使用的最先进工具是线性规划对偶性（LP duality），即便如此，它也只是以“我们可以用另一种方式书写同一个问题”的形式呈现。

### Quick recap: the model we’re trying to solve
### 快速回顾：我们要解决的模型

In the previous post, our hero was a fashion company in Germany sourcing winter clothing from Bangladesh. Demand ξ is a random variable; production x has to be decided before ξ is known. After demand is revealed, a recourse decision y patches up any shortfall at a higher cost (an emergency batch from Romania, say). If we assume a discrete distribution with S scenarios — values ξ¹, ξ², …, ξS with probabilities p₁, …, pS — the two-stage recourse model collapses into a single big LP, the deterministic equivalent: One copy of the second stage per scenario, all glued to the same first-stage x. Hand it to HiGHS or Gurobi, wait, and get a solution. End of story. Except sometimes the waiting becomes the whole story.

在上一篇文章中，我们的主角是一家从孟加拉国采购冬装的德国时尚公司。需求 ξ 是一个随机变量；生产量 x 必须在 ξ 已知之前决定。在需求揭晓后，追索决策 y 会以更高的成本（例如从罗马尼亚紧急补货）来弥补任何缺口。如果我们假设一个具有 S 个场景的离散分布（取值为 ξ¹, ξ², …, ξS，概率为 p₁, …, pS），那么两阶段追索模型就会坍缩成一个巨大的线性规划问题，即确定性等价模型：每个场景对应一个第二阶段的副本，所有副本都与同一个第一阶段变量 x 绑定。将其交给 HiGHS 或 Gurobi，等待结果即可。故事到此结束。但有时，等待过程本身就成了整个故事。

### When the deterministic equivalent hits a wall
### 当确定性等价模型遇到瓶颈时

For small S, everything is fine. You write the LP, you solve it, you go home. But scenarios tend to multiply faster than your patience: A continuous demand distribution that you approximate with sample average approximation might need thousands of samples before the optimal x stabilizes. A multi-stage model fans out exponentially with the number of stages. Three stages with ten branches each already gives you a thousand leaves; five stages and you’re well into the hundreds of thousands. In real applications, such as hydropower scheduling, supply-chain planning under demand shocks, or energy dispatch, S in the tens of thousands isn’t unusual. Hydropower people regularly model scenario trees that are too big to even enumerate, let alone hand to a solver.

对于较小的 S，一切都很顺利。你写好线性规划，求解，然后下班。但场景数量的增长速度往往超过你的耐心：一个通过样本平均近似（sample average approximation）处理的连续需求分布，可能需要数千个样本才能使最优解 x 趋于稳定。多阶段模型会随着阶段数的增加呈指数级扩张。三个阶段、每个阶段十个分支就已经产生了一千个叶节点；五个阶段则会达到数十万个。在水电调度、需求冲击下的供应链规划或能源调度等实际应用中，S 达到数万并不罕见。水电行业的人经常建模那些大到甚至无法枚举的场景树，更不用说交给求解器去处理了。

The deterministic equivalent dutifully grows with S. It has one block of constraints per scenario, one set of y_s variables per scenario, and one T_s x coupling each block to the shared first-stage x. If you take a look at the constraint matrix, it has a very particular shape: This is called block-angular structure. Each scenario contributes its own diagonal block W_s, plus a column of T_s on the left that ties it to x. The shared row at the bottom is the first-stage feasibility constraint Ax≥b. The shape is informative, but for the solver it’s bad news.

确定性等价模型会随着 S 的增加而忠实地增长。它为每个场景设置一个约束块，为每个场景设置一组 y_s 变量，并用一个 T_s x 将每个块与共享的第一阶段变量 x 耦合。如果你观察约束矩阵，会发现它有一种非常特殊的形状：这被称为块对角结构（block-angular structure）。每个场景贡献其自己的对角块 W_s，加上左侧将其与 x 绑定的 T_s 列。底部的共享行是第一阶段的可行性约束 Ax≥b。这种形状很有参考价值，但对求解器来说却是坏消息。

LP runtime grows superlinearly in problem size. A quick Google search will tell you it’s roughly O(n^2.5) for simplex-style methods and O(n^3.5) for interior-point methods in the worst case, which means doubling the number of scenarios doesn’t double the runtime — it sextuples it. At some point the problem stops fitting in memory entirely, and your friendly solver starts swapping to disk, which is a polite way of saying “this is too much.” So we need something cleverer than “shove the whole thing into a solver and hope for the best.”

线性规划的运行时间随问题规模呈超线性增长。快速搜索一下就会发现，在最坏情况下，单纯形法大约是 O(n^2.5)，内点法大约是 O(n^3.5)，这意味着场景数量翻倍并不会使运行时间翻倍，而是增加六倍。在某个临界点，问题将完全无法装入内存，你友好的求解器会开始使用磁盘交换，这是一种委婉的说法，意思是“这太难了”。因此，我们需要比“把所有东西塞进求解器并祈祷”更聪明的办法。

### Why naive tricks don’t help
### 为什么简单的技巧无济于事

Before we get to the clever idea, let’s rule out the obvious ones. Idea 1: Just use fewer scenarios. Sometimes this is fine; sometimes the answer changes meaningfully as you add scenarios, and your “optimum” was a coincidence of the sample you happened to draw. The sample average approximation literature is precisely about when you can get away with this and when you can’t. Idea 2: Solve each scenario separately and average the results. Tempting, but wrong. The whole point of having one x is that it has to work for all scenarios simultaneously. Solving scenario s on its own gives you the scenario-specific optimum (the “wait-and-see” solution from the last post), not a first-stage decision you can commit to. Idea 3: Solve the deterministic problem with mean demand 𝔼[ξ], then deal with the leftovers. This is the EV solution. It can be very wrong, and that wrongness is exactly what VSS measures. So we can’t shrink the problem, we can’t decouple it, and we can’t average our way around it. We need something that respects the structure of the deterministic equivalent while not solving it as one giant LP. That something has been around since 1962.

在进入那个聪明的想法之前，让我们先排除掉显而易见的方案。想法 1：只使用较少的场景。有时这没问题；但有时随着场景增加，答案会发生显著变化，你之前的“最优解”只是你恰好抽取的样本带来的巧合。样本平均近似的相关文献正是讨论何时可以这样做，何时不可以。想法 2：分别求解每个场景并对结果取平均。这很诱人，但却是错的。拥有一个 x 的全部意义在于它必须同时适用于所有场景。单独求解场景 s 只会给你特定场景下的最优解（上一篇文章中的“观望”解），而不是你可以承诺的第一阶段决策。想法 3：用平均需求 𝔼[ξ] 求解确定性问题，然后处理剩余部分。这就是期望值（EV）解。它可能非常错误，而这种错误程度正是 VSS（随机解的价值）所衡量的。因此，我们不能缩小问题规模，不能将其解耦，也不能通过取平均值来绕过它。我们需要一种既能尊重确定性等价模型结构，又不必将其作为一个巨大的线性规划来求解的方法。这种方法自 1962 年以来就一直存在。

### The one observation that saves us
### 拯救我们的关键观察点

Look at the constraint matrix again: Now imagine that x is fixed and pick any value, doesn’t matter which. The first-stage block Ax≥b either holds or it doesn’t. And the rest of the constraints decouple: scenario s’s constraint becomes W_s y_s ≥ h_s − T_s x, which involves only y_s. The connection between scenarios runs through x. Fix x, and the scenarios fall apart into S independent LPs. This is the key idea. The variable x is a complicating variable: if it were known, life would be much easier. Each subproblem is small (the size of one scenario, not all).

再次观察约束矩阵：现在想象 x 是固定的，并取任意值，取什么值并不重要。第一阶段的块 Ax≥b 要么成立，要么不成立。而其余的约束条件则解耦了：场景 s 的约束变为 W_s y_s ≥ h_s − T_s x，其中只涉及 y_s。场景之间的联系通过 x 贯穿始终。固定 x，场景就会分解为 S 个独立的线性规划问题。这就是核心思想。变量 x 是一个复杂变量：如果它已知，生活会轻松得多。每个子问题都很小（是一个场景的大小，而不是所有场景的总和）。
