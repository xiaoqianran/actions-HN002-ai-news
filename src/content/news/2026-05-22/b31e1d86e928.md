# Provably Learning Diffusion Models under the Manifold Hypothesis: Collapse and Refine
# 在流形假设下可证明地学习扩散模型：坍缩与精炼

**Abstract:** Diffusion models generate high-dimensional data with remarkable quality, yet how their training efficiently learns the score function, bypassing the curse of dimensionality when data is supported on low-dimensional manifolds, remains theoretically unexplained.

**摘要：** 扩散模型能够以极高的质量生成高维数据，然而，当数据分布在低维流形上时，其训练过程如何高效地学习分数函数（score function）并绕过维度灾难，在理论上尚无定论。

We identify a collapse-and-refine mechanism driven by the geometry of the score function itself: at small noise scales, the diverging singularity of the score drives a rapid dimensional collapse of the induced denoising map onto the data manifold projection; at moderate noise scales, training refines the intrinsic density on the learned manifold.

我们识别出一种由分数函数本身的几何结构所驱动的“坍缩与精炼”机制：在小噪声尺度下，分数的发散奇点驱动诱导去噪映射迅速向数据流形投影进行维度坍缩；在中等噪声尺度下，训练过程则在学习到的流形上精炼其内在密度。

We instantiate this principle as Score-induced Latent Diffusion (SiLD), a two-stage framework in which both manifold learning and density estimation emerge from a single denoising score matching objective, replacing the heuristic KL regularization of VAE-based latent diffusion models.

我们将这一原理实例化为“分数诱导潜在扩散”（Score-induced Latent Diffusion, SiLD）。这是一个两阶段框架，其中流形学习和密度估计均源自单一的去噪分数匹配目标，从而取代了基于 VAE 的潜在扩散模型中启发式的 KL 正则化方法。

We prove that the resulting sample complexity depends on the intrinsic dimension rather than the ambient dimension. Experiments on Stacked MNIST, CelebA variants, and molecular generation benchmarks show that SiLD matches or outperforms VAE-based LDMs in generation quality and consistently improves reconstruction, validating our theoretical predictions.

我们证明了由此产生的样本复杂度取决于内在维度而非环境维度。在 Stacked MNIST、CelebA 变体以及分子生成基准测试上的实验表明，SiLD 在生成质量上与基于 VAE 的潜在扩散模型（LDM）持平或更优，并能持续提升重构效果，从而验证了我们的理论预测。
