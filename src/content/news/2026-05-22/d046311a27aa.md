### Neural Estimation of Pairwise Mutual Information in Masked Discrete Sequence Models
### 掩码离散序列模型中成对互信息的神经估计

**Abstract:** Understanding dependencies between variables is critical for interpretability and efficient generation in masked diffusion models (MDMs), yet these models primarily expose marginal conditional distributions and do not explicitly represent inter-variable dependence.
**摘要：** 理解变量间的依赖关系对于掩码扩散模型（MDMs）的可解释性和高效生成至关重要，然而这些模型主要展示的是边缘条件分布，并未显式地表示变量间的依赖关系。

We propose a neural framework for estimating pairwise conditional mutual information (MI) directly from the hidden states of a pretrained MDM, using ground-truth MI computed from the model's own conditional distributions for supervision.
我们提出了一个神经框架，用于直接从预训练 MDM 的隐藏状态中估计成对条件互信息（MI），并利用模型自身条件分布计算出的真实互信息作为监督信号。

The resulting estimator captures the model's internal belief about dependency structure and predicts the full MI matrix in a single forward pass, enabling MI-guided parallel decoding by identifying conditionally independent subsets of variables.
由此得到的估计器能够捕捉模型对依赖结构的内在认知，并在单次前向传播中预测完整的互信息矩阵，通过识别变量的条件独立子集，实现互信息引导的并行解码。

We evaluate our approach on Sudoku and protein sequence generation with ESM-C, where the MI maps recover known structural constraints and enable a 3-5x magnitude reduction in inference-time forward passes compared to sequential decoding, while preserving generative quality and outperforming entropy-based parallelization methods.
我们在数独和基于 ESM-C 的蛋白质序列生成任务上评估了该方法。结果表明，互信息图能够恢复已知的结构约束，与顺序解码相比，推理阶段的前向传播次数减少了 3-5 倍，同时保持了生成质量，并优于基于熵的并行化方法。
