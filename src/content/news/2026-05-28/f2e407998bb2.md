# Sentinel: Embodied Cooperative Spatial Reasoning and Planning
# Sentinel：具身协作空间推理与规划

In this work, we study Cooperative Spatial Intelligence, the ability of decentralized embodied agents to coordinate effectively under dynamic environmental constraints across city-scale outdoor domains.
在这项工作中，我们研究了“协作空间智能”（Cooperative Spatial Intelligence），即去中心化具身智能体在城市规模的户外领域中，于动态环境约束下进行有效协调的能力。

We introduce Sentinel Challenge, a benchmark where multiple decentralized embodied agents must communicate in natural language to agree on a mutually safe and convenient meeting point within large, city-scale outdoor environments.
我们提出了“哨兵挑战”（Sentinel Challenge），这是一个基准测试。在测试中，多个去中心化的具身智能体必须通过自然语言进行交流，以在大型城市级户外环境中商定一个既安全又方便的会合点。

Each agent must then navigate safely while avoiding dynamic sentinels patrolling the area, using a tool that provides coarse spatial information.
随后，每个智能体必须在避开区域内巡逻的动态哨兵的同时安全导航，并利用一个提供粗略空间信息的工具辅助行动。

To address this, we propose CoSaR (Cooperative Spatial Reasoning and Planning), a framework that bridges the high-level communication and planning abilities of foundation models with the precision of classical spatial navigation algorithms.
为了解决这一问题，我们提出了 CoSaR（协作空间推理与规划）框架，该框架将基础模型的高级通信与规划能力，与经典空间导航算法的精确性相结合。

CoSaR enables agents to exchange situational updates, reason over evolving spatial constraints, and collaboratively replan trajectories.
CoSaR 使智能体能够交换态势更新、对不断变化的空间约束进行推理，并协作重新规划轨迹。

Evaluated across 14 city-level scenes with 3-5 agents, CoSaR consistently leads to faster gathering, shorter path lengths, and improved safety.
在包含 3-5 个智能体的 14 个城市级场景中进行的评估表明，CoSaR 始终能实现更快的集结速度、更短的路径长度以及更高的安全性。

Our results demonstrate that integrating dynamic communication with spatial reasoning is essential for robust multi-agent cooperation.
我们的研究结果表明，将动态通信与空间推理相结合，对于实现稳健的多智能体协作至关重要。

By formalizing this new setting and providing a scalable benchmark, we aim to build a foundation for advancing cooperative spatial intelligence in embodied multi-agent systems.
通过将这一新场景形式化并提供可扩展的基准测试，我们旨在为推进具身多智能体系统中的协作空间智能奠定基础。
