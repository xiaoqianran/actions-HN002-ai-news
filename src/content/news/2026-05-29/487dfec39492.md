# Beyond Motion Primitives: Behavioral Activity Recognition from Head-Mounted IMU
# 超越运动基元：基于头戴式惯性测量单元（IMU）的行为活动识别

**Abstract:** AR smart glasses need continuous behavioral context to offer proactive assistance, yet their most practical always-on sensor, the head-mounted Inertial Measurement Unit (IMU), detects only motion primitives such as walking or standing. We push beyond motion primitives to behavioral-level recognition, defining five categories that balance AR application need with sensor observability.

**摘要：** 增强现实（AR）智能眼镜需要持续的行为上下文信息来提供主动式辅助，然而其最实用的常开传感器——头戴式惯性测量单元（IMU），目前仅能检测到行走或站立等“运动基元”。我们致力于超越运动基元，实现行为层面的识别，并定义了五个类别，以平衡 AR 应用需求与传感器可观测性。

To this end, we construct a 160K-sample Ego4D dataset with a four-tier quality assurance framework spanning 8 activity scenarios, and propose HiT-HAR, a 703K-parameter hierarchical model that outperforms prior head-mounted IMU models on five-class action and eight-class scenario recognition.

为此，我们构建了一个包含 16 万个样本的 Ego4D 数据集，该数据集采用了涵盖 8 种活动场景的四级质量保证框架。同时，我们提出了 HiT-HAR，这是一个拥有 70.3 万参数的层级模型，在五类动作识别和八类场景识别任务中，其表现均优于现有的头戴式 IMU 模型。

We further map the observability frontier of head-mounted IMU through per-class separability analysis, identifying which behavioral categories are reliably observable (Locomotion), which benefit from temporal context (Object Transfer, Task Operation), and where scenario-dependent signal overlap poses remaining challenges.

我们进一步通过类间可分性分析，绘制了头戴式 IMU 的可观测性边界，明确了哪些行为类别是可靠可观测的（如移动），哪些行为受益于时间上下文（如物体转移、任务操作），以及在何种情况下场景依赖的信号重叠仍构成挑战。

Our results indicate that architectural choices exploiting temporal context and scenario structure outperform simply scaling model size. The code and dataset are publicly available at this https URL.

我们的研究结果表明，利用时间上下文和场景结构的架构选择，其效果优于单纯增加模型规模。代码和数据集现已在指定链接公开。
