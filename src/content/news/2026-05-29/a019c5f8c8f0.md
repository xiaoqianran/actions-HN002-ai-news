If you’ve ever tried to use AI to build a mathematical optimization model for a real business problem, you’ve probably run into the same wall: the AI works beautifully on textbook examples and falls apart the moment you hand it your actual data and your actual problem. That gap isn’t a coincidence. It’s by design, and it’s the reason why I built ORPilot.
如果你曾尝试使用 AI 为实际业务问题构建数学优化模型，你很可能遇到过同样的障碍：AI 在教科书式的案例中表现完美，但一旦你交给它真实的数据和实际问题，它就会立刻崩溃。这种差距并非巧合，而是设计使然，这也是我构建 ORPilot 的原因。

### The Promise of AI-Powered Optimization
### AI 驱动优化的前景

Operations Research (OR) has been quietly powering some of the most impactful decisions in business for decades — routing delivery trucks, scheduling factory production, designing supply chains, allocating cargo to carriers. The math is mature and the solvers are excellent. The bottleneck has always been the human expertise required to translate a business problem into a mathematical model.
几十年来，运筹学（OR）一直在默默地推动着商业领域中一些最具影响力的决策——例如配送车辆的路径规划、工厂生产调度、供应链设计以及货物分配。相关的数学理论已经非常成熟，求解器也十分出色。然而，瓶颈始终在于将业务问题转化为数学模型所需的专业人类经验。

Large Language Models (LLMs) seemed like the perfect solution. A growing body of research, including the OptiMUS series, OR-LLM, and others, has shown that state-of-the-art LLMs can generate correct solver code for well-specified linear programming (LP) and mixed integer programming (MIP) problems. The results looked impressive. The demos were compelling. Then you try to use one of these tools on a real problem, and the cracks appear immediately.
大语言模型（LLM）似乎是完美的解决方案。越来越多的研究（包括 OptiMUS 系列、OR-LLM 等）表明，最先进的 LLM 能够为定义明确的线性规划（LP）和混合整数规划（MIP）问题生成正确的求解器代码。这些结果看起来令人印象深刻，演示效果也很有说服力。但当你尝试将这些工具应用于实际问题时，问题立刻就暴露了出来。

### Where Existing Tools Break Down
### 现有工具的局限性

Almost every LLM-for-OR tool built to date shares a hidden assumption: the problem description is complete, unambiguous, and handed to the AI in a single, well-formatted prompt with all the data neatly embedded inline. That is not how real OR problems work. Not even close.
迄今为止，几乎所有为运筹学开发的 LLM 工具都隐含着一个假设：问题描述是完整且无歧义的，并且以单一、格式良好的提示词（Prompt）形式提交给 AI，所有数据都整齐地嵌入其中。但现实中的运筹学问题并非如此，甚至相去甚远。

Consider what actually happens when a supply chain team wants to build an optimization model:
考虑一下当供应链团队想要构建优化模型时，实际会发生什么：

*   **The problem description is incomplete and ambiguous.** A business analyst will say “we want to minimize transportation costs” and forget to mention that each distribution center has a throughput limit, that some routes don’t exist, or that opening a facility incurs a one-time fixed cost. These omissions aren’t carelessness. They’re assumptions the analyst considers obvious, which is exactly why they’re dangerous. An AI system that starts modeling before these details are nailed down produces a model that is technically correct but practically wrong.
    **问题描述不完整且模糊。** 业务分析师可能会说“我们想要最小化运输成本”，却忘了提到每个配送中心都有吞吐量限制、某些路线不存在，或者开设设施会产生一次性固定成本。这些遗漏并非粗心大意，而是分析师认为显而易见的假设，这正是其危险之处。如果 AI 系统在这些细节确定之前就开始建模，生成的模型在技术上可能是正确的，但在实际应用中却是错误的。

*   **The data is too large to fit in a prompt.** A real supply chain problem might involve hundreds of production sites, distribution centers, customers, and thousands of products over multiple periods. The demand table alone might have millions of entries. You cannot embed that in a prompt. Even if you could, flooding the context window with raw data dramatically increases the risk of hallucinations.
    **数据量太大，无法放入提示词中。** 一个真实的供应链问题可能涉及数百个生产基地、配送中心、客户，以及跨越多个周期的数千种产品。仅需求表就可能有数百万条记录。你无法将其嵌入提示词中。即使可以，用原始数据填满上下文窗口也会极大地增加模型产生幻觉的风险。

*   **The data you have is not the data the model needs.** The model might need a distance matrix between all pairs of locations. What you have is a table of GPS coordinates. The model might need aggregate demand by product and period. What you have is a transaction ledger with one row per order. Bridging this gap, namely computing derived parameters from raw data, is a significant engineering step that no existing LLM-for-OR tool handles automatically.
    **你拥有的数据并非模型所需的数据。** 模型可能需要所有地点对之间的距离矩阵，而你拥有的却是一张 GPS 坐标表。模型可能需要按产品和周期汇总的需求数据，而你拥有的却是每行代表一个订单的交易账本。弥合这一差距（即从原始数据计算派生参数）是一个重大的工程步骤，目前没有任何现有的运筹学 LLM 工具能自动处理。

*   **Once you have a working model, portability and reproducibility matter.** If you want to re-run the model on updated data, switch from Gurobi to an open-source solver, or hand the model off to a colleague on a different machine, you’re back to square one unless the tool produces a durable, solver-agnostic artifact. Most tools produce solver-specific code and nothing else.
    **一旦模型运行成功，可移植性和可重复性就至关重要。** 如果你想在更新的数据上重新运行模型、从 Gurobi 切换到开源求解器，或者将模型交给不同机器上的同事，除非该工具能生成持久且与求解器无关的产物，否则你将一切从零开始。大多数工具只会生成特定于求解器的代码，除此之外别无他物。

These aren’t edge cases. They are the standard conditions for any real-world OR deployment. Existing LLM-for-OR tools were built for a different world, a textbook world, and they show their seams the moment they leave it.
这些并非极端情况，而是任何现实世界运筹学部署的标准条件。现有的运筹学 LLM 工具是为另一个世界——即教科书世界——而构建的，一旦离开那个世界，它们就会立刻显露出破绽。

### Introducing ORPilot
### ORPilot 简介

ORPilot is an open-source AI agent built from the ground up for production conditions. It is, to my knowledge, the first LLM-based OR tool designed explicitly for the messy, large-scale, data-heavy reality of industrial optimization.
ORPilot 是一个专为生产环境从零构建的开源 AI 代理。据我所知，它是第一个明确为工业优化中混乱、大规模、数据密集型的现实情况而设计的基于 LLM 的运筹学工具。

Most AI tools for optimization jump straight to writing code the moment you describe your problem. ORPilot does something different: it asks questions first. That design decision, prioritizing understanding over speed, reflects a single guiding principle: an AI agent should work the same way a skilled human OR consultant would.
大多数优化 AI 工具在你描述问题时会直接开始编写代码。ORPilot 则不同：它会先提问。这一设计决策将理解置于速度之上，反映了一个核心指导原则：AI 代理的工作方式应与经验丰富的人类运筹学顾问相同。

A good consultant doesn’t walk into a client meeting and start writing a mathematical model on the whiteboard. They ask questions. They listen carefully. They push back when something is ambiguous. They make sure the data is in the right shape before the modeling begins. Only after all of that do they pick up the pen.
优秀的顾问不会走进客户会议室就直接在白板上写数学模型。他们会提问、仔细倾听、在模糊不清时提出质疑，并确保在建模开始前数据已准备就绪。只有在完成这一切之后，他们才会动笔。

ORPilot’s pipeline reflects this discipline through five sequentially connected stages.
ORPilot 的工作流程通过五个顺序连接的阶段体现了这种严谨性。

### Stage 1: Interview Agent
### 第一阶段：访谈代理

The interview agent is the entry point. It receives your initial description of the business problem, which can be vague, incomplete, or even self-contradictory, and engages you in a structured dialog to fill in the gaps. The key design principle is no modeling begins until the interview is complete. The agent is prompted to identify information gaps in the current description, ask at most one targeted clarifying question per turn (to avoid overwhelming you), and terminate once the objective function, decision variables, constraints, and data requirements are all unambiguously specified.
访谈代理是入口点。它接收你对业务问题的初步描述（这些描述可能是模糊、不完整甚至自相矛盾的），并通过结构化对话来填补空白。其核心设计原则是：在访谈完成前，绝不开始建模。代理会识别当前描述中的信息缺口，每轮最多提出一个有针对性的澄清问题（以避免让你感到负担），并在目标函数、决策变量、约束条件和数据需求都明确无误后结束访谈。

In practice, this means conversations like:
在实践中，这意味着如下对话：

*   ORPilot: “Once a facility is opened, does it remain open for all subsequent periods, or can it be closed later?”
    ORPilot：“一旦设施开启，它是会在所有后续周期内保持开启，还是可以在之后关闭？”
*   ORPilot: “Does this model handle a single product type or multiple products?”
    ORPilot：“该模型是处理单一产品类型还是多种产品？”
*   ORPilot: “You mentioned a transportation cost. Is this cost per unit shipped, per shipment regardless of quantity, or something else?”
    ORPilot：“你提到了运输成本。这是指每单位运输成本、每批次运输成本（无论数量多少），还是其他情况？”

Before ending the interview, the agent presents a full structured summary with objective function, decision variables, constraints, parameters, indices, and gives you the chance to correct anything.
在结束访谈前，代理会提供一份完整的结构化摘要，包含目标函数、决策变量、约束条件、参数和索引，并让你有机会进行修正。
