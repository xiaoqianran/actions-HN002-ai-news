# EmoNet: Speaker-Aware Transformers for Emotion Recognition — and What I’d Build Differently in 2026

**EmoNet：用于情感识别的说话人感知 Transformer —— 以及我在 2026 年会如何重构它**

A retrospective on my MS thesis, the leaderboard it placed on, and the LLM shift that has reshaped the field since. Biju Puthan Veetil, May 28, 2026.

这是一篇关于我硕士论文的回顾，包括它在排行榜上的表现，以及自那以后重塑该领域的大语言模型（LLM）浪潮。作者：Biju Puthan Veetil，2026 年 5 月 28 日。

In March 2024, I submitted my MS thesis on Emotion Recognition in Conversation (ERC). The model, EmoNet, achieved a Weighted F1 of 39.18 on EmoryNLP — competitive with the public PapersWithCode leaderboard at the time, sitting between TUCORE-GCN_RoBERTa (39.24) and S+PAGE (39.14), and improving over my chosen baseline, CoMPM, by +1.81 F1.

2024 年 3 月，我提交了关于对话情感识别（ERC）的硕士论文。该模型 EmoNet 在 EmoryNLP 数据集上取得了 39.18 的加权 F1 分数，在当时与 PapersWithCode 公开排行榜上的模型相比极具竞争力，处于 TUCORE-GCN_RoBERTa (39.24) 和 S+PAGE (39.14) 之间，并比我选择的基准模型 CoMPM 提升了 1.81 个 F1 点。

Two years later, I returned to look at where the field is now. The leaderboard is unrecognizable. The top entries are no longer encoder-only models with clever attention heads — they’re LLaMA-2–7B-based systems with LoRA fine-tuning and retrieval-augmented prompting: InstructERC, CKERC, BiosERC, LaERC-S. The methods are different. The compute is different. The mindset is different. And yet — when I read these new papers carefully, the core ideas I proposed in EmoNet show up inside them, just implemented at a different layer of the stack. This is the story of what I built, where it placed, and what I’d build now if I were starting over.

两年后，我回过头来看看该领域现在的状况。排行榜已经面目全非。顶尖模型不再是那些带有巧妙注意力机制的纯编码器（encoder-only）模型，而是基于 LLaMA-2–7B、结合 LoRA 微调和检索增强提示（RAG）的系统：如 InstructERC、CKERC、BiosERC 和 LaERC-S。方法变了，算力变了，思维方式也变了。然而，当我仔细阅读这些新论文时，我发现我在 EmoNet 中提出的核心思想依然存在于其中，只是在技术栈的不同层级上得到了实现。这就是我所构建的模型、它的表现，以及如果从头开始我会如何构建它的故事。

### What ERC is, and why text-only is hard
### 什么是 ERC，以及为什么纯文本处理如此困难

Emotion Recognition in Conversation is the task of assigning an emotion label to each utterance in a multi-turn dialogue. It’s distinct from sentiment analysis on isolated sentences in one important way: the emotion of an utterance is shaped by what came before it, and by who is speaking.

对话情感识别（ERC）的任务是为多轮对话中的每一句话分配一个情感标签。它与孤立句子的情感分析有一个重要的区别：一句话的情感是由它之前的内容以及说话人是谁所决定的。

Consider this exchange from the EmoryNLP dataset (sourced from the TV show Friends):
Monica: Wendy, we had a deal! Yeah, you promised! Wendy! Wendy! Wendy! [Mad]
Rachel: Who was that? [Neutral]
Monica: Wendy bailed. I have no waitress. [Mad]

以 EmoryNLP 数据集（源自美剧《老友记》）中的这段对话为例：
Monica: Wendy，我们说好的！你答应过的！Wendy! Wendy! Wendy! [愤怒]
Rachel: 那是谁？ [中性]
Monica: Wendy 跑了。我没有服务员了。 [愤怒]

In isolation, “Who was that?” is emotionally neutral. The label Neutral is only meaningful in context — it sits between two angry utterances from a different speaker and ERC models must capture this conversational dynamic. There’s a second wrinkle: multimodal information is missing. In real human conversation, tone of voice, facial expressions, and body language carry an enormous share of emotional signal. Text-only ERC strips all of that away. The same words — “Oh, great.” — can be sincere or sarcastic, and the text alone often can’t tell you which. This information loss is the central challenge. You have to extract emotion from a noisier signal than the human-grade benchmark.

孤立地看，“那是谁？”在情感上是中性的。只有在语境中，“中性”这个标签才有意义——它夹在来自另一位说话人的两句愤怒的话语之间，ERC 模型必须捕捉到这种对话动态。第二个难点是：多模态信息的缺失。在真实的人类对话中，语调、面部表情和肢体语言承载了巨大的情感信号。纯文本 ERC 剥离了所有这些信息。同样的词——“哦，太棒了。”——可能是真诚的，也可能是讽刺的，仅凭文本往往无法分辨。这种信息丢失是核心挑战。你必须从比人类基准更嘈杂的信号中提取情感。

### The 2024 landscape
### 2024 年的格局

When I started my thesis in late 2023, the EmoryNLP leaderboard was dominated by transformer-based architectures with various clever modifications. A quick tour:
- KET (Zhong et al., 2019) — knowledge-enriched transformer with affective graph attention, the first paper to bring transformers to ERC.
- DialogueGCN (Ghosal et al., 2019) — graph convolutional network that converted dialogues into node-classification problems.
- RGAT (Ishiwatari et al., 2020) — relation-aware graph attention with relational position encoding for speaker dependencies.
- DialogXL (Shen et al., 2020) — adapted XLNet with utterance recurrence and dialogue self-attention.
- HiTrans (Li et al., 2020) — hierarchical transformer with pairwise utterance speaker verification as auxiliary task.
- TUCORE-GCN (Lee & Choi, 2021) — heterogeneous dialogue graph with speaker-aware BERT.
- CoMPM (Lee & Lee, 2021) — combined dialogue context with pre-trained memory tracking for the speaker.

当我在 2023 年末开始我的论文时，EmoryNLP 排行榜被各种带有巧妙修改的 Transformer 架构所主导。简单回顾一下：
- KET (Zhong 等人, 2019) — 带有情感图注意力的知识增强 Transformer，是第一篇将 Transformer 引入 ERC 的论文。
- DialogueGCN (Ghosal 等人, 2019) — 将对话转换为节点分类问题的图卷积网络。
- RGAT (Ishiwatari 等人, 2020) — 带有用于说话人依赖关系的关系位置编码的关系感知图注意力模型。
- DialogXL (Shen 等人, 2020) — 改编自 XLNet，具有话语循环和对话自注意力机制。
- HiTrans (Li 等人, 2020) — 分层 Transformer，以成对的话语说话人验证作为辅助任务。
- TUCORE-GCN (Lee & Choi, 2021) — 带有说话人感知 BERT 的异构对话图。
- CoMPM (Lee & Lee, 2021) — 将对话上下文与说话人的预训练记忆追踪相结合。

I chose CoMPM as my base for two reasons. First, it explicitly modeled the speaker’s pre-trained memory as a separate module — which mapped to my intuition that who is speaking matters as much as what they’re saying. Second, its architecture was modular enough to extend without rewriting from scratch. The CoMPM paper showed that adding pre-trained memory to the context model gave a measurable boost — but their speaker identity was still local to each dialogue. The moment a new conversation began, everything the model had learned about a speaker was discarded. That seemed like a problem worth solving.

我选择 CoMPM 作为基础有两个原因。首先，它明确地将说话人的预训练记忆建模为一个独立的模块——这符合我的直觉，即“谁在说话”与“说了什么”同样重要。其次，它的架构模块化程度很高，无需重写即可扩展。CoMPM 的论文表明，在上下文模型中加入预训练记忆带来了可衡量的提升——但他们的说话人身份仅限于每段对话内部。一旦新对话开始，模型对该说话人所学到的一切都会被丢弃。这似乎是一个值得解决的问题。

### Three contributions, with intuition
### 三项贡献及其直觉

**1. Global Speaker Identity**
**1. 全局说话人身份**

The problem. In CoMPM and most prior work, speaker IDs are scoped to a single dialogue. Speaker A in scene 1 has no relationship to Speaker A in scene 14, even when they’re the same person. Hence, every dialogue starts cold.
The intuition. People have characteristic emotional patterns. Monica gets angry about specific things; Phoebe is reliably cheerful; Ross has predictable bouts of insecurity. If a model can carry information about this specific speaker across dialogues, it should be able to make better-calibrated predictions when that speaker reappears.
The implementation. Each unique speaker in the entire dataset gets a stable, dataset-wide ID. The first time Monica Geller appears, she’s assigned an ID — say, ID 7 — that stays with her. Every subsequent appearance — across episodes, seasons, scenes — she stays ID 7. The model can now learn speaker-specific patterns that persist. This sounds obvious in retrospect. In 2024 it was not how the leaderboard models worked.

问题所在：在 CoMPM 和大多数先前的工作中，说话人 ID 仅限于单次对话。场景 1 中的说话人 A 与场景 14 中的说话人 A 没有任何关联，即使他们是同一个人。因此，每段对话都是“冷启动”。
直觉：人们有典型的情感模式。Monica 会对特定的事情感到愤怒；Phoebe 总是很开朗；Ross 有可预测的不安全感。如果模型能够跨对话携带关于该特定说话人的信息，那么当该说话人再次出现时，它应该能够做出更准确的预测。
实现：数据集中的每个唯一说话人都获得一个稳定的、全局的 ID。当 Monica Geller 第一次出现时，她被分配一个 ID（比如 ID 7），这个 ID 会一直伴随她。在随后的每一次出现中——跨越剧集、季节、场景——她始终是 ID 7。模型现在可以学习持续存在的说话人特定模式。事后看来这很明显，但在 2024 年，排行榜上的模型并不是这样工作的。

**2. Speaker Behaviour Module**
**2. 说话人行为模块**

The problem. Global Speaker Identity alone is just a label. To make it useful, the model needs to do something with the speaker’s accumulated history. How do you give a transformer access to “everything Monica has ever said in this dataset,” without blowing out the context window or making training intractable?
The intuition. Recurrence. A GRU is a natural fit for sequentially compressing a speaker’s historical utterances into a single fixed-size representation. Recent utterances contribute more; older ones gradually dilute. A configurable sliding window bounds the GRU’s input — say, the last N utterances by this speaker — keeping compute and memory predictable.

问题所在：仅有全局说话人身份只是一个标签。为了使其有用，模型需要利用说话人积累的历史记录。如何让 Transformer 访问“Monica 在此数据集中说过的一切”，而不撑爆上下文窗口或导致训练无法进行？
直觉：循环（Recurrence）。GRU 非常适合将说话人的历史话语按顺序压缩成一个固定大小的表示。最近的话语贡献更大；较旧的话语逐渐淡化。一个可配置的滑动窗口限制了 GRU 的输入——比如该说话人的最后 N 句话——从而保持计算和内存的可预测性。
