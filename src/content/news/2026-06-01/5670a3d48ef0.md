### Unlawful by design: Exposing the human rights costs of generative AI
### 设计即违法：揭露生成式人工智能的人权代价

This briefing examines how standalone generative AI systems, based on unlawful web scraping, are in conflict with international human rights law (IHRL) and standards through their design, development and deployment.
本简报审视了基于非法网络抓取的独立生成式人工智能系统，如何在设计、开发和部署过程中与国际人权法（IHRL）及相关标准产生冲突。

While these technologies promise sophisticated automation and efficiency, they rely on data collection and model training practices that abuse privacy rights, enable discrimination, and threaten freedom of expression and thought.
尽管这些技术承诺带来先进的自动化和效率，但它们所依赖的数据收集和模型训练实践，却在滥用隐私权、助长歧视，并威胁到言论和思想自由。

Amnesty International finds that standalone generative AI systems, based on unlawful web scraping, depend on mass invasions of privacy by design, and are fundamentally incompatible with IHRL.
国际特赦组织发现，基于非法网络抓取的独立生成式人工智能系统，在设计上就依赖于大规模的隐私侵犯，且从根本上与国际人权法不相容。

As such, Amnesty International is calling for a prohibition of such systems.
因此，国际特赦组织呼吁禁止此类系统。
