**Introduction**
**引言**

Dashboards have never been killed because they operate within the Valley of Choice in BI. The Valley of Choice describes the perfect ratio between the complexity of a problem and the effort we are willing to put in to solve it.
仪表板之所以从未被淘汰，是因为它们处于 BI 的“选择之谷”中。“选择之谷”描述了问题复杂程度与我们愿意投入的解决成本之间的一种完美比例。

For example, a business critical question about how revenue is formed, and why different versions of revenue changed over time can warrant manual exploration. It can warrant deep discovery facilitated by multiple SQL queries and manually writing down the results and tracing through lineage. This is High Effort, High Accuracy.
例如，一个关于收入构成以及为何不同版本的收入随时间发生变化的商业关键问题，值得进行人工探索。它值得通过多次 SQL 查询、手动记录结果并追踪数据血缘来进行深度挖掘。这就是“高投入、高准确度”。

By contrast, simple questions on simple datasets like “How many sign-ups did I get yesterday?” can be satisfied by lower effort, simpler methods such as basic Text to SQL, as seen in tools like Thoughtspot.
相比之下，针对简单数据集的简单问题，例如“我昨天获得了多少注册用户？”，可以通过低投入、更简单的方法（如 Thoughtspot 等工具中常见的 Text-to-SQL）来解决。

In the centre are dashboards. Dashboards provide a moderate effort solution to a moderate complexity problem. They are capable of answering multiple questions and pivoting data to explain anomalies and forecast trends. They do not help discover insights, but help you deliver insights you have already found. When something becomes asked frequently enough, it becomes worthy of a dashboard, and immediately graduates from a tricky, one-off, manually provided insight to something simpler and more repeatable.
处于中间位置的就是仪表板。仪表板为中等复杂度的问题提供了中等投入的解决方案。它们能够回答多个问题，并通过数据透视来解释异常和预测趋势。它们无法帮助发现洞察，但能帮助你交付已经发现的洞察。当某个问题被频繁问及时，它就值得被做成仪表板，从而从一个棘手、一次性、手动提供的洞察，升级为更简单、更可重复的内容。

Agentic Analytics offers a new way to discover, and to serve insights. That is Text-to-SQL. This new way means that hitherto moderately complex queries can now be answered instantly. Tools like Hex incorporate memory into their platforms, which means that even complex queries, when combined with memory, allow Text-to-SQL to solve complex problems. This changes the necessary ratio of effort to complexity and will reshape how we think of analytics. Let’s see how.
代理式分析（Agentic Analytics）提供了一种发现并交付洞察的新方式，即 Text-to-SQL。这种新方式意味着，以往中等复杂度的查询现在可以瞬间得到解答。像 Hex 这样的工具将记忆功能整合到平台中，这意味着即使是复杂的查询，在结合记忆功能后，也能让 Text-to-SQL 解决复杂问题。这改变了投入与复杂度的必要比例，并将重塑我们对分析的思考方式。让我们看看它是如何做到的。

**Explaining the Valley of Choice in BI**
**解读 BI 中的“选择之谷”**

The Valley of Choice can be pictured below. It describes a tradeoff between speed and accuracy which is determined by the nature of the demand or insight. Holding all things constant, the most important questions demand the highest degree of accuracy, which in turn justifies more time-consuming (High effort) methods.
“选择之谷”如下图所示。它描述了速度与准确度之间的权衡，这种权衡由需求或洞察的性质决定。在其他条件不变的情况下，最重要的问题需要最高程度的准确性，这反过来证明了耗时（高投入）方法的合理性。

It is a bit like saying that you can buy a delicious sandwich from your deli for $20 or go to 11 Madison Park for $500. Two different products, two different options, two different price points. Dashboards are like a meal out at a normal restaurant. $100 per person, but not suitable if you’re an eligible bachelor in New York.
这有点像说，你可以花 20 美元从熟食店买一个美味的三明治，或者花 500 美元去 11 Madison Park 餐厅用餐。这是两种不同的产品、两种不同的选择、两种不同的价位。仪表板就像是在普通餐厅吃一顿饭，人均 100 美元，但如果你是纽约的单身贵族，这可能就不太合适了。

Now: Imagine there is a new restaurant. The New Restaurant is very exclusive. The food is incredible; everything is organic. The service excellent. And lots of celebrities go there. It is very desirable. You can get a delicious 3-course meal for $40. We can call it Hugo’s Diner.
现在：想象有一家新餐厅。这家新餐厅非常高端，食物令人惊叹，所有食材都是有机的，服务一流，还有许多名人光顾。它非常令人向往，你只需 40 美元就能享用一顿美味的三道菜大餐。我们可以称之为“雨果餐厅”（Hugo’s Diner）。

In fact, it is part of a new trend. Hugo has a vast restaurant empire. There is Hugo’s Diner, but there is also Casa Hugo, Chez Hugo, Hugo’s table, and even Hugo-San. Hugo-San offers the best omakase in the city for $39.50. This could be you.
事实上，这是一种新趋势的一部分。雨果拥有庞大的餐饮帝国。除了“雨果餐厅”，还有 Casa Hugo、Chez Hugo、Hugo’s table，甚至 Hugo-San。Hugo-San 以 39.50 美元的价格提供城里最好的主厨推荐料理（Omakase）。这可能就是你未来的选择。

What do you do? Where do you dine? 11 Madison Park is suddenly not so busy anymore. And this is what is happening with BI. Simply put, the cost and speed of running a query through new-age text-to-sql is incredibly low. It means there is essentially no reason to ever do anything else, unless of course you absolutely have to.
你会怎么做？你会去哪里用餐？11 Madison Park 突然不再那么繁忙了。这就是 BI 正在发生的事情。简而言之，通过新一代 Text-to-SQL 运行查询的成本和速度极低。这意味着，除非你绝对必须这样做，否则基本上没有理由再采用其他方式。

**The Shifting Valley: The bar for Analytics just got higher**
**移动的“山谷”：分析的门槛变高了**

Imagine you don’t need to work hard to get the semantic layer going and Text to SQL can solve, reliably, repeatably, 95% of your questions. An analyst becomes almost like a super analyst. They are only going to be asked the really gnarly questions. They are probably the person that designed the semantic layer in the first place.
想象一下，你不需要费力去构建语义层，而 Text-to-SQL 就能可靠、可重复地解决你 95% 的问题。分析师几乎变成了“超级分析师”。他们只会面对那些真正棘手的问题。而他们很可能就是当初设计语义层的那个人。

The price of accuracy has become much higher. To do something manually, you had better be asking an extremely important question. What does this mean for Analysts? Well, much the same as it means for software engineers. It now means that the proportion of folks actually being useful just got a lot smaller — in a meritocratic way, of course.
准确性的代价变得更高了。如果要进行人工操作，你最好是在处理一个极其重要的问题。这对分析师意味着什么？嗯，这与对软件工程师的意义大致相同。这意味着真正有价值的人员比例变小了——当然，这是基于精英管理的逻辑。

An Analyst that cannot be a semantic architect will be useless in 12 months. They will be replaced by insights agents. And if they persist, then CTOs are doing something wrong and should probably bring in Service Partners to help them implement their semantic layer. The bar for working in Analytics just got higher. There’s a new competitor on the block and their name is A. Gent. Just like software engineering — juniors are out, and seniors are in.
一个无法成为语义架构师的分析师在 12 个月内将变得毫无用处。他们将被“洞察代理”（Insights Agents）所取代。如果他们依然故我，那么 CTO 就做错了，应该引入服务合作伙伴来帮助实施语义层。分析工作的门槛变高了。行业里出现了一个新竞争对手，它的名字叫 A. Gent（代理）。就像软件工程一样——初级人员出局，高级人员上位。

**What about token costs?**
**那么 Token 成本呢？**

Token costs is an interesting point. Token costs have generally decreased by 95% since the first models. The cost of inference continues to fall, and is expected to be reduced by a further 95% over the next few years. Despite this, companies like Uber are steaming through their credit bills. After all, credits are fun. Claude is addictive. Tokenmaxxing is encouraged.
Token 成本是一个有趣的议题。自首批模型发布以来，Token 成本总体下降了 95%。推理成本持续下降，预计在未来几年内还将进一步降低 95%。尽管如此，像 Uber 这样的公司仍在疯狂消耗额度。毕竟，使用额度很有趣，Claude 让人上瘾，追求 Token 最大化（Tokenmaxxing）也受到鼓励。

Indeed, the price of frontier models has not fallen dramatically. Claude costs in 2023 were c.$32/million output tokens. Now it is still $25. In fact, token use is expected to rise exponentially as systems get more complicated — it is not clear when, if at all, benefits will arise.
事实上，前沿模型的价格并没有大幅下降。2023 年 Claude 的成本约为每百万输出 Token 32 美元，现在仍为 25 美元。事实上，随着系统变得越来越复杂，Token 的使用量预计将呈指数级增长——目前尚不清楚何时（甚至是否）能显现出成本效益。
