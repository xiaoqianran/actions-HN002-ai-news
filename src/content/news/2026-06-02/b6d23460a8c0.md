# It’s the Lessons We Learned Along the Way. Or, Is It?
# 我们一路学到的教训。真的是这样吗？

**Artificial Intelligence: It’s the Lessons We Learned Along the Way. Or, Is It?**
**人工智能：我们一路学到的教训。真的是这样吗？**

Research projects in the age of AI. Jacopo Tagliabue, Jun 1, 2026. 11 min read.
人工智能时代的研究项目。Jacopo Tagliabue，2026年6月1日，阅读时长11分钟。

Our research project: implementing a query API across data branches. TL;DR: I tried to give ChatGPT a typical month-long internship problem in the data space. The problem in some sense got “solved” but I’m not sure it means what I thought it would. For data and AI practitioners, this is now a very practical question. Many teams use interns or research spikes to explore ideas: is AI good enough now? Are these projects only about the final artifact?
我们的研究项目：实现跨数据分支的查询 API。简而言之：我尝试让 ChatGPT 完成一个数据领域典型的为期一个月的实习课题。从某种意义上说，这个问题被“解决”了，但我并不确定这是否意味着我预想的结果。对于数据和人工智能从业者来说，这现在是一个非常实际的问题。许多团队利用实习生或研究突击项目来探索想法：人工智能现在足够好了吗？这些项目仅仅是为了最终的产出物吗？

### Interns as explorers
### 作为探索者的实习生

Building a tech roadmap at an early-stage data startup is not that different from a typical video game map: A video game map, where the world is revealed one step at a time. The roadmap is much bigger not only than what you can do, but also what you can see. If only we could peek over “the (product) horizon” by sending an explorer to clear the map, then we’d gain some awareness of what waits for you once you get there (the explorer may die, so the analogy is good up to a point).
在一家早期数据初创公司构建技术路线图，与典型的电子游戏地图并无二致：在电子游戏地图中，世界是一步步揭示的。路线图不仅比你能做的要大得多，也比你能看到的要大得多。如果我们能通过派遣一名探索者去“开图”来窥探“（产品）地平线”之外的情况，我们就能预知到达那里后会遇到什么（探索者可能会牺牲，所以这个类比在一定程度上是成立的）。

Bauplan (which I co-founded in 2024) has made the unusual (for its size) choice of running summer internships from top institutions (Columbia University, CMU, University of Wisconsin–Madison) to peek over the horizon. It has worked very well so far. Aside from a better hiring funnel, community standing, and some social clout, explorations made their way into our product, and will be strategic assets as the company grows.
Bauplan（我于2024年联合创立）做出了一个（对于其规模而言）不同寻常的选择：从顶尖学府（哥伦比亚大学、卡内基梅隆大学、威斯康星大学麦迪逊分校）招募暑期实习生来窥探地平线。到目前为止，效果非常好。除了更好的招聘渠道、社区地位和一定的社会影响力外，这些探索成果已经融入了我们的产品，并将随着公司的成长成为战略资产。

As I send out internship offers for the summer of 2026, half of my X feed is telling me that I’m going about it all wrong. Far from being an hypothetical problem, at different stages, sizes and constraints, all data and AI teams today are facing the same question: is there now a better way to do research spikes with agents? If yes, what is a good, tested AI setup that is easy to adapt? In the hope our experience and perspective will be valuable for many data practitioners, this is our setup and the lessons we learned from a real research spike done by pairing with ChatGPT.
当我发出2026年暑期实习录取通知时，我的 X（原 Twitter）信息流里有一半的人在告诉我，我的做法完全错了。这绝非一个假设性的问题，在不同的阶段、规模和限制下，今天所有的数据和人工智能团队都面临着同一个问题：现在是否有更好的方法利用智能体（Agents）来进行研究突击？如果有，什么是经过验证、易于适配的优秀人工智能配置？希望我们的经验和视角能对许多数据从业者有所帮助，以下是我们的配置，以及我们通过与 ChatGPT 结对完成一次真实研究突击所学到的教训。

### First, they came for the consultants, and I did not speak out…
### 起初，他们针对咨询师，我没有发声……

At a time when AI threatens knowledge workers, the junior positions seem to be the ones falling first. Why should McKinsey hire an Ivy League analyst when a $200 subscription produces more reports, faster? Lately, my feed seems to indicate that AI may be coming after researchers too, with academics trying to automate themselves – “fully autonomous research from idea to paper” – and professors debating whether to hire assistants anymore.
在人工智能威胁知识工作者的时代，初级职位似乎首当其冲。当一份200美元的订阅服务能更快地生成更多报告时，麦肯锡为什么还要雇佣常春藤盟校的分析师呢？最近，我的信息流似乎表明人工智能也可能盯上了研究人员，学术界人士正试图实现自我自动化——“从想法到论文的全自动研究”——教授们也在争论是否还需要雇佣助理。

There are obvious arguments to resist the trend. We can attack the outcome, and argue that the tech is still buggy so the promised “Ivy League” parity is just not there. We can argue that the social contract is being broken: sure, young researchers have always been (in some sense) a “burden”, but that burden was both a way to pay it forward and an investment in the next generation. We can also highlight the potential long-term damage of replacing a well-understood thought process with a new, untested workflow.
抵制这种趋势的论点显而易见。我们可以抨击结果，认为技术仍然存在缺陷，因此所承诺的“常春藤水平”的对等性根本不存在。我们可以争辩说社会契约正在被破坏：诚然，年轻研究人员在某种意义上一直是一种“负担”，但这种负担既是一种回馈社会的方式，也是对下一代的投资。我们还可以强调，用一种未经测试的新工作流程取代一种成熟的思维过程，可能会带来长期的潜在损害。

While there is weight behind all of these arguments, parallel arguments could be superficially construed for the invention of cars or what-have-you. There’s always a time and place for these debates, but my interest today is way more localized and personal: what would it feel like if I were to ditch my interns for a $200 subscription? So (not unlike this physics experiment I recently discovered) I tried to squeeze the month of an intern into a weekend with ChatGPT.
虽然所有这些论点都有其分量，但对于汽车等发明的出现，表面上也可以构建类似的论点。这些辩论总有其时间和场合，但我今天感兴趣的是更局部、更个人的问题：如果我放弃实习生而选择200美元的订阅服务，会是什么感觉？因此（就像我最近发现的那个物理实验一样），我尝试用 ChatGPT 将实习生一个月的工作压缩到一个周末完成。

### The mythical agent-month
### 神话般的“智能体月”

While the exact problem is not terribly important, scoping the internship may be useful to get a feeling of the type of things interns do at Bauplan (feel free to skip!). Bauplan is a branching data platform: agents and humans can open Git-like branches on their tables. As a result, the same table may have different versions in different branches. In our motivating example, Acme Inc. is an online retailer in which a swarm of data agents is tasked with running different predictions on tomorrow’s sales.
虽然具体问题本身并不重要，但界定实习范围有助于了解实习生在 Bauplan 做什么（请随意跳过！）。Bauplan 是一个分支数据平台：智能体和人类可以在数据表上开启类似 Git 的分支。因此，同一张表在不同的分支中可能有不同的版本。在我们的激励示例中，Acme 公司是一家在线零售商，一群数据智能体被分配任务，对明天的销售额进行不同的预测。

Ideally, a human would verify the work, compare and contrast the findings, and then merge the predictions table as the canonical data representation. But what if somebody asks a question before this happens? Existing systems would just refuse to answer, even if this feels intuitively wasteful: two agents computing monthly revenue may disagree on the exact figure, yet both agree that revenue grew >10% quarter-over-quarter. In other words, even when there is no system-wide agreed-upon version of data, we could still answer many interesting questions.
理想情况下，人类会验证工作、对比分析结果，然后将预测表合并为规范的数据表示。但如果在此之前有人提问怎么办？现有的系统只会拒绝回答，尽管这在直觉上显得很浪费：两个计算月度收入的智能体可能对具体数字有分歧，但都同意收入环比增长超过10%。换句话说，即使没有系统范围内公认的数据版本，我们仍然可以回答许多有趣的问题。

Our internship goal is then to build a prototype of such a system. It requires learning about branching, picking up new math, designing a solution on top of Bauplan, and building both a text-to-SQL module (easy-ish) and a custom query path (hard-ish).
我们的实习目标就是构建这样一个系统的原型。这需要学习分支技术、掌握新的数学知识、在 Bauplan 之上设计解决方案，并构建一个文本转 SQL 模块（相对容易）和一个自定义查询路径（相对困难）。

### The AI setup
### 人工智能配置

Bauplaners recently had the privilege of seeing the one and only Wes McKinney giving a live demo of his setup, so I decided to adopt it (with some minor tweaks):
Bauplan 的成员最近有幸观看了 Wes McKinney 本人演示他的配置，所以我决定采用它（并做了一些微调）：

*   **ChatGPT 5.2** to plan and decide on strategies (i.e. how to design a benchmark highlighting the difference between engineering approaches);
*   **ChatGPT 5.2** 用于规划和决策策略（例如，如何设计一个基准测试来突出不同工程方法之间的差异）；
*   **Claude Code** inside Visual Studio to do the actual development loop;
*   **Visual Studio 中的 Claude Code** 用于执行实际的开发循环；
*   **Roborev** to locally review commits adversarially. Powered by Codex, the reviews highlight potential issues and suggest improvements;
*   **Roborev** 用于在本地对提交进行对抗性审查。在 Codex 的支持下，审查会突出潜在问题并提出改进建议；
*   **Roborev reviews** to tame project complexity every 10 commits or so: these reviews take an architectural point of view and help with cutting the bloat.
*   **Roborev 审查** 用于每提交约10次后控制项目复杂度：这些审查从架构角度出发，有助于精简冗余。
