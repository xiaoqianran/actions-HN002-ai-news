# Code Is Cheap. Engineering Judgement Is Now the Scarce Resource
# 代码已变得廉价，工程判断力才是稀缺资源

The barriers to building have collapsed. That shifts the bottleneck to ownership, validation, taste, and deciding what should actually exist.
构建产品的门槛已经崩塌。这使得瓶颈转移到了所有权、验证、品味以及决定什么东西真正应该存在上。

The game theory underlying society is changing. That was one of the ideas from Max Buckley’s talk at AI Engineer Singapore, and it has stuck with me ever since. For decades, software engineering was organised around scarcity. Code was expensive to write, engineers were scarce and features took time. This assumption shaped how teams worked. We prioritised carefully because every feature had a huge opportunity cost.
社会底层的博弈论正在发生改变。这是 Max Buckley 在新加坡 AI 工程师大会上演讲时提出的观点之一，自那以后一直萦绕在我的脑海中。几十年来，软件工程一直围绕着“稀缺性”来组织。编写代码成本高昂，工程师稀缺，功能开发也需要时间。这种假设塑造了团队的工作方式。我们必须谨慎地进行优先级排序，因为每一个功能都伴随着巨大的机会成本。

But AI has broken that assumption. As coding agents become more capable, the cost of implementation is falling dramatically. What used to take weeks can now be prototyped in days or even hours. Max, who is Head of Knowledge Research at Exa after spending 12.5 years at Google, framed this as a change in game theory: it is not just about asking what you should do, but what you should do when everyone else is also behaving this way and trying to win. And you cannot opt out of these changes. Whether or not we’re ready for it, the fundamental ways of working are changing.
但人工智能打破了这一假设。随着编程智能体（Coding Agents）能力越来越强，实现成本正在急剧下降。过去需要数周才能完成的工作，现在几天甚至几小时就能完成原型设计。Max 在谷歌工作了 12.5 年后，目前担任 Exa 的知识研究主管，他将此定义为博弈论的转变：这不仅仅是问你应该做什么，而是当其他人都在以同样的方式行事并试图获胜时，你该做什么。你无法选择退出这些变化。无论我们是否准备好，根本的工作方式正在发生改变。

But cheaper implementation does not mean better software. No amount of AI can save us from building the wrong thing. In fact, AI may make that problem worse. When building becomes easier, it becomes much easier to create things that are technically impressive but strategically irrelevant: More dashboards, more workflows, more internal tools, more apps that work but do not deserve to exist. That is why I think engineering judgment is becoming more valuable.
但更廉价的实现并不意味着更好的软件。再多的人工智能也无法阻止我们构建错误的东西。事实上，人工智能可能会加剧这个问题。当构建变得更容易时，创造出技术上令人印象深刻但在战略上无关紧要的东西就变得非常容易：更多的仪表盘、更多的工作流、更多的内部工具、更多能运行但本不该存在的应用程序。这就是为什么我认为工程判断力正变得越来越有价值。

One of Max’s examples stayed with me. In the old software economy, teams must narrow 30 ideas down to 3 before building anything. With coding agents today, the decision process changes. You can build more, evaluate more, benchmark more, and discard what does not work with less emotional attachment. The cost of trying is lower and experimentation becomes more attractive. That sounds liberating but it also creates a new bottleneck. If anyone can prototype an idea, attention is now a scarce resource.
Max 的一个例子让我印象深刻。在旧的软件经济中，团队必须在构建任何东西之前将 30 个想法精简为 3 个。而有了今天的编程智能体，决策过程发生了变化。你可以构建更多、评估更多、基准测试更多，并以更少的感情色彩丢弃那些行不通的东西。尝试的成本降低了，实验变得更具吸引力。这听起来很自由，但也创造了一个新的瓶颈。如果任何人都能对一个想法进行原型设计，那么“注意力”现在就成了稀缺资源。

AI is not removing the need for engineering discipline. It is moving that discipline to a different part of the system. Technical knowledge is changing shape too. Models have jagged intelligence: They can be extremely good at some tasks, but surprisingly bad at nearby tasks that seem equally easy to humans. Models often know the answers to complicated things but will not surface it unless you know what to ask. So the question is no longer just whether we can build something. It is whether it should exist.
人工智能并没有消除对工程纪律的需求。它只是将这种纪律转移到了系统的不同部分。技术知识的形态也在改变。模型具有“锯齿状智能”：它们在某些任务上可能极其出色，但在人类看来同样简单的相邻任务上却表现得令人惊讶地糟糕。模型通常知道复杂问题的答案，但除非你知道如何提问，否则它们不会主动提供。因此，问题不再仅仅是我们能否构建某样东西，而是它是否应该存在。

Jimmy Lai, Director of Next.js at Vercel, shared a similar sentiment from a different angle. His point was that AI has made creation cheap, but ownership more expensive. When building becomes easier, the number of things we can create goes up. But every prototype that survives becomes something someone has to maintain, debug, document, secure, and explain. The cost of writing the first version may fall, but the cost of owning the system does not disappear.
Vercel 的 Next.js 总监 Jimmy Lai 从另一个角度分享了类似的观点。他的核心观点是：人工智能让创造变得廉价，但让“所有权”变得更加昂贵。当构建变得更容易时，我们能创造的东西数量就会增加。但每一个存活下来的原型，都成了需要有人去维护、调试、记录、保护和解释的对象。编写第一个版本的成本可能会下降，但拥有该系统的成本并不会消失。

Jimmy made three predictions that stood out to me. First, we are now building for agents. Agents are becoming a new kind of software user. A stale README is no longer just annoying for a human. It is a hallucination waiting to happen. Second, we are now building with agents. Ironically, though it now gets easier to be able to build something you don’t quite understand, the truth is that the fundamentals have not changed and have in fact become more important than ever before. If you become excellent at building with agents while also being strong in the fundamentals, you become unstoppable. Third, we have to learn what not to own. Just because you can build something does not mean you should. The ease of creation has become a maintenance burden. This does not mean we should ship less. It means we should be more intentional about what we allow to survive.
Jimmy 提出了三个让我印象深刻的预测。第一，我们现在是在为智能体构建软件。智能体正在成为一种新型的软件用户。一份过时的 README 对人类来说不再仅仅是令人讨厌，它更是一个随时可能引发幻觉的隐患。第二，我们现在是在与智能体协作构建。讽刺的是，虽然现在构建你不完全理解的东西变得更容易了，但事实是基础知识并没有改变，反而变得比以往任何时候都更重要。如果你在擅长使用智能体构建的同时，又拥有扎实的基础知识，你将势不可挡。第三，我们必须学会什么不该拥有。仅仅因为你能构建某样东西，并不意味着你应该去构建它。创造的便捷性已经变成了一种维护负担。这并不意味着我们应该减少交付，而是意味着我们应该更有意识地决定让什么东西存活下来。

Finally, my last key takeaway came from a design talk. Phil Hedayatnia from Airfoil gave a talk on how to create design agents that actually have taste in a sea of very normal AI slop. I am not a designer, so I usually think of design in terms of what a good design should or should not contain. His talk reframed that for me. Design isn’t trying to teach someone about what to do and not what to do. That is training on outcomes. Good design is about understanding how people think, how they act, and why certain flows, visuals, and narratives resonate with them.
最后，我的最后一个关键收获来自一场设计讲座。来自 Airfoil 的 Phil Hedayatnia 谈到了如何在平庸的 AI 生成内容海洋中，创造出真正具有“品味”的设计智能体。我不是设计师，所以我通常从一个好的设计应该包含或不应该包含什么来思考设计。他的演讲重塑了我的认知。设计不是试图教导某人该做什么或不该做什么，那是对结果的训练。好的设计在于理解人们如何思考、如何行动，以及为什么某些流程、视觉效果和叙事能与他们产生共鸣。
