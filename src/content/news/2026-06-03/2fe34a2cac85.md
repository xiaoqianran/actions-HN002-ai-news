# Planktonzilla: Multimodal dataset and models for understanding plankton ecosystems
# Planktonzilla：用于理解浮游生物生态系统的多模态数据集与模型

Marine plankton underpin aquatic food webs and play a key role in global CO2 sequestration, making reliable species identification critical for understanding ocean health and climate feedbacks.
海洋浮游生物是水生食物网的基石，在全球二氧化碳封存中发挥着关键作用，因此可靠的物种识别对于理解海洋健康和气候反馈至关重要。

Existing classification models perform well on individual collections but fail to generalize across instruments and environments due to isolated training datasets and inconsistent labels.
现有的分类模型在单一数据集上表现良好，但由于训练数据集孤立且标签不一致，难以在不同仪器和环境之间实现泛化。

To address this, we introduce Planktonzilla-17M, a unified dataset consolidating publicly available plankton image collections spanning thirteen imaging systems.
为了解决这一问题，我们推出了 Planktonzilla-17M，这是一个统一的数据集，整合了涵盖 13 种成像系统的公开浮游生物图像集。

It comprises 17.4 million images with standardized taxonomy and geo-environmental metadata, including 3.74 million plankton images spanning over 602 taxonomic classes, of which 201 are identified at the species level, making it the largest and most comprehensive plankton image dataset to date.
该数据集包含 1740 万张图像，并配有标准化的分类学和地理环境元数据。其中包含 374 万张浮游生物图像，涵盖 602 个分类学类别，其中 201 个类别已精确到物种水平，使其成为迄今为止规模最大、最全面的浮游生物图像数据集。

Using this large-scale dataset, we perform a controlled comparison between supervised and CLIP-style image-text training on a shared ViT backbone.
利用这一大规模数据集，我们在共享的 ViT 主干网络上对监督学习和 CLIP 风格的图文训练进行了对照比较。

We find that a supervised classifier matches or exceeds CLIP-style training when trained using taxonomic lineage as text.
我们发现，当使用分类学谱系作为文本进行训练时，监督分类器的表现可以媲美甚至超过 CLIP 风格的训练。

We further observe that BioCLIP and BioCLIP2 perform poorly on plankton in zero-shot and few-shot settings.
我们进一步观察到，BioCLIP 和 BioCLIP2 在浮游生物的零样本（zero-shot）和小样本（few-shot）设置下表现不佳。

Leveraging Planktonzilla-17M improves plankton classification performance, highlighting the limitations of current biological foundation models in marine imaging domains.
利用 Planktonzilla-17M 可以提升浮游生物的分类性能，这也凸显了当前生物学基础模型在海洋成像领域存在的局限性。
