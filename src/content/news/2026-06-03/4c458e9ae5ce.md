# From Regex to Vision Models: Which RAG Technique Fits Which Problem
# 从正则表达式到视觉模型：哪种 RAG 技术适合哪类问题

Large Language Models From Regex to Vision Models: Which RAG Technique Fits Which Problem Enterprise Document Intelligence [Vol.1 #4] — A diagnostic across PDFs and questions, and a map of the techniques the rest of the series will cover.
大型语言模型：从正则表达式到视觉模型——哪种 RAG 技术适合哪类问题？企业文档智能 [第1卷 #4] —— 一份针对 PDF 和问题的诊断指南，以及本系列后续将涵盖的技术路线图。

Most RAG problems don’t deserve the classic playbook. Article 3 said there is no THE RAG technique. You still have to pick one. This article is the diagnostic that tells you which.
大多数 RAG 问题并不需要那种“经典剧本”。在第三篇文章中我们提到，并不存在所谓的“唯一 RAG 技术”。你仍然需要做出选择，而本文就是帮你做出诊断的指南。

Most teams building RAG systems reach for the same playbook: parse the document into chunks, embed every chunk, drop them in a vector store, embed the question, retrieve the top-k by cosine similarity, hand the result to an LLM. Call it the classic RAG playbook. Every tutorial teaches it. Every demo runs on it. The actual problems vary much more than the playbook suggests.
大多数构建 RAG 系统的团队都倾向于使用同一套剧本：将文档解析为块（chunks），对每个块进行向量化（embedding），存入向量数据库，对问题进行向量化，通过余弦相似度检索前 K 个结果，最后交给大模型（LLM）。这就是所谓的“经典 RAG 剧本”。每个教程都在教它，每个演示都在用它。但实际问题的复杂程度远超这套剧本的覆盖范围。

### A few real cases. Three cases at three different extremes.
### 几个真实案例：三个处于不同极端的场景。

**Templated, high-volume documents.** Insurance certificates, KYC forms, regulatory filings, monthly brokerage statements. The same software writes the same layout on every document. A hundred lines of regex extract the fields in microseconds. The classic playbook runs here too but it pays an LLM to do what the layout gave you for free. Same shape across industries: payroll slubs, bank statements, lab test reports, tax filings, compliance attestations, supplier invoices from one ERP. Wherever one piece of software writes every document, the layout is a contract.
**模板化、高频文档。** 如保险凭证、KYC 表格、监管备案文件、月度经纪账单。同样的软件在每份文档上生成同样的布局。只需一百行正则表达式，就能在微秒级时间内提取字段。经典剧本在这里也能用，但它却在花钱让大模型做布局本身就能免费提供的事情。各行各业都有类似情况：工资单、银行对账单、实验室检测报告、税务申报表、合规证明、来自同一 ERP 系统的供应商发票。只要是同一软件生成的文档，其布局就是一种“契约”。

**Sarcasm in customer-service transcripts.** “Find every sarcastic remark in this month’s call recordings.” Standard sentiment scoring (anger, frustration, joy) is largely solved by a sentiment lexicon: unacceptable, ridiculous, frustrated all flag clearly. Sarcasm is the canonical exception. “Oh, fantastic service, only had to wait 45 minutes” scores positive on every lexicon, and the embedding clusters it with the sincere version because the surface words are nearly the same. The only honest method is an LLM that reads each call in full and judges the gap between what is said and what is meant. Same shape across functions: HR exit interviews looking for hidden frustration, internal-chat archives looking for cultural red flags before an M&A close, earnings-call transcripts looking for places the CFO hedged, sales-call recordings looking for promises the contract did not authorise. Tone and intent, no anchor in the text.
**客户服务记录中的讽刺。** “找出本月通话记录中所有的讽刺言论。” 标准的情感评分（愤怒、沮丧、喜悦）通常可以通过情感词典解决：如“不可接受”、“荒谬”、“沮丧”等词汇标记非常清晰。但讽刺是典型的例外。“哦，服务真是太棒了，我只等了 45 分钟”，在任何词典中都会被评为正面，向量化模型也会将其与真诚的评价归为一类，因为它们的表面词汇几乎相同。唯一有效的方法是使用大模型完整阅读通话记录，并判断“所说内容”与“真实意图”之间的差距。在其他职能中也有类似情况：人力资源离职面谈中寻找隐藏的沮丧情绪、并购前内部聊天记录中寻找文化红线、财报电话会议中寻找 CFO 的避重就轻、销售通话中寻找合同未授权的承诺。这些都关乎语气和意图，在文本中没有固定的锚点。

**Engineering schematics (a different axis altogether).** Drawings, slides where data lives in the chart, technical specs with embedded images. Pure-text RAG returns the caption and misses the schematic. Vision models fit here, and only here. Same shape: architectural blueprints, scanned handwritten records, slide decks where data lives in the chart, lab notebook pages, medical imaging reports. Wherever the meaning lives in the pixels.
**工程图纸（完全不同的维度）。** 图纸、数据存在于图表中的幻灯片、带有嵌入图像的技术规格说明书。纯文本 RAG 只能返回标题，却错过了图表本身。视觉模型适用于这里，且仅适用于这里。类似场景：建筑蓝图、扫描的手写记录、数据依赖图表的幻灯片、实验室笔记本页面、医学影像报告。凡是意义存在于像素中的地方，都需要视觉模型。

The classic playbook is overkill on templated documents (regex would do), dimensionally wrong on call transcripts (no anchor exists), and modality-blind on schematics (vision is required). It fits a middle band of problems and ships as if it covered everything. That middle band is real and Section 3.3 catalogues it; the cost of mismatch on the rest is what this article exists to prevent.
经典剧本在模板化文档上属于“杀鸡用牛刀”（正则表达式足矣），在通话记录上维度错误（不存在锚点），在图纸上则是“模态盲”（需要视觉能力）。它只适合中间地带的一类问题，却被包装成万能方案。那个中间地带确实存在，第 3.3 节有详细分类；而本文的目的，正是为了防止在其他场景中因技术错配而付出代价。

### 1. Two axes: document complexity and question control
### 1. 两个维度：文档复杂度和问题控制度

Every problem we’ll meet in this series sits somewhere on two axes:
我们在本系列中遇到的每个问题，都位于以下两个坐标轴的某处：

*   **Document complexity:** How redundant is the structure across your documents? Can a parser address fields by position, by heading, or do you need a model that sees the page?
    **文档复杂度：** 文档结构的冗余度如何？解析器能否通过位置或标题定位字段，还是需要一个能“看懂”页面的模型？
*   **Question control:** Who frames the question? An engineer writing a fixed prompt, or a user typing freely into a chat box, possibly with no idea what to ask?
    **问题控制度：** 问题由谁提出？是工程师编写的固定提示词，还是用户在聊天框中自由输入（甚至可能不知道该问什么）？

These two axes are almost independent. The one coupling: a fixed-template document (Tier 1, below) usually forces engineer-templated questions (Tier A), since the user never types a question. Outside that corner, any document tier can pair with any question tier.
这两个轴几乎是独立的。唯一的耦合点是：固定模板文档（下文的第 1 层）通常强制要求使用工程师预设的问题（A 层），因为用户根本不会输入问题。除此以外，任何文档层级都可以与任何问题层级配对。
