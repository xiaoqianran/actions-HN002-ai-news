# I Spent May Evaluating Different Engines for OCR
# 我花了一个五月的时间来评估不同的 OCR 引擎

机器学习：我花了一个五月的时间来评估不同的 OCR 引擎。测试了 14 款引擎，处理了 93 份人类文档。作者：Ida Silfverskiöld，2026 年 6 月 3 日，阅读时长 17 分钟。分享。纯属娱乐——下文有一个真实的散点图 | 图片由作者提供。

Not all documents were supposed to be read by a machine. Old hotel invoices, bank statements, payslips, loan applications, medical bills, customs forms, court filings, work orders. Most companies use free tools alongside paid APIs to try to convert these documents, and if you want structured output, APIs like Textract Structured run you up to around $65 per 1k pages.
并非所有的文档都是为了让机器阅读而设计的。旧的酒店发票、银行对账单、工资单、贷款申请、医疗账单、海关表格、法庭文件、工作订单等。大多数公司会结合使用免费工具和付费 API 来尝试转换这些文档；如果你需要结构化输出，像 Textract Structured 这样的 API 每 1000 页的成本高达 65 美元左右。

In the last few years, though, a lot of new options have appeared: smaller open-source vision models specialized for OCR, general vision-language models, and document parsing tools like LlamaParse — changing what’s possible and the cost thereof.
然而，在过去几年中，出现了许多新的选择：专门用于 OCR 的小型开源视觉模型、通用视觉语言模型，以及像 LlamaParse 这样的文档解析工具——这些改变了 OCR 的可能性及其成本。

Rough timeline—we see more OCR solutions after 2024 | Image by author So it felt like a good time to do my own experiment to test some of these against documents of varying difficulty. I scouted 93 docs that could act as a proxy for what companies use OCR for — handwritten notes, tables, financial legacy docs, scanned invoices, receipts, charts, old newspapers, tax forms — then ran them all through 14 different engines.
大致时间线——我们在 2024 年后看到了更多的 OCR 解决方案 | 图片由作者提供。因此，我觉得现在是进行实验以测试这些工具在不同难度文档下表现的好时机。我搜集了 93 份文档，它们可以作为公司使用 OCR 场景的代表——手写笔记、表格、遗留财务文档、扫描发票、收据、图表、旧报纸、税务表格——然后用 14 种不同的引擎对它们进行了测试。

The idea was to see how they handled two things: text recovery and the ability to preserve useful table structure. The main question I wanted answered: do you really need to pay $65 for 1k structured pages, or can you slice that down to a fraction? And does the specialized models win over the general ones?
我的想法是观察它们如何处理两件事：文本恢复能力以及保留有用表格结构的能力。我想要回答的主要问题是：你真的需要为 1000 页结构化文档支付 65 美元吗？还是可以将成本降低到几分之一？此外，专用模型是否比通用模型表现更好？

When doing experiments like this you always find quite a few strange things, which I’ll cover too. But to answer that main question I’ll take you through what OCR is (skip if not new), the economics, the test, some of the results, and what else this showed me. Note: I did not test full field extraction, since that is harder to compare cleanly across fourteen engines.
在进行此类实验时，你总会发现一些奇怪的事情，我也会在文中提到。但为了回答那个核心问题，我将带你了解什么是 OCR（如果是老手可跳过）、经济性、测试过程、部分结果，以及这次实验还向我展示了什么。注：我没有测试完整的字段提取，因为这在 14 个引擎之间很难进行清晰的对比。

TL;DR There is no single best OCR engine. OCR is a routing problem. For clean high-volume documents, Tesseract is still hard to beat because it is free and fast. For mixed production documents, Gemini Flash was the best all-rounder in this test. For tables, Mistral OCR looked like the cheaper structured option. The smaller specialist models looked good inside their comfort zone, but failed harder at documents they hadn’t seen.
简而言之：没有单一的最佳 OCR 引擎。OCR 是一个路由问题。对于干净的大批量文档，Tesseract 仍然难以被超越，因为它免费且快速。对于混合生产文档，Gemini Flash 是本次测试中表现最全面的。对于表格，Mistral OCR 看起来是更便宜的结构化选项。较小型的专业模型在它们的“舒适区”内表现良好，但在处理未见过的文档时失败率更高。

The main takeaway is economic: don’t pay for expensive structured OCR when the document does not need it. Classify your docs, test engines on your own data, and route based on cost, accuracy, structure, and failure tolerance. Benchmarks are useful for discovery, but they will not tell you what works on your documents.
主要的结论是经济层面的：当文档不需要时，不要支付昂贵的结构化 OCR 费用。对你的文档进行分类，在自己的数据上测试引擎，并根据成本、准确性、结构和容错率进行路由选择。基准测试对于发现工具很有用，但它们无法告诉你什么对你的文档最有效。

Explain the OCR space to me. OCR (Optical Character Recognition) is how a machine turns a picture into machine readable text. Simple in principle, and for easier docs mostly solved, but harder when things become more human.
向我解释一下 OCR 领域。OCR（光学字符识别）是机器将图片转换为机器可读文本的技术。原理很简单，对于简单的文档来说基本已经解决，但当内容变得更“人性化”时，难度就会增加。

Just to give you a quick overview, older OCR found text on a page, sliced it into characters, and matched each one against a library of known shapes. Tesseract has done this since the 1980s. Modern OCR however (including newer versions of Tesseract) usually uses a neural network that looks at the whole page at once and outputs the document as text.
简单概述一下，早期的 OCR 是在页面上找到文本，将其切分成字符，并将每个字符与已知形状的库进行匹配。Tesseract 自 20 世纪 80 年代以来一直这样做。然而，现代 OCR（包括较新版本的 Tesseract）通常使用神经网络，一次性查看整个页面并将文档输出为文本。

So, if your document is a clean PDF or a high-quality scan in a standard font, OCR is mostly a solved problem. It stops being solved the moment things get messier: photographed receipts, handwritten notes, weird graphs and charts, dense financial tables, or scanned tax forms and loan applications.
因此，如果你的文档是干净的 PDF 或标准字体的高质量扫描件，OCR 问题基本已经解决。但一旦情况变得复杂，问题就不再那么容易解决了：比如拍摄的收据、手写笔记、奇怪的图表、密集的财务表格，或者扫描的税务表格和贷款申请。

Companies need this done well for obvious reasons, as it’s something every downstream system acts on. The better OCR gets, the more paperwork becomes something a system can reason over instead of something a human has to read by hand. There is also the fact that if we feed AI systems badly parsed docs, everything after it will be hard to trust.
公司需要很好地完成这项工作，原因显而易见，因为这是每个下游系统运作的基础。OCR 越好，文书工作就越能成为系统可以推理的对象，而不是必须由人工阅读的内容。此外，如果我们向 AI 系统输入解析糟糕的文档，后续的一切都将难以信任。

I’m all about economics, so this space caught my eye once I saw how much money is being poured into it. The Intelligent Document Processing (IDP) market is projected to grow to somewhere between $20 billion and $90 billion by the early 2030s, depending on which analyst you ask. Probably driven by companies paying $15–25 per invoice in manual handling costs.
我非常关注经济性，所以当我看到这个领域投入了多少资金时，它引起了我的注意。智能文档处理（IDP）市场预计到 2030 年代初将增长到 200 亿至 900 亿美元之间，具体取决于你咨询哪位分析师。这很可能是由公司每张发票支付 15-25 美元的人工处理成本所驱动的。

And because I stay close to the tech world, I’ve watched a wave of specialized small OCR models ship over the past year (mostly Chinese), now being used by developers everywhere.
由于我一直密切关注科技界，我观察到过去一年中涌现了一波专门的小型 OCR 模型（大多来自中国），现在正被各地的开发者使用。

Some of the OCR specialized models released last year | Image by author Which raises the question I wanted to test: can the small open-source models actually do the work the expensive APIs charge for or should we actually look towards the general vision models to handle OCR too?
去年发布的一些 OCR 专用模型 | 图片由作者提供。这就引出了我想测试的问题：小型开源模型真的能完成那些昂贵 API 所收费的工作吗？还是我们应该转向通用视觉模型来处理 OCR？

Skip the next section if you want to understand what this experiment showed. I have to go through the test setup first. The docs, the engines, and the metrics. This experiment comes down to three questions: what engines we used, what docs we tested with, and how we decided who won.
如果你想直接了解实验结果，可以跳过下一节。我必须先介绍测试设置：文档、引擎和指标。这个实验归结为三个问题：我们使用了哪些引擎，我们测试了哪些文档，以及我们如何判定胜负。

For the engines, I wanted a lineup that covered all the choices I talked about, this meant: old and new, open and closed, local and cloud, specialized and general. Tesseract became the classical choice. It runs locally and is very fast. Then I added two document-parsing pipelines: Docling and Marker. Docling is slower but runs on CPU, Marker is open-weight but needing a GPU to run fast, which shows up later in the price.
关于引擎，我想要一个涵盖我所讨论的所有选择的阵容，这意味着：新旧结合、开源与闭源、本地与云端、专用与通用。Tesseract 成为了经典选择。它在本地运行且速度非常快。然后我添加了两个文档解析流水线：Docling 和 Marker。Docling 较慢但在 CPU 上运行，Marker 是开源权重模型，但需要 GPU 才能快速运行，这在后续的成本中有所体现。

Then for the new wave of specialized open OCR models: GLM-OCR, PaddleOCR-VL, DeepSeek-OCR, and MinerU 2.5 (a borderline case, really a pipeline with a VLM inside). I picked them off OpenDataLab’s OmniDocBench leaderboard, where they ranked first, second, fourth, and fifth. I hosted them on Modal and served the applicable ones with vLLM, batching to speed things up. I counted the scale-up time when measuring latency later. I also added one close...
然后是新一波的专用开源 OCR 模型：GLM-OCR、PaddleOCR-VL、DeepSeek-OCR 和 MinerU 2.5（这是一个边缘案例，实际上是一个内置 VLM 的流水线）。我从 OpenDataLab 的 OmniDocBench 排行榜中挑选了它们，它们分别排名第一、第二、第四和第五。我将它们托管在 Modal 上，并使用 vLLM 为适用的模型提供服务，通过批处理来加速。我在后续测量延迟时计算了扩展时间。我还添加了一个闭源……
