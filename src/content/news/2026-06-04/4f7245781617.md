# Why AI Is NOT Stealing Your Job
# 为什么人工智能并没有“偷走”你的工作

**AI does not decide who gets fired. Companies do.**
**人工智能并不会决定谁被解雇，决定权在公司手中。**

The anxiety is there. You might lose your job because of AI. Maybe you already did. Maybe you are struggling to enter an industry that now hires fewer juniors. And if your job survives, competition may become harsher: fewer openings, higher expectations, lower bargaining power. However, AI alone is not a danger. It is AI deployed inside an economy where workers have little leverage over how productivity gains are distributed.
焦虑感是真实存在的。你可能会因为人工智能而失去工作，或许你已经失去了。也许你正挣扎着进入一个招聘初级岗位越来越少的行业。即便你的工作得以保留，竞争也可能变得更加残酷：职位空缺减少、期望值提高、议价能力下降。然而，人工智能本身并不是威胁。真正的威胁在于，人工智能被部署在一个劳动者对生产力收益分配几乎没有话语权的经济体系中。

### AI Anxiety Is Rational
### 对人工智能的焦虑是合理的

Technological change has always reshaped labor markets. Some jobs disappear, others emerge, and society adapts. So isn’t AI just another technology that will make some jobs obsolete in favor of more efficiency while creating new jobs? Many argue it is not. While it is hard to have a clear vision of what the future will look like, the belief is that AI is fundamentally different because of the scale and speed of the disruption it could cause.
技术变革一直在重塑劳动力市场。一些工作消失了，另一些工作随之产生，社会也在不断适应。那么，人工智能不就是另一种通过提高效率使某些工作过时，同时创造新工作的技术吗？许多人认为并非如此。虽然很难清晰地预见未来，但人们普遍认为，人工智能因其可能引发的颠覆规模和速度，而具有本质上的不同。

New agentic models can perform some programming tasks at a level approaching or exceeding experienced developers, and even show human qualities such as judgment and taste. Software engineering is only the first of many knowledge industries being transformed, as an AI which can code “can help build the next version of itself”. However, the impact of AI is visible in many different occupations. A translator who specialized in routine business documents sees clients disappear almost overnight. A customer support worker is told to supervise chatbots instead of talking to people. A junior legal assistant who once learned by summarizing case law or doing legal research now competes with seniors augmented by AI tools.
新的智能体模型（agentic models）执行某些编程任务的水平已接近甚至超过了经验丰富的开发人员，甚至展现出判断力和品味等人类特质。软件工程只是众多被转型知识产业中的第一个，因为能够编写代码的人工智能“可以帮助构建自身的下一个版本”。然而，人工智能的影响在许多不同职业中都显而易见。一名专门从事日常商业文档翻译的译员发现客户几乎在一夜之间消失了；一名客户支持人员被要求监督聊天机器人，而不是与人交流；一名曾经通过总结判例法或进行法律研究来学习的初级法律助理，现在不得不与由人工智能工具辅助的资深人士竞争。

Some are worried that a large share of computer-based knowledge work could eventually be automated, with massive layoffs happening simultaneously. Even though AI might create some new jobs, it doesn’t look like the new jobs created by AI will compensate for the scale and quality of jobs being displaced. Worse, job displacement is not merely a side effect. Many companies explicitly view AI as a tool for reducing headcount. We see this from the idealization of “a single-person-company […] reaching a billion dollar valuation” and the controversial ad campaigns inciting to “stop hiring humans” in favor of AI, which “won’t complain about work-life balance”.
有些人担心，很大一部分基于计算机的知识工作最终可能会被自动化，并伴随着大规模裁员。尽管人工智能可能会创造一些新工作，但这些新工作似乎无法弥补被取代工作的规模和质量。更糟糕的是，失业并非仅仅是副作用。许多公司明确将人工智能视为裁员的工具。我们从“单人公司……达到十亿美元估值”的理想化叙事，以及那些煽动“停止雇佣人类”、转而使用“不会抱怨工作与生活平衡”的人工智能的争议性广告活动中，就能看出这一点。

### AI Automation Is Not The Real Problem
### 人工智能自动化并非真正的问题所在

Businesses operating in competitive markets are structurally incentivized to reduce costs and increase productivity. A relevant part of it is to reduce the costs related to labor. So, if a technology exists, that is cheaper and more efficient than human labor, it is in the company’s interest to replace or reduce the number of humans involved in production. Costs go down, dividends go up. Nobody would argue against this picture. But this vision makes technology good for company owners, and bad for workers, promoting anti-technology stances such as Luddism.
在竞争激烈的市场中运营的企业，在结构上就有降低成本和提高生产力的动力。其中一个重要部分就是降低与劳动力相关的成本。因此，如果存在一种比人类劳动更廉价、更高效的技术，那么用它来替代或减少生产中的人力投入就符合公司的利益。成本下降，股息上升。没有人会反驳这一图景。但这种愿景使技术对公司所有者有利，而对劳动者不利，从而助长了诸如卢德主义（Luddism）等反技术立场。

There is a surprisingly overlooked way technology could benefit workers. Imagine a worker who becomes ten times more productive because of AI. Let’s say that production needs to remain unchanged due to unchanged demand. Society could respond in at least two ways: One worker keeps the job while nine lose theirs. Ten workers keep their jobs, but all work far fewer hours. Technology alone does not determine which outcome happens. Politics, labor power, and economic institutions do.
有一种被惊人忽视的方式可以让技术造福劳动者。想象一下，一名工人因为人工智能而变得高效了十倍。假设由于需求不变，生产总量也需要保持不变。社会至少可以通过两种方式来应对：要么留下一名工人，解雇其他九人；要么十名工人全部留任，但工作时长大幅缩短。技术本身并不能决定结果如何，政治、劳工力量和经济制度才能决定。

### Increased Productivity Did Not Decrease Working Hours
### 生产力的提高并未减少工作时长

History suggests that productivity gains are rarely distributed automatically in favor of workers. For example, since the most valuable thing we have in life is time, and technology is requiring less time to perform tasks, we would expect this to reflect on working hours. Since World War II, workers became dramatically more productive thanks to industrialization, computers, logistics, and automation. Yet, most productivity gains were not converted into leisure time. They were converted into profits, executive compensation, and shareholder value.
历史表明，生产力的收益很少会自动分配给劳动者。例如，由于我们生命中最宝贵的是时间，而技术减少了完成任务所需的时间，我们本应预期这会反映在工作时长上。自二战以来，得益于工业化、计算机、物流和自动化，工人的生产力大幅提高。然而，大部分生产力收益并没有转化为闲暇时间，而是转化为了利润、高管薪酬和股东价值。

Of course, sharing productivity gains does not necessarily mean only shorter working hours. It can also mean higher wages, stronger public services, worker ownership, or broader social participation in the wealth generated by automation. But if the overall impact of a technology is increasing inequality, we know that the gains were not shared enough. So the key political and economic question is how the resulting gains are distributed. If they go too much in favor of the owners, it might cause the catastrophic effects that we described earlier. If it is used in favor of the workers, it can improve the lives of ordinary people. Major historical economists, from Mill to Keynes, had a similar utopian point of view, arguing that the ultimate purpose of technology would be to reduce the amount of time devoted to work.
当然，分享生产力收益并不一定意味着缩短工作时间。它还可以意味着更高的工资、更强大的公共服务、工人持股，或更广泛的社会参与来分享自动化创造的财富。但如果一项技术的总体影响是加剧不平等，我们就知道这些收益没有得到充分共享。因此，关键的政治和经济问题在于如何分配由此产生的收益。如果收益过度向所有者倾斜，可能会导致我们前面描述的灾难性后果；如果收益被用于造福劳动者，它就能改善普通人的生活。从穆勒（Mill）到凯恩斯（Keynes），主要的经济学家们都有类似的乌托邦观点，认为技术的最终目的应该是减少投入到工作中的时间。

### Productivity Gains Have Always Been Political
### 生产力收益始终是一个政治问题

So the main point is how we are able to deal with this tension between the benefit of investors rather than workers. During the Industrial Revolution, workers did not automatically benefit from productivity gains. Factory owners became enormously wealthy while laborers endured brutal conditions and long hours. Improvements came later — through unions, political pressure, labor laws, and collective bargaining. Since the 1980s, labor movements became weaker, and the general trend has been that the impact of technology brought much more benefit to owners than to workers. Labor movements are now so weak, that we take for granted that all the benefit related to technology should go to company owners.
因此，核心问题在于我们如何处理投资者利益与劳动者利益之间的这种张力。在工业革命期间，劳动者并没有自动从生产力收益中获益。工厂主变得极其富有，而劳动者却忍受着残酷的条件和漫长的工作时间。改善是后来才出现的——通过工会、政治压力、劳动法和集体谈判。自20世纪80年代以来，劳工运动逐渐式微，总体趋势是技术带来的影响更多地惠及了所有者而非劳动者。如今，劳工运动已极其薄弱，以至于我们理所当然地认为，与技术相关的所有利益都应归于公司所有者。
