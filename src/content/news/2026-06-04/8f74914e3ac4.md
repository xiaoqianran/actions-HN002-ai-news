# Consistent Yet Wrong: Evidence Insensitivity in Spatial Vision-Language Models
# 一致却错误：空间视觉语言模型中的证据不敏感性

**Abstract:** Spatial reasoning is fundamental to robotics, autonomy, and embodied AI, yet modern vision-language models (VLMs) remain unreliable on metric distance queries. A common assumption is that consistent predictions across viewpoints reflect geometric grounding. We test this assumption and find the opposite: leading VLMs often produce view-invariant and consistent answers even when those answers are incorrect, indicating weak coupling between predictions and viewpoint-specific visual evidence.

**摘要：** 空间推理是机器人技术、自动驾驶和具身智能的基础，然而现代视觉语言模型（VLM）在度量距离查询方面仍然不可靠。一个常见的假设是，跨视点的一致性预测反映了模型具备几何基础。我们对这一假设进行了测试，结果却恰恰相反：领先的 VLM 往往会产生与视点无关且一致的答案，即使这些答案是错误的。这表明模型的预测与特定视点的视觉证据之间存在微弱的耦合。

We introduce **ViewDiag**, a controlled multi-view evaluation protocol built from Hypersim, ScanNet, and KITTI360, comprising 176 object-pair tracks across 80 scenes with 2--10 views per track. The protocol evaluates models along three axes: metric accuracy, distributional concentration, and a latent feature probe for internal collapse that distinguishes decision collapse from representation collapse. Across diverse models, we observe a consistent pattern of high prediction stability paired with substantial error, clustering in a regime characterized by strong consistency but low accuracy.

我们引入了 **ViewDiag**，这是一个基于 Hypersim、ScanNet 和 KITTI360 构建的受控多视点评估协议，包含跨越 80 个场景的 176 个对象对轨迹，每个轨迹有 2 到 10 个视点。该协议从三个维度评估模型：度量准确性、分布集中度，以及用于检测内部坍缩的潜在特征探测（区分决策坍缩与表征坍缩）。在各种模型中，我们观察到一种一致的模式：高预测稳定性伴随着显著的错误，模型聚集在一种“强一致性但低准确性”的状态中。

These results challenge the common use of cross-view consistency as a proxy for geometric understanding. Instead, we show that stable predictions may reflect prior-driven collapse rather than evidence-sensitive reasoning. ViewDiag provides a controlled benchmark and diagnostic framework for evaluating spatial VLMs beyond accuracy alone.

这些结果挑战了将跨视点一致性作为几何理解代理指标的普遍做法。相反，我们证明了稳定的预测可能反映了由先验驱动的坍缩，而非对证据敏感的推理。ViewDiag 提供了一个受控的基准和诊断框架，用于评估空间 VLM，而不仅仅是关注准确性。
