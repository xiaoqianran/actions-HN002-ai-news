# Making Brain-Computer Interfaces More Secure
# 让脑机接口更安全

**Abstract:** The development of brain-computer interfaces (BCIs) based on electroencephalograms (EEGs) has advanced significantly mainly to machine learning. Although the majority of earlier research has been on increasing classification accuracy, relatively little focus has been placed on security and robustness.

**摘要：** 基于脑电图（EEG）的脑机接口（BCI）开发主要得益于机器学习，已取得了显著进展。尽管早期的研究大多集中在提高分类准确率上，但对安全性和鲁棒性的关注相对较少。

According to recent research, EEG-based BCIs are susceptible to adversarial attacks, which can cause misdiagnosis due to minute, well-crafted disturbances. Evaluating model robustness against such perturbations is therefore critical for ensuring reliable deployment.

根据最近的研究，基于 EEG 的脑机接口容易受到对抗性攻击，这些攻击通过微小且精心设计的干扰，可能导致误诊。因此，评估模型针对此类扰动的鲁棒性对于确保其可靠部署至关重要。

In this study, we propose a lightweight custom Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) architecture to investigate adversarial robustness in EEG-based BCIs. The suggested method is assessed using two EEG datasets and contrasted with three novel CNN models tailored to EEG, namely EEGNet, DeepConvNet, and SleepEEGNet, under gradient-based adversarial attack scenarios.

在本研究中，我们提出了一种轻量级的定制卷积神经网络（CNN）架构，以研究基于 EEG 的脑机接口的对抗鲁棒性。该方法通过两个 EEG 数据集进行了评估，并在基于梯度的对抗攻击场景下，与三种专为 EEG 设计的新型 CNN 模型（即 EEGNet、DeepConvNet 和 SleepEEGNet）进行了对比。

According to experimental findings, the suggested model continuously performs better in classification under adversarial perturbations compared to baseline models, indicating improved robustness. These findings highlight the potential of lightweight architectures for enhancing the reliability of EEG-based BCI systems under adversarial conditions.

实验结果表明，与基准模型相比，该模型在对抗性扰动下的分类表现持续更优，这表明其鲁棒性得到了提升。这些发现凸显了轻量级架构在增强 EEG 脑机接口系统在对抗条件下可靠性方面的潜力。
