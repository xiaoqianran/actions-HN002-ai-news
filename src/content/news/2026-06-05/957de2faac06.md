# End-to-End Text Line Detection and Ordering
# 端到端文本行检测与排序

**Abstract:** Practical text-recognition pipelines for historical documents typically decompose layout analysis into line detection followed by a separate reading-order step, with the latter most often handled by a hand-coded geometric heuristic that struggles with marginalia, multiple columns, tables, and source-specific editorial conventions.

**摘要：** 针对历史文档的实用文本识别流程通常将版面分析分解为“行检测”和随后的“阅读顺序确定”两个独立步骤。后者往往通过人工编写的几何启发式规则来处理，但在面对页边注、多栏排版、表格以及特定来源的编辑惯例时，这些规则往往难以应对。

This article introduces Orli (Ordered Regression of Lines), an end-to-end model that casts both sub-tasks as a single image-to-sequence problem: from a page image, Orli autoregressively generates text-line baselines directly in reading order.

本文介绍了 Orli（Ordered Regression of Lines，文本行有序回归模型），这是一个端到端模型，它将上述两个子任务统一为一个“图像到序列”的问题：Orli 从页面图像出发，以自回归方式直接按阅读顺序生成文本行基线。

Baselines are represented in a chord-frame parameterization that anchors a line's position, orientation, and extent while encoding local geometry through perpendicular offsets; an iterative refinement head and a local visual refiner produce the final curve.

基线采用弦框架参数化（chord-frame parameterization）表示，该方法通过垂直偏移量编码局部几何特征，同时锚定文本行的位置、方向和范围；随后，通过迭代细化头（iterative refinement head）和局部视觉细化器（local visual refiner）生成最终曲线。

Trained on a heterogeneous corpus of 196,691 pages spanning ten writing systems, Orli marginally exceeds the previously reported state of the art for cBAD line detection without dataset-specific training, reaches near perfect coverage and ordering on multiple reading-order benchmarks zero-shot, and adapts to more specialized out-of-domain layouts with limited fine-tuning.

Orli 在包含十种书写系统、共 196,691 页的异构语料库上进行训练。在无需针对特定数据集进行训练的情况下，其 cBAD 行检测性能略微超过了此前报道的业界最佳水平；在多个阅读顺序基准测试中，它实现了零样本（zero-shot）下的近乎完美的覆盖率和排序效果，并且只需少量微调即可适应更专业的域外（out-of-domain）版面。

The method's source code and model weights are available under an open license at this https URL.

该方法的源代码和模型权重已在 [此链接](https://github.com/dhlab-epfl/orli) 以开源许可协议发布。
