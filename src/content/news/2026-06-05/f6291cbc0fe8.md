# When Seeing Is Not Believing -- A Benchmark for Search-Grounded Video Misinformation Detection
# 眼见不一定为实——基于搜索的视频虚假信息检测基准

**Abstract:** Video misinformation increasingly operates at the semantic and evidential level: authentic footage may be selectively edited, temporally reordered, spliced across sources, or augmented with AI-generated content to construct false narratives. Such evidence-dependent manipulations cannot be reliably verified from the input video alone, because the missing, reordered, replaced, or recontextualized evidence lies outside the video itself.

**摘要：** 视频虚假信息正越来越多地在语义和证据层面进行运作：真实的素材可能被选择性地剪辑、时间顺序重排、跨来源拼接，或通过人工智能生成的内容进行增强，从而构建虚假叙事。这类依赖证据的篡改行为无法仅凭输入视频本身进行可靠验证，因为缺失、重排、替换或重新语境化的证据存在于视频之外。

We introduce **EVID-Bench**, a benchmark for search-grounded video misinformation detection, where a system must search the open web for related videos and identify what information is false through cross-video comparison. EVID-Bench comprises 222 videos spanning 9 manipulation types across 3 categories: AI generation, single-source editing, and multi-source editing. All samples are verified to be undetectable by frontier models through visual inspection alone.

我们引入了 **EVID-Bench**，这是一个用于基于搜索的视频虚假信息检测的基准测试。在该基准中，系统必须在开放网络中搜索相关视频，并通过跨视频比对来识别哪些信息是虚假的。EVID-Bench 包含 222 个视频，涵盖了 3 大类共 9 种篡改类型：人工智能生成、单源编辑和多源编辑。所有样本均经核实，证明仅靠前沿模型进行视觉检查是无法检测出来的。

We evaluate nine frontier multimodal models using a retrieval-augmented verification baseline. The best system achieves only 61.43% point-level accuracy and 43.24% video-level accuracy, while AI-generated manipulations remain especially challenging. Error analysis reveals recurring challenges: models fixate on irrelevant anchors, misattribute synthetic content to editorial splicing, and terminate search prematurely before fully explaining the manipulation.

我们使用检索增强验证基准评估了九个前沿多模态模型。表现最好的系统仅达到了 61.43% 的点级准确率和 43.24% 的视频级准确率，其中人工智能生成的篡改内容仍然极具挑战性。错误分析揭示了反复出现的问题：模型往往固着于无关的锚点，将合成内容误归因于编辑拼接，并且在完全解释篡改行为之前就过早地终止了搜索。
