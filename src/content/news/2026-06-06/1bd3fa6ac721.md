# Recovering Physically Plausible Human-Object Interactions from Monocular Videos
# 从单目视频中恢复物理上合理的“人-物交互”

**Abstract:** In this paper, we propose RePHO, a method to reconstruct physically plausible human-object interactions (HOI) from monocular videos. While existing kinematic-based approaches produce visually plausible motion, they often result in physically implausible artifacts such as interpenetration and object floating.
**摘要：** 在本文中，我们提出了 RePHO，这是一种从单目视频中重建物理上合理的“人-物交互”（HOI）的方法。虽然现有的基于运动学的方法可以产生视觉上合理的动作，但它们往往会导致物理上不合理的伪影，例如物体穿模（interpenetration）和物体悬浮。

To overcome these issues, we introduce a physics-guided reconstruction framework. We begin with a kinematic estimate and then refine it by training a policy with reinforcement learning (RL). This policy is optimized to reproduce the interaction in a physics simulator.
为了克服这些问题，我们引入了一个物理引导的重建框架。我们首先进行运动学估计，然后通过强化学习（RL）训练策略来对其进行优化。该策略经过优化，旨在物理模拟器中重现交互过程。

Because kinematic estimates are typically noisy, naive RL training can fail. Therefore, we propose an adaptive sampling strategy with a dual self-updating mechanism that can identify the frames with the most informative and reliable kinematic reconstruction.
由于运动学估计通常存在噪声，简单的强化学习训练可能会失败。因此，我们提出了一种具有双重自更新机制的自适应采样策略，该策略能够识别出包含最丰富信息且最可靠的运动学重建帧。

Our process progressively improves reconstruction quality and yields physically consistent HOI sequences. We demonstrate our approach on two standard HOI benchmarks and achieve clear improvements in physical plausibility metrics over state-of-the-art methods.
我们的流程逐步提高了重建质量，并生成了物理上一致的 HOI 序列。我们在两个标准的 HOI 基准测试上验证了我们的方法，并在物理合理性指标上取得了优于现有最先进方法的显著提升。
