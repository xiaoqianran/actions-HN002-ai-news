# The Meta hack shows there’s more to AI security than Mythos
# Meta 的黑客事件表明：AI 安全不仅仅关乎 Mythos 模型

**EXECUTIVE SUMMARY** On June 5, 404 Media reported that attackers had been using Meta’s AI customer support agent to steal Instagram accounts. Their approach was simple: They asked the agent to link the accounts to email addresses that they controlled, and the agent complied. One attacker broke into the dormant Obama White House account and made pro-Iran posts; others took over accounts with valuable, single-word handles, possibly in order to sell them.

**执行摘要** 6 月 5 日，404 Media 报道称，攻击者一直在利用 Meta 的 AI 客户支持代理窃取 Instagram 账户。他们的方法很简单：要求 AI 代理将账户关联到他们控制的电子邮件地址，而 AI 代理竟然照办了。一名攻击者入侵了处于休眠状态的奥巴马白宫账户并发布了亲伊朗的帖子；其他人则接管了拥有高价值、单字 ID 的账户，可能意图将其出售。

AI cybersecurity concerns are nothing new. Since Anthropic announced in April that its Mythos model was too good at hacking to be released to the general public, commentators, researchers, and federal officials alike have fixated on the idea that superpowered AI systems could lay waste to our computer infrastructure. That’s not quite what this Instagram hack was: There, AI was the target rather than the attacker, and the method was far simpler than anything Mythos would cook up. But as companies offload more work to AI, these comparatively unsophisticated attacks could wreak their own havoc.

AI 网络安全问题并非新鲜事。自 Anthropic 在 4 月宣布其 Mythos 模型因黑客攻击能力过强而无法向公众发布以来，评论员、研究人员和联邦官员都专注于一种担忧：即超级 AI 系统可能会摧毁我们的计算机基础设施。但这次 Instagram 黑客事件并非如此：在这里，AI 是攻击目标而非攻击者，且其手段远比 Mythos 可能带来的威胁简单得多。然而，随着企业将更多工作交给 AI，这些相对简单的攻击也可能造成严重的破坏。

“As AI becomes more and more widely used—especially when AI is more and more widely used to automate our work flows, like account recovery—I think attackers are going to be more and more motivated to attack AI itself,” says Neil Gong, a professor of electrical and computer engineering at Duke University. Gong and other scholars have been issuing warnings about the security vulnerabilities of AI agents for a while. They publish papers and blog posts detailing exploits such as indirect prompt injection, which involves hijacking agents using commands hidden in websites, emails, or other seemingly anodyne data sources.

“随着 AI 变得越来越普及——尤其是当 AI 被广泛用于自动化工作流程（如账户恢复）时，我认为攻击者将更有动力去攻击 AI 本身，”杜克大学电气与计算机工程教授 Neil Gong 表示。Gong 和其他学者早已对 AI 代理的安全漏洞发出警告。他们发表论文和博客文章，详细介绍了诸如“间接提示注入”（indirect prompt injection）等攻击手段，即通过隐藏在网站、电子邮件或其他看似无害的数据源中的指令来劫持 AI 代理。

Compared with these techniques, the Meta hack was practically mindless. The only complication that hackers had to overcome was using a VPN that matched the true account owner’s location; then they directly asked the support agent to change the account’s email address, and it complied. Meta has not commented publicly on how this vulnerability slipped through the cracks. But given the simplicity of the exploit, Gong says, it should have been uncovered easily, before the agent was deployed. “It’s really surprising,” he says. “I don’t understand why they didn’t find this simple problem.”

与这些技术相比，Meta 的黑客事件简直是“无脑”操作。黑客唯一需要克服的困难是使用与真实账户所有者位置匹配的 VPN；随后，他们直接要求支持代理更改账户的电子邮件地址，而代理便照做了。Meta 尚未公开评论该漏洞是如何被遗漏的。但 Gong 表示，鉴于该漏洞的简单性，它本应在代理部署前就轻易被发现。“这真的很令人惊讶，”他说，“我不明白他们为什么没有发现这个简单的问题。”

Jessica Ji, a senior research analyst at Georgetown’s Center for Security and Emerging Technology, agrees. “It raises questions like: Were there even guardrails in place?” she says. “Did anyone think to test for this kind of scenario?” She notes that the oversight is particularly striking coming from a company like Meta, which has extensive expertise in both AI and cybersecurity. Meta did not respond to a request for comment for this article, but on Monday a Meta spokesperson said on X that the vulnerability had been resolved.

乔治城大学安全与新兴技术中心的高级研究分析师 Jessica Ji 对此表示赞同。“这引发了一些疑问：他们是否设置了防护栏？”她说，“有没有人想过测试这种场景？”她指出，这种疏忽对于像 Meta 这样在 AI 和网络安全领域都拥有丰富专业知识的公司来说，尤其令人震惊。Meta 未回应本文的置评请求，但周一 Meta 发言人在 X 上表示，该漏洞已得到解决。

As embarrassing a moment as this might be for Meta in particular, it also highlights some core vulnerabilities shared by all AI agents. Unlike traditional software, agents can respond in flexible—and unexpected—ways to new circumstances, which is why they might be able to substitute for human customer support agents. But AI agents can also be tricked in ways that humans wouldn’t be, and because they can take real-world actions, those mistakes have consequences.

尽管这对 Meta 来说是一个尴尬的时刻，但它也凸显了所有 AI 代理共有的核心漏洞。与传统软件不同，AI 代理可以以灵活且不可预测的方式应对新情况，这也是它们能够替代人类客服的原因。但 AI 代理也可能以人类不会被欺骗的方式被误导，且由于它们能够执行现实世界的操作，这些错误会带来严重的后果。

“A human would say, ‘Okay, why do you want to change the email address?’ and maybe respond with a security question,” says Somesh Jha, a professor of computer science at the University of Wisconsin–Madison. “What is going on with these agents is they’re very eager to finish the task. It’s almost like some elementary school student who just wants to please the teacher.”

“人类客服会问：‘好的，你为什么要更改电子邮件地址？’并可能通过安全问题进行核实，”威斯康星大学麦迪逊分校计算机科学教授 Somesh Jha 说。“这些代理的问题在于它们太急于完成任务了。这就像是一个只想取悦老师的小学生。”

There are ways to mitigate the risks. Companies can use traditional software to build guardrails that make sure agents follow strict rules, such as always asking for answers to security questions before sending sensitive account information to a new email address. And the experts consulted for this article all agree that agents should undergo rigorous red-teaming, a process in which developers try their best to attack a system in order to discover its vulnerabilities before it is deployed.

降低风险的方法是存在的。企业可以使用传统软件构建防护栏，确保代理遵循严格的规则，例如在将敏感账户信息发送到新电子邮件地址之前，始终要求回答安全问题。本文采访的专家一致认为，代理应进行严格的“红队测试”（red-teaming），即开发人员在系统部署前尽其所能攻击系统，以发现其漏洞。

But there are also countervailing forces. Companies want to deploy capable agents, and the more power an agent has—and the fewer guardrails it is subject to—the more work it can potentially take on. “Security and utility always have a trade-off,” says Bo Li, a professor of computer science at the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign. And adequate red-teaming can be expensive. Defenders have to expend more resources than attackers do, because attackers only need to discover a single exploit, while defenders try to discover and patch as many as they can. When attackers are working toward something as valuable as a single-word Instagram handle, they’ll pour resources into finding exploits, so defenders have to spend even more money to protect that prize.

但同时也存在制衡力量。企业希望部署能力强大的代理，而代理拥有的权限越大、受到的限制越少，它能承担的工作就越多。“安全性和实用性之间总是存在权衡，”伊利诺伊大学厄巴纳-香槟分校计算机科学教授 Bo Li 表示。此外，充分的红队测试成本高昂。防御者必须比攻击者投入更多资源，因为攻击者只需要发现一个漏洞，而防御者则必须尽可能多地发现并修补漏洞。当攻击者的目标是像单字 Instagram ID 这样有价值的东西时，他们会投入大量资源寻找漏洞，因此防御者必须花费更多的资金来保护这些资产。

As AI models continue to improve, hardening their defenses might actually get easier. Though the probabilistic nature of large language models means that LLM agents will always be vulnerable to some forms of attack, a more sophisticated model might have identified an attempt to change the email associated with the Obama White House account as suspicious. And AI systems can be used for agent red-teaming, much as participants in Anthropic’s Project Glasswing use Mythos to identify vulnerabilities in their software.

随着 AI 模型的不断改进，加强防御实际上可能会变得更容易。尽管大语言模型的概率特性意味着 LLM 代理始终容易受到某些形式的攻击，但更先进的模型或许能识别出更改奥巴马白宫账户关联邮箱的企图是可疑的。此外，AI 系统本身也可用于代理的红队测试，就像 Anthropic “Glasswing 项目”的参与者使用 Mythos 来识别其软件漏洞一样。

Still, experts expect that the problem of securing AI agents will only become more pressing in the future. As agents grow more capable, companies that adopt them may want to give them more power, both to provide more services with fewer humans and to avoid being left behind by their competitors. In the fast-moving world of AI, the time needed to carefully secure risky agentic systems might seem like an unconscionable delay. “Everybody wants to be the first to do something and just push things out without careful scrutiny and red-teaming,” Jha says. “I think it’s a very dangerous trend.”

尽管如此，专家们预计，AI 代理的安全问题在未来只会变得更加紧迫。随着代理能力增强，采用它们的企业可能会赋予其更多权限，既是为了用更少的人力提供更多服务，也是为了避免被竞争对手甩在后面。在瞬息万变的 AI 世界中，仔细保护高风险代理系统所需的时间似乎被视为一种无法接受的拖延。“每个人都想成为第一个吃螃蟹的人，在没有经过仔细审查和红队测试的情况下就匆忙发布产品，”Jha 说，“我认为这是一个非常危险的趋势。”
