### Title: Temporal Preference Concepts and their Functions in a Large Language Model
### 标题：大型语言模型中的时间偏好概念及其功能

**Abstract:** Large Language Models (LLMs) are increasingly being deployed to make decisions that require trading off near-term gains against long-term consequences, yet little is known about how they internally represent or resolve these tradeoffs. 
**摘要：** 大型语言模型（LLMs）正越来越多地被部署用于做出需要权衡短期收益与长期后果的决策，然而人们对于它们如何在内部表示或解决这些权衡知之甚少。

In this work, we causally localize an underlying subgraph for temporal preference in a distilled LLM (Qwen3-4B-Instruct-2507), identifying mid-to-upper-layer nodes through converging evidence from gradient-based attribution and activation patching. 
在这项工作中，我们在一个蒸馏后的 LLM（Qwen3-4B-Instruct-2507）中因果定位了一个用于时间偏好的底层子图，并通过基于梯度的归因和激活修补（activation patching）的汇聚证据，识别出了中上层的节点。

We find that the geometry of time horizon is encoded in the residual stream at the expected localized layers. 
我们发现，时间跨度的几何结构被编码在预期定位层的残差流（residual stream）中。

A behavioral analysis reveals that unintervened LLMs discount the future several times less steeply than humans, yet this preference is unstable across contexts, motivating explicit control rather than implicit reliance on training. 
行为分析显示，未经干预的 LLM 对未来的折现率比人类低数倍，但这种偏好在不同语境下是不稳定的，这促使我们需要进行显式控制，而不是隐式地依赖训练。

Finally, we find suggestive evidence that steering vectors can shift temporal preference. Our work demonstrates how mechanistic interpretability can bring us closer to reliable control over how LLMs plan and reason.
最后，我们发现了暗示性证据，表明引导向量（steering vectors）可以改变时间偏好。我们的工作展示了机械可解释性如何使我们更接近于可靠地控制 LLM 的规划和推理方式。
