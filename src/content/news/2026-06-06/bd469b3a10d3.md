# Do Models Share Safety Representations? Cross-Model Steering for Safe Visual Generation
# 模型是否共享安全表征？用于安全视觉生成的跨模型引导技术

**Abstract:** Recent progress in generative modeling has made safety control a central challenge, yet existing approaches remain largely model-specific, requiring retraining or tailored interventions for each new architecture. In this work, we ask whether safety can be represented as a portable latent direction, learned once and reused across heterogeneous generators.

**摘要：** 生成式建模的最新进展使得安全控制成为一个核心挑战，然而现有的方法大多仍局限于特定模型，每当出现新架构时，都需要重新训练或进行定制化的干预。在这项工作中，我们探讨了安全性能否被表征为一种可移植的潜在方向，即通过一次学习即可在不同的生成器之间重复使用。

We introduce the first framework for cross-model safety steering, in which a safety direction is estimated in a source LLM from paired safe-unsafe prompts, transported to a target generator through a lightweight alignment fitted on benign data alone, and applied at inference time. Crucially, our pipeline never accesses unsafe data on the target side, isolating whether safety can be transferred through shared representation geometry.

我们引入了首个跨模型安全引导框架。在该框架中，我们通过成对的安全与不安全提示词在源大语言模型（LLM）中估算出一个安全方向，通过仅在良性数据上拟合的轻量级对齐方式将其迁移到目标生成器，并在推理阶段应用。至关重要的是，我们的流程在目标端从不接触任何不安全数据，从而验证了安全性能否通过共享的表征几何结构进行迁移。

Beyond a single global direction, we also identify a multi-vector extension that captures category-specific safety behaviors, enabling more selective control. We evaluate our approach in text-to-image and text-to-video generation across diverse source-target model pairs.

除了单一的全局方向外，我们还确定了一种多向量扩展方法，能够捕捉特定类别的安全行为，从而实现更具选择性的控制。我们在多种源-目标模型对的文生图和文生视频任务中评估了我们的方法。

Across models, transferred safety directions achieve ASR reduction and CLIP-Score/FID trade-offs comparable to directions learned natively on the target model using unsafe data, while requiring no target-side unsafe data. This indicates that safety improvements do not come at the expense of generation quality.

在不同模型间的测试表明，迁移后的安全方向在降低攻击成功率（ASR）以及 CLIP-Score/FID 权衡方面，达到了与在目标模型上使用不安全数据原生学习的效果相当的水平，且无需目标端的不安全数据。这表明安全性的提升并不会以牺牲生成质量为代价。

Our results point to a modular view of safety: safety-relevant behavior is not purely model-local, but can be controlled through latent directions that persist across models. This suggests a new path toward lightweight, reusable safety mechanisms that do not require target-side unsafe data.

我们的研究结果指向了一种模块化的安全视角：与安全相关的行为并非完全局限于特定模型，而是可以通过在不同模型间通用的潜在方向进行控制。这为开发无需目标端不安全数据的轻量级、可复用安全机制开辟了新路径。
