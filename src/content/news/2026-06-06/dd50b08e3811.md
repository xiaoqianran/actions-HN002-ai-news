# PyCC.id: A package for hypothesis-driven equation discovery with structural identifiability

**PyCC.id：一个用于具有结构可辨识性的假设驱动方程发现的软件包**

Data-driven equation discovery is fundamentally an inverse problem that seeks to infer the governing differential equations of a system directly from time-series measurements. A known issue is the ill-conditioned nature of the inverse problem, which frequently produces multiple mathematical models that fit the data similarly well.

数据驱动的方程发现本质上是一个反问题，旨在直接从时间序列测量数据中推断系统的控制微分方程。该反问题的一个已知问题是其病态性质，这经常导致产生多个对数据拟合效果相似的数学模型。

One path to address this issue is by incorporating known hypotheses and constraints into the training phase beforehand. While this approach effectively reduces the search space, it still results in multiple candidate models, forcing practitioners to rely on post-hoc manual filtering based on their own domain expertise.

解决此问题的一种途径是在训练阶段预先纳入已知的假设和约束。虽然这种方法有效地缩小了搜索空间，但它仍然会产生多个候选模型，迫使从业者依赖于基于自身领域知识的事后人工筛选。

A recent approach incorporates structural `skeletons' inspired by characteristic curves (CCs), defining a hypothesis-driven methodology. In this methodology, practitioners define a skeleton, which is associated with a family of ordinary differential equations (ODEs), and then add their hypotheses and priors based on their domain knowledge to refine the obtained model iteratively.

最近的一种方法引入了受特征曲线（CCs）启发的结构“骨架”，从而定义了一种假设驱动的方法论。在这种方法中，从业者定义一个与常微分方程（ODE）族相关联的骨架，然后根据其领域知识添加假设和先验，以迭代地优化所获得的模型。

An important advantage of this approach is that some skeletons have demonstrable structural identifiability properties, which are useful for checking whether the skeleton is correct or should be discarded. Furthermore, this formalism enables the use of multiple equation discovery paradigms due to its modularity (such as neural networks, symbolic regression, and sparse regression).

这种方法的一个重要优势是，某些骨架具有可证明的结构可辨识性属性，这对于检查骨架是否正确或是否应被舍弃非常有用。此外，由于其模块化特性，这种形式体系支持使用多种方程发现范式（如神经网络、符号回归和稀疏回归）。

In this work, we present the Python library PyCC, which condenses these efforts into a flexible tool that allows researchers and engineers to seamlessly define their skeletons and hypotheses to discover ODEs from time-dependent data.

在这项工作中，我们介绍了 Python 库 PyCC，它将这些研究成果浓缩为一个灵活的工具，使研究人员和工程师能够无缝地定义其骨架和假设，从而从随时间变化的数据中发现常微分方程。
