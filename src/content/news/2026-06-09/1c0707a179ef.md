# How to Keep Quantum Information Alive for Machine Learning
# 如何让量子信息在机器学习中保持“存活”

Exploring one of the biggest obstacles in scalable quantum machine learning: protecting fragile quantum information from noise and decoherence.
探索可扩展量子机器学习中最大的障碍之一：如何保护脆弱的量子信息免受噪声和退相干的影响。

Modern machine learning systems perform an extraordinary number of operations every second. Training large neural networks involves massive matrix multiplications, memory transfers, and continuous information flow across hardware. Despite this scale, classical computers remain remarkably reliable because modern computational systems are built on layers of error correction and fault tolerance.
现代机器学习系统每秒执行的操作数量惊人。训练大型神经网络涉及海量的矩阵乘法、内存传输以及硬件间持续的信息流。尽管规模如此庞大，经典计算机依然保持着极高的可靠性，这是因为现代计算系统建立在多层纠错和容错机制之上。

Errors still occur in classical hardware. Electrical noise, thermal fluctuations, and even cosmic rays can occasionally corrupt information. Yet classical information is surprisingly robust. The reason is remarkably simple: Bits can be copied and checked without changing their state. This seemingly ordinary property forms the foundation of classical error correction. By introducing redundancy and performing consistency checks, classical systems can detect and correct errors before they propagate through a computation.
经典硬件中依然会发生错误。电噪声、热波动，甚至宇宙射线偶尔都会破坏信息。然而，经典信息却出奇地稳健。原因非常简单：比特可以在不改变其状态的情况下被复制和检查。这一看似普通的特性构成了经典纠错的基础。通过引入冗余并执行一致性检查，经典系统能够在错误扩散到整个计算过程之前检测并纠正它们。

Quantum systems operate very differently. In Quantum Machine Learning (QML), information is encoded into fragile quantum states that evolve through superposition and entanglement. Unlike classical bits, quantum states cannot simply be copied for backup. Worse still, directly inspecting a quantum state can disturb the very information we are trying to protect. This creates one of the biggest challenges in quantum computing: How do we keep quantum information alive long enough to perform meaningful computation? The answer lies in Quantum Error Correction (QEC), a collection of techniques designed to protect quantum information from the noisy and imperfect world around it.
量子系统的运作方式则大不相同。在量子机器学习（QML）中，信息被编码在脆弱的量子态中，通过叠加和纠缠进行演化。与经典比特不同，量子态无法简单地复制以进行备份。更糟糕的是，直接检查量子态可能会干扰我们试图保护的信息。这构成了量子计算中最大的挑战之一：我们如何让量子信息保持足够长的“存活”时间以执行有意义的计算？答案在于量子纠错（QEC），这是一系列旨在保护量子信息免受周围嘈杂且不完美环境影响的技术。

### How errors arise in classical and quantum systems
### 错误在经典系统和量子系统中是如何产生的

No physical system is perfect. Whether we are transmitting information across the internet, storing data in memory, or training a machine learning model on specialised hardware, information is constantly exposed to disturbances from the surrounding environment. In classical systems, these disturbances can originate from many sources. Electrical noise can alter voltages in a circuit, thermal fluctuations can affect electronic components, and even high-energy cosmic rays occasionally strike memory cells, causing bits to flip unexpectedly. Such events are rare, but given the billions of operations performed every second in modern computers, they cannot be ignored.
没有任何物理系统是完美的。无论我们是在互联网上传输信息、在内存中存储数据，还是在专用硬件上训练机器学习模型，信息始终暴露在周围环境的干扰之下。在经典系统中，这些干扰可能源于多种因素。电噪声会改变电路中的电压，热波动会影响电子元件，甚至高能宇宙射线偶尔也会撞击存储单元，导致比特发生意外翻转。此类事件虽然罕见，但考虑到现代计算机每秒执行数十亿次操作，它们是不容忽视的。

Fortunately, classical information is relatively resilient. Since bits can be copied and checked without changing their value, redundancy can be introduced to detect and correct errors before they propagate through a computation. Quantum systems face a similar problem but under far more restrictive rules and their sensitivity to the noise is extremely high. A quantum computer is never completely isolated from its surroundings. Interactions with the environment, imperfections in quantum gates, noise in control electronics, and inaccuracies during state preparation can all disturb the quantum state being processed.
幸运的是，经典信息具有相对的韧性。由于比特可以在不改变其值的情况下被复制和检查，因此可以引入冗余来在错误扩散到计算过程之前进行检测和纠正。量子系统面临着类似的问题，但其规则更为严格，且对噪声的敏感度极高。量子计算机永远无法完全与周围环境隔绝。与环境的相互作用、量子门的缺陷、控制电子设备中的噪声以及状态制备过程中的不准确性，都会干扰正在处理的量子态。

Unlike classical systems, however, even a small disturbance can significantly alter a quantum computation. Furthermore, quantum information is far more complex as it is not limited to the binary states 0 and 1. A qubit may exist in a superposition of both states simultaneously, making the effects of noise far more subtle than a simple bit flip. As a result, understanding how errors arise in quantum systems requires a different framework, one that accounts for both the probabilistic nature of quantum mechanics and the unavoidable interaction between a quantum system and its environment.
然而，与经典系统不同的是，即使是微小的干扰也会显著改变量子计算的结果。此外，量子信息要复杂得多，因为它不仅限于二进制的 0 和 1 状态。量子比特可以同时处于两种状态的叠加态，这使得噪声的影响比简单的比特翻转要微妙得多。因此，理解量子系统中的错误是如何产生的，需要一个不同的框架，该框架既要考虑量子力学的概率特性，也要考虑量子系统与其环境之间不可避免的相互作用。

### Why quantum information is fundamentally fragile
### 为什么量子信息本质上是脆弱的

As we have already established classical information is stored in bits that exist in one of two states: 0 or 1. A bit may occasionally become corrupted, but its state can be copied, inspected and verified without fundamentally changing the information being stored. Quantum information behaves very differently. A qubit can exist in a superposition of states, allowing it to represent both 0 and 1 simultaneously. This property lies at the heart of quantum computing’s potential advantage, but it also makes quantum information exceptionally delicate.
正如我们已经确定的，经典信息存储在比特中，这些比特存在于 0 或 1 两种状态之一。比特偶尔会损坏，但其状态可以在不从根本上改变所存储信息的情况下被复制、检查和验证。量子信息的表现则大不相同。量子比特可以存在于状态的叠加中，使其能够同时表示 0 和 1。这一特性是量子计算潜在优势的核心，但也使量子信息变得异常脆弱。

Even a small interaction with the surrounding environment can disturb a quantum state. This process is known as decoherence and remains one of the greatest obstacles to building large-scale quantum computers. The challenge becomes even more severe because quantum information cannot be treated like classical information. Suppose a classical memory bit is suspected to be corrupted. We can simply create multiple copies, compare them and identify inconsistencies and fix the issue. A quantum state does not permit such a strategy. The No-Cloning Theorem states that an arbitrary unknown quantum state cannot be perfectly copied.
即使是与周围环境的微小相互作用也会干扰量子态。这一过程被称为“退相干”，它仍然是构建大规模量子计算机的最大障碍之一。这一挑战之所以更加严峻，是因为量子信息不能像经典信息那样处理。假设一个经典内存比特被怀疑损坏了，我们可以简单地创建多个副本，比较它们，识别不一致之处并修复问题。而量子态不允许这种策略。不可克隆定理（No-Cloning Theorem）指出，任意未知的量子态无法被完美复制。

At the same time, directly measuring a quantum state is not a harmless operation. Measurement collapses the state and can destroy the very superposition we are trying to preserve. This places quantum information in a peculiar position. It is highly sensitive to errors, yet the two most natural strategies for detecting those errors—copying the information and directly inspecting it—are fundamentally prohibited by quantum mechanics.
