# WorldBench: A Challenging and Visually Diverse Multimodal Reasoning Benchmark
# WorldBench：一个具有挑战性且视觉多样化的多模态推理基准

**Abstract:** In real-world applications, models are expected to perform reliably across diverse settings. Yet, many existing multimodal benchmarks expand task types without capturing the visual diversity needed to handle open-ended visual inputs. 

**摘要：** 在现实世界的应用中，模型需要在各种环境下可靠地运行。然而，许多现有的多模态基准测试在扩展任务类型时，未能捕捉到处理开放式视觉输入所需的视觉多样性。

We present WorldBench, a challenging and visually diverse reasoning benchmark to evaluate Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs). We build a taxonomy of thousands of visual concepts across multiple domains (e.g., living things). Guided by this taxonomy, we curate a broad collection of images from search engines and existing datasets to comprehensively represent the visual world. 

我们提出了 WorldBench，这是一个旨在评估多模态大语言模型（MLLMs）的、具有挑战性且视觉多样化的推理基准。我们构建了一个涵盖多个领域（如生物）的数千个视觉概念的分类体系。在该体系的指导下，我们从搜索引擎和现有数据集中精心挑选了大量图像，以全面呈现视觉世界。

Through structured trial-and-error, we manually design challenging questions that frontier MLLMs fail to answer. On quantitative and human evaluations, WorldBench achieves higher visual diversity than any existing diverse benchmark. 

通过结构化的反复试验，我们手动设计了前沿 MLLMs 无法回答的挑战性问题。在定量评估和人工评估中，WorldBench 的视觉多样性均高于任何现有的多样化基准测试。

Evaluating 15 MLLMs on WorldBench reveals weaknesses in visual understanding: even the strongest model reaches only 64.0% accuracy, while some models perform marginally above chance-level. We hope our work highlights the importance of visual diversity in building multimodal benchmarks.

对 15 个 MLLMs 在 WorldBench 上的评估揭示了它们在视觉理解方面的弱点：即使是最强的模型，准确率也仅达到 64.0%，而一些模型的表现仅略高于随机水平。我们希望这项工作能凸显视觉多样性在构建多模态基准测试中的重要性。
