# Offline Reinforcement Learning for Plasma Control in Nuclear Fusion: Codebase and Benchmark
# 核聚变等离子体控制的离线强化学习：代码库与基准测试

**Abstract:** Offline reinforcement learning (RL) offers a promising route for developing plasma controllers from historical tokamak data, since online trial-and-error on real devices is costly and risky. However, progress in this direction remains difficult to measure due to the lack of a standardized offline RL benchmark for realistic multi-actuator, long-horizon plasma control problems in nuclear fusion.

**摘要：** 离线强化学习（RL）为利用历史托卡马克数据开发等离子体控制器提供了一条有前景的途径，因为在真实设备上进行在线试错既昂贵又具有风险。然而，由于缺乏针对核聚变中现实的多执行器、长时程等离子体控制问题的标准化离线强化学习基准，该方向的进展仍然难以衡量。

We introduce RL4F, an Offline Reinforcement Learning Benchmark for Plasma Control in Nuclear Fusion, providing closed-loop evaluation environments and baseline comparisons across four full-profile tracking tasks: rotation, density, temperature, and pressure. The dynamics function underlying the evaluation environment is built from historical discharge data from DIII-D, a real-world Tokamak.

我们推出了 RL4F，这是一个用于核聚变等离子体控制的离线强化学习基准，它提供了闭环评估环境，并针对四项全剖面跟踪任务（旋转、密度、温度和压力）进行了基准比较。评估环境底层的动力学函数是基于现实世界托卡马克 DIII-D 的历史放电数据构建的。

We evaluate a broad set of imitation learning and offline RL baselines under a unified protocol. We find that offline model-based RL methods obtain the best average performance on most objectives, although no single method dominates all tasks, highlighting the importance of dynamics modeling in complex, long-horizon plasma control tasks.

我们在统一的协议下评估了一系列广泛的模仿学习和离线强化学习基准方法。我们发现，基于模型的离线强化学习方法在大多数目标上获得了最佳的平均性能，尽管没有单一方法能在所有任务中占据主导地位，这凸显了动力学建模在复杂、长时程等离子体控制任务中的重要性。

To foster further research, we open-source the codebase, datasets, and evaluation framework, providing a benchmark not only for the fusion community but also for algorithm development in offline RL.

为了促进进一步研究，我们开源了代码库、数据集和评估框架，不仅为聚变社区，也为离线强化学习领域的算法开发提供了一个基准。
