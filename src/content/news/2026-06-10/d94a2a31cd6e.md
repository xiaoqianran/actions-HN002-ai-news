# 我们在生产环境中不断发现的 10 个常见 RAG 错误

大型语言模型：我们在生产环境中不断发现的 10 个常见 RAG 错误。企业文档智能 [第 1 卷 #4bis] —— 这是一篇关于“砖块式”陷阱的合著者笔记，解释了为何我们将架构拆分为四个模块，随后第二部分将介绍具体的修复方案。

I am the coauthor of this series with Angela Shi. This pitfalls article lists the failure modes we both kept seeing on production RAG systems, and that pushed us toward the four-brick contract in the first place.
我是本系列文章的合著者，另一位是 Angela Shi。这篇关于陷阱的文章列出了我们在生产级 RAG 系统中不断遇到的故障模式，正是这些问题促使我们最终采用了“四模块”架构契约。

I’ll admit something. Even when we work on this series, we dump big documents into ChatGPT. One PDF, one question, send, read the answer. The model is good, the vendor pays the token bill, and for a one-off that is the right path. The series exists for the other case.
我得承认一件事。即使在撰写本系列文章时，我们有时也会直接把大文档扔进 ChatGPT。一个 PDF，一个问题，发送，阅读答案。模型表现很好，供应商承担了 Token 费用，对于一次性任务来说，这是正确的路径。但本系列文章的存在是为了解决另一种情况。

In enterprise work the question is almost never about one document. A claims handler runs the same question across a broker’s full back-catalogue. A compliance team scans every contract in a portfolio. At that scale, dump-it-in-ChatGPT stops working, and it gets expensive fast. What follows is the recap of the mistakes we keep seeing, one brick at a time. The fixes live in Part II.
在企业工作中，问题几乎从不局限于单个文档。理赔员需要针对经纪人的全部历史记录运行同一个问题；合规团队需要扫描投资组合中的每一份合同。在那种规模下，“直接扔进 ChatGPT”的方法就行不通了，而且成本会迅速飙升。接下来我们将逐一回顾我们不断发现的错误。修复方案将在第二部分中给出。

### 1. Parsing: how the document loses its shape
### 1. 解析：文档是如何失去其结构的

Parsing fails when the team treats the document as text rather than as a structured object. Three patterns keep showing up: discarding tables and layout (Pitfall 1), dumping the entire document into the prompt (Pitfall 2), and chunking the document into fixed-size windows that ignore its structure (Pitfall 3).
当团队将文档视为纯文本而非结构化对象时，解析就会失败。三种模式不断出现：丢弃表格和布局（陷阱 1）、将整个文档塞入提示词（陷阱 2），以及将文档切分成忽略其结构的固定大小窗口（陷阱 3）。

The fix is a structural parser that produces typed tables instead of strings or arbitrary windows. A meta-version of these three runs through the rest of the article too. Teams skip parsing correctness and spend weeks tuning chunk size, reranker thresholds, top-K, and embedding choices, and never reach the precision they expected.
解决方案是使用结构化解析器，生成类型化的表格，而不是字符串或任意窗口。这三种模式的元版本也贯穿了本文的其余部分。团队往往跳过解析的正确性，花费数周时间调整分块大小、重排序阈值、Top-K 和嵌入选择，却始终无法达到预期的精度。

Every lever they measure sits on top of the parser’s output: a parser that flattened the table on page 47 produces noise no chunker can recover, a parser that lost the column headers produces ambiguity no reranker can rank past. The literature does not help. The most-cited vendor writeup on RAG techniques runs 194 pages on chunking and zero on parsing. Fix parsing first. Retrieval tuning is for pipelines whose parser already preserves structure.
他们测量的每一个杠杆都建立在解析器的输出之上：一个将第 47 页表格扁平化的解析器会产生任何分块器都无法恢复的噪声；一个丢失了列标题的解析器会产生任何重排序器都无法解决的歧义。现有的文献对此毫无帮助。关于 RAG 技术引用率最高的供应商文档中，有 194 页在讲分块，而关于解析的内容却为零。请先修复解析。检索调优只适用于那些解析器已经保留了结构的流水线。

#### 1.1 Pitfall 1: The PDF had a table. The parser returned a string.
#### 1.1 陷阱 1：PDF 里有表格，解析器却返回了字符串。

The default reflex is to extract the PDF as a single blob of text and let the LLM sort it out. Modern parsers make this easy: one function call, one string back, done. The cost shows up the first time a table arrives with grouped row labels.
默认的反应是将 PDF 提取为一大块文本，然后让 LLM 去整理。现代解析器让这一切变得简单：一个函数调用，返回一个字符串，搞定。但当遇到带有分组行标签的表格时，代价就显现出来了。

A claims contract has a benefit table where the same row name (Premium, Deductible) appears under two categories (Health, Dental). Flattened to text, the categories disappear into the token stream and the LLM sees Plan A Plan B Plan C Health Premium 100 200 300 Deductible 5 10 15 Dental Premium 50 80 120 Deductible 2 4 6.
一份理赔合同中有一个福利表，其中相同的行名（保费、免赔额）出现在两个类别（健康、牙科）下。扁平化为文本后，类别消失在 Token 流中，LLM 看到的是：计划 A、计划 B、计划 C、健康、保费 100 200 300、免赔额 5 10 15、牙科、保费 50 80 120、免赔额 2 4 6。

Ask “what is the Premium for Plan B?” and there are two valid answers: 200 (Health) or 80 (Dental). The flat string carries no grouping marker. The model picks one. It picks wrong some of the time, and there is no signal that says it picked wrong.
问“计划 B 的保费是多少？”时，有两个有效答案：200（健康）或 80（牙科）。扁平的字符串没有任何分组标记。模型会选一个。它有时会选错，而且没有任何信号表明它选错了。

The same problem hits multi-column layouts (a contract page with a footnote sidebar), headers and footers (page number polluting every retrieval), and reading order on scanned PDFs. Each one is a different failure mode, but they share the same root: the parser threw away the structure the document carried.
同样的问题也出现在多栏布局（带有脚注侧边栏的合同页面）、页眉和页脚（页码污染了每一次检索）以及扫描版 PDF 的阅读顺序上。每一个都是不同的故障模式，但它们有着共同的根源：解析器丢弃了文档本身携带的结构。

The fix is a relational parser that produces typed tables (line_df, page_df, toc_df, …) instead of a flat string. Each line carries its bounding box, its page, its font, its section. Tables get their own grain. Downstream bricks read structure, not blobs.
解决方案是使用关系型解析器，生成类型化的表格（line_df, page_df, toc_df 等），而不是扁平的字符串。每一行都带有其边界框、页码、字体和章节信息。表格拥有自己的粒度。下游模块读取的是结构，而不是数据块。

#### 1.2 Pitfall 2: Pay for 1200 pages on every question
#### 1.2 陷阱 2：每个问题都要为 1200 页文档付费

A second mistake on the parsing side is one Angela and I do ourselves on small projects: skip parsing entirely and stuff the whole PDF into the chat. It is fast to write, it works for one document, and the vendor pays the token bill on the free tier.
解析方面的第二个错误是 Angela 和我在小型项目中也会犯的：完全跳过解析，把整个 PDF 塞进聊天窗口。这种方法写起来很快，对单个文档有效，而且在免费层级下供应商承担了 Token 费用。

On a real corpus, the same reflex becomes expensive in three steps. First, the PDF is no longer 12 pages, it is 1200. Second, the question is no longer one, it is 200 per day. Third, the team adds five more documents to the chat to “give the model more context”, and the per-question token count grows linearly. The bill goes from cents to thousands per month, and the answers get worse because the model has more haystack and the same needle.
在真实的语料库中，同样的反应会分三步变得昂贵。首先，PDF 不再是 12 页，而是 1200 页。其次，问题不再是一个，而是每天 200 个。第三，团队为了“给模型更多上下文”而在聊天中添加了更多文档，每个问题的 Token 数量呈线性增长。账单从每月几美分变成几千美元，而且答案质量反而下降了，因为模型面对的是更大的干草堆，却要找同一根针。

The fix is to match the technique to the document and the question: when the answer fits on three pages, send three pages, not 1200. The same principle here: parsing once, retrieval scoped, generation on the smallest context that holds the answer.
解决方案是将技术与文档和问题相匹配：当答案只需三页就能涵盖时，就发送三页，而不是 1200 页。原则是一样的：一次解析，范围检索，在包含答案的最小上下文中进行生成。
