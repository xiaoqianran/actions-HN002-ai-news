# Is Language Visual? An Experiment with Chinese Characters
# 语言是视觉的吗？一项关于汉字的实验

Last year, a post went widely discussed on Douban — a Chinese social platform — about a broken printer. The owner remarked that when the printer ran low on ink, every character came out with only its top half printed. And yet, the text was completely readable. Look at these three versions of 人工智能 (“artificial intelligence”):
去年，中国社交平台豆瓣上的一篇帖子引发了广泛讨论，内容是关于一台坏掉的打印机。机主提到，当打印机墨水不足时，打印出来的每个字都只有上半部分。然而，这些文字依然完全可读。看看下面这三个版本的“人工智能”：

You can read all three instantly: The full character, 80% retained, 50% retained. That’s not a trick — that’s probably something fundamentally rooted in the Chinese system. One clarification: the 80% and 50% refer to the proportion of the image itself retained, not individual characters. Noticing that each character occupies a different number of pixels in the image, we simply cut the image horizontally at a fixed height. This made me thinking: is language — at least Chinese — fundamentally visual? I spent a few days turning this over in my brain, and finally decided to find out with the way I know how: train some language models and see what really happens.
你可以瞬间读懂这三个版本：完整字符、保留80%和保留50%。这并非戏法，而是可能根植于汉字系统本质的东西。需要说明的是：80%和50%指的是图像本身保留的比例，而非单个字符。注意到每个字符在图像中占据的像素数量不同，我们只是在固定的高度水平切割了图像。这让我思考：语言——至少是中文——本质上是视觉的吗？我花了几天的功夫琢磨这件事，最终决定用我擅长的方式找出答案：训练一些语言模型，看看究竟会发生什么。

### The Experiment: Pixels In, Tokens Out
### 实验：像素输入，Token输出

Every language model has to deal with tokenization first. The basic idea is: computers don’t understand text, so we assign each word or character an ID, that is, a number. For example, the character 你 becomes 100, 好 becomes 3, etc. From there, the LLM learns everything from scratch. In this sense, when you reduce characters such as 山 (mountain) and 水 (water) to simple integers, you throw away their shapes. And Chinese characters have beautiful shapes — stroke configurations, radical components, spatial layouts that carry real information.
每个语言模型首先都要处理分词（Tokenization）。基本思路是：计算机不理解文本，所以我们为每个词或字分配一个ID，也就是一个数字。例如，“你”变成100，“好”变成3，以此类推。在此基础上，大语言模型从零开始学习一切。从这个意义上说，当你把“山”和“水”这样的汉字简化为简单的整数时，你就丢弃了它们的形状。而汉字拥有优美的形状——笔画结构、偏旁部首、空间布局，这些都承载着真实的信息。

Another example: 打 (hit), 拍 (pat), and 拉 (pull) all share the radical 扌 (hand). You reduce them to IDs 423, 1089, and 2341, and that relationship is gone. So instead of token IDs, I rendered each character as a grayscale image and fed it to a language model. The model’s job was to predict the next character.
再举个例子：“打”、“拍”和“拉”都共享“扌”（提手旁）。如果你把它们简化为ID 423、1089和2341，这种关联就消失了。因此，我没有使用Token ID，而是将每个汉字渲染为灰度图像，并将其输入到语言模型中。模型的任务是预测下一个字符。

### You Don’t Need Great Eyesight
### 你不需要极好的视力

If you’ve ever taken off your glasses to read, you know that blurry text is still readable. The same principle happens here. Take a look at these 8×8 pixel versions of 人工智能 (hold your screen at arm’s length):
如果你曾经摘下眼镜阅读，就会知道模糊的文字依然可读。同样的原理也适用于这里。看看这些8×8像素版本的“人工智能”（请将屏幕保持在手臂距离观看）：

Each character is 64 pixels. And the model, trained on inputs at this resolution, performs just as well as one trained on 80×80 images. Indeed, we tested image resolutions from 4×4 all the way to 80×80, and found that: Going from 8×8 to 80×80 — 100 times more pixels — buys essentially nothing. The cropping results are even more striking and exciting. With 50% of each character removed, accuracy drops by less than 2%. The model doesn’t need the whole clear picture. It turns out that it needs just enough structure to know which radical family a character belongs to.
每个字符仅由64个像素组成。而在这个分辨率下训练的模型，其表现与在80×80图像上训练的模型一样好。事实上，我们测试了从4×4到80×80的各种图像分辨率，发现：从8×8增加到80×80——像素增加了100倍——本质上没有任何提升。裁剪实验的结果甚至更加惊人且令人兴奋。在删除了每个字符50%的部分后，准确率下降不到2%。模型并不需要完整的清晰图像。事实证明，它只需要足够的结构信息来识别字符所属的偏旁部首家族即可。

### The Hot-Start Effect
### “热启动”效应

So, is the visual model better than the text-based one? Not in the end. Both converge to essentially identical final accuracy. But the journey looks very different, especially the beginning. After seeing only 0.4% of training steps, the visual model is already twice as accurate as the text-based baseline.
那么，视觉模型比基于文本的模型更好吗？最终结果并非如此。两者最终收敛到的准确率基本相同。但过程看起来大不相同，尤其是在初期。在仅经过0.4%的训练步数后，视觉模型的准确率就已经达到了基于文本基准模型的两倍。

This is what we call the hot-start effect. The visual model arrives at training already knowing something useful: that 打, 拍, and 拉 look similar, and probably behave similarly. The text-based model starts with random embeddings and has to figure this out from scratch. If you look at the embedding space at initialization — before any training — you can see this directly: You can see that characters sharing the same radical cluster together at the very early training stage. Cosine similarity for radical-sharing pairs: ~0.27 for visual embeddings, ~0.002 for random token embeddings.
这就是我们所说的“热启动”效应。视觉模型在训练开始时就已经掌握了一些有用的信息：它知道“打”、“拍”和“拉”看起来很像，并且行为可能相似。而基于文本的模型从随机嵌入（Embedding）开始，必须从零开始摸索。如果你观察初始化时的嵌入空间——在任何训练之前——你可以直接看到这一点：你可以看到共享相同偏旁的字符在训练初期就聚在一起。共享偏旁字符对的余弦相似度：视觉嵌入约为0.27，而随机Token嵌入仅为0.002。

### Why the Race Ends in a Tie
### 为什么比赛以平局告终

Here’s the key thing: the visual prior encodes visual similarity, but not linguistic co-occurrence. However, next-character prediction ultimately depends on the latter. Yes, 打, 拍, and 拉 all share 扌 and look similar. But in actual text, they can appear in very different contexts — 打击犯罪 (combat crime), 拍摄照片 (take photos), 拉动经济 (stimulate the economy), etc. Once the text-based model has seen enough data to learn these patterns, the visual priors start no longer matters. In other words, visual inputs warm-starts the optimization. But, well, it doesn’t change the information ceiling.
关键在于：视觉先验编码了视觉相似性，但没有编码语言共现性。然而，下一个字符的预测最终取决于后者。没错，“打”、“拍”和“拉”都共享“扌”且看起来相似。但在实际文本中，它们可能出现在完全不同的语境中——打击犯罪、拍摄照片、拉动经济等。一旦基于文本的模型看到了足够多的数据来学习这些模式，视觉先验就不再重要了。换句话说，视觉输入加速了优化过程的启动，但它并没有改变信息上限。

### Where This Actually Matters
### 这在哪些方面真正重要？

Despite of the same destination, there are real situations where it matters:
尽管最终目的地相同，但在某些实际情况下，这确实很重要：

*   **Low-resource settings:** When you don’t have much training data, the visual head start translates into a real practical advantage. In our experiments, with just 10K samples, visual models already outperform a fully trained text baseline on downstream Chinese benchmarks (C-eval).
*   **低资源环境：** 当你没有太多训练数据时，视觉带来的领先优势会转化为实际的实用价值。在我们的实验中，仅使用1万个样本，视觉模型在中文下游基准测试（C-eval）中就已经超过了经过充分训练的文本基准模型。
*   **Damaged historical texts:** This is another exciting one. A visual can help check classical Chinese manuscripts, damaged books, and handwritten documents where strokes are missing or faded.
*   **受损的历史文献：** 这是另一个令人兴奋的应用。视觉模型可以帮助识别古籍手稿、破损书籍以及笔画缺失或褪色的手写文档。
