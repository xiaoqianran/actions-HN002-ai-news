# Not Truly Multilingual: Script Consistency as a Missing Dimension in VLM Evaluation
# 并非真正的多语言：脚本一致性——视觉语言模型（VLM）评估中缺失的维度

**Abstract:** Current multilingual evaluations for Vision-Language Models (VLMs) assume a one-to-one mapping between language and orthography, overlooking billions of users of multi-script languages. 

**摘要：** 当前针对视觉语言模型（VLM）的多语言评估假设语言与书写系统之间存在一一对应关系，却忽视了数十亿使用多脚本语言的用户。

We introduce PuMVR (Punjabi Multimodal Visual Reasoning), a benchmark of 1,000 strictly parallel image-text instances across Punjabi's three active scripts: Gurmukhi, Shahmukhi, and Roman. 

我们引入了 PuMVR（旁遮普语多模态视觉推理），这是一个包含 1,000 个严格平行图文实例的基准测试，涵盖了旁遮普语的三种活跃脚本：古木基文（Gurmukhi）、沙木基文（Shahmukhi）和罗马字母（Roman）。

Evaluating 10 state-of-the-art VLMs, we expose a substantial and systematic Script Gap. Models frequently solve visual tasks in one script while failing identical tasks in another, with accuracy deltas reaching 16%. 

通过对 10 个最先进的 VLM 进行评估，我们揭示了一个实质性且系统性的“脚本差距”（Script Gap）。模型经常能在一种脚本下解决视觉任务，却在另一种脚本下处理相同的任务时失败，准确率差异高达 16%。

Crucially, visual input boosts absolute performance uniformly yet does not close the orthographic gap. Furthermore, cross-script in-context transfer is highly brittle, exposing script-locked knowledge representation. 

至关重要的是，视觉输入虽然能统一提升绝对性能，却无法弥合书写系统带来的差距。此外，跨脚本的上下文迁移能力非常脆弱，这暴露了模型中存在的“脚本锁定”知识表征问题。

Supported by McNemar tests across all script pairs, our findings demonstrate that current "multilingual" VLMs are not truly multi-script. 

通过对所有脚本对进行 McNemar 检验，我们的研究结果表明，当前的“多语言”VLM 并非真正意义上的多脚本模型。

We propose the Script Consistency Rate (SCR), which falls as low as 24.8% on our benchmark, as a mandatory metric for script-agnostic evaluation to ensure equitable AI access. 

我们提出了“脚本一致性率”（Script Consistency Rate, SCR）作为脚本无关评估的强制性指标，以确保 AI 访问的公平性。在我们的基准测试中，该指标最低仅为 24.8%。
