### Correct When Paired, Wrong When Split: Decoupling and Editing Modality-Specific Neurons in MLLMs
### 成对时正确，拆分时错误：多模态大模型中模态特定神经元的解耦与编辑

**Abstract:** Although Knowledge Editing provides an efficient mechanism for updating the knowledge of Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs), we find that current paradigms still suffer from an important yet remain underexplored issue: editing decoupling failure, where entity-related knowledge can be updated when the model is triggered by multimodal inputs (text--image query pairs), however, it often reverts to outdated pre-edit facts when the paired inputs are split into unimodal ones.

**摘要：** 尽管知识编辑为更新多模态大模型（MLLMs）的知识提供了一种高效机制，但我们发现当前的范式仍存在一个重要且尚未被充分探索的问题：编辑解耦失效。即当模型由多模态输入（文本-图像查询对）触发时，实体相关知识可以被更新；然而，当这些成对输入被拆分为单模态输入时，模型往往会回退到编辑前的过时事实。

Our in-depth empirical analysis reveals that the entity knowledge in MLLMs is not stored as a unified representation, but is instead distributed across disentangled modality-specific pathways. As a result, updates biased toward multimodal queries fail to propagate effectively to unimodal circuits.

我们深入的实证分析表明，MLLMs 中的实体知识并非以统一的表征形式存储，而是分布在解耦的模态特定路径中。因此，偏向多模态查询的更新无法有效地传播到单模态电路中。

To bridge this gap, we propose DECODE, which explicitly disentangles and localizes modality-specific neuron groups for targeted knowledge. Extensive experiments demonstrate that DECODE consistently achieves effective knowledge updates under different modality triggers, thereby mitigating editing decoupling failures.

为了弥补这一差距，我们提出了 DECODE，它能够显式地解耦并定位针对特定知识的模态特定神经元组。大量实验证明，DECODE 在不同的模态触发条件下始终能实现有效的知识更新，从而缓解了编辑解耦失效的问题。
