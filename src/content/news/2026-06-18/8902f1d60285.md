# Your Churn Threshold Is a Pricing Decision
# 你的流失阈值其实是一个定价决策

**How unit economics should set your classification cutoff, and why they rarely do.**
**单位经济效益应如何设定你的分类阈值，以及为什么它们很少被这样使用。**

Fabio Oliveira | Jun 17, 2026 | 15 min read
Fabio Oliveira | 2026年6月17日 | 15分钟阅读

When a churn model says “this customer’s probability of leaving is 0.4” and your code does `predict(X) >= 0.5`, you have just made a pricing decision: you decided that the cost of sending a retention offer to a customer who would have stayed is exactly equal to the cost of losing one who would have left, and on the IBM Telco dataset (arguably the most-recycled churn dataset on Kaggle and GitHub), that decision is wrong by a factor of 13.
当流失模型显示“该客户流失概率为0.4”，而你的代码执行 `predict(X) >= 0.5` 时，你实际上已经做出了一个定价决策：你认定“向本会留存的客户发送挽留优惠的成本”与“失去一名本会流失的客户的成本”完全相等。而在 IBM Telco 数据集（这可能是 Kaggle 和 GitHub 上被重复使用次数最多的流失数据集）上，这一决策的误差高达 13 倍。

I assembled a corpus of 36 publicly available IBM Telco churn analyses (Kaggle notebooks, GitHub repositories, blog posts, peer-reviewed papers), and the reporting pattern is striking: roughly nine in ten report classification accuracy or F1, just over one in seven report a profit curve, and none use survival analysis to compute lifetime value.
我收集了 36 份公开的 IBM Telco 流失分析报告（包括 Kaggle 笔记本、GitHub 仓库、博客文章和同行评审论文），其报告模式令人震惊：约十分之九的报告仅展示分类准确率或 F1 分数，仅七分之一多一点的报告展示了利润曲线，且没有一份报告使用生存分析来计算客户终身价值（LTV）。

The result is a literature where the same dataset has been re-modelled hundreds of times, and every default-threshold model leaves money on the floor: about $86 per customer in avoidable burn on the standard 20% test split, and scaled to a 100,000-subscriber book with the same churn profile that would represent $8.6 million in recoverable cost; the IBM Telco churn rate (26.5% annual) is unusually high, and a healthier B2C SaaS book with 5–8% annual churn would see the per-customer figure drop by roughly 3–4×, so what stays invariant across any cost-sensitive setting is not the headline dollar amount but the asymmetry — 13× more expensive to miss a churner than to over-treat a loyalist.
结果是，在相关文献中，同一数据集被重复建模了数百次，而每一个使用默认阈值的模型都在浪费资金：在标准的 20% 测试集划分下，每位客户约有 86 美元的成本本可避免。若将其扩展到拥有相同流失特征的 10 万订阅用户规模，则意味着 860 万美元的可挽回成本。IBM Telco 的流失率（年化 26.5%）异常高，而更健康的 B2C SaaS 业务（年化流失率 5–8%）其单客成本数字会下降约 3–4 倍。因此，在任何成本敏感的场景下，不变的不是具体的金额，而是这种不对称性——漏掉一个流失客户的代价，是过度挽留一个忠诚客户代价的 13 倍。

This piece lays out three things, in order: first, what the IBM Telco literature reports and what it leaves out; second, how to compute the dollar cost of a misclassification using public 2026 B2C SaaS benchmarks and Kaplan-Meier survival analysis, with no hand-waved CAC; third, why the textbook Bayes-optimal threshold formula loses to a brute-force sweep when the model is trained on SMOTE-balanced data, and what to do about it. Every number in this article is reproducible from the scripts linked at the end.
本文将按顺序阐述三件事：第一，IBM Telco 相关文献报告了什么以及遗漏了什么；第二，如何利用 2026 年公开的 B2C SaaS 基准数据和 Kaplan-Meier 生存分析来计算误分类的美元成本，且无需随意估算获客成本（CAC）；第三，当模型在经过 SMOTE 平衡处理的数据上训练时，为什么教科书式的贝叶斯最优阈值公式会输给暴力搜索法，以及该如何应对。本文中的每一个数字都可以通过文末链接的脚本进行复现。

### 1. The 36-article gap
### 1. 36 篇文献中的缺口

The IBM Telco Customer Churn dataset is small (7,032 cleaned rows), tidy, labelled, and has been the canonical introductory churn dataset on Kaggle for nearly a decade. To get a feel for what the public corpus actually measures, I indexed 36 analyses across Kaggle, GitHub, and the major data-science blogs, scoring each one on ten reporting dimensions ranging from F1 score to a CAC-and-LTV-grounded profit curve.
IBM Telco 客户流失数据集规模较小（7,032 行清洗后的数据）、整洁、已标注，近十年来一直是 Kaggle 上最经典的入门级流失数据集。为了了解公开文献实际衡量的内容，我索引了 Kaggle、GitHub 和各大主流数据科学博客上的 36 份分析报告，并根据从 F1 分数到基于 CAC 和 LTV 的利润曲线等十个维度对每一份报告进行了评分。

Three findings worth pulling out:
有三个值得注意的发现：

*   **Saturated:** F1, accuracy, AUC, confusion-matrix screenshots, and SMOTE-vs-no-SMOTE comparisons appear in 80–90% of the corpus, with hyperparameter tuning via Optuna or grid search a near-universal trope.
    **饱和：** F1 分数、准确率、AUC、混淆矩阵截图以及 SMOTE 与非 SMOTE 的对比出现在 80–90% 的文献中，通过 Optuna 或网格搜索进行超参数调优几乎是通用的套路。
*   **Uncommon:** a profit curve (total dollar cost of misclassifications as a function of decision threshold) appears in fewer than 15% of the analyses I reviewed, and when it does appear, the FN/FP cost numbers are usually picked from a textbook example without anchoring them to real CAC or LTV.
    **罕见：** 利润曲线（作为决策阈值函数的误分类总美元成本）出现在我审阅的分析中不到 15%，且即便出现，其假阴性（FN）/假阳性（FP）的成本数字通常也是从教科书案例中直接照搬，并未锚定真实的 CAC 或 LTV。
*   **Absent:** none of the 36 analyses I indexed compute customer lifetime value via survival analysis on tenure; most either skip LTV entirely or use the steady-state Skok formula `LTV = ARPU / monthly_churn_rate`, which assumes a homogeneous customer base — a strong claim for a dataset where contract type, payment method, and tenure all materially shift retention.
    **缺失：** 我索引的 36 份分析中，没有一份通过基于留存时间的生存分析来计算客户终身价值；大多数分析要么完全跳过 LTV，要么使用稳态的 Skok 公式 `LTV = ARPU / monthly_churn_rate`。该公式假设客户群是同质的——对于一个合同类型、支付方式和留存时间都会显著影响留存率的数据集来说，这是一个过于强硬的假设。

Skipping survival analysis matters because the threshold decision is a function of LTV: if you misjudge LTV by 2x you misjudge the cost of a missed churner by 2x, and the cost-optimal threshold moves with it. The next two sections build the missing piece, then plug it back into the threshold problem.
跳过生存分析之所以重要，是因为阈值决策是 LTV 的函数：如果你对 LTV 的判断偏差了 2 倍，那么你对漏掉流失客户的成本判断也会偏差 2 倍，而成本最优阈值也会随之改变。接下来的两节将构建缺失的部分，并将其重新应用到阈值问题中。
