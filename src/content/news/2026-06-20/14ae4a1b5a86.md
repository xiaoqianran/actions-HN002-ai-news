### Performance Analysis and Optimization of 3D Generative Diffusion Models across GPU Architectures
### 跨 GPU 架构的 3D 生成式扩散模型性能分析与优化

**Abstract:** Diffusion models have become essential for high-fidelity 3D MRI synthesis, yet their deployment remains constrained by substantial GPU resource demands arising from hundreds of U-Net evaluations per sample and a highly heterogeneous kernel behavior.
**摘要：** 扩散模型已成为高保真 3D MRI 合成的核心技术，但其部署仍受限于巨大的 GPU 资源需求——这源于每个样本需要进行数百次 U-Net 推理，以及高度异构的内核（Kernel）行为。

This paper performs a comprehensive performance analysis of the state-of-the-art medical diffusion model, Med-DDPM, across three generations of NVIDIA architectures to study kernel-level runtime breakdowns, instruction-mix characteristics, memory system utilization, warp-level activities, and profiler priority-score estimates.
本文对最先进的医学扩散模型 Med-DDPM 在三代 NVIDIA 架构上的性能进行了全面分析，研究了内核级运行时间分布、指令集混合特征、内存系统利用率、Warp 级活动以及性能分析器的优先级评分估计。

We show that training is overwhelmingly dominated by cuDNN convolution and implicit-GEMM kernels, with inefficiencies arising from memory-access patterns, tensor-layout conversions, and limited Tensor Core utilization.
研究表明，训练过程主要由 cuDNN 卷积和隐式 GEMM 内核主导，而内存访问模式、张量布局转换以及 Tensor Core 利用率不足等问题导致了效率低下。

Guided by these insights, we evaluate two architecture-aware optimizations—TF32 Tensor Core activation and a 3D channels-last layout—and demonstrate that they reduce SM cycles by up to 100x, cut dynamic instructions by 100x, raise Tensor Core utilization from 1.45 to 9.98x, and increase IPC by 7% on A100, all without degrading synthesis quality.
基于这些洞察，我们评估了两种架构感知优化方案——TF32 Tensor Core 激活和 3D 通道最后（channels-last）布局。实验证明，这些优化在不降低合成质量的前提下，将 SM 周期减少了高达 100 倍，动态指令减少了 100 倍，Tensor Core 利用率从 1.45 倍提升至 9.98 倍，并在 A100 上将 IPC（每周期指令数）提高了 7%。
