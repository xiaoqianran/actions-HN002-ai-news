# Computational Identifiability (计算可识别性)

**Abstract:** Identification conditions describe the computability of a target query or parameter of interest as a function of the type and amount of information available. In causal identification, this information is often expressed in the form of a causal graph, and data are observed or collected for some subset of variables in the graph. Target queries may be for a single effect alone or for a class of effects in a given model.

**摘要：** 识别条件描述了目标查询或感兴趣参数的可计算性，它是可用信息类型和数量的函数。在因果识别中，这些信息通常以因果图的形式表示，数据则是针对图中变量的某个子集进行观察或收集的。目标查询可以是针对单一效应，也可以是针对给定模型中的一类效应。

The derivation of an identification algorithm then defines mathematically the process by which the desired causal effect(s) can be uniquely determined, theoretically, in expectation. Identifiability in expectation, or 'theoretical identifiability,' generally assumes asymptotic properties, infinite data, or other mathematically idealized conditions.

识别算法的推导在数学上定义了在期望意义下，理论上唯一确定所需因果效应的过程。期望意义下的可识别性，即“理论可识别性”，通常假设渐近性质、无限数据或其他数学上的理想化条件。

In this paper, we explore a fundamental distinction between this theoretical, idealized notion of identifiability and a proposed alternative that is computation-bound. The framework we propose - 'computational identifiability' - is to instead define a finite computational search procedure for an empirical estimator. If this process finds an estimator empirically, within a desired error tolerance, then identifiability is satisfied, conditional on the specified assumptions of the search (i.e., a prior distribution over the parameters) and conditional on the search procedure itself.

在本文中，我们探讨了这种理论上的、理想化的可识别性概念与一种基于计算约束的替代方案之间的根本区别。我们提出的框架——“计算可识别性”——旨在为经验估计量定义一种有限的计算搜索过程。如果该过程能在期望的误差容限内经验性地找到一个估计量，那么在满足搜索的特定假设（即参数的先验分布）以及搜索过程本身的条件下，可识别性即得到满足。

Through several experiments, we demonstrate how this framework allows us to answer fine-grained, practical identification questions, such as identification with small finite samples, with ambiguous graphical criteria, with mixed observational-interventional data, and across counterfactual data and estimands. Code is available at this [URL](https://github.com/lucius-bynum/computational-identifiability).

通过多项实验，我们展示了该框架如何帮助我们回答细粒度的实际识别问题，例如在小样本、模糊图形准则、观测与干预混合数据，以及跨反事实数据和被估计量情况下的识别问题。代码可在该[链接](https://github.com/lucius-bynum/computational-identifiability)获取。
