# Human-like autonomy emerges from self-play and a pinch of human data
# 通过自我博弈与少量人类数据实现类人自主驾驶

**Abstract:** Self-play reinforcement learning has recently emerged as a way to train driving policies without any human data. It uses cheap, large-scale simulations to substitute expensive, large-scale human driving demonstrations.
**摘要：** 自我博弈强化学习（Self-play reinforcement learning）近期已成为一种无需人类数据即可训练驾驶策略的方法。它利用低成本、大规模的模拟来替代昂贵且大规模的人类驾驶演示。

A key limitation of this approach is that policies trained through pure self-play can learn effective but alien driving conventions incompatible with people. Previous works attempt to mitigate such behavioral misalignments through extensive reward engineering and domain randomization, which are brittle and labor-intensive.
该方法的一个主要局限在于，通过纯自我博弈训练出的策略可能会习得有效但与人类习惯不兼容的“异类”驾驶惯例。以往的研究试图通过大量的奖励工程（reward engineering）和域随机化（domain randomization）来缓解这种行为偏差，但这些方法往往脆弱且耗费人力。

Instead of completely discarding human demonstrations, our method treats them as a regularization objective on top of a minimal safe goal-reaching reward. Like the spice in a good stew, we find that a little human data goes a long way: our method uses only 30 minutes of human demonstrations, 2500x fewer than comparable imitation learning approaches.
我们的方法并没有完全摒弃人类演示，而是将其作为一种正则化目标，叠加在最基本的安全目标达成奖励之上。就像炖菜中加入的调料一样，我们发现少量的人类数据就能发挥巨大作用：我们的方法仅使用了 30 分钟的人类演示数据，比同类模仿学习方法少用了 2500 倍。

Resulting policies coordinate with held-out human trajectories and complete training in 15 hours on a single consumer-grade GPU. Videos and full source code are available at this URL.
由此产生的策略能够与未参与训练的人类轨迹进行协调，并且仅需在单张消费级显卡上训练 15 小时即可完成。相关视频和完整源代码可在该网址获取。
