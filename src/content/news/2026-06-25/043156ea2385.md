# Listening makes Vision Clear for VLMs
# 倾听让视觉大模型（VLM）视野更清晰

Recent work typically assesses vision--language consistency using attention distributions of answer-side tokens. However, we observe that highest attention regions are not always consistent with the intended semantic token. This probably stems from decoding drift, where language priors from previously generated answer tokens accumulate and mismatch with visual attention.

近期的研究通常通过分析回答端（answer-side）Token 的注意力分布来评估视觉与语言的一致性。然而，我们观察到注意力最高的区域并不总是与预期的语义 Token 相符。这很可能源于解码漂移（decoding drift），即先前生成的回答 Token 所带来的语言先验不断累积，导致其与视觉注意力产生错位。

Besides the priors from previous answer tokens, we find that structural tokens, e.g., modality boundary markers, may encompass the entire context and generate high attention to areas unrelated to the target. To avoid these distortions and provide consistency evaluation for large VLMs, we adopt prompt-side semantics and propose Prompt-Vision Token Activation Map (PV-TAM).

除了来自先前回答 Token 的先验外，我们还发现结构性 Token（例如模态边界标记）可能会覆盖整个上下文，并对与目标无关的区域产生高注意力。为了避免这些干扰并为大型视觉语言模型（VLM）提供一致性评估，我们采用了提示端（prompt-side）语义，并提出了“提示-视觉 Token 激活图”（Prompt-Vision Token Activation Map, PV-TAM）。

PV-TAM further incorporates a filter to remove systematic bias induced by modality boundary markers. Unlike traditional methods that evaluate overlap solely through masks while ignoring activation intensity, our metrics leverage the peak distribution of attention to measure the alignment between prompts and visual regions. In experiments, PV-TAM consistently improves both attention-based and IoU-style localization metrics over answer-side baselines on various datasets.

PV-TAM 进一步集成了一个过滤器，用于消除由模态边界标记引起的系统性偏差。与传统仅通过掩码（mask）评估重叠度而忽略激活强度的方法不同，我们的指标利用注意力的峰值分布来衡量提示与视觉区域之间的对齐程度。实验表明，在多个数据集上，PV-TAM 在基于注意力的定位指标和 IoU 风格的定位指标上，均持续优于基于回答端的基准方法。
