# Deciphering Fingerprints of 3D Molecular Surfaces for Accurate Epitope Prediction
# 解码 3D 分子表面指纹以实现精准表位预测

**Abstract:** Molecular surfaces encode the geometric and physicochemical patterns that determine antibody-antigen recognition, central to epitope prediction. However, existing methods rely on sequences or backbone structures and struggle to capture discontinuous, surface-driven epitopes.
**摘要：** 分子表面编码了决定抗体-抗原识别的几何和物理化学模式，这是表位预测的核心。然而，现有的方法多依赖于序列或骨架结构，难以捕捉不连续的、由表面驱动的表位。

This study presents SurfBind, a surface-centric learning framework for epitope prediction that operates directly on molecular surface representations. SurfBind integrates geometric and physicochemical cues through a Transformer-based architecture with patch-level surface modeling, binder-aware cross-attention, and a hierarchical coarse-to-fine prediction paradigm.
本研究提出了 SurfBind，这是一个以表面为中心的表位预测学习框架，直接作用于分子表面表示。SurfBind 通过基于 Transformer 的架构整合了几何和物理化学线索，并结合了补丁级（patch-level）表面建模、结合物感知交叉注意力机制以及分层式的从粗到细预测范式。

Experiments on challenging epitope identification benchmarks, including SAbDab and DB5.5, demonstrate that SurfBind achieves state-of-the-art performance and strong generalization across unseen antibodies and conformational states, highlighting the value of interaction-aware surface modeling for understanding the crucial mechanisms of protein-protein interactions.
在包括 SAbDab 和 DB5.5 在内的具有挑战性的表位识别基准测试中，实验证明 SurfBind 达到了最先进的性能，并在未见过的抗体和构象状态下表现出强大的泛化能力，凸显了交互感知表面建模在理解蛋白质-蛋白质相互作用关键机制方面的价值。
