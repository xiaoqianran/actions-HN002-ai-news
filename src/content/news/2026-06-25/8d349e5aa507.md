# Low-power analogue neural networks with trainable nonlinear connections for continuous control
# 用于连续控制的具有可训练非线性连接的低功耗模拟神经网络

**Abstract:** Physical neural networks promise low-power machine learning by computing directly with analogue device physics, but most architectures force nonlinear device responses to act as scalar weights.
**摘要：** 物理神经网络通过直接利用模拟器件物理特性进行计算，有望实现低功耗机器学习，但大多数架构强行将非线性器件响应作为标量权重使用。

Inspired by Kolmogorov-Arnold networks, we place trainable nonlinear functions on the connections, making each physical connection a learnable computational element.
受柯尔莫哥洛夫-阿诺德网络（Kolmogorov-Arnold networks）的启发，我们将可训练的非线性函数置于连接上，使每个物理连接都成为一个可学习的计算单元。

Realising these functions as analogue band-pass filters on field-programmable analogue arrays, we find that the benefit is task-dependent and follows from the smoothness of the physical basis: the networks represent smooth, continuously valued targets, including robotic kinematics, continuous control, and photovoltaic maximum-power-point tracking, with far fewer nodes and connections than multilayer perceptrons, but offer no parameter-efficiency advantage on classification-like decision boundaries.
通过在现场可编程模拟阵列（FPAA）上将这些函数实现为模拟带通滤波器，我们发现其优势取决于具体任务，并源于物理基础的平滑性：该网络在处理平滑、连续值的目标（包括机器人运动学、连续控制和光伏最大功率点跟踪）时，所需的节点和连接数远少于多层感知机，但在处理类似分类的决策边界时，并不具备参数效率优势。

Trained networks transfer to hardware across approximately 35,000 connections with quantified fidelity, and a dedicated CMOS implementation is projected to operate at approximately 30 microwatts.
训练后的网络以量化的保真度迁移到拥有约 35,000 个连接的硬件上，且专用的 CMOS 实现方案预计功耗约为 30 微瓦。

A memristive realisation reproduces the same behaviour in simulation, indicating that the advantage comes from placing trainable nonlinearity on connections, rather than from a particular device.
忆阻器实现方案在仿真中重现了相同的行为，这表明该优势源于在连接上放置可训练的非线性，而非源于某种特定的器件。
