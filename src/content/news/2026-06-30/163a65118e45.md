# PairSAE: Mechanistic Interpretability from Pair Representations in Protein Co-Folding
# PairSAE：蛋白质共折叠中基于配对表示的机制可解释性研究

Foundation models for structural biology have achieved remarkable performance in predicting biomolecular structure and show promise for the design of proteins and small molecules. Yet understanding which internal features drive their outputs remains challenging.
结构生物学基础模型在预测生物分子结构方面取得了显著成就，并在蛋白质和小分子设计领域展现出巨大潜力。然而，理解究竟是哪些内部特征驱动了这些模型的输出仍然是一个挑战。

Standard sparse autoencoders (SAEs), effective on transformer-style sequence embeddings, do not transfer cleanly to pairformer-like architectures: naively operating on pairwise representations yields a quadratic blow-up of features and obscures concepts distributed jointly across sequence and pair representations.
在 Transformer 风格的序列嵌入上表现有效的标准稀疏自编码器（SAE），无法直接平滑地迁移到 Pairformer 类架构中：如果简单地对成对表示进行操作，会导致特征数量呈二次方爆炸，并掩盖了分布在序列和配对表示中的联合概念。

We introduce PairSAE, which summarizes pairwise tensors via an N-mode SVD into token-wise interaction roles, then uses a sparse autoencoder to learn a shared set of token-level features that decode into both sequence and pair representations.
我们引入了 PairSAE，它通过 N-模奇异值分解（N-mode SVD）将成对张量总结为标记级的交互角色，然后利用稀疏自编码器学习一组共享的标记级特征，这些特征可以解码为序列和配对表示。

Evaluated on Boltz-2 activations for PLINDER protein-ligand complexes, PairSAE yields interpretable features that align with UniProt annotations and predict Boltz-2 affinity values.
在针对 PLINDER 蛋白质-配体复合物的 Boltz-2 激活评估中，PairSAE 产生了与 UniProt 注释相符且能预测 Boltz-2 亲和力值的可解释特征。

These results indicate that PairSAE links the latent space of foundation models for structural biology to interpretable structural concepts, clarifying what the model "knows" while avoiding pairformer-induced pitfalls that limit conventional SAEs.
这些结果表明，PairSAE 将结构生物学基础模型的潜在空间与可解释的结构概念联系起来，在阐明模型“认知”内容的同时，避免了限制传统 SAE 的 Pairformer 架构带来的缺陷。
