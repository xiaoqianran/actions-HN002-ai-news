### The inevitable weakness of metrics
### 指标不可避免的局限性

There are plenty of useful things a metric can reveal. There are even more that it can obscure or corrupt. Like a lot of people bitten by the self-quantifying bug, I started gathering personal data to pursue a nebulous collection of goals and desires. I wanted to feel better physically and emotionally, get outside more, and bring order to the messiness and uncertainty of my daily existence. But external metrics and data can never capture what's truly important. Worse, they inevitably redefine your core sense of what's important, whether you're aware of the trap or not. Dive into the dangers of quantifying our lives with metrics. —Bryan Gardiner
指标可以揭示许多有用的信息，但它掩盖或扭曲的信息可能更多。像许多热衷于“自我量化”的人一样，我开始收集个人数据，试图追求一系列模糊的目标和愿望。我希望在身体和情感上感觉更好，多去户外走走，并为我日常生活中混乱和不确定的状态带来秩序。然而，外部指标和数据永远无法捕捉到真正重要的东西。更糟糕的是，无论你是否意识到这个陷阱，它们都会不可避免地重新定义你对“重要性”的核心认知。深入了解用指标量化生活的危险性。——Bryan Gardiner

### Elephant alert! AI warning systems aim to avoid deadly clashes
### 大象警报！AI 预警系统旨在避免致命冲突

India is home to about 60% of the world’s wild Asian elephants, and around 80% of their habitat lies outside protected areas. That brings them into close contact with people, and clashes can turn lethal: there have been some 3,000 human casualties in the last five years and over 1,000 elephant deaths since 2014. In response, state forest departments, NGOs, and locals are designing, testing, and deploying a range of AI systems that cut response and warning times to minutes—or even seconds. They range from wildlife eyes in Maharashtra to infrared drones in Chhattisgarh. Find out how they work in our interactive map. —Kanika Gupta
印度拥有全球约 60% 的野生亚洲象，其中约 80% 的栖息地位于保护区之外。这使得它们与人类频繁接触，冲突可能导致致命后果：过去五年中约有 3,000 人伤亡，自 2014 年以来有超过 1,000 头大象死亡。为此，各邦林业部门、非政府组织和当地居民正在设计、测试并部署一系列 AI 系统，将响应和预警时间缩短至几分钟甚至几秒钟。这些系统包括马哈拉施特拉邦的“野生动物之眼”和恰蒂斯加尔邦的红外无人机。通过我们的交互式地图了解它们的工作原理。——Kanika Gupta

### One More Thing

**Why Generation Z falls for online misinformation**
Research shows that young people are more likely to believe and pass on misinformation if they feel a sense of common identity with the person who shared it. Offline, teenagers rely on community context; social media, however, promotes credibility based on identity. As young people participate in more political discussions online, those who cultivate identity-based credibility could become de facto community leaders. While this empowers marginalized groups, it also exacerbates the threat of misinformation. Find out what we can all learn about how young people evaluate truth online. —Jennifer Neda John
**为什么 Z 世代容易相信网络虚假信息**
研究表明，如果年轻人对分享信息的人产生身份认同感，他们就更容易相信并传播虚假信息。在现实生活中，青少年依赖社区背景；然而，社交媒体却基于身份来建立可信度。随着年轻人参与更多在线政治讨论，那些建立起基于身份的可信度的人可能会成为事实上的社区领袖。虽然这赋予了边缘群体权力，但也加剧了虚假信息的威胁。了解我们能从年轻人评估网络真相的方式中学到什么。——Jennifer Neda John
