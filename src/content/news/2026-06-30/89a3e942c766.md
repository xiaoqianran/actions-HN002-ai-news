# I Completed Five Years in Analytics Consulting: 5 Lessons That Changed How I Work
# 我在分析咨询领域工作了五年：改变我工作方式的 5 个经验教训

The tools I use for analytics and reporting have changed more than I expected, yet my questions for any analytics project haven't moved much.
我所使用的分析和报告工具的变化超出了我的预期，但对于任何分析项目，我所关注的核心问题却几乎没有改变。

In June 2021, I started my first ever full-time job as a Senior Data Analyst at a leading health insurance company right after completing my graduate school, bringing with me a strong foundation in analytics and business. Five years later, I’ve now worked on an array of analytics skills, including reporting, data visualization, stakeholder management, business strategy discussions, and, more recently, AI-assisted development. These five years have taught me lessons that have little to do with specific tools but a lot to do with understanding people, decisions, and outcomes.
2021 年 6 月，研究生毕业后，我进入一家领先的健康保险公司，担任高级数据分析师，这是我的第一份全职工作，当时我已具备了扎实的分析和商业基础。五年后的今天，我已掌握了一系列分析技能，包括报告撰写、数据可视化、利益相关者管理、商业战略讨论，以及最近的 AI 辅助开发。这五年教会我的经验与具体工具关系不大，但与理解人、决策和结果息息相关。

My journey in data & analytics started seven years ago, when I started grad school to study analytics and business. Alongside my studies, I interned first as an R&D Intern working with data sources and creating BI solutions. Then came my Data Science internship where the code grew more complex, the data got messier, and the dashboards needed to meet executive standards. That experience became the cornerstone of my current success. I realized that being someone who codes in Python or crunches numbers is not enough. I have to be a strategic problem-solver.
我的数据与分析之旅始于七年前，当时我开始攻读分析与商业研究生学位。在学习的同时，我先是担任研发实习生，负责处理数据源并创建商业智能（BI）解决方案。随后是数据科学实习，那里的代码变得更加复杂，数据更加混乱，仪表板也需要达到高管标准。那段经历成为了我今天成功的基础。我意识到，仅仅会写 Python 代码或处理数字是不够的，我必须成为一名战略性的问题解决者。

Reflecting on half a decade in the field, from a Data Analyst to Senior Analytics Consultant, I’ve witnessed three major shifts: Analytics has become more business-driven than technical; Storytelling is more valuable than reporting; AI is reshaping what “technical skills” mean. Recollecting my time as an Analytics Consultant, I want to share five lessons that transformed the way I approach my job and can help anyone working in analytics.
回顾这五年，从数据分析师到高级分析顾问，我见证了三个重大转变：分析工作变得比技术本身更具商业导向；讲故事比单纯的报告更有价值；AI 正在重塑“技术技能”的定义。回首我作为分析顾问的时光，我想分享五个改变了我工作方式的经验教训，希望能帮助到每一位从事分析工作的人。

### 1. Storytelling with data is more important than data itself
### 1. 用数据讲故事比数据本身更重要

As you grow in your career, you find yourself more often in rooms where decisions are made, you quickly realize that data alone rarely drives impact. How that data is communicated and consumed is what truly influences outcomes. From my experience working with stakeholders with varying levels of technical experience, a stakeholder may not remember the regression model you built or understands model accuracy, but they surely remember the story that helped them make a decision.
随着职业生涯的发展，你会发现自己更多地出现在决策场合，并很快意识到仅凭数据本身很难产生影响力。数据的沟通和消费方式才是真正影响结果的关键。根据我与不同技术背景的利益相关者合作的经验，他们可能记不住你构建的回归模型，也不一定理解模型准确率，但他们一定会记住那个帮助他们做出决策的故事。

The value of data isn’t in its mere existence but it’s in its ability to be understood, trusted, and acted upon. At a meetup a few years ago, a speaker shared that narratives make data far more memorable than numbers alone, and that stayed with me. Since then, I’ve approached most of my analyses with three simple questions: What happened? Why does it matter? What should happen next?
数据的价值不在于其存在本身，而在于它能否被理解、被信任并被付诸行动。几年前的一次聚会上，一位演讲者分享说，叙事比单纯的数字更能让数据令人难忘，这句话深深影响了我。从那时起，我在进行大多数分析时都会问三个简单的问题：发生了什么？为什么这很重要？接下来应该做什么？

### 2. The hardest part of analytics isn’t analysis. It’s asking better questions.
### 2. 分析中最难的部分不是分析，而是提出更好的问题。

I was taught in graduate school that as an analyst, we should be curious people. Because curiosity helps us find patterns and make sense of data. But over time, I realized it’s not just curiosity or the data itself that gives us great insights. It’s the questions we ask about it. You can have the cleanest datasets and the most advanced tools, but without the right questions, your analysis will drift aimlessly.
研究生时，老师教导我们作为分析师要保持好奇心，因为好奇心能帮助我们发现规律并解读数据。但随着时间的推移，我意识到，真正带来深刻见解的不仅仅是好奇心或数据本身，而是我们针对数据提出的问题。你可以拥有最干净的数据集和最先进的工具，但如果没有正确的问题，你的分析就会漫无目的地漂移。

Over time, I learned that better questions come from collaborating closely with subject matter experts (SMEs) and unpacking the problem statement with them. These conversations surface assumptions and lead your way where to dig deeper, which also reinforces the value of building a strong network for when an SME isn’t available. Your takeaway in one line: start with curiosity, and then apply critical thinking. Don’t jump straight to the data. Pause and ask what’s really going on, then layer your thinking with the why, what, who, and when.
后来我了解到，更好的问题源于与领域专家（SME）的密切合作，并与他们共同拆解问题陈述。这些对话会揭示潜在的假设，并指引你深入挖掘的方向，这也强调了在专家不在场时建立强大的人脉网络的重要性。总结一句话：从好奇心出发，然后运用批判性思维。不要直接跳进数据里。停下来问问到底发生了什么，然后用“为什么”、“是什么”、“谁”和“什么时候”来构建你的思考逻辑。

### 3. Knowing when to keep digging and when to stop
### 3. 知道何时继续深挖，何时适可而止

For the first couple of years, I genuinely believed if I want to be a good analyst, I should not stop at the first answer. I should gather more, filter more, ask more. That instinct served me well, until it didn’t. I was once working on an effort to create a service intensity report, to analyze clients who needed more support, cost more to the organization, and identify what drives the service intensity. The data was incomplete and inconsistent from the start. However, instead of pressure-testing whether it could even support the project objective, I kept pushing forward by pulling in more datasets, testing more hypotheses, and chasing anomalies that turned out to be noise. After nearly five weeks of trying to force the data to work, I finally told my manager we couldn’t proceed. That experience taught me one of the most important lessons I now share with every junior analyst I mentor.
最初的几年里，我真心认为如果想成为一名优秀的分析师，就不应该止步于第一个答案。我应该收集更多、筛选更多、询问更多。这种直觉曾让我受益匪浅，直到它不再奏效。有一次，我负责制作一份服务强度报告，旨在分析哪些客户需要更多支持、为组织带来了更高成本，并找出驱动服务强度的因素。数据从一开始就是不完整且不一致的。然而，我没有去压力测试这些数据是否能支撑项目目标，而是一味地通过引入更多数据集、测试更多假设、追逐那些最终被证明是噪音的异常值来强行推进。在尝试了近五周后，我终于告诉经理我们无法继续。那次经历教会了我现在分享给每一位我指导的初级分析师的最重要的一课。
