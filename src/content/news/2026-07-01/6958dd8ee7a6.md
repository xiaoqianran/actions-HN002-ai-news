# Zero-Label Driving Scenario Complexity Detection via Joint Embedding Predictive Architecture
# 基于联合嵌入预测架构的零标签驾驶场景复杂度检测

**Abstract:** Identifying complex and safety-critical driving scenarios in large unlabelled datasets is an important but expensive problem. Existing approaches rely on human annotators, supervised classifiers, or carefully engineered rule sets, all of which require substantial prior knowledge about what constitutes a difficult scenario.
**摘要：** 在大规模未标注数据集中识别复杂且具有安全关键性的驾驶场景是一个重要但昂贵的问题。现有的方法依赖于人工标注、监督分类器或精心设计的规则集，所有这些方法都需要关于“何为困难场景”的大量先验知识。

We ask whether a model can discover scenario complexity on its own, with no labels at any stage. We train a minimal Joint Embedding Predictive Architecture (JEPA) on structured agent state data from the nuPlan mini dataset and use the temporal prediction error as a zero-shot complexity score.
我们探讨了模型是否可以在没有任何阶段标签的情况下，自主发现场景的复杂度。我们在 nuPlan mini 数据集的结构化智能体状态数据上训练了一个极简的联合嵌入预测架构（JEPA），并将时间预测误差用作零样本复杂度评分。

Without access to any ground-truth labels during training or evaluation setup, the model assigns significantly higher scores to scenarios involving unprotected turns, crosswalk interactions, and pedestrian proximity, and significantly lower scores to lane-following and stationary-traffic scenarios.
在训练或评估设置期间，模型无需访问任何真实标签，即可为涉及无保护转弯、人行横道交互和行人靠近的场景分配显著更高的分数，并为车道保持和静止交通场景分配显著更低的分数。

We validate this finding through four ablation experiments that isolate the source of the signal, and through a downstream anomaly detection evaluation that achieves Average Precision of 0.512 against a 0.436 chance baseline. The results show that temporal prediction error in a self-supervised latent world model is a practical proxy for driving scenario complexity.
我们通过四项消融实验（用于隔离信号来源）以及一项下游异常检测评估验证了这一发现，该评估实现了 0.512 的平均精度（Average Precision），优于 0.436 的随机基准。结果表明，自监督潜在世界模型中的时间预测误差是衡量驾驶场景复杂度的实用代理指标。
