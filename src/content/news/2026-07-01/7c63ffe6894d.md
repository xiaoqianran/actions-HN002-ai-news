# Surviving the Data Science Behavioral Interview
# 在数据科学行为面试中脱颖而出

In the age of AI, standing out here means a lot more than ever. Here are three tips to walk into your next interview with confidence.
在人工智能时代，要在面试中脱颖而出比以往任何时候都更加重要。以下是三个建议，助你自信地走进下一场面试。

### No, It’s Not the “Easy” Round
### 不，这绝不是“轻松”环节

When I first started joining in on interviews at my previous company, I used to think the behavioral part of the interview was the easy part. That if someone was technically proficient and could wow the interviewers with their analytical mind, they’d be the top contender for any job.
当我刚开始参与前公司的面试工作时，我曾以为行为面试部分是最轻松的。我当时认为，只要一个人技术过硬，能用分析思维让面试官惊叹，他就是任何职位的最佳人选。

Then I witnessed a highly technically skilled applicant lose out to a more socially competent one. Not because he lacked experience. He had done good work, and he obviously knew what he was doing. He just had no idea how to tell us about what he’d done in a way that landed, and how to connect his work as a data scientist to the things my team actually cared about: collaboration, communication, and decision-making under uncertainty.
后来，我目睹了一位技术能力极强的申请者输给了一位社交能力更强的人。这并不是因为他缺乏经验——他做过出色的工作，显然也深谙其道。他只是不知道如何以一种有效的方式向我们讲述他的经历，也不知道如何将他作为数据科学家的工作与我团队真正关心的事物联系起来：协作、沟通以及在不确定性下的决策能力。

Here’s the thing about behavioral interviews for data science roles specifically: they’re different from behavioral interviews for other fields. Companies aren’t just checking if you’re a nice person. They’re figuring out if you can translate technical work into business value, manage relationships with non-technical stakeholders, and handle situations where the data doesn’t give you a clean answer.
关于数据科学职位的行为面试，有一点很特别：它们与其他领域的行为面试不同。公司不仅仅是在考察你是否是一个好相处的人，他们是在评估你是否能将技术工作转化为商业价值，是否能管理与非技术利益相关者的关系，以及在数据无法提供明确答案时，你是否具备处理问题的能力。

Here are 3 tips I’d give anyone before a behavioral interview.
以下是我给所有准备参加行为面试的人的 3 个建议。

---

### 1. Treat Every Story as a Stakeholder Communication Problem
### 1. 将每个故事都视为一个利益相关者沟通问题

The biggest mistake I see data scientists make in behavioral interviews is telling the technical story when the interviewer wants the business story. You’re asked: “Tell me about a time you had a difficult project.” You launch into a detailed explanation of your cross-validation approach, the hyperparameter tuning you did, the precision-recall tradeoff you navigated. The interviewer’s eyes glaze over.
我发现数据科学家在行为面试中犯的最大错误是：当面试官想听商业故事时，他们却在讲技术故事。当被问到“请讲一个你经历过的困难项目”时，你开始详细解释你的交叉验证方法、超参数调优过程，以及你如何处理精确率与召回率的权衡。此时，面试官的眼神往往会变得呆滞。

Here’s what I’ve learned, both from my own interviews and from watching other data scientists navigate their careers: at most companies, the data scientist who can explain their model’s business impact in plain English is more valuable than the one who can explain the math better. Your interviewer doesn’t need the technical deep-dive. They need to know: What was the problem? What did you do? Why did it matter?
无论是从我自己的面试经历，还是观察其他数据科学家的职业发展中，我学到了一点：在大多数公司，能够用通俗易懂的语言解释模型商业影响的数据科学家，比那些只会解释数学原理的人更有价值。面试官不需要深入的技术细节，他们需要知道的是：问题是什么？你做了什么？为什么这很重要？

Before your interview, practice framing your stories using this structure:
在面试前，请练习使用以下结构来构建你的故事：
*   What was the business problem (not the technical problem)? 商业问题是什么（而非技术问题）？
*   Who was affected or involved? 谁受到了影响或参与其中？
*   What was your contribution, in plain language? 用通俗的语言描述你的贡献。
*   What was the measurable outcome? 可衡量的结果是什么？

Instead of saying “I built a time series forecasting model using lag features and Random Forest that reduced RMSE by 40%,” try: “We had a recurring issue where our team was over-ordering energy resources by a wide margin every month, which had real cost implications. I built a forecasting model that gave us a more accurate week-ahead prediction, which directly cut our overage costs.”
与其说“我使用滞后特征和随机森林构建了一个时间序列预测模型，将 RMSE 降低了 40%”，不如尝试这样说：“我们团队每个月都会出现能源资源过度订购的问题，这带来了实际的成本负担。我构建了一个预测模型，提供了更准确的周预测，直接削减了我们的超额成本。”

---

### 2. Do Your Research
### 2. 做好调研

I recommend starting with a basic search on Google: “[Company Name] behavioral interview questions”. You may find information on Glassdoor, Reddit, and other smaller websites. For larger companies especially, you’ll often find threads where past candidates share the actual questions they were asked, what the format looked like, and how the process felt.
我建议先从 Google 的基础搜索开始：“[公司名称] 行为面试题”。你可能会在 Glassdoor、Reddit 和其他小型网站上找到相关信息。特别是对于大型公司，你经常能找到过往候选人分享的真实面试题、面试形式以及面试感受。

Keep in mind that teams change their questions over time, so don’t treat old reviews as gospel, but they’ll still give you a strong sense of what the company values and how they like to probe for it. You can also look up a general list of behavioral interview questions for your specific role (Data scientist, data engineer, data analyst).
请记住，团队的问题会随时间变化，所以不要把旧的评论奉为圭臬，但它们依然能让你很好地了解公司看重什么，以及他们倾向于如何考察这些能力。你也可以搜索针对你特定职位（数据科学家、数据工程师、数据分析师）的通用行为面试题列表。

Search for YouTube videos of behavioral mock interviews or people who have conducted many rounds of data science interviews. Seeing how someone else answers will teach you more than reading a list of tips. Pay attention to:
在 YouTube 上搜索行为模拟面试的视频，或者观看那些进行过多次数据科学面试的人的分享。观察别人如何回答，比阅读一堆建议更有用。请注意：
*   What situations the candidate brought up, and what similar ones you’ve been in. 候选人提到了什么情境，以及你是否经历过类似的情况。
*   The candidate’s facial expressions and overall demeanor. 候选人的面部表情和整体仪态。

---

### 3. Prepare a Few Situations Ahead of Time
### 3. 提前准备几个情境

A lot of behavioral interview prep advice focuses on conflict: “Tell me about a time you disagreed with a colleague” or “Describe a situation where you failed.” Those questions matter, but for data science roles, the harder category is ambiguity.
许多行为面试的准备建议都集中在冲突上，例如“讲一次你与同事产生分歧的经历”或“描述一次你的失败”。这些问题固然重要，但对于数据科学职位来说，更难的一类问题是关于“模糊性”的。

*   “Tell me about a time you had to make a decision without having all the information you needed.” “讲一次你必须在信息不全的情况下做出决策的经历。”
*   “Describe a project where the requirements changed partway through.” “描述一个中途需求发生变化的项目。”
*   “How do you handle situations where the data doesn’t support a clear answer?” “当数据无法提供明确答案时，你如何处理？”

These questions are specifically designed to assess something that matters a lot in data science: your tolerance for uncertainty and your ability to move forward without perfect information. The best way to plan for these is by using the STAR method.
这些问题旨在评估数据科学中非常重要的一点：你对不确定性的容忍度，以及在信息不完美的情况下继续推进工作的能力。应对这些问题的最佳方法是使用 STAR 法则。

STAR stands for:
STAR 代表：
*   **S**ituation: What was the context/background? 情境：背景是什么？
*   **T**ask: What were you specifically tasked with doing/solving? 任务：你具体被要求做什么或解决什么？
*   **A**ction: What steps did you take to solve the problem? 行动：你采取了哪些步骤来解决问题？
*   **R**esult: What was the outcome? 结果：最终结果如何？

Let’s walk through a specific example of the STAR method: “Tell me about a time you had to make a decision without having all the information you needed.”
让我们通过一个具体的例子来应用 STAR 法则：“讲一次你必须在信息不全的情况下做出决策的经历。”

*   **Situation**: Midway through a forecasting project, I discovered that two months of historical energy consumption data had been logged incorrectly due to a meter error in the middle of the training window I was planning to use.
    **情境**：在一个预测项目进行到一半时，我发现由于仪表故障，我计划使用的训练窗口期内有两个月的历史能耗数据记录有误。
*   **Task**: My stakeholders needed a working model delivered by end of sprint. I had to decide whether to delay the project to investigate the data issue.
    **任务**：利益相关者要求在冲刺结束前交付一个可用的模型。我必须决定是推迟项目以调查数据问题，还是采取其他方案。
