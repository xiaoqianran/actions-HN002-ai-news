# Computer Vision for Wildlife Monitoring: Detecting Brown Howler Monkeys using YOLO
# 用于野生动物监测的计算机视觉：使用 YOLO 检测褐吼猴

**Abstract:** Urban expansion threatens global biodiversity, especially affecting arboreal species due to the fragmentation of forest habitats. The movement of arboreal species across disjointed forest patches increases mortality risk and, thus, compromises their conservation. 

**摘要：** 城市扩张威胁着全球生物多样性，由于森林栖息地的破碎化，树栖物种受到的影响尤为严重。树栖物种在不连续的森林斑块间移动会增加死亡风险，从而危及它们的保护工作。

In this context, the installation of canopy bridges can be a viable strategy; yet continuous monitoring of their use by arboreal species is essential for ensuring their effectiveness, typically carried out with the aid of camera traps. However, this method often produces false-positive images that demand time from conservationists for review. 

在此背景下，安装树冠桥梁是一种可行的策略；然而，为了确保其有效性，必须对树栖物种的使用情况进行持续监测，这通常借助红外相机（camera traps）来完成。然而，这种方法往往会产生大量误报图像，需要保护工作者花费大量时间进行人工审核。

In this context, computer vision algorithms can optimize the task of detecting target species using the canopy bridges. In this study, we explored the automatic detection of brown howler monkeys (*Alouatta guariba*) in videos obtained by camera traps. 

在这种情况下，计算机视觉算法可以优化检测目标物种使用树冠桥梁的任务。在本研究中，我们探索了利用红外相机拍摄的视频自动检测褐吼猴（*Alouatta guariba*）的方法。

Given the need for a large number of annotated images of the target animals to train the algorithms, we tested the incorporation of auxiliary data to improve detection models, fine-tuning the YOLOv10 framework using varying proportions of them. 

鉴于训练算法需要大量目标动物的标注图像，我们测试了引入辅助数据以改进检测模型的方法，并使用不同比例的辅助数据对 YOLOv10 框架进行了微调。

The improvement of these automatic detection techniques contributes to conservation efforts, by providing automatic tools to monitor solutions that minimize the impact of human interference in animals' habitats.

这些自动检测技术的改进有助于保护工作，通过提供自动化工具来监测那些旨在最大限度减少人类干扰对动物栖息地影响的解决方案。
