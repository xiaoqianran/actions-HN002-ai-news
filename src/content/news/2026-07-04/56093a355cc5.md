# From Approximation to Emergence: A Theory of Deep Learning
# 从逼近到涌现：深度学习理论

**Abstract:** Deep learning has outgrown any single mathematical explanation. *From Approximation to Emergence* develops a unified, proof-oriented account of modern deep learning theory, tracing a path from the classical foundations of approximation, optimization, and generalization to the contemporary mechanisms of overparameterization, robustness, generative modeling, transformers, in-context learning, scaling laws, interpretability, alignment, and emergence.

**摘要：** 深度学习的发展已经超越了任何单一的数学解释。《从逼近到涌现》（*From Approximation to Emergence*）一书为现代深度学习理论构建了一个统一的、以证明为导向的叙述框架。它追溯了从逼近、优化和泛化等经典基础，到过参数化、鲁棒性、生成式建模、Transformer、上下文学习、缩放定律、可解释性、对齐以及涌现等当代机制的发展路径。

Rather than presenting isolated results, the book organizes a broad literature into a coherent research narrative: each theory is examined through the object it controls, the assumptions that make it valid, and the phenomena it leaves unexplained.

该书并未罗列孤立的研究成果，而是将浩如烟海的文献整理成一个连贯的研究叙事：通过分析每种理论所控制的对象、使其成立的前提假设，以及它尚未解释的现象，对各项理论进行了深入剖析。

Written for researchers, graduate students, and mathematically trained practitioners, this monograph offers a rigorous map of deep learning theory as it stands today: powerful, incomplete, and increasingly centered on the question of how learned mechanisms arise from scale, data, architecture, and training.

本书专为研究人员、研究生以及受过数学训练的从业者编写，为当今的深度学习理论提供了一份严谨的蓝图：它既强大又尚不完整，且日益聚焦于一个核心问题——学习机制是如何从规模、数据、架构和训练中涌现出来的。
