# A Novel Machine Learning Approach for Central Nervous System Tumor Classification from DNA Methylation
# 一种基于DNA甲基化的中枢神经系统肿瘤分类机器学习新方法

DNA methylation profiling has become a powerful approach for central nervous system (CNS) tumor classification, yet important challenges remain regarding cross-cohort transferability, methodological correctness, and robust multiclass evaluation.
DNA甲基化分析已成为中枢神经系统（CNS）肿瘤分类的一种强大手段，但在跨队列迁移能力、方法论的严谨性以及稳健的多分类评估方面，仍存在重要挑战。

In this work, we propose a novel and methodologically rigorous machine-learning approach for methylation-based CNS tumor classification that combines Sparse Random Projection for dimensionality reduction with multinomial logistic regression for classification.
在这项工作中，我们提出了一种基于甲基化的CNS肿瘤分类的新型且方法论严谨的机器学习方法，该方法结合了用于降维的稀疏随机投影（Sparse Random Projection）和用于分类的多项逻辑回归（Multinomial Logistic Regression）。

We evaluate the proposed approach in the same general experimental setting established by a widely used reference classifier. On the 2,801-sample reference cohort, our method achieves a mean accuracy of 96% under stratified 3-fold cross-validation.
我们在一个广泛使用的参考分类器所建立的相同通用实验环境下对所提方法进行了评估。在包含2,801个样本的参考队列中，我们的方法在分层3折交叉验证下达到了96%的平均准确率。

On the independent 1,104-sample clinical evaluation cohort, it reaches 86% accuracy at the 91-class level and 93% when predictions are evaluated at the methylation class family level.
在包含1,104个样本的独立临床评估队列中，该方法在91个类别水平上的准确率为86%，而在甲基化类别家族水平上评估预测结果时，准确率达到了93%。

These results improve upon the corresponding state-of-the-art reference figures of 82% class-level concordance and 88% family-level concordance, yielding absolute gains of approximately 4 and 5 percentage points, respectively.
这些结果优于目前最先进的参考数据（即82%的类别水平一致性和88%的家族水平一致性），分别实现了约4个和5个百分点的绝对提升。

This improvement is clinically relevant: in a diagnostic setting, a 5-point increase in correct tumor classification can directly affect cancer subtype assignment and, in turn, influence treatment selection and downstream clinical decision-making.
这种改进具有临床意义：在诊断环境中，肿瘤分类准确率提高5个百分点，可以直接影响癌症亚型的判定，进而影响治疗方案的选择及后续的临床决策。

Our results show that the proposed model, grounded in stronger methodological practice in machine learning, consistently outperforms the previous state of the art across evaluation settings and can materially improve the reliability of CNS tumor classification.
我们的研究结果表明，该模型基于更严谨的机器学习方法论实践，在各项评估设置中均持续优于现有的最先进技术，并能实质性地提高CNS肿瘤分类的可靠性。
