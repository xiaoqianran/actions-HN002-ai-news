# Redesign Work Before You Add More AI Agents
# 在增加更多 AI 智能体之前，先重塑工作流程

**Agentic AI: Redesign Work Before You Add More AI Agents. Map AI value, design workflows, redefine talent, upgrade the executive team, and measure the business impact.**
**智能体 AI：在增加更多 AI 智能体之前，先重塑工作流程。规划 AI 价值、设计工作流、重新定义人才、升级管理团队并衡量业务影响。**

### 1. AI Everywhere. Workflows Untouched.
### 1. AI 无处不在，工作流却纹丝不动

While working on Agentic AI workflow enablement across enterprise teams, I kept seeing the same problem. Teams told me they were excited about AI. Then I watched their day-to-day: a workflow pulling from different data sources, multiple rounds of Excel copy-paste, and manual handoffs before anything reached a checkpoint. The most important intelligence rarely made it into any system. It stayed in people’s heads, undocumented, scattered across emails, slide decks, and chat threads. People were using AI, but the work itself still ran the old way.
在为企业团队赋能智能体 AI 工作流的过程中，我不断发现同一个问题。团队告诉我他们对 AI 很兴奋，但我观察他们的日常工作时却发现：工作流涉及从不同数据源提取信息、多轮 Excel 复制粘贴，以及在到达检查点之前的各种手动交接。最重要的情报很少进入任何系统，而是留存在人们的脑海中，没有记录，散落在电子邮件、幻灯片和聊天记录里。人们虽然在使用 AI，但工作本身仍以旧方式运行。

I kept hearing the same proposed solution too. When teams talk about AI, someone says: let’s put a nice chatbot on top of our files and data, and everything will work out. I have spent weeks sitting down with teams to do a deep dive on their current processes. It is slow, sometimes quite painful, and it is the necessary step that unlocks the AI value. And I am certain these cases are far from unique.
我也一直听到同样的解决方案。当团队谈论 AI 时，总有人说：“让我们在文件和数据之上放一个好用的聊天机器人，一切就都解决了。”我花了数周时间与团队深入探讨他们当前的流程。这个过程很慢，有时甚至很痛苦，但这是释放 AI 价值的必要步骤。我确信这些案例绝非个例。

People love to discuss AI adoption at a high level. But what I want to share may sound hard to hear for a lot of people. AI becomes business value only when it reaches your products and business processes. The truth is that before you introduce more AI tools and agents, you need to redesign and improve the workflow.
人们喜欢在高层面上讨论 AI 的应用。但我接下来要分享的内容对许多人来说可能并不中听：只有当 AI 触及你的产品和业务流程时，它才能转化为商业价值。事实是，在引入更多 AI 工具和智能体之前，你需要先重塑并改进工作流程。

### 2. Start with Your Business Value
### 2. 从业务价值出发

The right starting point of an AI enablement strategy is identifying where AI can create disproportionate advantage in cost, growth, innovation, or business model expansion. BCG’s 2026 AI Radar shows companies expect AI spending to roughly double in 2026, and nearly three quarters of CEOs say they are the main AI decision maker in their organization. The same research says nearly all CEOs believe AI agents will produce measurable returns in 2026.
AI 赋能战略的正确起点，是识别 AI 在成本、增长、创新或商业模式扩展方面能创造超额优势的领域。BCG 的《2026 AI 雷达》显示，企业预计 2026 年的 AI 支出将翻倍，近四分之三的 CEO 表示他们是组织内 AI 的主要决策者。同一项研究指出，几乎所有 CEO 都相信 AI 智能体将在 2026 年产生可衡量的回报。

Johnson & Johnson’s experience is the strongest case here. Broad experimentation across nearly 900 use cases helped the company learn, but the impact came from narrowing resources toward the 10% to 15% of initiatives producing most of the value. Then prioritize by asking the team one question: which 10% of our AI work could create 80% of the business value?
强生公司的经验是最好的例证。在近 900 个用例中进行广泛实验帮助公司积累了经验，但真正的成效来自于将资源集中在产生大部分价值的那 10% 到 15% 的项目上。因此，请通过问团队一个问题来确定优先级：我们 10% 的 AI 工作中，哪一部分能创造 80% 的业务价值？

### 3. Redesign the Work: From Individual Role to Human-Agent System
### 3. 重塑工作：从个人角色到“人机协作系统”

The old question was: who is the right person for this role? The better question is: which parts of this workflow should be owned by people, which parts should be handled by agents, and where does human judgment need to stay in control? McKinsey’s agents for growth article shows organizations create more value when agents improve end-to-end processes instead of isolated tasks.
过去的问题是：“谁是这个职位的合适人选？”更好的问题是：“工作流的哪些部分应由人负责，哪些部分应由智能体处理，以及在哪些环节需要保持人类的判断力？”麦肯锡关于“增长智能体”的文章表明，当智能体改进端到端流程而非孤立任务时，组织能创造更多价值。

Microsoft’s 2026 WTI reaches a similar conclusion: advanced AI users are already using agents for multi-step workflows and rethinking how work gets done. A customer service agent that only helps employees write faster replies improves one step. A better workflow predicts issues, triggers outreach, routes exceptions to humans, and closes the loop with personalized resolution. That is a system design decision, and no AI tool deployment makes it for you.
微软的《2026 工作趋势指数》(WTI) 得出了类似的结论：先进的 AI 用户已经在利用智能体处理多步骤工作流，并重新思考工作完成的方式。一个仅能帮助员工更快回复邮件的客服智能体，只能改进一个步骤。而更好的工作流能够预测问题、触发主动沟通、将异常情况转交给人工，并以个性化的解决方案闭环。这属于系统设计决策，没有任何 AI 工具部署能替你完成。

### 4. Redefine Talent: AI Super Users Are Workflow Designers
### 4. 重新定义人才：AI 超级用户即工作流设计师

The most valuable employees in the AI workplace are not always the people writing the most prompts. They are the people who can focus on the right problem, explain the current process, identify weak handoffs, implement and test AI solutions, and make the improved work scalable for others. PwC’s 2026 Global AI Jobs Barometer shows why this talent standard is rising. Jobs requiring AI skills are growing almost eight times faster than the overall job market, and the average wage premium for AI skills has reached 62%.
在 AI 职场中，最有价值的员工并不总是那些写提示词最多的人。他们是那些能够聚焦正确问题、解释当前流程、识别薄弱交接环节、实施并测试 AI 解决方案，并将改进后的工作推广给他人的人。普华永道的《2026 全球 AI 就业晴雨表》揭示了这一人才标准为何在提升：要求 AI 技能的岗位增长速度几乎是整体就业市场的八倍，而 AI 技能的平均薪资溢价已达到 62%。

So find your existing AI super users. Give them a workflow mandate instead of a side project. Ask them to document how they work, where AI helped, where it failed, and what other teams can reuse. The opportunity is significantly larger than individual productivity. The best AI super users can help the company redesign how work gets done.
所以，找出你现有的 AI 超级用户。给他们分配工作流重塑的任务，而不是仅仅当作副业。要求他们记录工作方式、AI 在哪里发挥了作用、在哪里失败了，以及其他团队可以复用什么。这带来的机遇远超个人生产力的提升。最优秀的 AI 超级用户能够帮助公司重塑工作完成的方式。

### 5. Educate the Executive Team Before Scaling More AI Agents
### 5. 在扩展更多 AI 智能体之前，先培训管理团队

While senior leaders sponsor a lot of AI pilots, they might not have an aligned approach to decide which work deserves AI agents, which talent should be reassigned, and which business metrics they need to measure impact. This is now a CEO-level operating issue. BCG’s AI Radar shows 72% of CEOs say they are the main AI decision maker, and half believe their job depends on getting AI right.
虽然高层领导赞助了许多 AI 试点项目，但他们可能尚未就如何决定哪些工作值得引入 AI 智能体、哪些人才需要重新分配，以及需要衡量哪些业务指标来评估影响达成一致。这现在已成为 CEO 级别的运营问题。BCG 的《AI 雷达》显示，72% 的 CEO 表示他们是 AI 的主要决策者，半数 CEO 认为他们的职业前景取决于能否正确应用 AI。
