# Inside the Subspace Where Spurious Correlations Are Born
# 探秘虚假相关性诞生的子空间

**Data Science: Why small samples can produce large correlations by chance, and why large does not always mean meaningful**
**数据科学：为什么小样本会偶然产生大相关性，以及为什么“大”并不总是意味着“有意义”**

Suppose the relationship between cholesterol and blood pressure is measured in 10 patients. The observed correlation is 0.62. Is this enough evidence that a relationship exists?
假设在 10 名患者中测量了胆固醇和血压之间的关系，观察到的相关系数为 0.62。这是否足以证明两者之间存在关系？

Studies that measure 20,000 gene expression levels in 10 mice routinely apply multiple-testing corrections and stringent correlation thresholds. Researchers know that the more correlations they examine, the more likely they are to observe large values purely by chance, even when the genes are completely unrelated.
在对 10 只小鼠进行 20,000 个基因表达水平测量的研究中，通常会应用多重假设检验校正和严格的相关性阈值。研究人员知道，他们检查的相关性越多，就越有可能纯粹出于偶然观察到较大的数值，即使这些基因之间完全无关。

With small studies, researchers often assume they are safer from spurious correlations. Are they? The distribution of a sample correlation is the same regardless of the total number of variables measured. It depends primarily on the number of subjects. High-dimensional datasets do not create spurious correlations—they simply make them easier to encounter.
在小规模研究中，研究人员通常认为他们更不容易受到虚假相关性的影响。真的是这样吗？无论测量的变量总数是多少，样本相关性的分布都是相同的。它主要取决于受试者的数量。高维数据集并不会创造虚假相关性——它们只是让人们更容易遇到这种情况。

This article answers the question: what correlation values should we expect when the variables are independent and the true population correlation is zero? It also explains why such correlations occur. Using the geometry of Pearson’s correlation coefficient, the article visualizes the effects of centering and normalization. It then discusses the role of rotational invariance. Together, these ideas build intuition for the exact distribution of Pearson’s correlation coefficient and its asymptotic normal approximation.
本文回答了这样一个问题：当变量相互独立且真实的总体相关性为零时，我们应该预期什么样的相关系数值？它还解释了为什么会出现这种相关性。通过利用皮尔逊相关系数的几何特性，本文可视化了中心化和归一化的影响，并讨论了旋转不变性的作用。这些概念共同为理解皮尔逊相关系数的精确分布及其渐近正态近似建立了直觉。

Finally, the article offers practical guidance on how to use this intuition when reading and conducting research. The code to reproduce all figures and experiments is available in the accompanying notebook.

### The Gaussian Experiment
### 高斯实验

Imagine measuring $d$ variables on $n$ individuals. To study correlations that arise purely by chance, consider a simulated dataset whose columns are independent Gaussian random vectors of dimension $n$.
想象一下在 $n$ 个个体上测量 $d$ 个变量。为了研究纯粹出于偶然产生的相关性，我们考虑一个模拟数据集，其列是维度为 $n$ 的独立高斯随机向量。

What is the sample correlation between two vectors? Even though the population correlation between any pair of variables is exactly zero, the sample correlations we observe are almost always nonzero. This raises a question: when the columns are truly independent, what values should we expect for the sample correlation?
两个向量之间的样本相关性是多少？尽管任意一对变量之间的总体相关性恰好为零，但我们观察到的样本相关性几乎总是非零的。这就提出了一个问题：当各列确实相互独立时，我们应该预期样本相关性会出现什么值？

The intuition behind its derivation becomes clearer by understanding how Pearson’s correlation coefficient transforms the data.
通过理解皮尔逊相关系数如何转换数据，其推导背后的直觉会变得更加清晰。

#### Step 1: centering moves vectors onto a hyperplane
#### 第一步：中心化将向量移动到超平面上

The numerator is effectively centering the vectors. Centering subtracts the sample mean from each column. After centering, the coordinates of every column sum to 0. This means every centered column now lies in the $(n-1)$-dimensional subspace perpendicular to the vector of ones. The vectors remain $n$-dimensional, but after centering they are restricted to an $(n-1)$-dimensional hyperplane. They no longer vary in the direction of the all-ones vector.
分子实际上是对向量进行了中心化。中心化是从每一列中减去样本均值。中心化后，每一列的坐标之和为 0。这意味着每个中心化后的列现在都位于垂直于全 1 向量的 $(n-1)$ 维子空间中。这些向量仍然是 $n$ 维的，但在中心化后，它们被限制在一个 $(n-1)$ 维的超平面上。它们不再在全 1 向量的方向上变化。

#### Step 2: normalizing moves vectors onto the unit sphere
#### 第二步：归一化将向量移动到单位球面上

Notice that Pearson’s correlation is exactly the cosine of the angle between two centered vectors. The distribution of the sample correlation between two independent random vectors is therefore the same as the distribution of the cosine of the angle between their centered versions. When the centered columns are normalized, the correlation matrix simplifies. Centering moved the vectors onto an $(n-1)$-dimensional hyperplane, removing information about location. Normalizing removes differences in length, removing information about scale.
请注意，皮尔逊相关系数恰好是两个中心化向量之间夹角的余弦值。因此，两个独立随机向量之间的样本相关性分布，与它们中心化版本之间夹角的余弦分布相同。当中心化后的列被归一化时，相关矩阵会简化。中心化将向量移动到了 $(n-1)$ 维超平面上，消除了关于位置的信息。归一化消除了长度上的差异，消除了关于尺度（规模）的信息。

#### Step 3: Gaussianity allows the derivation of the exact distribution
#### 第三步：高斯性允许推导精确分布

The standard multivariate normal distribution is rotationally invariant. This means that the probability density of a random vector depends only on its distance from the origin, not on its direction. Conditional on a radius, directions are uniformly distributed on a sphere. Therefore, after normalization to unit length, the vectors become uniformly distributed on the unit sphere. Assuming the vectors are independent and Gaussian, the exact sampling distribution of the correlation $C$ can be derived by fixing one direction and measuring the surface area of all points on the sphere.
标准多元正态分布具有旋转不变性。这意味着随机向量的概率密度仅取决于其到原点的距离，而不取决于其方向。在给定半径的条件下，方向在球面上均匀分布。因此，在归一化为单位长度后，这些向量在单位球面上均匀分布。假设向量是独立且服从高斯分布的，通过固定一个方向并测量球面上所有点的表面积，可以推导出相关系数 $C$ 的精确抽样分布。
