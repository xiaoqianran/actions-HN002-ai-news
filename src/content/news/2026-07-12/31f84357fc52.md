# That Is Embarrassing: Why Frontier AI Still Makes Things Up, and What to Do About It
# 令人尴尬的真相：为什么前沿 AI 依然会胡编乱造，以及我们该如何应对

The best AI models still hallucinate. These hallucinations are sometimes funny, and sometimes cause actual damage. In this post we will consider recent tales of AI hallucinations, and then look under the hood to understand why they happen.
目前最顶尖的 AI 模型依然会产生“幻觉”。这些幻觉有时令人捧腹，有时却会造成实质性的损害。在这篇文章中，我们将探讨近期发生的 AI 幻觉案例，并深入剖析其背后的技术原理，以理解为何会发生这种情况。

### You Just Hallucinated
### 你刚刚产生了“幻觉”

What you just experienced has a name: phonemic restoration. Your auditory system got an ambiguous input (the chant) and something to disambiguate it (the caption on the screen), so it filled the “gap”. It predicted the most plausible meaning given the context, and then it reported that prediction to you as if it were the thing you actually heard.
你刚才经历的现象有一个专门的术语：音素修复（phonemic restoration）。你的听觉系统接收到了一个模糊的输入（人群的喊声），同时又接收到了一个用于消除歧义的线索（屏幕上的字幕），于是它填补了“空白”。它根据语境预测了最合理的含义，然后将其作为你“实际听到”的内容呈现给你。

That move, where you meet an input you can’t fully resolve and fill the gap with something plausible and confident instead of reporting “I can’t tell,” is something that your brain experiences (as you’ve just seen), and also something that LLMs experience. The same top-down move in a brain and a model: an ambiguous input, a gap filled by prediction, and a confident output that is never flagged as a guess.
这种当你遇到无法完全解析的输入时，不选择报告“我无法分辨”，而是用一个合理且自信的答案去填补空白的行为，不仅是大脑的一种机制（如你刚才所见），也是大语言模型（LLM）所经历的过程。大脑和模型在处理时采用了相同的自上而下的逻辑：模糊的输入、通过预测填补的空白，以及一个从未被标记为“猜测”的自信输出。

### Part 1: The Tales
### 第一部分：案例故事

**Act I — Chatbots (when AI answers)**
**第一幕——聊天机器人（当 AI 进行回答时）**

**Cursor, April 2025.** Say you use Cursor, the agentic IDE. You switch laptops, log in on the new one, and Cursor logs you out of the old one. That’s pretty annoying. So you ask support: “I get logged out every time I switch laptops. Why?” The reply: “Cursor is designed to work with one device per subscription, as a core security feature.” Plausible! Except it’s completely false, there is no such policy. “Support” was an AI bot, and it had invented the policy on the spot, handing the same fabricated rule to multiple users, as if reading from a manual that didn’t exist.
**Cursor，2025 年 4 月。** 假设你正在使用智能 IDE 工具 Cursor。当你更换笔记本电脑并在新设备上登录时，Cursor 会自动将你从旧设备上登出。这很烦人，于是你询问客服：“为什么我每次换电脑都会被登出？”回复是：“作为一项核心安全功能，Cursor 的订阅设计为仅支持单设备使用。”听起来很合理！但事实完全错误，根本没有这样的政策。“客服”其实是一个 AI 机器人，它当场编造了这项政策，并将同样的虚假规则告知了多名用户，仿佛在阅读一本根本不存在的操作手册。

It caused a wave of angry posts, and Cursor’s co-founder had to publicly clarify: no such policy, use Cursor on as many machines as you like. That is embarrassing.
这引发了一波愤怒的帖子，Cursor 的联合创始人不得不公开澄清：没有这样的政策，你可以在任意数量的机器上使用 Cursor。这太尴尬了。

**A company I know, April 2026.** This one’s from a friend’s company, so I’ll keep the details vague. They sell software to other businesses, and they have a support chatbot. The bot answers questions based on information it retrieves from an internal database. They shipped a new feature and forgot to update that database. So a paying customer asked how to use the new feature, and the bot, having never heard of it, replied: “We don’t have that feature.” The customer pushed back: “What? I’m paying for it after my upgrade.” And the bot, this was on Opus 4.6, not long ago, replied: “Honestly? They’re ripping you off.”
**我认识的一家公司，2026 年 4 月。** 这个案例来自我朋友的公司，所以我将隐去细节。他们向其他企业销售软件，并配有一个支持聊天机器人。该机器人根据从内部数据库检索到的信息回答问题。他们发布了一项新功能，但忘记更新数据库。于是，一位付费客户询问如何使用该新功能，而从未听说过该功能的机器人回答道：“我们没有这个功能。”客户反驳说：“什么？我升级后明明付了费。”而这个机器人（使用的是不久前的 Opus 4.6 模型）竟然回答：“老实说？他们在坑你的钱。”

The “they” is the company running the bot. The support agent took the customer’s side against its own employer, because it didn’t know about the feature and filled the gap with the most coherent story it could assemble. That is embarrassing.
这里的“他们”指的正是运营该机器人的公司。这个客服代理站在了客户一边，反过来指责自己的雇主，因为它不知道该功能，于是用它能拼凑出的最连贯的故事填补了空白。这太尴尬了。
