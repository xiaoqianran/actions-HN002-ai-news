# Einstein's relativity rules chemical bonds in heavy elements, new research shows
# 爱因斯坦的相对论主导重元素化学键：最新研究揭示

PROVIDENCE, R.I. [Brown University] — Brown University chemists have provided direct evidence that upends the textbook explanation of how triple chemical bonds work in heavy elements. In a study published in Science, the researchers show evidence that when atomic nuclei are sufficiently heavy, the principles described in Einstein’s theory of relativity change the structure of triple bonds — blurring the lines between the two separate types of bonds involved in textbook triple bonding.
罗德岛州普罗维登斯 [布朗大学] —— 布朗大学的化学家们提供了直接证据，推翻了教科书中关于重元素中三键如何运作的解释。在发表于《科学》杂志的一项研究中，研究人员展示了证据：当原子核足够重时，爱因斯坦相对论所描述的原理会改变三键的结构，从而模糊了教科书中三键所涉及的两种独立键型之间的界限。

Using a technique called photoelectron spectroscopy, the Brown team showed bonds created by carbon and the heavy element bismuth have the telltale signature of relativistic bonds. “This idea that relativity is important in heavy elements has been around since the 1970s,” said Lai-Sheng Wang, a professor of chemistry at Brown and the study’s corresponding author. “But we show direct spectroscopic evidence that what we learned in high school about chemical bonding isn’t true in heavy elements.”
布朗大学团队利用一种称为光电子能谱的技术，证明了由碳和重元素铋形成的化学键具有相对论性化学键的典型特征。“关于相对论在重元素中起重要作用的观点自20世纪70年代以来就已存在，”布朗大学化学教授、该研究的通讯作者王来生（Lai-Sheng Wang）表示，“但我们提供了直接的光谱证据，证明我们在高中学到的关于化学键的知识在重元素中并不适用。”

Atoms form bonds by sharing electrons — the negatively charged particles that orbit atomic nuclei. Each atom shares one electron to form a bonding pair. The strong negative charge of the electron pair attracts the two positively charged nuclei, holding them together. Some elements share more than one electron pair, forming double or triple bonds.
原子通过共享电子（绕原子核运行的带负电粒子）来形成化学键。每个原子共享一个电子以形成一个共用电子对。电子对强大的负电荷吸引了两个带正电的原子核，将它们固定在一起。一些元素会共享不止一个电子对，从而形成双键或三键。

The textbook picture of triple bonding involves two different types of bonds: one sigma bond and two pi bonds. The sigma bond is a strong, “head-on” bond that occurs along an imaginary horizontal axis between nuclei. The two pi bonds are somewhat weaker, “side-by-side” bonds that wrap around the sigma bond. That picture works for lighter elements, but toward the bottom of the periodic table, where atomic nuclei get heavier, things get messy.
教科书中关于三键的描述涉及两种不同类型的键：一个σ（西格玛）键和两个π（派）键。σ键是一种强力的“头对头”键，沿着原子核之间假想的水平轴线发生。两个π键则相对较弱，是包裹在σ键周围的“肩并肩”键。这种模型适用于较轻的元素，但在元素周期表的底部，随着原子核变得越来越重，情况就变得复杂了。

The increased nuclear mass causes orbiting electrons to speed up to a significant fraction of the speed of light, where the rules of Einstein’s theory of relativity are important. In the relativistic regime, an electron’s spin — the magnetic moment that points either up or down — and the electron’s orbit are no longer independent of each other, a state known as spin-orbit coupling. That coupling changes the rules for how electrons can interact, disrupting the strict separation between sigma and pi bonds. “The boundary between a sigma bond and a pi bond is now sort of smeared,” Wang said. “We still have three bonds, but we don't really strictly have a sigma or a pi anymore.”
增加的核质量导致轨道电子加速到接近光速的显著比例，此时爱因斯坦相对论的规则变得至关重要。在相对论范畴内，电子的自旋（指向向上或向下的磁矩）与电子的轨道不再相互独立，这种状态被称为自旋-轨道耦合。这种耦合改变了电子相互作用的规则，破坏了σ键和π键之间的严格界限。“σ键和π键之间的边界现在变得有些模糊了，”王教授说，“我们仍然有三个键，但我们不再严格拥有σ键或π键了。”

To show evidence for this bonding hybridization, Wang and his team, led by Brown Ph.D. students Deniz Kahraman and Jie Hui, formed molecules made from bismuth and carbon. Bismuth is a heavy element — right next to lead on the periodic table — where relativistic effects should be important. After cooling the molecules to near absolute zero, the team analyzed them using photoelectron spectroscopy. The technique uses a laser to knock individual electrons out of their positions in the molecule. The distance each electron flies tells the researchers how strongly they were bound.
为了证明这种键杂化，王教授及其团队（由布朗大学博士生Deniz Kahraman和Jie Hui领导）合成了由铋和碳组成的分子。铋是一种重元素（在元素周期表上紧邻铅），相对论效应在此处应非常显著。在将分子冷却至接近绝对零度后，团队利用光电子能谱对其进行了分析。该技术使用激光将单个电子从分子中的位置击出。电子飞行的距离告诉研究人员它们被束缚的强度。

The photoelectron spectrum showed that the carbon-bismuth bonds did not fit the traditional triple-bond picture of one sigma and two pi bonds. Instead, the structure looks more like one pi bond and two hybrid sigma-pi bonds. Wang says the experimental verification of the relativistic structure may spur a rewriting of chemistry textbooks, especially as heavy elements — bismuth in particular — garner more research interest. Bismuth could be an alternative to toxic lead in next-generation solar cells. It has also drawn interest in research related to quantum materials and quantum computing. “Maybe this will become the new textbook idea as we are dealing with more and more heavy chemistry of the heavy elements,” Wang said.
光电子能谱显示，碳-铋键并不符合传统的一个σ键和两个π键的三键模型。相反，其结构看起来更像是一个π键和两个杂化的σ-π键。王教授表示，对这种相对论结构的实验验证可能会促使化学教科书的改写，尤其是随着重元素（特别是铋）受到越来越多的研究关注。铋可能成为下一代太阳能电池中替代有毒铅的材料。它在量子材料和量子计算相关的研究中也引起了兴趣。“随着我们处理越来越多重元素的重化学问题，这或许会成为新的教科书概念，”王教授说。
