# How I’m Making Sure My Analytics Career Doesn’t Get Eaten by AI
# 我是如何确保自己的数据分析职业生涯不被 AI 取代的

The analytics career I signed up for five years ago doesn't exist anymore, and honestly, I am fine with that.
五年前我所投身的数据分析职业已经不复存在了，老实说，我对此并不介意。

Every few weeks, someone at work brings up a version of this question: is AI going to take my job? I’ll admit that I have asked some version of that same question myself. But having talked to the AI experts, the creators of some of these AI agents, having seen the evolution of AI, and after actually integrating AI into how I work, the question if AI is going to take my job no longer scares me. I am just more curious and a lot more deliberate about what I spend my time learning.
每隔几周，同事们就会提出类似的问题：AI 会取代我的工作吗？我承认自己也曾问过类似的问题。但在与 AI 专家和 AI 智能体开发者交流，见证了 AI 的演进，并真正将 AI 融入工作流程后，这个问题不再让我感到恐惧。我反而变得更加好奇，也更加审慎地规划自己学习的内容。

When I started my analytics job in 2021, I thought writing SQL or Python code and building dashboards were valuable skills, and they really were. I quickly realized that translating a messy business problem into a data problem, and then surfacing insights that actually made sense to people is the real skill I should hone on. But now with the AI boom, I don’t know for how long I can even call that my strength.
2021 年我刚开始从事数据分析工作时，认为编写 SQL 或 Python 代码以及构建仪表板是核心价值技能，它们确实如此。我很快意识到，将复杂的业务问题转化为数据问题，并挖掘出对人们真正有意义的洞察，才是真正值得磨练的技能。但随着 AI 的爆发，我不知道自己还能将此视为核心优势多久。

When ChatGPT became a household conversation in 2022, I had a feeling that AI is overrated in the short-term and underrated in the long term and I feel this has become more and more true. The industry is moving faster than most of us can admit, and not even the people building these systems know exactly where it’s heading.
当 ChatGPT 在 2022 年成为家喻户晓的话题时，我就有一种感觉：AI 在短期内被高估了，但在长期内被低估了，而且我觉得这一点正变得越来越真实。行业的发展速度超出了大多数人的认知，甚至连构建这些系统的开发者都无法确定其最终走向。

AI tools are getting better every month at absorbing the kind of knowledge that used to live only in the heads of senior people, like the business context you’d normally only pick up after several years on the job. When that knowledge gets documented and handed to an AI system, it becomes available to anyone who needs it, rather than residing in the heads of the subject matter experts.
AI 工具每个月都在进步，它们能够吸收那些曾经只存在于资深员工脑海中的知识，比如通常需要工作多年才能积累的业务背景。当这些知识被记录并输入 AI 系统后，任何需要的人都能获取，而不再仅仅局限于领域专家。

When tribal knowledge gets written down, the lines between roles blur. A data analyst is expected to take on a data engineer’s scope. A software engineer can interpret an A/B test result—a task that used to sit squarely with a data scientist. With the help of AI agents, someone with no technical background at all can produce a dashboard that, five years ago, would have taken a trained analyst a full afternoon.
当这些“部落知识”被文档化后，岗位之间的界限就模糊了。数据分析师被要求承担数据工程师的工作范畴；软件工程师可以解读 A/B 测试结果——这曾经是数据科学家的专属任务。在 AI 智能体的帮助下，一个完全没有技术背景的人也能制作出仪表板，而这在五年前需要一名训练有素的分析师花费整整一个下午。

I watched this happen so closely just last week: a scrum master needed to combine project delivery data from two platforms and, with help from Copilot, he was able to design a data pipeline and build a working Power BI dashboard without relying on a data analyst for the foundational work. By the time I was brought in, he only needed help automating the process and enhancing the storytelling.
上周我就亲眼目睹了这一幕：一位 Scrum Master 需要合并两个平台的项目交付数据，在 Copilot 的帮助下，他设计了数据流水线并构建了一个可用的 Power BI 仪表板，完全无需依赖数据分析师来完成基础工作。当我介入时，他只需要我在自动化流程和增强数据叙事方面提供帮助。

This could be a normal Tuesday for anyone but for me, it was a reminder that AI is rapidly blurring the lines between roles, making many technical skills broadly accessible. None of this means analytics is going away. It simply indicates that the barriers to execution are falling down and our value will increasingly come from judgment, context, influence, and the ability to turn information into meaningful decisions.
对别人来说这可能只是平常的周二，但对我而言，这提醒我 AI 正在迅速模糊岗位界限，使许多技术技能变得触手可及。但这并不意味着数据分析行业会消失。它只是表明执行门槛正在降低，我们的价值将越来越多地源于判断力、业务背景、影响力，以及将信息转化为有意义决策的能力。

My educated guess is that in the next five years, the straight line career progression from data analyst to senior analyst to principal analyst may not exist in the shape we know it today. The traditional entry-level role of writing queries, building dashboards, running reports probably will demand much more than that. What we will see instead are hybrid roles, sitting at the intersection of AI, business, data analytics, and software engineering.
我的推测是，未来五年内，从数据分析师到高级分析师再到首席分析师的线性职业晋升路径，可能不再以我们今天所知的形式存在。传统的入门级角色（编写查询、构建仪表板、运行报告）可能需要具备远超于此的能力。取而代之的将是混合型角色，处于 AI、业务、数据分析和软件工程的交叉点。

I’ve stopped treating query-writing, chart-building, and report-generating as my entire value proposition. AI is enabling a lot of people to do that work themselves, without needing me in the process. If that’s all I offer, I’m quietly competing with the tool instead of using it. With that understanding, I’m working to grow myself even more at the intersection of business knowledge, analytical judgment, and AI system design.
我已经不再将编写查询、制作图表和生成报告视为我的全部价值主张。AI 正在让许多人能够独立完成这些工作，而无需我的参与。如果我提供的仅限于此，我就是在与工具竞争，而不是在使用它。基于这种理解，我正致力于在业务知识、分析判断力和 AI 系统设计这三个领域的交叉点上提升自己。

I’m trying to understand how the systems actually work: how AI agents reason, how to structure context for them, how to build the connective tissue between AI and my data. This will soon no longer be a nice-to-have knowledge, but a staple in an analyst toolkit.
我正在努力理解这些系统是如何运作的：AI 智能体如何推理，如何为它们构建上下文，以及如何建立 AI 与我的数据之间的连接。这很快将不再是“锦上添花”的知识，而是分析师工具箱中的必备技能。

**Double down on the judgment**
**加倍提升判断力**

AI still struggles to replicate for things like:
AI 在以下方面仍难以替代人类：
* Knowing when AI is quietly lying to you by making up insights (知道 AI 何时在悄悄编造洞察来欺骗你)
* Spotting survivorship bias before it shapes a decision (在幸存者偏差影响决策前将其识别出来)
* Holding the line between correlation and causation (区分相关性与因果关系)
* Catching your own confirmation bias before it catches you (在确认偏误影响你之前将其捕捉)
* Telling the difference between an observation and an actual insight (区分观察结果与真正的洞察)
* Negotiating what a metric should even mean in the first place, before I start measuring it (在开始衡量指标之前，先界定该指标的真正含义)

I’m also continuing to build on human skills. I love to read about cognitive science and how humans adapt to change, and I’ve learned that human (soft) skills don’t get commoditized the way a SQL query does. They require sitting with ambiguity, understanding a business well enough to know what a number should look like before you’ve even seen it.
我也在继续培养人类特有的技能。我喜欢阅读认知科学以及人类如何适应变化的内容，我了解到人类的“软技能”不会像 SQL 查询那样被商品化。它们需要你能够与模糊性共处，并对业务有足够的理解，以至于在看到数据之前就能预判出数字应该呈现的样子。

Also, hard skills get you the job but soft skills get you the promotion, so that’s where I’m putting a lot of my energy right now. I’m trying to build a strong sense of judgment into systems that scale, rather than keeping it locked away in your own head, you end up with something genuinely valuable.
此外，硬技能让你获得工作，但软技能让你获得晋升，所以我现在投入了大量精力在软技能上。我试图将强大的判断力融入可扩展的系统中，而不是将其锁在自己的脑海里，这样你最终才能创造出真正有价值的东西。

I’ve started using AI agents across three levels of work: execution, optics, and impact. With the correct prompting, I am trying to get AI to accelerate execution by automating research, analysis, and content creation, while improving optics by turning work into clear, compelling narratives for stakeholders. The result of this effort has allowed me to effectively communicate the business impact and provide better visibility into the value being created.
我已经开始在三个工作层面使用 AI 智能体：执行、呈现和影响力。通过正确的提示词（Prompting），我试图让 AI 通过自动化研究、分析和内容创作来加速执行，同时通过将工作转化为清晰、引人入胜的叙事来提升呈现效果，从而更好地向利益相关者展示成果。这一努力的结果使我能够有效地传达业务影响，并更清晰地展现所创造的价值。
