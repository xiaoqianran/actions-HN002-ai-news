# Unified Backbone Refinement for Diffusion Models via Internal-Latent Analysis
# 通过内部潜空间分析实现扩散模型的统一骨干网络优化

**Abstract:** Diffusion models have achieved remarkable success across diverse domains, with performance closely related to the denoising backbones that parameterize the score function.
**摘要：** 扩散模型在多个领域取得了显著成就，其性能与参数化分数函数的去噪骨干网络密切相关。

In this paper, we present a systematic, phase-aware analysis of diffusion components and show that abrupt, early-stage fluctuations in deep latents are strongly associated with artifacts.
在本文中，我们对扩散模型的组件进行了系统的、阶段感知的分析，并指出深层潜空间中早期的剧烈波动与生成图像的伪影（artifacts）密切相关。

Guided by these findings, we introduce DUNE (Diffusion Unified Network refiNEr), a training-free refinement framework that detects abrupt deviations in deep low-noise internal latents using a shared EMA-based criterion, and applies backbone-specific suppression to the detector-selected entries.
基于这些发现，我们引入了 DUNE (Diffusion Unified Network refiNEr)，这是一个无需训练的优化框架。它利用基于共享 EMA（指数移动平均）的准则，检测深层低噪声内部潜空间中的剧烈偏差，并对检测器选定的条目应用针对骨干网络的抑制策略。

Although derived from U-Net, the same detect-suppress principle extends naturally to Transformer-based diffusion models by acting on the latents of deep self-attention blocks.
尽管该方法源于 U-Net，但通过作用于深层自注意力模块的潜空间，这一“检测-抑制”原则可以自然地扩展到基于 Transformer 的扩散模型中。

Extensive experiments across multiple backbones indicate that DUNE improves fidelity while reducing hallucinations, offering new insight into where and when diffusion backbones should be controlled.
在多种骨干网络上进行的广泛实验表明，DUNE 在提高生成保真度的同时减少了幻觉现象，为扩散模型骨干网络应在何处及何时进行控制提供了新的见解。
