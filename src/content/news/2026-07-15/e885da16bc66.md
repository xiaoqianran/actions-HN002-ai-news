# Cross-Subject Modeling for Widefield Calcium Imaging via Atlas-Aligned Spatiotemporal Tokenization
# 通过图谱对齐的时空标记化实现宽场钙成像的跨主体建模

**Abstract:** Large-scale, multi-subject widefield calcium imaging provides unprecedented access to brain-wide cortical dynamics. However, the high dimensionality, complex spatiotemporal structure, and substantial task-irrelevant activity in widefield recordings have largely restricted modeling efforts to single-session analyses, limiting scalability and generalization.

**摘要：** 大规模、多主体的宽场钙成像技术为研究全脑皮层动态提供了前所未有的途径。然而，宽场记录中存在的高维度、复杂的时空结构以及大量与任务无关的活动，在很大程度上将建模工作限制在单次会话分析中，从而限制了其可扩展性和泛化能力。

While multi-subject pretrained models have been explored for some neural modalities, multi-subject models for widefield calcium imaging have not yet been demonstrated; further, subject-invariant zero-shot behavior decoding remains elusive for multi-subject models across neural modalities more broadly.

尽管针对某些神经模态的多主体预训练模型已有探索，但针对宽场钙成像的多主体模型尚未得到验证；此外，对于更广泛的神经模态而言，多主体模型实现主体无关的零样本行为解码仍然是一个难题。

As a first step toward foundation modeling of widefield data, we introduce WiCAT, a multi-subject model that leverages self-supervised pretraining to both outperform single-session models and enable zero-shot behavior decoding on unseen subjects.

作为迈向宽场数据基础模型的第一步，我们引入了 WiCAT。这是一个利用自监督预训练的多主体模型，它不仅在性能上优于单次会话模型，还实现了对未见主体（unseen subjects）的零样本行为解码。

WiCAT introduces an atlas-grounded tokenization scheme without session-specific components and learns globally shared spatiotemporal representations. Across multiple widefield datasets, the pretrained model supports lightweight downstream decoding, transfers across subjects, tasks, and datasets, and outperforms baseline models.

WiCAT 引入了一种基于图谱（atlas-grounded）的标记化方案，无需特定于会话的组件，并学习全局共享的时空表征。在多个宽场数据集上，该预训练模型支持轻量级的下游解码，能够跨主体、任务和数据集进行迁移，且性能优于基准模型。

Notably, the model also achieves robust zero-shot continuous behavior decoding and left-out brain region reconstruction on unseen subjects.

值得注意的是，该模型还在未见主体上实现了稳健的零样本连续行为解码以及缺失脑区的重建。
