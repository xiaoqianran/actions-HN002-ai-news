# Active Learning for Efficient Annotation of Surgical Videos with Weak Supervision
# 基于弱监督学习的手术视频高效标注主动学习方法

**Abstract:** Precise spatial-temporal annotation of laparoscopic videos is time-consuming and requires expert knowledge. We propose a human-in-the-loop knowledge acquisition framework that combines active learning with dual-loss optimization to significantly reduce the annotation effort needed for automatic localization and segmentation of objects in the surgical field.

**摘要：** 对腹腔镜视频进行精确的时空标注既耗时又需要专业知识。我们提出了一种人机协同（human-in-the-loop）的知识获取框架，该框架结合了主动学习与双重损失优化，旨在显著降低手术视野中物体自动定位与分割所需的标注工作量。

Our method employs a foundation model to generate temporally consistent class activation maps (CAMs) from video using two complementary training objectives: a weak supervision loss on video-level tool presence labels for weakly annotated data, and an image-level mask loss on human-corrected annotations obtained through active learning.

我们的方法利用基础模型，通过两个互补的训练目标从视频中生成时间一致的类激活图（CAM）：一是针对弱标注数据的视频级工具存在标签的弱监督损失，二是针对通过主动学习获得的人工修正标注的图像级掩码损失。

Rather than requiring dense pixel-level annotation upfront, our pipeline iteratively proposes pseudo-masks that guide the expert annotator to refine the knowledge previously captured by the model. We demonstrate that our framework reduces the effort of surgical video annotation by 50% by the end of training in comparison to fully manual annotation.

我们的流程无需预先进行密集的像素级标注，而是迭代地提出伪掩码，引导专家标注者对模型先前捕获的知识进行精炼。实验证明，与完全人工标注相比，我们的框架在训练结束时可将手术视频标注的工作量减少 50%。

Through eliminating the need for large, fully annotated datasets from the start, this framework enables scalability to the development of surgical tool segmentation models. This iterative human-in-the-loop refinement supports efficient knowledge acquisition with minimal expert input, providing a practical and deployable strategy for expanding tool segmentation to larger, more diverse datasets and real-world clinical settings.

通过消除对大规模全标注数据集的初始需求，该框架增强了手术工具分割模型开发的可扩展性。这种迭代式的人机协同精炼过程以极少的专家投入实现了高效的知识获取，为将工具分割技术扩展到更大、更多样化的数据集及现实临床环境提供了一种实用且可部署的策略。
