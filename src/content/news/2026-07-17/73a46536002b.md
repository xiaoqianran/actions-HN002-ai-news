**TL;DR:** Before AI can take on recurring work, the work itself needs to be clearly defined. That means knowing what the task is, giving AI the right business context, explaining what good work looks like, and deciding when AI can move ahead on its own and when it should ask for human judgment. This article walks through five reusable assets that help teams build workflows AI can support consistently and with confidence.
**摘要：** 在 AI 能够承担重复性工作之前，工作本身必须被清晰定义。这意味着要明确任务内容、为 AI 提供正确的业务背景、解释何为高质量的工作，并决定 AI 何时可以自主推进、何时需要寻求人类判断。本文将介绍五项可复用的资产，帮助团队构建 AI 能够稳定且自信地支持的工作流。

In my last article, *Redesign Work Before You Add More AI Agents*, I argued that companies should start by redesigning the workflow before rolling out more AI tools and agents. You may wonder, if creating more agents is not the first step, where should we start? If a team wants to move beyond individual productivity, isolated pilots, and scattered demos, and make AI part of its product lifecycle and daily operations, what should happen first?
在我的上一篇文章《在增加更多 AI 智能体之前，先重新设计工作流程》中，我提出企业在部署更多 AI 工具和智能体之前，应先重新设计工作流。你可能会问，如果创建更多智能体不是第一步，那我们应该从哪里开始？如果一个团队想要超越个人生产力、孤立的试点项目和零散的演示，将 AI 融入产品生命周期和日常运营中，首先应该做什么？

The more AI workflow enablement projects I work on, the more convinced I become that the missing piece is preparing the workflow itself. Before AI can reliably perform any recurring work, someone has to define what the work is, why it exists, which information is important, what a successful result looks like, and where AI must stop and ask for human judgment.
我参与的 AI 工作流赋能项目越多，就越确信缺失的一环在于对工作流本身的准备。在 AI 能够可靠地执行任何重复性工作之前，必须有人定义这项工作是什么、它为何存在、哪些信息是重要的、成功的成果是什么样子的，以及 AI 在何处必须停下来寻求人类的判断。

The reality today is that this preparation rarely gets documented. Most teams jump straight to prompts and agents. Many believe that giving teams access to advanced models will produce better results. Here is the counterintuitive part: the better the model performs, the more costly those missing definitions can become.
现实情况是，这种准备工作很少被记录下来。大多数团队直接跳到了提示词（Prompts）和智能体阶段。许多人认为，让团队使用先进的模型就能产生更好的结果。但反直觉的是：模型表现越好，这些缺失的定义所带来的代价就可能越高。

The difference is how you ask. A chat interaction begins with a request: “Analyze this.” “Make a presentation.” “Summarize these files.” An operational workflow begins with a well-defined job. What outcome is required? Which sources are authoritative? What decisions can AI make on its own? What does a passing output look like? When should AI stop and ask for help?
区别在于你的提问方式。聊天互动始于一个请求：“分析这个”、“做个演示”、“总结这些文件”。而运营工作流始于一份定义明确的工作。需要什么结果？哪些来源是权威的？AI 可以自主做出哪些决定？合格的输出是什么样的？AI 何时应该停下来寻求帮助？

In this article, I want to show what you can do before handing recurring work to an AI assistant or agent. For your team, the work can start with these five reusable assets. Instead of continuing to collect better prompts, create these assets, improve them through practical use, and carry them across the AI models and tools you adopt over time.
在本文中，我想展示在将重复性工作交给 AI 助手或智能体之前，你可以做些什么。对于你的团队来说，工作可以从这五项可复用资产开始。与其不断收集更好的提示词，不如创建这些资产，通过实际使用来改进它们，并将其应用到你未来采用的各种 AI 模型和工具中。

### 1. The Repeated Work Asset
### 1. 重复性工作资产

Find the tasks that happen regularly, take meaningful time, follow repeatable steps, use the same types of inputs, or carry enough value or risk to justify a reusable AI workflow. It can be a weekly report, a monthly business review, a customer proposal, a contract review, a product launch package, or a quarterly planning process. You need a simple inventory of how you and your team spend your time, which work repeats, and where a reusable process could help. The repeated work list helps you choose tasks based on frequency, effort, risk, and value.
找出那些定期发生、耗时较长、遵循可重复步骤、使用相同类型输入，或具有足够价值/风险以证明值得构建可复用 AI 工作流的任务。它可以是周报、月度业务回顾、客户提案、合同审查、产品发布包或季度规划流程。你需要一份简单的清单，记录你和团队的时间分配、哪些工作是重复的，以及哪里可以使用可复用流程。这份重复性工作清单能帮助你根据频率、工作量、风险和价值来选择任务。

### 2. The Task Asset
### 2. 任务资产

Most people provide the topic and leave the rest to AI. They ask AI to “analyze the data,” “prepare the presentation,” or “summarize the documents.” The model then has to infer the audience, decision, format, source priority, level of detail, and quality threshold. AI often fills in missing information on its own. When those assumptions are wrong, the output can sound confident while still pointing in the wrong direction.
大多数人只提供主题，剩下的交给 AI。他们要求 AI“分析数据”、“准备演示文稿”或“总结文档”。模型随后必须推断受众、决策、格式、来源优先级、详细程度和质量阈值。AI 经常会自行填补缺失的信息。当这些假设错误时，输出的内容听起来可能很自信，但方向却是错的。

A task asset helps remove these hidden assumptions. It turns a vague request into an assignment AI can execute. Define the objective, the audience, the materials, the constraints, the steps, what good looks like, and when the system should stop and ask. It also gives you something concrete to review before giving AI more responsibility.
任务资产有助于消除这些隐藏的假设。它将模糊的请求转化为 AI 可以执行的任务。定义目标、受众、材料、约束条件、步骤、何为好的结果，以及系统何时应该停下来询问。它还为你提供了一些具体的内容，以便在赋予 AI 更多责任之前进行审查。

### 3. The Context Asset
### 3. 背景资产

Context helps AI see what is important. Teams can reorganize, priorities can change, results get updated, policies are rewritten, and the information you relied on six months ago may go out of date. A useful context tells AI what it knows and what it still needs to verify. Stop re-explaining your work in every conversation. One short document can cover who you are, what you are working on, which sources you can trust, how you make decisions, and what kind of output you never want. Useful context should not become a kitchen sink for everything that has ever happened. Keep it brief and current. Do not dump months of old chat history into the context, because outdated details and unrelated conversations can bury the valuable.
背景信息有助于 AI 识别重点。团队可能会重组、优先级会改变、结果会更新、政策会被重写，而你六个月前依赖的信息可能已经过时。有用的背景信息能告诉 AI 它已知晓什么，以及仍需核实什么。不要在每次对话中都重新解释你的工作。一份简短的文档就可以涵盖你是谁、你在做什么、哪些来源值得信任、你如何做决策，以及你绝对不想要什么样的输出。有用的背景信息不应成为堆砌所有过往事件的“大杂烩”。保持简短且时效性。不要将数月的旧聊天记录一股脑塞进背景信息中，因为过时的细节和无关的对话会掩盖真正有价值的内容。
