# Disentangling Knowledge States with Ability and Proficiency Modeling for Knowledge Tracing
# 通过能力与熟练度建模解耦知识追踪中的知识状态

**Abstract:** Knowledge tracing (KT) aims to predict students' future performance by modeling their evolving knowledge states from historical interactions. Existing KT methods usually treat the raw interaction sequence as a unified behavioral process, overlooking the phase-specific nature of learning behaviors.
**摘要：** 知识追踪（KT）旨在通过对学生历史交互中不断演变的知识状态进行建模，来预测其未来的表现。现有的 KT 方法通常将原始交互序列视为一个统一的行为过程，忽略了学习行为中阶段性的本质特征。

Our preliminary observations show that students are more likely to correctly answer previously failed knowledge concepts after sufficient practice, suggesting a transition from ability-building to proficiency-oriented learning. Motivated by this, we propose Phase-Aware Knowledge Tracing (PAKT), a KT framework that decomposes student interactions into ability and proficiency phases based on the tailored decomposition mechanism.
我们的初步观察表明，学生在经过充分练习后，更有可能正确回答之前未能掌握的知识概念，这暗示了学习过程从“能力构建”向“熟练度导向”的转变。受此启发，我们提出了阶段感知知识追踪（PAKT），这是一个基于定制化分解机制，将学生交互分解为能力阶段和熟练度阶段的 KT 框架。

To effectively exploit the decomposed sequences, we design a multi-branch Transformer with a type-aware readout module to jointly capture phase-specific and holistic knowledge states. We further provide a causal analysis to reveal the confounding bias caused by entangling complex learning behaviors in phase-agnostic KT models.
为了有效利用分解后的序列，我们设计了一个多分支 Transformer，并配备了类型感知读取模块，以联合捕捉阶段性知识状态和整体知识状态。此外，我们还进行了因果分析，揭示了在不区分阶段的 KT 模型中，将复杂的学习行为混为一谈所导致的混淆偏差。

Extensive experiments on six public benchmarks demonstrate that our method consistently outperforms representative baselines, with a maximum AUC gain of 1.33% and an average gain of 0.82%.
在六个公开基准测试上的大量实验表明，我们的方法始终优于具有代表性的基准模型，AUC 最高提升了 1.33%，平均提升了 0.82%。
