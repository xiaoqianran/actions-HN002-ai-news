**Why your regression coefficients keep changing, and what geometry has to do with it.**

### When two directions become one
### 当两个方向合二为一

Last quarter, I was presenting a marketing mix model to a senior director. The slide showed two beta coefficients side by side: Linear TV at +2.4, Digital TV at +1.8. He nodded, satisfied. Then he asked the question I was dreading. “Let’s assume we run this with last week’s refreshed data. Same channels, same model, same everything, but one extra week of observations. Will these numbers move?”
上个季度，我正在向一位高级总监展示营销组合模型。幻灯片上并排显示了两个回归系数（beta）：线性电视为 +2.4，数字电视为 +1.8。他满意地点了点头。随后，他问出了我最担心的问题：“假设我们用上周更新的数据再跑一遍。同样的渠道、同样的模型、一切照旧，只是多了一周的观测数据。这些数字会变吗？”

They moved. A lot. Linear TV slid to +0.9, Digital TV jumped to +3.2. Same channels. Same model. Slightly different data. Wildly different story about who’s driving sales and how much. To a non-technical stakeholder, this looks like a broken model. To anyone who has spent time inside marketing analytics, it looks painfully familiar.
它们变了。而且变化巨大。线性电视降至 +0.9，数字电视跃升至 +3.2。同样的渠道，同样的模型，仅仅是数据略有不同，关于谁在驱动销售以及驱动了多少的结论却大相径庭。对于非技术背景的利益相关者来说，这看起来像是模型坏了。但对于任何在营销分析领域工作过的人来说，这看起来却痛苦地熟悉。

The model isn’t broken. It’s sick, sick with something specific, with a name, and with a deep geometric reason behind it: multicollinearity. Most data science courses and articles treat multicollinearity as a checkbox: run the VIF, drop a feature, move on. But that diagnostic-and-discard approach hides what’s actually happening inside the matrix. Why do the coefficients explode? Why does the model suddenly stand on quicksand?
模型并没有坏。它只是“病”了，患上了一种特定的疾病，它有一个名字，背后有着深刻的几何学原因：多重共线性（Multicollinearity）。大多数数据科学课程和文章将多重共线性视为一个复选框任务：运行方差膨胀因子（VIF）检测，剔除一个特征，然后继续。但这种“诊断并丢弃”的方法掩盖了矩阵内部真正发生的事情。为什么系数会爆炸？为什么模型会突然变得摇摇欲坠？

In this article, we are going deep. We’ll peel back the layers of linear algebra to see multicollinearity not as a statistical nuisance, but as a geometric collapse. We’ll use Linear TV vs. Digital TV (two channels that real-world marketing models constantly fight to disentangle) as our running example, all the way from the OLS equation to the moment the determinant hits zero and everything falls apart.
在本文中，我们将深入探讨。我们将剥开线性代数的层层外壳，不再将多重共线性视为一种统计上的麻烦，而是将其视为一种几何上的坍塌。我们将以线性电视与数字电视（现实营销模型中不断试图解耦的两个渠道）作为贯穿全文的案例，从普通最小二乘法（OLS）方程一直推导到行列式归零、一切分崩离析的那一刻。

### Section 1: Solving for the betas
### 第一部分：求解回归系数（Betas）

Before we dive into multicollinearity’s mathematical headache, let’s start with the basics. Linear regression boils down to this equation:
在深入探讨多重共线性的数学难题之前，让我们先从基础开始。线性回归归结为以下方程：

$y = X\beta + \epsilon$

Breaking it down: $y$ is what we’re trying to predict, $X$ holds all our input features, $\beta$ are the coefficients we want to find (basically, how much each feature contributes), and $\epsilon$ is the error, the part we can’t explain. Simple enough!
拆解来看：$y$ 是我们要预测的目标，$X$ 包含所有输入特征，$\beta$ 是我们要寻找的系数（本质上是每个特征的贡献度），而 $\epsilon$ 是误差，即我们无法解释的部分。很简单！

#### The Loss Function
#### 损失函数

We define our error as the difference between the observed values ($y$) and our model’s predictions ($X\beta$), using the Sum of Squared Residuals (SSR). In matrix notation, our loss function $L(\beta)$ is expressed as:
我们将误差定义为观测值 ($y$) 与模型预测值 ($X\beta$) 之间的差异，并使用残差平方和（SSR）来衡量。用矩阵符号表示，我们的损失函数 $L(\beta)$ 为：

$L(\beta) = (y - X\beta)^T (y - X\beta)$
where $y \in \Re^{n\times1}, X \in \Re^{n\times p}, \beta \in \Re^{p\times1}$

#### The Scalar Nature and Expansion
#### 标量本质与展开

A critical point that often confuses beginners is the nature of this equation. The loss function $L(\beta)$ results in a scalar (a single number representing total error). When we expand the quadratic form, we get:
一个经常让初学者困惑的关键点是该方程的本质。损失函数 $L(\beta)$ 的结果是一个标量（代表总误差的单一数字）。当我们展开这个二次型时，得到：

$L(\beta) = y^Ty - 2\beta^TX^Ty + \beta^TX^TX\beta$

#### Optimization: Setting the Derivative to Zero
#### 优化：将导数设为零

To find the minimum of this loss function, we take the partial derivative with respect to the vector $\beta$. According to the rules of matrix calculus, we are looking for the point where the gradient is zero:
为了找到该损失函数的最小值，我们对向量 $\beta$ 求偏导数。根据矩阵微积分规则，我们要寻找梯度为零的点：

$\frac{dL}{d\beta} = -2X^Ty + 2X^TX\beta = 0 \iff X^TX\beta = X^Ty$

hence we can conclude:
因此我们可以得出：

$\hat{\beta} = (X^TX)^{-1} X^Ty$

as the final expression that will compute our betas. Let’s pause and zoom out. The closed-form solution of $\hat{\beta}$ is beautifully compact, but it hides a fragile assumption; the one that $X^TX$ actually behaves! To see what happens when it doesn’t, let’s return to our two TV channels and watch a linear regression model produce, with mathematical confidence, complete nonsense.
这就是计算回归系数的最终表达式。让我们停下来退后一步看。$\hat{\beta}$ 的闭式解非常简洁优美，但它隐藏了一个脆弱的假设：即 $X^TX$ 必须表现良好！为了看看当它表现不佳时会发生什么，让我们回到那两个电视频道，看看线性回归模型是如何在数学上的“自信”中，产生出完全荒谬的结果的。

### Section 2: The Textbook Extreme
### 第二部分：教科书式的极端情况

#### When Features Are Truly Identical
#### 当特征完全相同时

Imagine, for a moment, a data engineering bug. Your pipeline accidentally creates two columns from the same source. Let’s call them `linear_TV1` and `linear_TV2` and both end up in your feature matrix. Numerically, they’re identical: every row, every week, exactly the same spend. What happens when you fit a regression?
试想一下，如果出现了一个数据工程错误。你的流水线意外地从同一个源创建了两个列。我们称之为 `linear_TV1` 和 `linear_TV2`，它们都进入了你的特征矩阵。在数值上，它们是完全相同的：每一行、每一周，支出完全一致。当你拟合回归模型时会发生什么？

If the duplication is perfect, the model breaks outright: the matrix $X^TX$ becomes singular, its determinant collapses to zero, and the inverse simply does not exist. Python throws an error, and you fix the bug before lunch.
如果这种重复是完美的，模型会直接崩溃：矩阵 $X^TX$ 变成奇异矩阵，其行列式坍塌为零，逆矩阵根本不存在。Python 会抛出错误，你可以在午饭前修复这个 Bug。

But suppose the duplication is almost perfect. For example, one of the columns has tiny floating-point noise from a unit conversion. Now the matrix is technically invertible, but only because of rounding errors. The model fits and the predictions look reasonable. And suddenly the coefficients are absurd. Why? Because there is no unique way to split a single signal into two.
但假设这种重复几乎是完美的。例如，其中一列因为单位换算产生了一点点浮点噪声。现在矩阵在技术上是可逆的，但这仅仅是因为舍入误差。模型拟合了，预测看起来也合理。但突然间，系数变得荒谬了。为什么？因为没有唯一的方法将一个单一的信号拆分为两个。

If both channels carry exactly the same information, the model can assign +5 to one and 0 to the other, or +2.5 to each, or +100 to one and -95 to the other. All of these produce the same prediction. The model has no mathematical reason to prefer any particular split rather than another. It picks one, essentially at random, dictated by numerical noise and reports it back to you with full confidence, just to surprise your director and undermine your credibility!
如果两个渠道携带的信息完全相同，模型可以给一个分配 +5，另一个分配 0；或者各分配 +2.5；甚至给一个分配 +100，另一个分配 -95。所有这些组合产生的预测结果都是一样的。模型没有任何数学理由偏好某一种拆分方式。它本质上是随机选择了一种，由数值噪声决定，并满怀信心地报告给你，结果就是让你的总监大吃一惊，并损害了你的信誉！

This is the textbook extreme of multicollinearity. Easy to spot once you know what you’re looking for, and easy to fix: just remove the duplicate column. But this is not the case that gets you in trouble in practice. The case that gets you in trouble looks much more innocent.
这就是多重共线性的教科书式极端情况。一旦你知道自己在找什么，它很容易被发现，也容易修复：只需删除重复的列即可。但这并不是在实践中让你陷入麻烦的情况。真正让你陷入麻烦的情况看起来要“无辜”得多。

#### 现实世界的情况：当特征几乎相同时
