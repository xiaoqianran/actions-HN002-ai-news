**Title:** Elite Young Runners Are Becoming Freakishly Fast. Welcome to ‘Trackflation’  
**标题：** 精英年轻跑者正变得异常迅猛。欢迎来到“田径通胀”时代

**内容：**  

Save this storySave this storyA couple of years ago, my teenage son decided to spend the long New York winter running indoor track. An accomplished soccer player, he joined the team with the modest goal of staying fit during his main sport’s offseason, but before long his competitive streak kicked in. He trained like a demon for two months straight, brutalizing his legs and lungs with high-intensity workouts. The suffering paid off: His time in the 1,600-meter run (just shy of a mile) dropped so much that he earned a spot in our sectional championship, competing against runners from three nearby counties.  
保存这个故事。几年前，我十几岁的儿子决定在漫长的纽约冬季里参加室内田径训练。作为一名有成就的足球运动员，他加入队伍时只抱着在主要运动项目休赛期保持体能的简单目标，但不久后他的竞争意识就被点燃了。他像魔鬼一样连续训练了两个月，用高强度训练残酷折磨他的双腿和肺腑。这种痛苦得到了回报：他在1600米跑（略短于一英里）中的成绩大幅提升，赢得了参加我们地区预赛的资格，与来自三个邻近县的跑者同场竞技。  

My son had the race of his life at sectionals, shaving several seconds off his personal best. He also got absolutely smoked. The top three boys all finished in well under 4 minutes and 30 seconds. The next six broke 4:38. By the time my son collapsed across the finish line in second-to-last place, the winner was deep into his post-race stretching routine.  
在地区预赛中，我儿子发挥出了职业生涯最佳水平，将个人最好成绩提高了数秒。但他也被彻底碾压。前三名男孩的成绩均远低于4分30秒。接下来的六人跑进了4分38秒。当我儿子以倒数第二名的成绩瘫倒在终点线时，冠军早已进入赛后的拉伸环节。  

Had teenage runners at mid-tier meets always been that absurdly fast? Based on my limited knowledge of track, I suspected not, so I looked up the 1,600-meter results from the 2015 sectionals. Back then, only three boys broke 4:40, and my son would have finished in the middle of the pack. I was now curious whether kids beyond my provincial corner of New York were getting a lot faster too.  
在中等水平的比赛中，青少年跑者一直这么快得离谱吗？基于我对田径的有限了解，我怀疑并非如此，于是查了2015年地区预赛的1600米成绩。那时，只有三名男孩跑进4分40秒，而我儿子本可以排在中间位置。我现在好奇的是，纽约这个偏远角落以外的孩子们是否也快了很多。  

I followed a few track social media accounts, and soon the algorithms deluged me with highlights of young runners obliterating records on what seemed like a daily basis. A 12th-grade girl from Ohio ran an indoor 2-miler in an otherworldly 9:37.15, breaking a national high school record that had stood since 2013. A 10th-grade boy from Texas ran an outdoor 100-meter dash in 9.92 seconds, just a tenth of a second off the collegiate record. Crunch the data on any race in virtually any school-age meet and you’ll find that the top and median times have plunged since the late 2010s, often by several seconds.  
我关注了几个田径社交媒体账号，很快算法就向我狂轰滥炸般地推送青少年跑者几乎每天打破纪录的精彩片段。一名来自俄亥俄州的12年级女生在室内2英里跑出了9分37秒15的惊人成绩，打破了自2013年以来一直保持的全国高中纪录。一名来自德克萨斯州的10年级男生在室外100米跑出了9.92秒，仅比大学纪录慢十分之一秒。分析几乎任何学龄赛事的数据，你会发现顶尖和中等成绩自2010年代末以来大幅下降，往往快出数秒。  

My favorite videos were made by a former University of Pittsburgh hurdler named Shyheim Wright, a master at channeling the bewilderment of track fans. “Pack it up, pack it up right now!” he marveled over footage of a ninth-grade girl from Georgia running 100 meters in 11.01 seconds, a time that made her faster than all but the very best college sprinters. “I’m so glad I don’t run no more, I’m so glad. The times now is getting ridiculous.”  
我最喜欢的视频是由前匹兹堡大学跨栏运动员Shyheim Wright制作的，他擅长传达田径粉丝的困惑。“收工吧，现在就收工！”他在一段视频中惊叹道，画面中一名来自佐治亚州的9年级女生在100米跑出11.01秒，这个成绩让她比几乎所有顶尖大学短跑选手都快。“我真高兴我不跑了，真高兴。现在的成绩太离谱了。”

The track world, I discovered, has coined a name for this much-discussed phenomenon of kids getting ultra-fast: trackflation. In June 2025 at the New Balance Nationals, an annual championship that draws around 6,000 top youth athletes, more than 100 kids beat previous meet records. “There might now be a hundred kids that run under 4:10 in the full mile,” says Will Rodgers, who ran track at the University of Alabama in Huntsville in the early 2010s and now co-owns RunningLane, a coaching and events business. “Back when we were competing in high school, it might’ve been like 20 at max.”  
我发现，田径圈为这种被广泛讨论的青少年跑得超快的现象起了一个名字：田径通胀。2025年6月在新百伦全国赛（一项每年吸引约6000名顶尖青少年运动员的锦标赛）上，100多名孩子打破了之前的赛会纪录。“现在可能有一百名孩子能在完整一英里跑进4分10秒，”Will Rodgers说，他曾在2010年代初在阿拉巴马大学亨茨维尔分校跑田径，现在是RunningLane（一家教练和赛事公司）的联合所有者。“我们上高中比赛时，最多也就20个左右。”  

I had to know why kids today are essentially breaking track. And that meant trying to understand the dramatic shift happening at the sport’s roots.  
我必须知道为什么今天的孩子们 essentially 是在打破田径纪录。这意味着要试图理解这项运动根源处发生的剧烈转变。  

In early May, I drove down to Baltimore to attend the East Coast International Showcase, a two-day outdoor track meet sponsored by the shoe company Puma. One of its main draws was an 800-meter race featuring two fixtures of the elite youth circuit: Henry Bartle of Carmel, California, and Gunner Hammett of Williamsburg, Virginia.  
五月初，我开车前往巴尔的摩参加东海岸国际展示会，这是一项由鞋业公司Puma赞助的两天室外田径赛。其主要亮点之一是一场800米赛， featuring 精英青少年巡回赛的两大常客：来自加州卡梅尔的Henry Bartle和来自弗吉尼亚州威廉斯堡的Gunner Hammett。  

As these two stars warmed up alongside Morgan State University’s faded blue track, I spotted Hammett’s dad wearing a custom hoodie that listed his son’s credentials: “2 Time World Record Holder 800m, 18 Time All-American, 5-Time National Record Holder.”  
当这两位明星在摩根州立大学褪色的蓝色跑道旁热身时，我注意到Hammett的父亲穿着一件定制卫衣，上面列着他儿子的资历：“800米两届世界纪录保持者、18次全美最佳、5次全国纪录保持者。”  

Those achievements were all the more impressive given Hammett’s tender age: He was 13, just a bit younger than the 14-year-old Bartle. Both boys were still weeks away from their eighth-grade graduations.  
考虑到Hammett的稚嫩年龄，这些成就更令人印象深刻：他13岁，仅比14岁的Bartle小一点。两个男孩离八年级毕业都还有数周时间。  

The thin-limbed, bleached-blond Bartle ran the two-lap race in a personal best of 2:03.52, an outrageously good time. Three years ago that would have been enough to win the middle-school 800 meters at the New Balance Nationals. But he was nowhere close to Hammett, who coasted to a 1:57.75—a performance that put him on par with his home state’s fastest high school runners.  
四肢纤细、漂白头发的Bartle以2分03秒52的个人最好成绩完成了两圈赛跑，这个成绩好得离谱。三年前，这足以赢得新百伦全国赛初中组800米冠军。但他远远不是Hammett的对手，Hammett以1分57秒75轻松取胜——这一表现足以与他家乡州最快的中学跑者比肩。  

“Guess I got smoked by you again,” a genial Bartle told Hammett as they gasped for air past the finish line. Bartle has, unfortunately, grown accustomed to falling short. Seven weeks earlier, he and another kid both shattered California’s age-group record for the indoor mile, but Bartle still finished six seconds off the podium. In the one-line caption for his Instagram post about that race, Bartle captured the essence of trackflation: “Living in a time where a state record still doesn’t medal at Nationals.”  
“看来我又被你碾压了，”友好的Bartle在冲过终点线喘着气时对Hammett说。不幸的是，Bartle已经习惯了屈居人后。七周前，他和另一个孩子都打破了加州室内一英里的年龄组纪录，但Bartle仍然落后奖牌线6秒。在他关于那场比赛的Instagram帖子的单行说明中，Bartle捕捉到了田径通胀的本质：“活在一个州纪录仍然无法在全国赛获奖牌的时代。”  

Once Bartle had recouped enough oxygen to function, he gave me a thoughtful breakdown of his recent progress. “Last year, even if I had somewhat of an injury, I was very stubborn and I trained through it,” he told me. Now he’s prioritizing recovery as part of his regimen, and he believes a sub-two-minute 800 and a sub-4:30 mile are well within his grasp.  
一旦Bartle恢复了足够的氧气得以正常交流，他 thoughtful 地分析了自己近期的进步。“去年，即使我有点伤病，我也非常固执，带伤训练，”他告诉我。现在他将恢复作为训练计划的一部分，并相信跑进2分钟（800米）和4分30秒（一英里）是完全可以实现的。  

Until seven or eight years ago, few eighth-grade runners operated with Bartle’s degree of maturity and determination. There were no middle-school divisions at the big national meets, and 13- and 14-year-olds rarely put serious weekly mileage on their legs. But perhaps inevitably, the youth sports boom expanded to include track.  
在七八年前，很少有八年级跑者拥有Bartle这种程度的成熟度和决心。大型全国赛没有初中组别，13、14岁的孩子很少在腿上积累严肃的周跑量。但或许不可避免的是，青少年体育热潮扩展到了田径领域。  

As millions of exhausted parents can attest, sports such as soccer and baseball have become dauntingly competitive over the past generation. Even for third and fourth graders, there’s a constant scramble for spots on prestigious travel teams, followed by nerve-racking fights for playing time. Some families decided they could avoid that madness by steering their kids toward track, which had relatively few participants at its entry-level ranks.  
正如数百万疲惫不堪的父母所见证的，过去一代里，足球和棒球等运动变得竞争异常激烈。即使是三四年级的孩子，也要为加入 prestige 的旅行队而不断争夺，随后是为上场时间而令人神经紧张的斗争。一些家庭决定通过引导孩子转向田径来避免那种疯狂，因为田径在入门级别参与者相对较少。
