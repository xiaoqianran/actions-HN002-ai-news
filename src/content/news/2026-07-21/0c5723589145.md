# Airbus Takes Flight from AWS  
# 空客从AWS起飞  

---

columnists Airbus takes flight from AWS. What happens next is critical Which way to the Land of the Free again? Rupert Goodwins Rupert Goodwins Register columnist Published mon 20 Jul 2026 // 10:15 UTC OPINION  
专栏作家：空客从AWS起飞。接下来会发生什么至关重要。再次通往“自由之地”的路在何方？鲁珀特·古德温斯，《注册》报专栏作家。发表于2026年7月20日星期一 // 10:15 UTC 观点  

European aerospace giant Airbus has lined up on the runway and is pushing the throttles forward as it prepares to evict apps from America. Airbus has named French cloud services provider Scaleway as the destination for some 900 applications, with an initial 70 getting priority onboarding, as it seeks to put critical systems and data under European control.  
欧洲航空航天巨头空客已停在跑道上，正推动油门，准备将应用程序从美国“驱逐”。空客已指定法国云服务提供商Scaleway作为约900个应用程序的迁移目的地，其中首批70个将优先接入，旨在将关键系统和数据置于欧洲控制之下。  

AWS, like all American platforms, can't protect Airbus's crown jewels from Donnie the Data Burglar, so it's best to repatriate them sharpish.  
与所有美国平台一样，AWS无法保护空客的“皇冠明珠”免受“数据窃贼唐尼”（指美国《云法案》等数据调取法律）的侵扰，因此最好迅速将它们 repatriate（ repatriate 此处指数据回流本土）。  

This by itself shows that digital sovereignty is a real commercial driver and not something concocted by Europe as some sort of virtue signal or a bargaining chip. Nobody would put sensitive data where the Russian or Chinese states could help themselves. America is now on that list.  
这本身就表明，数字主权是一个真正的商业驱动力，而非欧洲凭空捏造的某种“道德表演”或“谈判筹码”。没有人会将敏感数据置于俄罗斯或中国政府可以随意获取的地方。如今，美国也已被列入这份名单。  

The details of the announcement are arresting too, with the critical list including ERP, CRM, and manufacturing systems.  
公告的细节同样引人注目，关键迁移清单包括ERP（企业资源计划）、CRM（客户关系管理）和制造系统。  

What isn't mentioned is supply chain management, which sure looks like a critical system from here considering that Airbus has 18,000 suppliers globally.  
未被提及的是供应链管理。考虑到空客在全球拥有18,000家供应商，这显然看起来也是一个关键系统。  

This is a thorny problem, one that's far harder to bring home than even a 900-app migration. Supply chain management is all about moving data between companies, and of those 18,000 suppliers, 17,998 will be Microsoft shops.  
这是一个棘手的问题，其难度甚至超过900个应用程序的迁移。供应链管理的核心是在公司间移动数据，而在那18,000家供应商中，17,998家将是微软产品的使用者。  

Even if the supply chain is managed through a lovely EU-hosted portal, all the supplier data will also live on those suppliers' systems. Airbus isn't ditching Microsoft any time soon because there's nowhere to go. Likewise Google productivity apps because the only place to go is Microsoft.  
即使供应链通过一个漂亮的欧盟托管门户进行管理，所有供应商的数据仍将存在于那些供应商的系统中。空客短期内不会抛弃微软，因为没有其他地方可去。谷歌的生产力应用也是如此，因为唯一的选择就是微软。  

It doesn't matter that all of the above is as vital to Airbus pushing the tin out of the door as anything else. There's no quick fix, and no medium-term fix either. Longer term is another matter, and Airbus's announcement will figure heavily in this.  
上述所有内容对空客“把飞机推出大门”都至关重要，但这已无关紧要。没有快速解决方案，也没有中期解决方案。长期来看是另一回事，而空客的公告将在此扮演重要角色。  

The other big lacuna in the announcement is AI. On the face of it, this is even more intractable. America is making a trillion-a-year gamble on developing frontier models and bit barn bonanzas, and if you want in, you have to be there.  
公告中另一个重大缺失是人工智能。从表面看，这甚至更难处理。美国每年投入万亿美元豪赌前沿模型和“比特谷盛宴”（指AI算力基础设施），如果你想参与其中，就必须置身其中。  

Thing is, Airbus doesn't want in. It has a six-pronged AI strategy, but none of those prongs involve agents or code generation or line-of-business automation. Knowledge extraction, support chatbots, decision support, flight automation – all things that can be built and deployed on-prem.  
关键是，空客不想参与。它有一个六管齐下的AI战略，但 none of those prongs（这些方面）不涉及智能体、代码生成或业务流程自动化。知识提取、支持聊天机器人、决策支持、飞行自动化——所有这些都可以在本地构建和部署。  

Besides, would you even want your aircraft running code by Claude?  
此外，你真的想让你的飞机运行由Claude（Anthropic的AI模型）编写的代码吗？  

We can't truly know the mind of Airbus from what it chooses to tell us, but it looks very much like it doesn't want AI in critical systems, and where that's unavoidable, it wants to do it itself.  
我们无法从空客选择告知我们的内容中真正了解其想法，但看起来它非常不希望AI进入关键系统，而在不可避免的情况下，它希望自己来做。  

AI may, in time, become non-optional for businesses – much as productivity software and data interchange are now – yet for digital sovereignty to work, there has to be an EU-based market in both that can provide flight paths back home that corporates of all types can take.  
AI最终可能对企业变得不可或缺——就像如今的生产力软件和数据交换一样——然而，为了让数字主权发挥作用，必须有一个基于欧盟的市场，既能提供各类企业都能使用的“返航航线”。  

Airbus has shown that the cloud service market can work with digital sovereignty, and it did it the old-fashioned way by announcing a tendering process, in itself a market-making event.  
空客已表明云服务市场可以与数字主权协同工作，并且它通过宣布招标流程——这本身就是一个市场创造事件——以传统方式做到了这一点。  

It could do the same elsewhere, but while even a big migration is a tasty target for a competent cloud service, there's no such sector for productivity and desktop stuff.  
它可以在其他领域做同样的事，但即使是一次大规模迁移对一家能干的云服务商来说也是美味目标，却不存在针对生产力和桌面软件领域的此类市场。  

Yes, yes, of course there's Linux and LibreOffice and all that, but there's no organization that can respond to a competitive tender request for a tightly specified Office/Workspace replacement. Nobody's done that before, which, for a risk-averse outfit like Airbus, is a big red flag.  
是的，当然有Linux和LibreOffice等等，但没有一个组织能够响应针对严格指定的Office/工作空间替代方案的竞争性招标请求。以前没有人这样做过，这对于像空客这样规避风险的机构来说是一个巨大的危险信号。  

There are people who have the track record of managing large projects at that level, and there are people who understand building open source, but they don't live at the same address.  
有在该级别管理大型项目经验的人，也有理解构建开源软件的人，但他们不住在同一个“地址”（指缺乏具备双重能力的组织）。  

Microsoft is losing the battle to protect license lucre. It better get used to the feeling  
微软正在输掉保护许可证利润的战斗。它最好习惯这种感觉。  

Lessons from the VMwars – nothing virtual about the Broadcom vs Tesco slugfest  
来自VMware战争的教训——博通与特易购的激战毫无虚拟可言。  

Apple's chips are the core of a new landscape, but its biggest win is Windows  
苹果的芯片是新格局的核心，但它最大的胜利是Windows。  

Open source's new mission: Rebuild a continent's tech stack  
开源的新使命：重建一个大陆的技术栈。  

The good thing about productivity software is the global market. Were Airbus and others to ask for tenders at a sufficiently large scale, the concept of a blue-chip startup made from safe hands is not only plausible but positively tempting. It would take cooperation between enterprises, which would be a big test of how seriously they take true digital sovereignty.  
生产力软件的好处在于全球市场。如果空客和其他公司以足够大的规模要求招标，一个由“可靠之手”打造的蓝筹初创公司的概念不仅可行，而且极具吸引力。这将需要企业间的合作，这将是它们对真正数字主权重视程度的一次重大考验。  

On the plus side, building on open source means there are no secrets to shield, so American tools and American companies would both be welcome.  
从积极方面看，基于开源构建意味着没有需要保护的秘密，因此美国工具和美国公司都将受到欢迎。  

Once there is a market in digitally sovereign software, it becomes much easier to look ahead at AI and other hard-to-escape aspects of the American digital hegemony. A full-fat, enterprise-grade software and services market will be a wonder to behold.  
一旦数字主权软件市场形成，展望AI以及美国数字霸权其他难以逃避的方面就会变得容易得多。一个成熟的企业级软件和服务市场将是一大奇观。  

It could also change a lot more than how Europe runs its digital affairs. Those in other countries, distrusting their government and fearful of persecution, will be welcome to enjoy the protection and liberties that European digital sovereignty can provide. Call it the Reverse Mayflower. As they used to say in America, you're welcome.  
它可能改变的远不止欧洲如何管理其数字事务。其他国家的那些不信任其政府、害怕迫害的人们，将欢迎享受欧洲数字主权所能提供的保护和自由。称之为“反向五月花号”吧。就像美国人过去常说的那样：欢迎。
