**Title: The EU is about to sell our most sensitive data to the US for visa-free travel**  
**标题：欧盟即将为免签旅行向美国出售我们最敏感的数据**

The EU is about to sell our most sensitive data to the US for visa-free travel The European Commission is currently finalising negotiations with the Trump administration to conclude an “Enhanced Border Security Partnership” (EBSP) Framework Agreement allowing border control authorities to screen travellers against biometric databases and profile them for security concerns. The leaked draft text suggests that the Commission significantly caved in to US’s excessive demands for unfettered information access, exacerbating travel surveillance and putting our fundamental rights at risk.  
欧盟即将为免签旅行向美国出售我们最敏感的数据。欧盟委员会目前正与特朗普政府敲定谈判，以达成一项“加强边境安全伙伴关系”（EBSP）框架协议，允许边境管制机构通过生物识别数据库筛查旅客，并基于安全担忧对其进行画像分析。 leaked draft text 表明，欧盟委员会大幅让步于美国对无限制信息访问的过度要求，加剧了旅行监控，并使我们的基本权利面临风险。

By EDRi · July 20, 2026 US requires access to biometric databases to keep visa free travel  
据 EDRi · 2026年7月20日 报道 美国为维持免签待遇要求获取生物识别数据库访问权限

In 2022, the US government announced that it would require access to biometric databases of countries if they wish to keep visa exemption for their citizens travelling to the US, including European Union (EU) Member States. These new “Enhanced Border Security Partnerships” (EBSP) involve the automated exchange of personal data for the purposes of screening and identity verification of travellers. While the European Commission started discussing the scheme with the US administration in 2022, it received the official mandate by the Council of the EU to lead the negotiations on behalf of Member States only in December 2025. The goal of these negotiations is to establish a “Framework Agreement” which would set out the modalities of the information exchange and general rules on processing of personal data between the US and Member States. It is on this basis that EU countries would then adopt their own bilateral EBSP agreements directly with the US (or adapt their existing arrangements).  
2022年，美国政府宣布，若希望其公民赴美旅行继续享受免签待遇（包括欧盟成员国），则必须允许美国访问其生物识别数据库。这些新的“加强边境安全伙伴关系”（EBSP）涉及为筛查和验证旅客身份而自动交换个人数据。尽管欧盟委员会自2022年起便与美国政府讨论该计划，但其直到2025年12月才获得欧盟理事会授权，代表各成员国主导谈判。这些谈判的目标是建立一项“框架协议”，规定美国与成员国之间信息交换的方式及个人数据处理的一般规则。在此基础上，欧盟各国将随后直接与美国签订各自的双边EBSP协议（或调整现有安排）。

The EU is giving into excessive US demands on immense profiling and surveillance of travellers The US’s excessive demands for travellers’ profiling and surveillance in order to stay in its Visa Waiver Programme are nothing less than blackmail. The EU accepting the terms of this power struggle is extremely worrying for the rights of Europeans and any third country nationals registered in European databases. The scheme implies systematic transfers of biometric data and other highly sensitive and subjective “indications of risk” based on European national databases to the US government, which has shown a blatant disregard for basic human rights in its harsh and inhumane treatment of migrants and visitors.  
欧盟正屈服于美国对旅客大规模画像和监控的过度要求。美国为维持在免签证计划中的席位而对旅客画像和监控提出的过度要求，无异于勒索。欧盟接受这场权力博弈的条款，令人极度担忧欧洲人及任何登记在欧洲数据库中的第三国国民的权利。该计划意味着基于欧洲国家数据库的生物识别数据及其他高度敏感且主观的“风险指标”被系统性传输至美国政府，而后者在残酷和不人道的移民及访客待遇中表现出对基本人权的公然漠视。

In that regard, the draft text does nothing to protect from discrimination based on political opinions, which is worrying because the US is already cracking down political dissent, including its unlawful practice of screening travellers’ social media profiles. The sharing of individual risk assessments to supposedly protect “public security and public order” under the EBSP could further target opposition to the Trump administration, support to transgender people’s rights or protests against the genocide in Gaza expressed (publicly) on social media, with potentially significant impacts, including detention at the borders.  
在这方面，草案文本未采取任何措施防止基于政治观点的歧视，这令人担忧，因为美国已在镇压政治异见，包括其非法筛查旅客社交媒体资料的做法。根据EBSP为所谓保护“公共安全和公共秩序”而共享的个人风险评估，可能进一步针对在社交媒体上公开表达的对特朗普政府的反对、对跨性别者权利的支持或对加沙种族灭绝的抗议，可能导致包括在边境被拘留在内的严重后果。

Furthermore it is very difficult to know the terms of the negotiations as the talks are shrouded in secrecy. However, in May 2026, EDRi member Statewatch leaked a “revised version” of the draft Framework Agreement, revealing the direction the future deal has taken. According to EDRi’s analysis, the negotiated text seems to almost entirely reflect US demands for unfettered information access. This poses two major problems: (1) the text departs significantly from the Member States’ negotiation mandate; (2) its provisions are, in large parts, not compliant with EU primary and secondary law, notably the Charter of Fundamental Rights. Notably, they fall short of meeting EU fundamental requirement of reaching an essentially equivalent level of protection for international data transfers – something that the Court of Justice of the EU would have no doubt of and declare the agreement incompatible with EU law.  
此外，由于谈判处于保密状态，其条款很难被外界知晓。然而，2026年5月，EDRi成员组织Statewatch泄露了框架协议的“修订版”，揭示了未来协议的方向。根据EDRi的分析，谈判文本似乎几乎完全反映了美国对无限制信息访问的要求。这带来了两个主要问题：(1) 文本与各成员国的谈判授权严重背离；(2) 其条款在很大程度上不符合欧盟主要和次要法律，尤其是《基本权利宪章》。 notably，它们未能满足欧盟对国际数据传输达到基本等效保护水平的根本要求——欧盟法院无疑会认定该协议与欧盟法律不相容。

Our call to the EU: resist US pressure, protect key safeguards EU leaders must fight to protect our fundamental rights against yet another instance of the US bullying the EU into dismantling key safeguards. A strong Europe needs a strong rulebook and our lawmakers must be focused on strengthening core protections against data exploitation and privacy violations – rather than acquiescing to the whims of the US. EDRi recommends the Commission and the Council to push back against the US government’s blackmail and refuse to sell people’s personal data to a country with a very worrying human rights violations record and rapid democratic backsliding.  
我们的呼吁：欧盟必须抵制美国压力，保护关键保障措施。欧盟领导人必须为保护我们的基本权利而斗争，反对美国又一次欺凌欧盟、瓦解关键保障措施的行为。一个强大的欧洲需要一套强有力的规则，我们的立法者必须专注于加强针对数据剥削和隐私侵犯的核心保护——而不是屈从于美国的异想天开。EDRi建议欧盟委员会和理事会反击美国政府的勒索，拒绝向一个存在严重人权侵犯记录且民主迅速倒退的国家出售个人数据。
