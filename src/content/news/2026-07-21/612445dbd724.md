Sony Music Entertainment has filed another lawsuit against Udio, accusing the AI music generator of infringing the copyright of more than 30,000 of its songs, ranging from Elvis Presley’s Hound Dog to Beyoncé’s Say My Name, and Harry Styles’ As It Was. The lawsuit, filed in a New York court on Monday, claims that this list represents “only a small portion of Plaintiffs’ works that Udio infringed,” as reported earlier by Music Business Worldwide.  
索尼音乐娱乐已对Udio提起另一项诉讼，指控其AI音乐生成器侵犯了超过3万首歌曲的版权，涵盖从埃尔维斯·普雷斯利的《Hound Dog》到贝莉的《Say My Name》以及哈里·斯泰尔斯的《As It Was》。据《音乐商业周刊》此前报道，该诉讼于周一在纽约法院提交，声称这份清单仅代表“原告被Udio侵权作品的很小一部分”。

In 2024, Sony, along with Universal Music Group and Warner Records, filed a lawsuit against Udio and the AI music-maker Suno. After gaining access to Udio’s training data through discovery, Sony claims it identified additional songs using an “audio fingerprinting” technique. Sony filed a request to add more than 30,000 songs that Udio allegedly “copied and ingested into its generative AI models,” but a judge later rejected the company’s motion, leaving the scope of the original lawsuit at 333 works.  
2024年，索尼与环球音乐集团和华纳唱片一同对Udio及AI音乐制作公司Suno提起诉讼。通过取证获取Udio的训练数据后，索尼声称使用“音频指纹技术”识别了更多歌曲。索尼曾请求增加超过3万首据称被Udio“复制并输入其生成式AI模型”的歌曲，但法官后来驳回了该动议，将原诉讼范围限制在333部作品内。

Sony has now made the more than 30,000 songs the subject of its new lawsuit, which the company says it could expand. It claims Udio “admitted that its generative AI models were ‘constructed by showing the program a vast amount of different kinds of sound recordings,’” including from YouTube. UMG and Warner Music Group have since settled with Udio and are now partnering with the company as the music industry embraces AI.  
索尼现已将这3万多首歌曲作为新诉讼的主题，并称可能进一步扩大。公司声称Udio“承认其生成式AI模型是通过向程序展示大量不同种类的录音构建的”，包括来自YouTube的内容。随着音乐行业拥抱AI，环球和华纳音乐集团已与Udio和解，并现与该公司合作。

You can read the full list of songs Sony is suing Udio over in this filing. The company is asking to block Udio from infringing its works, as well as for up to $150,000 per work infringed.  
您可在该文件中查看索尼起诉Udio涉及的完整歌曲清单。该公司要求阻止Udio继续侵权，并针对每部被侵权作品索赔最高15万美元。
