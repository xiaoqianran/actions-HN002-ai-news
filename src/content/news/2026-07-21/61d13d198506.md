# Title: China’s AI models have Trump’s AI world at war with itself  
# 标题：中国AI模型让特朗普的AI阵营陷入内斗

Over the weekend, several current and former advisors to President Donald Trump on AI publicly lobbed insults at the country’s leading AI companies.  
上周末，几位现任及前任特朗普总统的AI顾问公开抨击了美国领先的AI公司。  

David Sacks, the president’s AI and crypto “czar” until March, branded Anthropic’s models as “lobotomized” and “woke.”  
曾任总统AI与加密货币“沙皇”的大卫·萨克斯将Anthropic的模型贬称为“被切除脑叶”和“觉醒”。  

Emil Michael, a top Pentagon official, called OpenAI’s new head of strategic futures a “supreme village idiot.”  
五角大楼高官埃米尔·迈克尔称OpenAI新任战略未来负责人为“顶级乡村白痴”。  

It began because no one can agree on what to do about Kimi, a free, open source model that Chinese AI company Moonshot launched last week.  
争论源于无人能就如何处理中国AI公司Moonshot上周推出的免费开源模型Kimi达成共识。  

It appears to rival the intelligence of models from OpenAI and Anthropic, which are very much not free.  
Kimi的智能水平似乎可与OpenAI和Anthropic的付费模型相媲美。  

Kimi and other Chinese models like it pose a real problem for Trump.  
Kimi及其他类似中国模型对特朗普构成切实问题。  

And they’re dividing the top AI strategists in his orbit into factions.  
它们正将特朗普阵营中的顶尖AI战略家分裂成不同派系。  

Every time a new smart, free model from China like Kimi gets released, US companies see less reason to fork out money to access models from Anthropic or OpenAI.  
每当中国推出像Kimi这样新的智能免费模型，美国公司就少一分付费使用Anthropic或OpenAI模型的理由。  

Given that enthusiasm for these and other AI companies is driving an outsized share of economic growth, China’s AI models create both economic and political problems for the president.  
鉴于对这些AI公司的热情正推动经济中不成比例的增长份额，中国的AI模型为总统制造了经济与政治双重难题。  

They are “a threat for an administration that really doesn’t want more economic bad news,” Anton Leicht, a fellow at the Carnegie Endowment, wrote on X.  
卡内基国际和平基金会研究员安东·莱希特在X上写道：它们是“一个不希望再有经济坏消息的政府的威胁”。  

They’ve already rattled US stocks.  
它们已震动美国股市。  

What is Trump to do?  
特朗普该如何应对？  

First, consider that this is all happening just a week after New York imposed the country’s first state ban on new data centers.  
首先，需注意这一切发生纽约实施全美首个州级新数据中心禁令仅一周之后。  

There is growing distrust of AI companies, and I imagine a not-insignificant share of Americans would have little sympathy for OpenAI or Anthropic as they fend off cheaper competitors, and would say it’s not the government’s job to protect their interests.  
对AI公司的不信任日益增长，我想相当一部分美国人对OpenAI或Anthropic抵御低价竞争对手不会同情，并会认为保护其利益并非政府职责。  

On this point, they’d see a sliver of agreement (and really just a sliver) with David Sacks, who on July 19 criticized top AI companies that “want the government to eliminate their open source competition.”  
就此而言，他们会与大卫·萨克斯存在一丝（真的只是一丝）共识——萨克斯在7月19日批评了那些“希望政府消灭其开源竞争对手”的顶尖AI公司。  

He has also argued that Chinese AI models have become popular because they come with fewer restrictions on how people can use them (putting aside the built-in state censorship).  
他还辩称中国AI模型流行是因为它们对使用限制更少（暂且不论内置的国家审查）。  

Sacks, however, is out of a job.  
然而，萨克斯已失业。  

He no longer has a formal role advising Trump, and his position that more open AI is better has been largely replaced in the administration by one that sees a larger role for government intervention.  
他不再拥有正式顾问身份，其“更开放的AI更好”的立场在政府内已被认为政府干预应发挥更大作用的观点取代。  

The thinking behind this view is that because AI models have gotten strong enough to pose threats to national security, the government must control how they’re used.  
这种观点的逻辑是：因AI模型已强大到足以威胁国家安全，政府必须控制其使用方式。  

This position has fueled the new White House review process that aims to vet AI models’ security before they’re released.  
该立场催生了新的白宫审查流程，旨在AI模型发布前审核其安全性。  

Dean Ball, a former Trump AI advisor who now works for OpenAI, criticized it over the weekend as a “de facto licensing regime for frontier AI.”  
前特朗普AI顾问、现就职于OpenAI的迪恩·鲍尔周末将其批评为“前沿AI的实际许可制度”。  

Ball predicted Trump may solve his Chinese open source problem with a bit of soft power, perhaps by making US companies afraid to use models like Kimi.  
鲍尔预测特朗普可能通过一点软实力解决中国开源问题，例如让美国公司害怕使用Kimi这类模型。  

That drew a response from Michael, who, with Secretary of Defense Pete Hegseth, has been the agency’s main liaison with AI companies.  
这引来了迈克尔的回应——他与国防部长皮特·赫格塞斯一直是该机构与AI公司的主要联络人。  

Michael called Ball the AI industry’s “supreme village idiot,” bristling at the suggestion that the government would quietly strong-arm companies rather than, as Michael put it, go through “the democratic process not some Deep State scheme.”  
迈克尔称鲍尔为AI行业的“顶级乡村白痴”，对“政府会暗中胁迫公司而非如迈克尔所言走‘民主程序而非某些深层政府阴谋’”的说法表示愤慨。  

Left out of the conversation has been how a model like Kimi got so good in the first place.  
讨论中遗漏了Kimi这类模型最初如何变得如此优秀。  

For much of the Biden administration and even the beginning of Trump’s second administration, keeping China from getting top chips was a priority.  
在拜登政府大部分时期乃至特朗普第二任期初期，阻止中国获得顶级芯片是优先事项。  

Those export controls have loosened—Trump made the controversial decision to allow Nvidia to sell more chips to China, in exchange for the US government taking a cut—and the government has alleged that some chip smuggling has taken place.  
这些出口管制已放松——特朗普做出争议决定，允许英伟达向中国销售更多芯片，以换取美国政府分成——且政府指控存在一些芯片走私行为。  

But China nonetheless has limited computing power, and it’s not clear what chips the company behind Kimi used to train the model.  
但中国计算能力仍有限，Kimi背后的公司使用何种芯片训练模型尚不明确。  

It’s possible that the process involved some distillation, a practice in which AI models are trained on the outputs of existing AI models.  
该过程可能涉及“蒸馏”——一种用现有AI模型输出训练新AI模型的做法。  

OpenAI and Anthropic have long complained that Chinese AI companies do this, and they have requested government help to put a stop to it.  
OpenAI和Anthropic长期抱怨中国AI公司这样做，并请求政府帮助制止。  

In April, they got it, when the Trump administration announced a series of efforts to curb the practice.  
今年4月，他们如愿以偿——特朗普政府宣布一系列遏制该做法的措施。  

But Kimi is out there and free, and it is nearly as good as the Anthropic model the US government deemed so powerful that it was briefly shut down because it threatened national security.  
但Kimi已免费存在，且其能力几乎与美国政府认为威胁国家安全而短暂关闭的Anthropic模型相当。  

The weekend’s sparring suggests many in Trump’s orbit see that as a wake-up call.  
周末的争论表明，特朗普阵营中许多人视此为警钟。  

But nobody can agree on what for.  
但无人能就“为何”达成共识。
