**Title: The Space Force is now seeking to buy up to $30 billion in rocket launches**  
**标题：美国太空军现寻求采购高达300亿美元的火箭发射服务**

---

It seems as though $5.6 billion wasn’t enough. That was the news from the US Space Force on Friday, when military officials announced they were tripling the maximum value of one of the service’s National Security Space Launch contracts to $17 billion.  
看来56亿美元似乎不够。美国太空军周五发布消息称，军方宣布将其一项国家安全太空发射（NSSL）合同的最大价值提高两倍，达到170亿美元。

The expansion of the Space Force’s National Security Space Launch (NSSL) Phase 3 contract comes as the Pentagon signals rising demand for military satellite launches.  
此次太空军NSSL第三阶段合同的扩大，正值五角大楼发出军事卫星发射需求上升的信号。

The NSSL program is set up to allow Space Systems Command, which oversees the Space Force’s launch program, to select from a pool of launch providers for individual missions to deliver the military’s satellites to orbit.  
NSSL项目旨在让负责监管太空军发射计划的太空系统司令部，从一批发射供应商中为每次任务选择承包商，将军方卫星送入轨道。

The NSSL program has two parts. Lane 1 covers the Space Force’s more risk-tolerant missions, such as medium-lift launches with experimental payloads or rideshare missions carrying satellites for the Pentagon’s surveillance or data relay constellations.  
NSSL项目分为两部分。1号通道涵盖太空军风险承受能力较高的任务，例如搭载实验载荷的中型发射，或为五角大楼监视或数据中继星座搭载卫星的拼单发射任务。

Lane 2 includes higher-priority战略 missions, like the government’s largest and most expensive spy satellites, or radiation-hardened communications satellites designed to survive a nuclear war.  
2号通道包括更高优先级的战略任务，如政府最大、最昂贵的间谍卫星，或为核战争环境设计的抗辐射通信卫星。

Lane 1 is open to commercial launch providers without requiring extensive certification and oversight from the Space Force. This is the part the Pentagon has now authorized for $17 billion in launch procurement expenses.  
1号通道对商业发射供应商开放，无需太空军进行 extensive 认证和监督。五角大楼现已批准该部分170亿美元的发射采购费用。

Missions in Lane 2 can only launch on rockets that go through the military’s lengthy certification reviews. Only SpaceX’s Falcon 9 and Falcon Heavy, and United Launch Alliance’s Vulcan rocket are certified for Lane 2 missions.  
2号通道的任务只能使用通过军方漫长认证审查的火箭发射。目前只有SpaceX的猎鹰9号和猎鹰重型火箭，以及联合发射联盟（ULA）的 Vulcan 火箭获得2号通道任务认证。

---

More money for more launches  
**更多资金，更多发射**

There are more launch companies qualified for Lane 1 contracts. Space Systems Command originally selected SpaceX, ULA, and Blue Origin for Lane 1 in the Phase 3 round of the NSSL program in 2024.  
有更多公司有资格竞标1号通道合同。太空系统司令部最初在2024年NSSL项目第三阶段选中SpaceX、ULA和蓝色起源作为1号通道供应商。

Rocket Lab, Stoke Space, and most recently, Relativity Space and Impulse Space were added to the roster of companies competing to launch Lane 1 missions.  
火箭实验室（Rocket Lab）、斯托克空间（Stoke Space），以及最近加入的相对论空间（Relativity Space）和脉冲空间（Impulse Space）被加入竞标1号通道任务的公司名单。

The Space Force issues a request for bids on a number of Lane 1 missions each year, and then doles out individual fixed-price “task orders” to the winners.  
太空军每年对一批1号通道任务发出招标，然后向中标者分发 individually 固定价格的“任务订单”。

SpaceX has won the lion’s share of Lane 1 task orders to date, and Blue Origin won its first earlier this year.  
迄今为止，SpaceX赢得了大部分1号通道任务订单，蓝色起源在今年早些时候赢得了其首个订单。

The Space Force originally estimated the Lane 1 launch providers would compete for at least 30 task orders over a five-year period. Officials put a ceiling of $5.6 billion on the Lane 1 contract to cover these 30 missions.  
太空军最初估计，1号通道发射供应商将在五年内竞标至少30个任务订单。官员们为1号通道合同设置了56亿美元的上限，以覆盖这30次任务。

The Space Force capped the Lane 2 contract at $13.7 billion last year, a number officials thought was sufficient to cover an estimated 54 launches projected for procurement through 2029.  
去年，太空军将2号通道合同上限设定为137亿美元，官员们认为这个数字足以覆盖预计到2029年采购的54次发射。

But things have changed rapidly. In April, Space Systems Command said it had identified 25 additional Lane 2 missions on top of the 54 included in the original contract just one year before.  
但情况迅速变化。今年4月，太空系统司令部表示，在一年前原始合同包含的54次任务基础上，又 identified 了25项额外的2号通道任务。

Now, the Space Force is tripling the maximum value for the Lane 1 contract. The service has not said how many more missions it expects to order in Lane 1 beyond the initial estimate of 30.  
现在，太空军正在将1号通道合同的最大价值提高两倍。该军种未说明在最初估计的30次任务之外，预计还会订购多少次1号通道任务。

With Friday’s announcement, the combined maximum value of Lane 1 and Lane 2 has reached more than $30 billion, and the number is likely to climb again.  
随着周五的宣布，1号和2号通道合同的总价值已超过300亿美元，并且这个数字很可能再次上升。

Space Force officials have not publicly identified exactly what missions they plan to add to each of the NSSL contracts.  
太空军官员尚未公开说明他们计划为每份NSSL合同增加哪些具体任务。

But there are a few obvious programs driving the military’s rising launch demand.  
但有几个明显的项目正在推动军方不断上升的发射需求。

One is the Space Force’s recent award of multibillion-dollar contracts to SpaceX for the deployment of numerous satellites for the Pentagon’s Space Data Network and Airborne Moving Target Indicator programs.  
一是太空军最近授予SpaceX价值数十亿美元的合同，用于为五角大楼的太空数据网络（SDN）和机载移动目标指示器（AMTI）项目部署大量卫星。

The SDN and AMTI satellite constellations will provide global connectivity and targeting information for US forces.  
SDN和AMTI卫星星座将为美军提供全球连接和目标指示信息。

Another is Golden Dome, the Trump administration’s proposed missile defense shield, which is expected to include an untold number of space-based missile warning sensors and space-based interceptors.  
另一个是“金色穹顶”（Golden Dome），即特朗普政府提出的导弹防御盾，预计将包括数量未知的太空导弹预警传感器和太空拦截器。

The Trump administration requested $71.1 billion for the Space Force in fiscal year 2027, up from approximately $40 billion allocated for fiscal year 2026.  
特朗普政府为2027财年的太空军申请了711亿美元，高于2026财年 allocated 的约400亿美元。

The House Appropriations Committee’s draft 2027 budget for the Pentagon includes $55.5 billion for the Space Force, somewhat less than the White House’s request.  
众议院拨款委员会为五角大楼起草的2027年预算草案中，为太空军拨款555亿美元，略低于白宫的申请额度。

The Senate has not released its Pentagon budget bill.  
参议院尚未公布其五角大楼预算案。
