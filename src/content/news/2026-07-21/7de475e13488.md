# Title: F1 in Belgium: Machine learning algorithms are ruining the sport  
# 标题：F1比利时站：机器学习算法正在毁掉这项运动  

---

Formula 1 returned to the Spa-Francorchamps circuit for the Belgium Grand Prix this weekend. This ribbon of tarmac in the Ardennes is one of the sport’s historic venues, featuring in the first F1 season way back in 1950. But as yesterday’s race showed, the current F1 machinery is utterly ill-suited to this dramatic venue.  
本周末，F1重返斯帕-弗朗科尔尚赛道，举办比利时大奖赛。这条位于阿登地区的赛道是这项运动的历史圣地之一，早在1950年首届F1赛季就已亮相。但正如昨天的比赛所示，当前的F1赛车完全不适合这条充满戏剧性的赛道。

---

As we saw at Suzuka in Japan earlier this year, a 2026 F1 battery is able to power the race car’s electric motor for only a few seconds a lap; for much of the rest of the lap, the energy-starved cars have less power going to their rear wheels than in the junior F2 feeder series.  
正如今年早些时候在日本铃鹿所见，2026年F1电池仅能为赛车的电动机提供每圈几秒的能量；在圈中大部分时间里，这些能量匮乏的赛车向后轮输出的动力甚至不如 junior F2 系列赛。

---

The best race track in the world  
世界最佳赛道  

For many, including much of the current F1 grid, Spa is considered perhaps the planet’s greatest race track. In fact, both Spa and the Belgian Grand Prix predate the F1 world championship, with its first race held in 1922.  
对许多人来说，包括不少现役F1车手，斯帕被认为是地球上最伟大的赛道。事实上，斯帕赛道和比利时大奖赛都早于F1世界锦标赛，其首届比赛可追溯至1922年。

---

Back then, the circuit was a 9-mile loop of public roads, taking in the towns of Malmedy and Stavelot before returning to Francorchamps. In the postwar years, the layout shrank slightly to 8.7 miles (14.1 km) and was ferociously fast, with average lap speeds of up to 150 mph (240 km/h)—and ferociously dangerous, given cars with huge amounts of power and minimal concessions to safety.  
当时，赛道是一条9英里长的公共道路环线，途经马尔梅迪和斯塔沃洛特镇，最后返回弗朗科尔尚。战后几年，赛道布局略微缩短至8.7英里（14.1公里），速度极快，平均圈速高达150英里/小时（240公里/小时）——考虑到当时汽车动力巨大且安全措施极少，这也极其危险。

---

The Spa we know and love today was reconfigured in 1979, cutting out about half of the original layout, though at 4.3 miles (6.9 km), it’s still the longest track on the F1 calendar. Interestingly, some of the circuit remained as public roads until 2000, when it became a dedicated race track only.  
我们今天熟知和热爱的斯帕赛道于1979年重新设计，削减了约一半的原始布局，但4.3英里（6.9公里）的长度仍使其成为F1赛历上最长的赛道。有趣的是，部分赛道直到2000年仍是公共道路，之后才成为专用赛道。

---

Eau Rouge and Raidillon are its two most famous corners—a quick left, then right, that climbs 77 feet (24 m) of steep hill. But in the era of high downforce race cars, other corners like Pouhon have become greater tests of skill.  
欧鲁什和拉迪隆是其最著名的两个弯角——一个快速的左弯接右弯，爬升77英尺（24米）的陡坡。但在高下压力赛车的时代，其他如波鸿弯角已成为对车手技术的更大考验。

---

This is one of F1’s most storied venues. Credit: Marc Fleury/Alessio Morgese/NurPhoto via Getty Images  
这是F1最具历史底蕴的赛道之一。图片来源：Marc Fleury/Alessio Morgese/NurPhoto via Getty Images  

---

Not this year. As I’ve detailed more than a few times now, the current breed of F1 car is not at all suited to tracks with high-speed corners. Because the hybrid side of the new power units provides such little energy, going flat out all the time is not the fastest way to complete a lap.  
但今年不是这样。正如我已多次详述，当前一代F1赛车完全不适合拥有高速弯角的赛道。由于新动力单元中的混合动力部分提供的能量极少，全程全速并不是完成一圈的最快方式。

---

Corners that once required a driver’s commitment and maybe a little bravery to drive at the limit are now coasted into to allow the engine to divert some of its power to recharge the battery.  
那些曾经需要车手全力以赴、甚至需要一点勇气才能极限通过的弯角，如今却被减速通过，以便发动机将部分动力转移给电池充电。

---

Obviously, the drivers aren’t thrilled. “For most of sector two, you run just on the engine,” said Red Bull’s Max Verstappen. “So what is that? 450 bhp, 500 bhp? Something like that, which is, I guess, more or less what a Formula 3 car has but with F1 downforce. So you can imagine, of course, that that is not very exciting to drive.”  
显然，车手们并不高兴。“在第二赛段的大部分时间里，你只靠发动机动力行驶，”红牛车队的马克斯·维斯塔潘说。“那大概是多少？450马力，500马力？差不多吧，我猜这差不多就是F3赛车的动力水平，但带着F1的下压力。所以你可以想象，这当然没什么驾驶乐趣。”

---

“But honestly, I don’t want to sit here and complain again because probably someone will shoot me outside the door,” he joked.  
“但老实说，我不想坐在这里再次抱怨，因为可能有人会在门外开枪打我，”他开玩笑说。

---

Rumors continue to swirl about Verstappen’s future. As he is not in the top two of the driver’s championship, he could potentially activate an escape clause in his Red Bull contract, and some think a move to McLaren could be possible. A complete F1 sabbatical is another alternative, as Verstappen has made no secret of his desire to win some of the world’s other great races outside of F1, like the 24-hour endurance races at Le Mans, the Nürburgring, and, yes, Spa.  
关于维斯塔潘未来的传闻持续不断。由于他不在车手积分榜前两名，他理论上可以激活与红牛合同中的解约条款，一些人认为他可能转会迈凯伦。彻底从F1休战一年也是另一种选择，因为维斯塔潘毫不掩饰他渴望赢得F1之外的其他世界级大赛，比如勒芒、纽博格林和斯帕的24小时耐力赛。

---

While I’d love to see Verstappen in more GT3 and possibly prototypes, I’m guessing he’ll stay in F1, and with Red Bull.  
虽然我很想看到维斯塔潘参加更多GT3乃至原型车比赛，但我猜他会留在F1，而且留在红牛。

---

“I’m mentally just adjusting to it. I’m trying to make the best out of it,” he told the media after qualifying. “Even though, of course, it’s not what I like, not what I love to do in Formula 1. But I can also sit at home and drive nothing. But that also doesn’t do anything. So I’m just trying my best.”  
“我只是在心理上适应它。我正努力从中做到最好，”排位赛后他告诉记者。“尽管这当然不是我喜欢的，不是我在F1中热爱做的事。但我也可以坐在家里什么都不开。但那也于事无补。所以我只是尽力而为。”

---

Audi’s Gabriel Bortoleto is proving to be something of a whiz at Spa. He finished eighth, earning himself and Audi another four points in the process.  
奥迪车队的加布里埃尔·博托莱托在斯帕证明了自己是个高手。他获得第八名，为自己和奥迪又赢得了四分。

---

That qualifying session went rather well for the Dutch driver, too. His teammate, saddled with a grid penalty for changing an engine, helped punch a hole through the air for Verstappen, giving him a tow through the final sector of the track, letting Verstappen claim second on the grid.  
那位荷兰车手的排位赛也进行得相当顺利。他的队友因更换引擎受罚，但他在最后赛段为维斯塔潘破风，让维斯塔潘获得尾流，从而帮助维斯塔潘夺得第二位。

---

On pole? Mercedes’ Kimi Antonelli yet again, with a margin of three tenths of a second to the Red Bull.  
杆位？又是梅赛德斯的基米·安东内利，领先红牛0.3秒。

---

F1 is another thing AI has ruined  
F1是AI毁掉的又一样东西  

Things did not go nearly as well for Antonelli’s teammate, George Russell. Declared this year’s champion-in-waiting before the start of the season, the British driver has been comprehensively outdriven by his teenage teammate this year.  
安东内利的队友乔治·拉塞尔则远没有那么顺利。这位英国车手在赛季前被宣布为今年的准冠军，但今年却被他的少年队友全面超越。

---

But Antonelli’s ease with the new cars and Russell’s discomfort with having to adopt a radically new driving style can’t explain the half-second gap between the pair in qualifying.  
但安东内利对新车的适应和拉塞尔对必须采用全新驾驶风格的不适，并不能解释两人在排位赛中0.5秒的差距。

---

Over the weekend, the blame seemed to shift from Russell’s car to the driver, then back to the car again. There was—and is—something definitely wrong with Russell’s car, which was clearly slower than its sister car on several flat-out sections.  
整个周末，责任似乎从拉塞尔的赛车转移到车手，又转回赛车。拉塞尔的赛车肯定有问题——而且现在仍有问题——在几个全油门路段明显比队友的赛车慢。

---

The self-learning AI systems that teams are now using to manage their energy deployment strategies across a lap are looking increasingly likely to blame.  
车队如今用于管理单圈能量部署策略的自学习AI系统，越来越可能是罪魁祸首。

---

By their nature, they’re fairly opaque to the driver. And because the electrical energy is so limited—just 4 MJ capacity in the battery—even tiny deployments to fill in extra torque to help the V6 or from getting a bit of wheelspin is enough to throw off the energy management and leave a driver with a fraction of the power they thought they should have.  
这类系统本质上对车手来说相当不透明。而且由于电能极其有限——电池容量仅4兆焦耳——即使是为了补充额外扭矩帮助V6引擎或防止轻微打滑而进行的微小能量释放，也足以打乱能量管理，让车手得到的动力远低于预期。

---

“过去36小时我的全部精力都集中在直线速度上。而不是集中在调校、轮胎或任何东西上，因为我们……”
