**Title: OpenAI is scared of open-weight models. Should the US be?**  
**标题：OpenAI 害怕开源权重模型。美国也应该害怕吗？**

---

The impressive capabilities of Chinese lab Moonshot’s Kimi K3, the biggest open-weight large language model, has kicked off a debate that conflates two things: the economic possibilities of American AI giants and the future of LLMs as a technology.  
中国实验室 Moonshot 的 Kimi K3——目前最大的开源权重大语言模型——其令人印象深刻的能力引发了一场辩论，这场辩论混淆了两件事：美国 AI 巨头的经济可能性，以及 LLM 作为一种技术的未来。

OpenAI’s head of strategic futures, Dean W. Ball, went so far as to argue that the US government should find a pretext to create regulatory fear, uncertainty, and distrust around the new models, since open-weight models must necessarily deter capital spending by the frontier labs.  
OpenAI 战略未来负责人 Dean W. Ball 甚至辩称，美国政府应寻找借口，对这些新模型制造监管恐惧、不确定性和不信任，因为开源权重模型必然阻碍前沿实验室的资本支出。

People freaked out, with tech luminaries like Yann LeCun and Martin Casado arguing that open software can accelerate innovation and coexist with proprietary projects.  
人们对此感到震惊，科技界名人如 Yann LeCun 和 Martin Casado 认为开源软件可以加速创新，并与专有项目共存。

Ball soon retracted his claims that a regulatory crackdown was the White House’s “best strategy” and that open-weight models necessarily slow down advances in the technology.  
Ball 很快收回了他关于监管打压是白宫“最佳策略”以及开源权重模型必然减缓技术进展的说法。

However, Axios reports that the Trump administration is considering banning K3 and other advanced Chinese models at the behest of American frontier labs.  
然而，Axios 报道称，特朗普政府正应美国前沿实验室的要求，考虑禁止 K3 及其他先进的中国模型。

Another report from Politico said that the Department of Commerce would not take that step anytime soon.  
另一份来自 Politico 的报告称，商务部短期内不会采取这一步骤。

The benefit for major AI companies is clear: Open-weight models, running on independent infrastructure or inside major enterprises, offers cheaper intelligence than Anthropic or OpenAI’s class-leading models.  
对主要 AI 公司而言，好处是显而易见的：在独立基础设施或大型企业内部运行的开源权重模型，比 Anthropic 或 OpenAI 的领先模型提供更便宜的智能服务。

If users increasingly spend more outside the closed labs, that means smaller return on their massive investments in model training.  
如果用户越来越多地在封闭实验室之外消费，这意味着他们在模型训练上的巨额投资回报将减少。

That view extends far beyond OpenAI. “Strong, frontier-caliber open source models will place a squeeze on the margins and will bring down the prices of the frontier companies,” Braden Hancock, the co-founder of Snorkel AI and a research partner at the Laude Institute, told TechCrunch.  
这种观点远不止 OpenAI 持有。“强大的、达到前沿水平的开源模型将挤压利润空间，并拉低前沿公司的价格，”Snorkel AI 联合创始人、Laude 研究所研究伙伴 Braden Hancock 对 TechCrunch 表示。

“It will not necessarily mean that the amount of AI usage goes down a little bit. You know, obviously, quite the opposite.”  
“这不一定意味着 AI 使用量会小幅下降。你知道，显然恰恰相反。”

That’s not a problem for people without shares in Anthropic and OpenAI. AI will still proliferate.  
对于没有 Anthropic 和 OpenAI 股票的人来说，这不是问题。AI 仍将普及。

So what’s the justification for the government to block Americans from purchasing something in our ostensibly free markets?  
那么，政府有什么理由在美国这个名义上的自由市场中阻止美国人购买某些东西？

Concerns over Chinese models come in several flavors. One is protecting US data from the Chinese government; the US banned the import of modern Chinese EVs over concerns about their data gathering.  
对 Chinese 模型的担忧有多种形式。一是保护美国数据免受中国政府窥探；美国曾因担忧数据收集而禁止进口现代中国电动汽车。

But experts tend to think that open-weight models run on US servers are unlikely to leak data back to China, although it’s not impossible that such a thing could be done.  
但专家倾向于认为，在美国服务器上运行的开源权重模型不太可能向中国泄露数据，尽管这种可能性并非不存在。

Another is that the models may have implicit bias toward the PRC — but it’s not clear what that might mean for, say, coding tasks.  
另一种担忧是模型可能对中华人民共和国存在隐性偏见——但尚不清楚这对编码任务等意味着什么。

A third common worry is that Chinese models lack the guardrails that the US government has mandated (through an opaque process), which aim to prevent leading US LLMs from being used to exploit closed computer systems or create weapons.  
第三个常见担忧是，中国模型缺乏美国政府（通过一个不透明流程）强制要求的护栏，这些护栏旨在防止领先的美国 LLM 被用于攻击封闭计算机系统或制造武器。

However, those same guardrails may make US companies more vulnerable: David Sacks, the venture capitalist and Trump adviser, has been sharing cases of US companies turning to Chinese LLMs to close security gaps when US frontier models refuse to do the tasks.  
然而，这些同样的护栏可能使美国公司更脆弱：风险投资家、特朗普顾问 David Sacks 一直在分享美国公司转向中国 LLM 以填补安全漏洞的案例，因为美国前沿模型拒绝执行这些任务。

But the most significant motivation for restricting the models is that fear that China will be able to outpace the US if the frontier labs slow down.  
但限制这些模型最显著的动机是，担心如果前沿实验室放缓，中国将能够超越美国。

Sam Bresnick, a China-focused research fellow at Georgetown’s Center for Security and Emerging Technology, says the growing importance of AI to the US military operations gives the US a reason to support continued investment in AI at the frontier labs.  
乔治城大学安全与新兴技术中心中国问题研究研究员 Sam Bresnick 表示，AI 对美国军事行动日益增长的重要性，为美国提供了支持前沿实验室持续投资 AI 的理由。

But the whole question, he says, is fraught. “Why should the weight of the U.S. government be aimed at protecting these these companies from competitors that are being locked out from the U.S. market based on their origins?” Bresnick asks.  
但他说，整个问题充满争议。“为什么美国政府的重心应放在保护这些公司，使其免受因其出身而被排除在美国市场之外的竞争者的威胁？”Bresnick 问道。

Advocates for open AI say that the frontier companies are creating a false binary between innovation and closed models.  
开源 AI 的支持者表示，前沿公司正在创新与封闭模型之间制造一种虚假的二元对立。

“The bigger impact of having these open source models come from China is less that they’re sneaking in back doors, and more that they are owning the innovation,” Hancock told TechCrunch.  
“这些开源模型来自中国的更大影响，与其说是它们偷偷植入后门，不如说是它们掌握了创新，”Hancock 对 TechCrunch 表示。

“You end up with, effectively, an expanded workforce on your model. PyTorch became the industry standard because it was open source, and so the whole community could contribute to it rather than just one company, and it grew and grew, and all the rest of the deep learning libraries kind of died in comparison.”  
“你最终会拥有一个实际上扩大的模型开发团队。PyTorch 成为行业标准是因为它是开源的，整个社区都可以为其做贡献，而不仅仅是一家公司，它不断成长，相比之下其他所有深度学习库都衰落了。”

Hancock and other advocates fear that Chinese LLMs will become the locus of international research.  
Hancock 和其他支持者担心，中国 LLM 将成为国际研究的中心。

Already, US graduate programs mainly build on open-weight Chinese models, and Hancock says that half of the papers students study are coming from Chinese institutions, with American frontier labs increasingly reticent about sharing their work widely.  
目前，美国研究生课程主要基于开源权重的中国模型，Hancock 表示，学生研究的论文有一半来自中国机构，而美国前沿实验室越来越不愿广泛分享其工作成果。

“Restricting open models wouldn’t make AI safer,” said Clem Delangue, the CEO of Hugging Face, a platform for open AI collaboration.  
“限制开源模型不会让 AI 更安全，”开源 AI 协作平台 Hugging Face 的首席执行官 Clem Delangue 表示。

“It would simply hide the risks, concentrate power in the hands of a few and make it harder for the next generation of builders, researchers, academia, non-profits, governments to participate in making AI safer and more beneficial for all.”  
“它只会隐藏风险，将权力集中在少数人手中，并使下一代建设者、研究人员、学术界、非营利组织和政府更难参与让 AI 对所有人更安全、更有益。”

Bresnick says that the real way to slow China would be to focus more on chip export controls.  
Bresnick 表示，真正减缓中国发展的方式是更侧重于芯片出口管制。

A better way to preserve US AI leadership would be to stop selling Nvidia H200 processors to China.  
维护美国 AI 领导地位的更好方法是停止向中国销售英伟达 H200 处理器。

“That,” he says, “could potentially keep us out of this thorny debate about banning open source technologies that huge numbers of US companies want to use.”  
“那，”他说，“可能会让我们避免陷入这场关于禁止美国大量公司希望使用的开源技术的棘手辩论。”

Part of the problem is that uncertainty around AI economics. “The open business model, the proprietary business model — neither one is figured out. AI companies are struggling to figure out how to make money on their tools, especially as training costs need to go up and up,” Bresnick points out.  
问题的一部分在于 AI 经济的不确定性。“开源商业模式、专有商业模式——两者都尚未定型。AI 公司正在努力寻找如何从其工具中赚钱，尤其是随着训练成本需要不断攀升，”Bresnick 指出。
