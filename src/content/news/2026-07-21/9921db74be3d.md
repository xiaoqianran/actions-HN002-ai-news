Save this story Mushroom hunting is cool and fun, but you know what’s not cool and fun? Having slugs or other mushroom hunters discover your secret spot. Or, worst of all, accidentally collecting the wrong kind of mushroom that could make you quite sick—or worse.

Ideally, you’d be able to grow your favorite kind of mushrooms safely in your own home or yard, whether that’s shiitakes, morels, or lion’s manes, just like you would vegetables. But like many gardening-adjacent pursuits, it’s not that simple. Mushrooms, like all fungi, need just three things to grow: spores, a food source, and a humid environment.  
理想情况下，你可以在自己家里或院子里安全地种植最喜欢的蘑菇，无论是香菇、羊肚菌还是 lion’s mane（狮子鬃菇），就像种蔬菜一样。但就像许多与园艺相关的活动一样，这并不简单。蘑菇和所有真菌一样，只需要三样东西就能生长：孢子、食物来源和潮湿的环境。

However, because mushrooms are incredibly sensitive to light, heat, and contamination, it doesn’t take much to create a prohibitive growing environment. And then there’s the question of whether it’s possible to simulate the critical symbiotic relationships that many mushrooms, especially more coveted ones like porcinis and chanterelles, must maintain with tree roots to thrive.  
然而，由于蘑菇对光线、温度和污染极其敏感，很容易就创造出不适宜的生长环境。还有一个问题：能否模拟许多蘑菇（尤其是牛肝菌和鸡油菌等珍贵品种）为茁壮成长必须与树根维持的关键共生关系？

I am far from a mycology expert, but during the past year, I, a busy full-time-working parent, have engaged in five different attempts to grow mushrooms in my house and backyard: shiitakes on an inoculated log, oysters on a cardboard-enclosed fruiting block, wine caps in spawn buried in bark chips in my garden, oysters from grain spawn in a bucket, and oysters and lion’s manes in a monotub setup.  
我远非真菌学专家，但过去一年里，作为一名忙碌的全职家长，我在房子和后院尝试了五种不同的种菇方法：接种菌种的香菇木段、纸板包裹的出菇块、埋入花园树皮碎屑中的葡萄酒盖蘑菇菌种、桶装谷物菌种培育的平菇，以及单桶培养箱中的平菇和 lion’s mane。

I laughed, I cried, I made an epic mess of chopped straw in my bathroom, and only two of the five methods succeeded. If you, too, are mushroom-curious and looking to grow your own, here’s what I did—and, in some cases, wish I had done differently.  
我又笑又哭，在浴室里弄了一地切碎的秸秆，五种种菇方法只有两种成功。如果你也对种菇好奇并想自己尝试，以下是我做过的事——以及某些情况下，我希望当时能做得不同的地方。

There’s nothing more natural, simple, and affordable than a mushroom log. Many mushroom logs available for purchase are meant exclusively for outdoor use, as they’re just plain old logs with sealed holes full of colonized mushroom spawn. But this 12-inch-tall option from Williams Sonoma is meant for indoor display (though you can also place it outside if you’d prefer), with smooth waxed ends and a rustic metal tag printed with its creation date.  
没有什么比蘑菇木段更自然、简单和实惠了。许多可购买的蘑菇木段专供户外使用，因为它们只是普通的原木，密封的孔洞里填满了已定植的蘑菇菌种。但 Williams Sonoma 这款12英寸高的产品适合室内展示（如果你愿意，也可以放在室外），末端有光滑的蜡封，并挂着一张印有生产日期的复古金属标签。

Soak it for 24 hours and—theoretically—you’ll have mushrooms pinning in about 30 days. Even though oyster mushrooms are the easiest variety to grow, for reasons known only to me at the time, I chose a shiitake log, which came with a piece of paper identifying it as a “younger log.” This means the spawn was still in the process of colonizing and should fruit seven to nine months from the date stamped on its tag, which was December 2025. I soaked the log in mid-May and again in early July, but I’m still waiting to see any pins. Williams Sonoma says the log is guaranteed to fruit, so it continues to sit on my dining room table, in a saucer full of water, tented with a plastic produce bag. Some green mold has started to appear on the top, but I remain optimistic, even though this is not my first shiitake mushroom log failure (I tried to grow one from a different brand some years ago, outside under a bush).  
浸泡24小时后——理论上——大约30天你就能看到蘑菇形成原基。尽管平菇是最容易种植的品种，但当时只有我自己知道的原因，我选择了香菇木段，它附带一张纸条标明为“较年轻的木段”。这意味着菌种仍在定植过程中，应从标签上的日期（2025年12月）起七到九个月后出菇。我在五月中旬和七月初分别浸泡了这根木段，但仍在等待看到任何原基。Williams Sonoma 保证木段会出菇，所以它继续放在我的餐桌上，盛满水的碟子里，用塑料食品袋罩着。顶部开始出现一些绿霉，但我仍然乐观，尽管这已不是我第一次香菇木段种植失败（几年前我曾尝试用另一个品牌的木段在户外灌木下种植）。

If you’d rather just use an existing log or logs outdoors on your property, buy spawn plugs for your preferred variety, drill holes for them, and seal the holes with wax. (You can buy the spawn plugs themselves, or in a kit with the wax and proper drill bit.) To learn which type of wood might be the best host for which kinds of mushrooms, North Spore has a helpful tutorial.  
如果你宁愿使用自家现有的户外木段，可以购买你 preferred 品种的菌种 plugs，钻孔后插入并用蜡密封孔洞。（你可以单独购买菌种 plugs，也可以购买包含蜡和合适钻头的套装。）要了解哪种木材最适合哪种蘑菇，North Spore 有一个有用的教程。

If you’re brand-new to mushroom growing, don’t need large quantities, are happy to skip unusual varieties, and just want near-guaranteed success without fussing over grain spawn and humidistats, an oyster mushroom boxed kit is a good place to start. Oyster mushrooms are hardy; fast-growing; and easy to incorporate into cooking, with a mild flavor not unlike the white button mushrooms you see in the grocery store.  
如果你是种菇新手，不需要大量产量，乐于跳过稀有品种，并且只想在不折腾谷物菌种和湿度计的情况下几乎保证成功，那么平菇盒装套件是一个不错的起点。平菇耐寒、生长快，易于入菜，味道温和，类似于超市里的白蘑菇。

Two brands of boxed kits I've tried and liked are Back to the Roots and North Spore. The former comes with a dehydrated fruiting block. When you’re ready to use it, soak it for six to 10 hours, set it upright in a location with indirect light, and mist it regularly to keep up the humidity. After taking these steps, you should have a bumper crop of mushrooms within days.  
我尝试并喜欢过的两个盒装套件品牌是 Back to the Roots 和 North Spore。前者附带一个脱水的出菇块。当你准备使用时，浸泡6到10小时，直立放在有间接光线的位置，并定期喷水以保持湿度。完成这些步骤后，几天内你就能获得大丰收。

North Spore’s blocks ($30) are shipped fresh, so they don’t need to be rehydrated and will produce flushes (the name for each mushroom “crop”) over and over again, but because the fresh block is more moist, it can attract fungus gnats. When I had this problem, North Spore recommended I add a few drops of dish soap, a tablespoon of vinegar, and a tablespoon of baking soda per cup of water, and spray the mixture on the block before the mushrooms fruit. Unfortunately, my fungus-gnat problem cropped up after the mushrooms had fruited, so it was messy and challenging to spray the block underneat
