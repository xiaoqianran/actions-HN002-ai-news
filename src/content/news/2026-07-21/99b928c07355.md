**Title: Even Mild Sleep Deprivation May Lead to Weight Gain**  
**标题：即使是轻微的睡眠不足也可能导致体重增加**

---

Many studies examining the link between sleep deprivation and obesity have focused on severe sleep deprivation, such as restricting participants’ sleep to about four hours a night. These studies suggest that this may lead to increased appetite and overeating, which in turn could contribute to weight gain.  
许多研究在探讨睡眠不足与肥胖之间的联系时，都集中于严重的睡眠剥夺，例如将参与者的睡眠时间限制在每晚约四小时。这些研究表明，这可能会导致食欲增加和暴饮暴食，进而可能导致体重增加。

Because very few people can endure such severe sleep restrictions for more than a few days, though, the practical utility of this research is limited, and it hasn’t necessarily clarified whether more common and milder types of sleep deprivation, such as getting five to six hours of sleep per night, actually lead to weight gain.  
然而，由于很少有人能连续数天以上忍受如此严重的睡眠限制，这项研究的实际应用价值有限，它并未 necessarily 阐明更常见、更轻微的睡眠不足类型（例如每晚睡五到六小时）是否真的会导致体重增加。

This is the backdrop for a recent study by a research team at Columbia University’s Vagelos School of Medicine, which revealed that chronic, mild sleep deprivation—which is estimated to affect about 30 percent of adults—can lead to weight gain.  
这就是哥伦比亚大学瓦格洛斯医学院一个研究团队最近一项研究的背景，该研究揭示，长期、轻微的睡眠不足——估计影响约30%的成年人——可能导致体重增加。

“Our study shows that getting adequate sleep may help reduce the risk of weight gain and obesity-related conditions like heart disease and diabetes,” explained Marie-Pierre St-Onge, a professor at Columbia University specializing in nutritional medicine, in a release.  
“我们的研究表明，获得充足的睡眠可能有助于降低体重增加以及肥胖相关疾病（如心脏病和糖尿病）的风险，”哥伦比亚大学营养医学专家玛丽-皮埃尔·圣-翁热教授在一份声明中解释道。

---

**Less Sleep, More Weight**  
**睡眠越少，体重越重**

St-Onge and her research team studied 95 adults who typically slept at least seven hours a day and had cardiovascular and metabolic risks. These participants were asked to delay their bedtime by 90 minutes for six weeks, and their sleep duration and physical activity were recorded using wrist-worn monitors.  
圣-翁热和她的研究团队研究了95名通常每天睡眠至少七小时且具有心血管和代谢风险的成年人。这些参与者被要求将 bedtime 推迟90分钟，持续六周，他们的睡眠时长和身体活动通过佩戴在手腕上的监测器进行记录。

For another six weeks, the participants maintained their usual sleep schedules. During both periods, the researchers measured the participants’ weight, waist circumference, body composition, and the fasting blood levels of several hormones involved in appetite regulation.  
在接下来的六周里，参与者保持他们通常的睡眠时间表。在这两个时期，研究人员测量了参与者的体重、腰围、身体成分以及几种与食欲调节相关的空腹激素水平。

Analysis revealed that during the period when bedtime was delayed and sleep duration was reduced by approximately 80 minutes, participants’ average weight increased by about 0.45 kilogram—about a pound—compared to the period when they slept normally. Extrapolating these results over a year, significant weight gain could be associated with chronic sleep deprivation.  
分析显示，在 bedtime 被推迟、睡眠时长减少约80分钟的时期，与正常睡眠时期相比，参与者的平均体重增加了约0.45公斤——大约一磅。将这些结果 extrapolate 到一年，显著的体重增加可能与长期睡眠不足有关。

In addition, during the sleep-restricted period, the average amount of time spent sitting increased by 17 minutes per day. When limited to men and postmenopausal women, sedentary time increased by nearly 30 minutes per day. In other words, even accounting for the longer time spent awake, participants spent more time sitting than when they got enough sleep.  
此外，在睡眠受限期间，平均每天久坐时间增加了17分钟。当仅限于男性和绝经后女性时，久坐时间每天增加了近30分钟。换句话说，即使考虑到更长的清醒时间，参与者的久坐时间也比他们获得充足睡眠时更多。

On the other hand, levels of leptin—a hormone that suppresses appetite—were actually higher during the period of sleep restriction. The theory put forward by studies on severe sleep restriction that disruptions in hormones leads to overeating does not apply, at least as far as leptin is concerned.  
另一方面，瘦素——一种抑制食欲的激素——的水平在睡眠限制期间实际上更高。由严重睡眠限制研究提出的、激素紊乱导致暴饮暴食的理论并不适用，至少对于瘦素而言是如此。

---

**The Next Step**  
**下一步**

A previous study involving a subset of the same participants (38 women) also demonstrated broader health implications. When women at high risk for cardiovascular and metabolic diseases had their sleep reduced by approximately 80 minutes per night over a six-week period, their insulin resistance—a risk factor for type 2 diabetes—increased. This effect was particularly pronounced in postmenopausal women.  
之前涉及同一组部分参与者（38名女性）的一项研究也展示了更广泛的健康影响。当患有心血管和代谢疾病高风险的女性在六周内每晚睡眠减少约80分钟时，她们的胰岛素抵抗——2型糖尿病的风险因素——增加了。这种效应在绝经后女性中尤为明显。

Furthermore, another study involving the same participants showed that a mild reduction in sleep duration among men and women at high risk for heart disease caused immune cells in the blood to become more likely to migrate to sites of inflammation.  
此外，另一项涉及相同参与者的研究表明，对于患有心脏病高风险的男性和女性，睡眠时长的轻微减少会导致血液中的免疫细胞更容易迁移到炎症部位。

This study suggests that even mild sleep deprivation—which many people experience on a daily basis—can have measurable effects on weight, sedentary time, and hormones related to appetite. The finding that the accumulation of even slight sleep deficits can pose a health risk challenges modern lifestyles that tend to prioritize other activities over sleep.  
这项研究表明，即使是许多人每天经历的轻微睡眠不足，也会对体重、久坐时间和与食欲相关的激素产生可测量的影响。即使是轻微的睡眠不足累积也可能构成健康风险这一发现，挑战了现代生活中倾向于将其他活动置于睡眠之上的生活方式。

“All of our findings suggest that insufficient sleep increases the risk of obesity-related conditions like type 2 diabetes and heart disease,” St-Onge says. “The next step is to understand what health benefits people who are chronically sleep-deprived can gain by improving their sleep duration.” To what extent can reassessing daily sleep duration help with weight management and the prevention of lifestyle-related diseases? Further research is needed to understand the mechanisms involved.  
“我们所有的发现都表明，睡眠不足会增加与肥胖相关的疾病（如2型糖尿病和心脏病）的风险，”圣-翁热说。“下一步是了解长期睡眠不足的人通过改善睡眠时长可以获得哪些健康益处。”重新评估每日睡眠时长在多大程度上有助于体重管理和预防生活方式相关疾病？需要进一步研究以了解其中的机制。
