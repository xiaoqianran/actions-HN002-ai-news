**AI is more likely than humans to form biases when hiring**  
**AI在招聘时比人类更容易形成偏见**

The next time you apply for a job, AI may screen your résumé before any human sees it. But there’s good reason to question whether AI will judge you fairly. We already know that LLMs pick up human biases from their training data. New research suggests they can also develop their own biases from experience—and stereotype job applicants more than humans do. As AI companies race to build agentic models that remember the tiniest details about users, they may be handing them ammunition for forming those biases. Read the full story on AI’s alarming potential to stereotype job applicants. —Michelle Kim  
下次你申请工作时，AI可能会在任何人看到你的简历前就对其进行筛选。但很有理由质疑AI是否会公平地评判你。我们已经知道大语言模型会从训练数据中吸收人类偏见。新研究表明，它们还能从经验中发展出自己的偏见——并且比人类更倾向于对求职者产生刻板印象。随着AI公司竞相构建能记住用户最细微细节的智能体模型，它们可能正在为这些偏见的形成提供弹药。阅读关于AI对求职者产生刻板印象的惊人潜力的完整报道。 ——Michelle Kim

**The risk of weather data sabotage is rising**  
**气象数据破坏的风险正在上升**

Every morning, airline dispatchers, grid operators, and farmers around the world make decisions based on weather forecasts. More recently, the forecasts have become relevant for another industry: prediction markets, where people bet money on all kinds of real-world events, including the weather. The temptation to manipulate weather data to get an edge in these markets, combined with a collective move toward data-driven AI weather forecasting, is starting to put the accuracy of weather predictions at risk. As experts in the field, we can foresee scenarios where the risks snowball into far bigger, more systemic problems. Find out why the threats to weather data are growing—and how to stay ahead of them. —Monique Kuglitsch, Jesper Dramsch, Franz G. Kuglitsch, & Andrea Toreti  
每天早晨，全球的航班调度员、电网运营商和农民都根据天气预报做出决策。最近，这些预报对另一个行业也变得相关：预测市场，人们在那里对各种现实世界事件（包括天气）下注。操纵气象数据以在这些市场中获得优势的诱惑，加上向数据驱动的AI气象预报的集体转变，正开始危及天气预报的准确性。作为该领域的专家，我们可以预见风险演变成更大、更系统性问题的场景。了解为什么对气象数据的威胁正在增长——以及如何领先于这些威胁。 ——Monique Kuglitsch, Jesper Dramsch, Franz G. Kuglitsch, & Andrea Toreti
