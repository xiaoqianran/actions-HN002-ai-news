**Sleep**  
**睡眠**  

Our understanding of how the human body works is constantly evolving. This is good. However, between grifters on social media, biased research funded by industry, and the evolving understanding provided by science I mentioned earlier, you could sometimes reasonably convince yourself that there's evidence to support the claim that juggling live salmon cures male pattern baldness.  

我们对人体运作方式的理解在不断演进。这很好。然而，介于社交媒体上的骗子、行业资助的偏见研究，以及我前面提到的科学提供的演进理解之间，你有时可能会合理地相信，有证据支持“抛接活鲑鱼可以治愈男性型脱发”的说法。  

I have a PhD in physics. I built lasers that spit out 80 femtosecond pulses, and I did quantum mechanics simulations. I am so scarred by that experience that my default stance on science-related (which includes health) headlines and social media posts is "bullshit until proven otherwise", where "proven" includes but is not limited to "corroborated by my doctor or a consensus of medical professionals." And to be clear, that distrust is not distrust of science, it's distrust that nuance has been stripped away to fit into a headline, essentially. I am not a doctor, and I'm very aware of that.  

我拥有物理学博士学位。我建造过发射 80 飞秒脉冲的激光器，也做过量子力学模拟。那段经历给我留下了如此深的创伤，以至于我对任何与科学相关（包括健康）的标题和社交媒体帖子的默认立场是“在证明之前都是扯淡”，这里的“证明”包括但不限于“得到我的医生或医学专业人士共识的证实”。需要明确的是，这种不信任不是对科学的不信任，而是对细节被剥离以适应标题这一事实的不信任，本质上。我不是医生，我非常清楚这一点。  

One piece of knowledge I feel safe clinging to and saying out loud to the internet is that sleep is probably good for you. Everything seems to get better with sleep: your immune system, your cognitive function, recovery from physical activity, etc.  

我觉得可以安全抓住并大声对互联网说的一件事是：睡眠可能对你有好处。似乎一切都会因睡眠而改善：你的免疫系统、认知功能、身体活动后的恢复等等。  

Unfortunately, I've always had a complicated relationship with sleep. Namely because my neuro is divergent. Neurodivergent people tend to be night owls as a survival/recovery mechanism. Having some time to ourselves where no one is placing demands on our attention, behavior, etc, gives us some space to exist as our actual selves, not our "capitalism demands that I perform on this schedule, in this manner, to these people, otherwise I can't feed myself" selves. This means that our sleep routines can be either misaligned with our work schedules, or just irregular in general.  

不幸的是，我和睡眠的关系一直很复杂。主要是因为我的神经是 divergent（神经多样性）。神经多样性人群往往倾向于成为夜猫子，作为一种生存/恢复机制。拥有一些属于自己的时间，在这段时间里没有人对我们的注意力、行为等提出要求，这给了我们一些空间以真实的自我存在，而不是“资本主义要求我按这个时间表、以这种方式、为这些人表现，否则我无法养活自己”的自我。这意味着我们的睡眠规律可能与工作安排不一致，或者总的来说是不规律的。  

If it's not clear yet, I have ADHD. This means that my brain never shuts up. Like, ever. This is not conducive to "winding down for bed." I developed a reading in bed routine from a pretty early age, which cuts down on distractions and acts as a signpost my nervous system can recognize as "ah, we typically fall asleep after doing this for a while, let's start that process."  

如果还不清楚，我有 ADHD（注意缺陷多动障碍）。这意味着我的大脑从不闭嘴。永远不。这不利于“为睡觉放松下来”。我从很小的时候就养成了在床上阅读的惯例，这减少了干扰，并作为一个路标，我的神经系统可以识别为“啊，我们通常做这件事一段时间后就睡着了，让我们开始这个过程吧。”  

Luckily I was diagnosed with ADHD pretty young, and I've been medicated since childhood. For people that are diagnosed as adults, I see one of two reactions to starting ADHD medication: Holy shit, is this how normal people feel all the time? It's so much quieter! Ah fuck, I'm autistic too.  

幸运的是，我很小就被诊断出 ADHD，并且从童年起就一直在服药。对于成年后才被诊断的人，我看到开始服用 ADHD 药物后有两种反应：我靠，正常人一直都有这种感觉吗？安静太多了！啊靠，我也是自闭症。  

If it's not clear yet, I'm autistic. The overlap between autism and ADHD is pretty significant, and in many cases the ADHD is just masking the autism. That's why some people go undiagnosed until they start medication. Not me though, I happen to be exceptionally good at burning mental energy in exchange for social skills. I was only diagnosed about two years ago, but there were signs.  

如果还不清楚，我是自闭症。自闭症和 ADHD 的重叠非常显著，在许多情况下，ADHD 只是在掩盖自闭症。这就是为什么有些人直到开始服药才被诊断出来。不过我不是，我恰好非常擅长用消耗脑力来换取社交技能。我大约两年前才被诊断出来，但早有迹象。  

Many people with autism have sensory issues, as in certain textures or sensations are abnormally bothersome. Imagine the reaction that some people have to nails on a chalkboard, or the sound of someone chewing, etc, but getting that reaction to a texture, certain kinds of touch, etc. Similarly, many people with autism have trouble regulating their body temperature to the same degree as neurotypical people.  

许多自闭症患者有感官问题，即某些质地或感觉异常恼人。想象一下有些人对指甲刮黑板、或别人咀嚼的声音等的反应，但把这种反应转移到某种质地、某种触感等上。类似地，许多自闭症患者调节体温的困难程度与神经典型人群不同。  

When it comes to sleep, this means that I have trouble feeling comfortable unless the temperature is just right, the weight of the comforter/duvet is high enough, etc. I also just moved to Portland (the cool one, not the lobster one). This is one of the rare cities in the US where air conditioning isn't a given. For most of the US this is like living in the stone age. I guess for a long time the climate was temperate enough that it wasn't needed, but the climate is changing has changed. It now gets certifiably hot as fuck in Portland. My new apartment at least has a window unit, but it's in the living room, not the bedroom. There's also no ceiling fans. I also live in a high-rise, and heat rises. This means that my bedroom gets pretty stuffy at night. I bought temperature sensors to prove that my bedroom is the hottest room in the apartment and everything.  

说到睡眠，这意味着除非温度恰到好处、被子/羽绒被的重量足够大等，否则我很难感到舒适。我还刚搬到了波特兰（那个凉爽的波特兰，不是龙虾那个）。这是美国少数几个空调不是标配的城市之一。对美国大部分地区来说，这就像住在石器时代。我猜长期以来气候足够温和所以不需要，但气候已经变了。现在波特兰热得绝对要命。我的新公寓至少有个窗式空调，但它在客厅，不在卧室。也没有吊扇。我还住在一栋高层建筑里，而且热空气上升。这意味着我的卧室晚上相当闷热。我买了温度传感器来证明我的卧室是公寓里最热的房间，等等。  

So, to recap:  
所以，回顾一下：  

*   Brain never shuts up  
    大脑从不闭嘴  
*   I have medical grade stimulants coursing through my veins  
    我有医疗级兴奋剂在我血管里奔流  
*   Me no likey stuffy bedroom  
    我不喜欢闷热的卧室  
*   Not sleeping well is pretty bad for you  
    睡不好对你 pretty bad（非常不好）  
*   Please, PLEASE help me sleep better  
    求你了， PLEASE（请）帮我睡更好  

That's why I said fuck it and dropped RAM money on a temperature control system for my bed. Spoiler: it's good.  

这就是为什么我说 fuck it（去他的），并为我的床买了一个温度控制系统（花了相当于内存条的钱）。剧透：它很好。
