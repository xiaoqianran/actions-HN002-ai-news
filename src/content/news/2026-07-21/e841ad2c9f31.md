**Title: The FCC is planning to retroactively ban disguised DJI gadgets**  
FCC计划追溯禁止伪装的大疆设备

Last October, we told you how the FCC had given itself the power to retroactively ban gadgets that have already received its approval to be imported and sold in the United States. Now, the FCC’s getting ready to wield that power for the first time, by cracking down on the “DJI front companies” suspected of sneaking the Chinese company’s tech past its foreign drone ban.  
去年十月，我们曾报道FCC已获得权力，可追溯禁止已获批准进口并在美国销售的产品。如今，FCC正准备首次行使这一权力，打击那些涉嫌让中国公司技术绕过外国无人机禁令的“大疆空壳公司”。

Two Fridays ago, the FCC had already proposed a $25,000 fine on eight of those “front companies,” including the ones behind the Skyrover drones and Xtra cameras. But now, the FCC is proposing a lot more than a slap-on-the-wrist — it’s planning to ban those same companies from continuing to import, distribute, market, and sell their existing drones and cameras.  
两周前（两个星期五前），FCC已对八家此类“空壳公司”提出25,000美元罚款，包括Skyrover无人机和Xtra相机的背后公司。但现在，FCC提出的不仅仅是轻微处罚——它计划禁止这些公司继续进口、分销、营销和销售其现有的无人机和相机。

For example, we highlighted a deal on Xtra’s version of the DJI Osmo Pocket 3 just two weeks ago, a product that already got FCC approval before the drone ban ever came down, and a proudct which Amazon offers with next-day shipping. If the FCC’s retroactive ban goes into effect, that product should disappear from all major online retailers and Xtra’s own website, and the company may even need to write off however many cameras are still sitting in US warehouses like Amazon’s.  
例如，两周前我们曾报道Xtra版大疆Osmo Pocket 3的优惠，该产品在无人机禁令出台前已获FCC批准，且亚马逊提供次日达配送。如果FCC的追溯禁令生效，该产品应从所有主要在线零售商和Xtra官网消失，公司甚至可能不得不注销仍滞留在亚马逊等美国仓库中的库存相机。

The Xtra Muse and the DJI Osmo Pocket 3 — too close to even call it a “clone.“ Photo by Sean Hollister / The Verge  
Xtra Muse与大疆Osmo Pocket 3——相似到甚至不能称之为“克隆”。照片：Sean Hollister / The Verge

The ban wouldn’t affect gadgets that have already been purchased, and it wouldn’t happen immediately. The FCC’s currently taking public comments for 30 days first. While the FCC has “tentatively” concluded that these companies are selling equipment that should be banned over national security fears, it claims it’ll listen to “specific evidence” otherwise.  
该禁令不会影响已购买的产品，也不会立即生效。FCC目前正在首先进行30天的公众意见征询。虽然FCC“初步”认定这些公司销售应因国家安全担忧而被禁的设备，但声称会听取“具体证据”反驳。

But that should maybe be taken with a grain of salt: the FCC largely ignored public comments about net neutrality and lied about a cyberattack. It’s also worth pointing out that the US government has never provided specific public evidence that foreign drones pose a national security threat to begin with, or why other products like cameras should be caught up in that ban. If you want to comment anyhow, you can do so with an “Express Filing” here.  
但对此应谨慎对待：FCC largely ignored public comments about net neutrality and lied about a cyberattack. 也值得指出的是，美国政府从未提供具体公开证据证明外国无人机一开始就构成国家安全威胁，或为何相机等其他产品也应被纳入禁令。如果你想评论，可在此进行“快速申报”。

In the meantime, the FCC says it has “temporarily deferred the grantee codes of these companies,” a phrase that it does not define. (A former FCC official tells The Verge he’s never heard that phrase before; we’re asking the FCC if that means these companies can’t get future gadgets authorized for import and sale.)  
与此同时，FCC表示已“暂时推迟这些公司的授权代码”，但未定义该短语。（一位前FCC官员告诉The Verge他从未听过该短语；我们正在询问FCC这是否意味着这些公司无法获得未来产品的进口和销售授权。）

The specific companies that the FCC is targeting include Cogito Tech, Fixaxo Technology, Lyno Dynamics, Skyhigh Tech, Spatial Hover, SZ Knowact and WaveGo Tech (both of whom are behind Skyrover), and Xtra Technology, none of which responded to the FCC’s requests for information. It also includes agricultural drone brand XAG, which did reportedly reply to the FCC once, but not with the information that the FCC asked for.  
FCC针对的具体公司包括Cogito Tech、Fixaxo Technology、Lyno Dynamics、Skyhigh Tech、Spatial Hover、SZ Knowact和WaveGo Tech（两者均为Skyrover背后公司）以及Xtra Technology，这些公司均未回应FCC的信息请求。还包括农业无人机品牌XAG，据报道曾回复FCC一次，但未提供FCC要求的信息。

All save XAG were brought to the FCC’s attention by Konrad Iturbe, who figured out how to track the “FCC front companies” last year. The FCC also cites our story about Xtra.  
除XAG外，所有公司均由Konrad Iturbe提请FCC注意，他去年发现了追踪“FCC空壳公司”的方法。FCC also cites our story about Xtra.  
（注：此处“cites our story”译为“引用了我们的报道”，但原文保留英文短语，因涉及特定引用。）

Today, the FCC is also announcing that it’s parting ways with one of the Chinese test labs that helped some of these products make it to market: SGS-CSTC Shenzhen. While SGS-CSTC did reply to the FCC to claim it’s not controlled by the Chinese government, as the China-owned CSTC only has 15 percent ownership of the lab, US law currently considers another entity to have control if it has 10 percent or more or a company — at least when it comes to authorizing radios for use in the US.  
今天，FCC还宣布与一家帮助这些产品进入市场的中国测试实验室分道扬镳：SGS-CSTC Shenzhen。虽然SGS-CSTC确实回复FCC声称其不受中国政府控制，因为中国拥有的CSTC仅持有该实验室15%的股份，但美国法律目前认为，如果另一实体拥有公司10%或以上股份，即视为控制——至少在美国授权无线电使用时如此。

On July 10th, the FCC said it might take even more actions against the eight “DJI front companies” if they didn’t reply by today, July 20th. We’ll let you know what we hear.  
7月10日，FCC表示如果这八家“大疆空壳公司”未在今日（7月20日）前回复，可能采取更多行动。我们会随时通报最新消息。
