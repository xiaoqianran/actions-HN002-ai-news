### AliExpress hit with record $625M fine after failing to make EU-ordered fixes  
欧盟因阿里Express未按要求整改，开出创纪录的6.25亿美元罚单  

Yesterday, the European Commission slapped AliExpress with the largest fine yet under the Digital Services Act (DSA), exceeding $625 million.  
昨日，欧盟委员会依据《数字服务法》（DSA）对阿里Express处以迄今最高额罚款，超过6.25亿美元。  

In a press release, the EC said that AliExpress failed to “diligently assess and mitigate risks relating to the sale of illegal, unsafe, or counterfeit products on its e-commerce platform.”  
欧盟委员会在新闻稿中表示，阿里Express未能“勤勉评估并减轻其电商平台上非法、不安全或假冒产品销售相关的风险”。  

Officials found that AliExpress not only failed to staff teams dedicated to removing counterfeit and dangerous items from the platform, but it also made it easy for bad actors to evade detection.  
官员们发现，阿里Express不仅未组建专门团队清理平台上的假冒和危险商品，还让不良商家轻易规避检测。  

Staff was so limited that some content moderators were sometimes given just “tens of seconds” to evaluate if a flagged product met EU standards, The Guardian reported.  
据《卫报》报道，审核人员严重不足，部分内容审核员有时仅获“数十秒”时间判断 flagged 商品是否符合欧盟标准。  

The result, the EC said, was that it “found millions of products that reappeared online” after being flagged for removal, “which sometimes stayed for longer than a month.”  
欧盟委员会称，结果导致“数百万被标记下架的商品重新上线”，“有时滞留时间超过一个月”。  

Bad actors can easily skirt AliExpress safeguards, the EC said.  
欧盟委员会指出，不良商家可轻易绕过阿里Express的防护措施。  

All some sellers had to do to circumvent automatic takedowns was miscategorize their products so that fewer checks were required before posting dangerous goods.  
部分卖家只需将商品错误分类，即可减少发布危险品前的审核环节，从而规避自动下架机制。  

For shady sellers, the risks of detection appeared low.  
对可疑卖家而言，被检测到的风险似乎很低。  

The e-commerce site’s mandatory brand authorization system was also ineffective and understaffed, the EC found, and AliExpress did not penalize traders for selling illegal products as its policy claims it would.  
欧盟委员会发现，该电商平台的强制品牌授权系统同样无效且人员不足，阿里Express也未按政策宣称的那样对销售非法商品的商家进行处罚。  

Making things worse, AliExpress “inadequately assessed how its recommender and advertising systems exacerbate the spread of illegal products,” the EC said.  
更糟糕的是，欧盟委员会称阿里Express“对其推荐和广告系统加剧非法商品传播的评估不足”。  

So rather than remove illegal products, AliExpress was recommending them to consumers and helping to maximize exposure.  
因此，阿里Express非但未下架非法商品，反而向消费者推荐并助其扩大曝光。  

Talking to the press, the European Union’s tech chief, Henna Virkkunen, noted that one in five Europeans shop monthly at retail sites like AliExpress, Temu, and Shein.  
欧盟 tech 负责人 Henna Virkkunen 向媒体指出，五分之一的欧洲人每月会在阿里Express、Temu 和 Shein 等零售网站购物。  

AliExpress also relied on a single quantitative metric to gauge how effectively its systems were working to weed out illegal products.  
阿里Express还依赖单一量化指标来衡量其系统清理非法商品的效率。  

And that metric did not properly measure the extent of the harm.  
该指标未能准确衡量实际危害程度。  

EC testing found that “a high volume of illegal products”—including unsafe toys and dangerous cosmetics—“continued to circulate despite AliExpress’ moderation efforts.”  
欧盟委员会测试发现，“大量非法商品”——包括不安全玩具和危险化妆品——“在阿里Express的审核努力下仍持续流通”。  

In June 2025, AliExpress was ordered to bring its platform into compliance with the DSA but failed to make the necessary changes, the EC said.  
欧盟委员会称，2025年6月，阿里Express被要求使其平台符合《数字服务法》，但未能做出必要整改。  

The fine was calculated to be proportionate to the nature of the violations, which the EC considered “particularly serious infringements,” and to penalize AliExpress’s delayed interventions to mitigate flagged risks.  
罚款金额根据违规性质（欧盟委员会视为“特别严重侵权”）以及惩罚阿里Express延迟干预已标记风险的行为而裁定。  

The fine dwarfs prior penalties that Temu and X were ordered to pay for the earliest DSA violations.  
该罚款远超此前 Temu 和 X 因早期违反《数字服务法》而需支付的罚金。  

Last December, X was the first to be fined. Among other violations, X was ordered to pay nearly $140 million after Elon Musk started selling blue checkmarks as the only way to verify an account.  
去年12月，X 成为首家被罚公司。除其他违规外，因 Elon Musk 将蓝标认证变为唯一验证方式，X 被勒令支付近1.4亿美元。  

The Commission found that the change made it easier to deceive users on X, and the DSA “clearly prohibits online platforms from falsely claiming that users have been verified, when no such verification took place,” the EC said.  
欧盟委员会认为，该变更使用户在 X 上更易受骗，且《数字服务法》“明确禁止在线平台在未实际验证的情况下虚假声称用户已通过验证”。  

Temu was then fined more than $225 million after evidence showed that consumers in the EU were very likely to encounter illegal items on Temu.  
随后，Temu 因证据显示欧盟消费者极可能在其平台遭遇非法商品而被罚超2.25亿美元。  

Reacting to its much larger fine, AliExpress told Ars it was “surprised” by the “disproportionate” fine.  
面对更高额罚款，阿里Express向 Ars Technica 表示对该“不成比例”的罚款“感到惊讶”。  

AliExpress said it plans to appeal the decision, claiming the EC ignored its “sound risk management framework and the significant, proactive enhancements we have made.”  
阿里Express称将上诉，并指控欧盟委员会无视其“健全的风险管理框架及我们已做出的重大主动改进”。  

The massive online retailer noted that its EU market is substantially smaller than its China market and said that it invests “substantial resources in risk assessment and mitigation, product safety and consumer protection” and “has been and continues to be committed to meeting our obligations to consumers.”  
这家大型电商巨头指出，其欧盟市场远小于中国市场，并称已投入“大量资源用于风险评估与缓解、产品安全和消费者保护”，且“始终并继续致力于履行对消费者的义务”。  

On Sunday, however, Virkkunen, the EU tech chief, said e-commerce giants can’t rely on scale as an excuse for failing to police illegal goods.  
然而，上周日，欧盟 tech 负责人 Virkkunen 表示，电商巨头不能以规模为由逃避对非法商品的监管责任。  

“The spread of counterfeit clothing, unsafe toys, dangerous cosmetics and other illegal and harmful products is not an unavoidable cost of shopping online—it is a failure by AliExpress to comply with its obligations under the Digital Services Act,” Virkkunen said.  
Virkkunen 称：“假冒服装、不安全玩具、危险化妆品及其他非法有害产品的泛滥并非网购不可避免的成本——而是阿里Express未履行《数字服务法》义务的失败。”  

“Scale is not an excuse; risks must be identified and addressed systematically to ensure consumers can safely shop online.”  
“规模不是借口；必须系统性地识别和应对风险，以确保消费者能安全网购。”
