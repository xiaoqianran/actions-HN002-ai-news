# Title: An Ebike Company Was Sued for Misleading Info on Safety. It Points to a Big Problem  
# 标题：一家电动自行车公司因发布误导性安全信息被起诉。这揭示了一个大问题  

Content:  
内容：  

Save this storySave this storyAmazon and UL, a company that tests and certifies that products meet safety standards, settled a lawsuit in July with several Chinese companies that manufacture ebikes under the Aipas brand name.  
保存此故事保存此故事亚马逊和UL（一家测试并认证产品符合安全标准的公司）在7月与多家生产Aipas品牌电动自行车的中国公司达成和解。  

The US companies accused the Chinese firms in January of claiming in Amazon listings and on Aipas’ website that the ebikes had been certified by UL, giving recognizable, brand-name assurance to online shoppers worried about risks like ebike lithium-ion battery fires. But that wasn’t true.  
这些美国公司在1月指控这些中国公司，称其在亚马逊商品列表和Aipas网站上声称电动自行车已通过UL认证，为担心电动自行车锂离子电池火灾等风险的网购者提供了知名的品牌保证。但事实并非如此。  

On July 15, a federal judge signed off on a permanent injunction in which the companies agreed that they would no longer use the UL mark. It also bars them from “assisting, aiding, or abetting any other person or business” in doing the same.  
7月15日，一名联邦法官批准了一项永久禁令，这些公司同意不再使用UL标志。该禁令还禁止他们“协助、帮助或纵容任何其他个人或企业”从事相同行为。  

The filing notes that the Chinese companies didn’t admit liability or wrongdoing tied to Amazon’s and UL’s accusations of knowingly using fraudulent safety-certification marks. (The enterprises involved are Jiangmen Meijiasheng Bicycle, Hong Kong Manchester International Trading, Shenzhen Aibosi Sport Technology, Guangzhou Aierfeile Sport Technology, and the individual Tang Shuhui, who the lawsuit alleges was involved in an Amazon selling account.)  
文件指出，这些中国公司并未承认与亚马逊和UL指控其故意使用伪造安全认证标志相关的责任或不当行为。（涉及的企业包括江门美佳盛自行车、香港曼彻斯特国际商贸、深圳艾博斯运动科技、广州爱尔菲勒运动科技，以及个人唐树辉，诉讼指控其参与了一个亚马逊销售账户。）  

But WIRED has found a Facebook account associated with Aipas Ebike still running ads that include UL’s bright red logo. A WIRED reporter was served one of the ads in mid-July, even after the companies had agreed to a permanent injunction.  
但《连线》杂志发现，一个与Aipas电动自行车相关的Facebook账户仍在投放包含UL鲜红色标志的广告。《连线》记者在7月中旬仍看到其中一条广告，尽管这些公司已同意永久禁令。  

At least one other ad using the UL mark has been actively running on Facebook since April, months after the lawsuit was filed, according to Meta's Ad Library. Another active ad including the UL mark and first posted in May appeared to disappear from the Ad Library after WIRED contacted both Aipas and UL for comment.  
据Meta广告库显示，至少另一条使用UL标志的广告自4月以来一直在Facebook上活跃运行，而诉讼 filed 已数月。另一条于5月首次发布、包含UL标志的活跃广告，在《连线》联系Aipas和UL寻求评论后似乎从广告库中消失了。  

WIRED also found that while some Aipas ebikes and their batteries did eventually receive safety certifications from a nationally recognized organization, seemingly months after the company began advertising its UL safety marks, some of those certificates are now inactive.  
《连线》还发现，尽管一些Aipas电动自行车及其电池最终获得了国家认可机构的安全认证（似乎是在公司开始宣传其UL安全标志数月后），但其中一些证书现已失效。  

A spokesperson for Amazon declined to comment on the lawsuit. Bradley Carlson, a spokesperson for UL, also declined to comment on the lawsuit but said the UL team is “aware and working to resolve” the issues related to the Facebook ads. Neither Aipas Ebike nor the lawyer representing the Chinese businesses in court responded to multiple requests for comment.  
亚马逊发言人拒绝就该诉讼置评。UL发言人布拉德利·卡尔森也拒绝评论诉讼，但表示UL团队“已意识到并正在努力解决”与Facebook广告相关的问题。Aipas电动自行车及代表这些中国企业的法庭律师均未回应多次置评请求。  

While fights over fraudulent product safety certifications don’t always break out into public view, “this is a common problem and has been a common problem for quite some time,” says Donald Mays, a longtime product safety expert who now runs his own consulting firm.  
产品安全认证欺诈纠纷并不总是公开化，但长期产品安全专家、现经营自己咨询公司的唐纳德·梅斯表示：“这是一个常见问题，而且已经持续很长时间了。”  

The trend has troubling implications, especially for electric mobility devices like ebikes. A report published by the US government in April found that 45 fatalities between 2017 and 2024 were associated with fires related to lithium-ion batteries in mobility devices, 19 of them related to ebikes alone.  
这一趋势令人担忧，尤其对电动自行车等电动移动设备而言。美国政府4月发布的一份报告发现，2017年至2024年间，有45起死亡事故与移动设备中锂离子电池引发的火灾有关，其中19起仅与电动自行车相关。  

The New York City Fire Department, which tracks lithium-ion battery fires, logged 277 fires tied to the tech in 2024, compared with 268 in 2023, and linked them to ebikes.  
追踪锂离子电池火灾的纽约市消防局记录显示，2024年与该技术相关的火灾有277起，高于2023年的268起，并将其与电动自行车联系起来。  

Many of the country’s ebike fires were associated with replacement battery packs, as noted by the Consumer Product Safety Commission, an independent federal agency. The agency has urged consumers to use only micromobility devices “designed, manufactured, and certified for compliance by an accredited laboratory with the applicable consensus safety standards,” according to its website.  
独立联邦机构消费品安全委员会指出，该国许多电动自行车火灾与更换电池组有关。该机构在其网站上敦促消费者仅使用“由认可实验室设计、制造并认证符合适用共识安全标准”的微型移动设备。  

The false certification is more prevalent on online marketplaces like Amazon, says Gabe Knight, a senior policy analyst at Consumer Reports. “Just as consumers are more at risk to buy a counterfeit product, period, from third-party sellers, they are more at risk to buy a product with a fabricated certification mark,” she says.  
消费者报告高级政策分析师盖布·奈特表示，虚假认证在亚马逊等在线市场上更为普遍。“就像消费者从第三方卖家那里购买假冒产品风险更大一样，他们购买带有伪造认证标志的产品风险也更大，”她说。  

In general, retailers who sell products to consumers directly, rather than simply hosting sales, do a better job of guaranteeing their merchandise’s safety claims, she says; these retailers usually have internal compliance programs to verify manufacturers’ basic safety claims.  
她表示，一般来说，直接向消费者销售产品（而非仅提供销售平台）的零售商更能保证其商品的安全声明；这些零售商通常有内部合规计划来核实制造商的基本安全声明。  

“It’s really polluting the marketplace when consumers can’t trust the information they see online,” Knight says.  
“当消费者无法信任他们在网上看到的信息时，这真的在污染市场，”奈特说。  

The US government seems to be interested in cracking down on fake safety certifications. In May, the Consumer Product Safety Commission posted that it is “aware of instances in which the misuse of certification marks appears to be part of broader efforts to evade US safety requirements.” It asked for public input to better understand the prevalence of counterfeit marks.  
美国政府似乎有意打击虚假安全认证。5月，消费品安全委员会发布声明称，“意识到滥用认证标志似乎是更广泛规避美国安全要求行为的一部分”。该委员会征求公众意见，以更好地了解伪造标志的普遍性。  

Separately, in June, the CPSC proposed a rule to establish federal mandatory safety standards for the lithium-ion batteries used in micromobility products, including ebikes. (Some but not all states and local governments require lithium-ion batteries sold in their localities to meet mandatory safety standards, though there are federal standards for shipping batteries.) The agency will accept public comments through August. Spokespeople for the CPSC did not respond to WIRED’s questions.  
此外，6月，CPSC提议制定一项规则，为包括电动自行车在内的微型移动产品所用锂离子电池建立联邦强制性安全标准。（部分但非所有州和地方政府要求在其地区销售的锂离子电池符合强制性安全标准，尽管电池运输已有联邦标准。）该机构将接受公众评论至8月。CPSC发言人未回应《连线》的提问。  

Scott Hardesty, a YouTube ebike reviewer who posted videos critical of Aipas last fall, says his interactions with the company pushed him to create a new reviewing procedure. “After dealing with them, I started making companies send me their UL certificates,” he tells WIRED. “I need them to send them to me in an email.” (Hardesty says he receives commissions from companies for ebike sales made through his YouTube channel.)  
去年秋季发布批评Aipas视频的YouTube电动自行车评测员斯科特·哈迪斯蒂表示，他与该公司的互动促使他制定了新的评测程序。“与他们打交道后，我开始要求公司给我发送他们的UL证书，”他告诉《连线》。“我需要他们通过电子邮件发给我。”（哈迪斯蒂表示，他通过YouTube频道产生的电动自行车销售从公司获得佣金。）  

In the Aipas lawsuit, lawyers for UL and Amazon said the companies were cracking down on counterfeits and fraudulent claims. They pointed to seven other recent legal actions against companies using fraudulent safety certification marks, including one 2023 case in which both Amazon and UL sued several Chinese companies for illegally using UL marks on smoke detectors without obtaining the required safety testing.  
在Aipas诉讼中，UL和亚马逊的律师表示，这些公司正在打击假冒产品和欺诈性声明。他们提到了另外七起近期针对使用伪造安全认证标志公司的法律行动，其中包括2023年的一起案件，亚马逊和UL共同起诉多家中国公司，因其在未获得所需安全测试的情况下在烟雾探测器上非法使用UL标志。

去年，纽约市否决了一项使用Fly电动自行车的计划
