## Data centers expected to use 4x more electricity by 2035  
数据中心预计到2035年用电量将增至四倍

Data centers are expected to use one-fifth of the electricity generated in the U.S. by 2035, four times that of today, according to a new report from BloombergNEF. A surge in AI compute will push data center capacity to nearly 200 gigawatts over the next decade, the report predicts.  
根据彭博新能源财经（BloombergNEF）的一份新报告，预计到2035年，数据中心将消耗美国发电量的五分之一，是当前的四倍。报告预测，未来十年AI计算需求的激增将推动数据中心容量接近200吉瓦。

Nearly half of that capacity will be devoted to training and inference, and most of that will remain concentrated in the U.S. By 2033, the country will host 64% of AI chips by power demand. Based on previous forecasts, those figures could be conservative. BloombergNEF’s new estimate for electricity demand in 2035 is 83% higher than what the consultancy predicted in December.  
其中近一半容量将用于AI模型训练和推理，且大部分仍将集中在美国。到2033年，按电力需求计算，美国将容纳全球64%的AI芯片。基于以往预测，这些数据可能偏保守。彭博新能源财经对2035年电力需求的新预估比其去年12月的预测高出83%。

Other organizations have raised their forecasts, too. EPRI, an electrical industry nonprofit, has more than doubled its 2024 estimate, while S&P’s forecast rose by more than a third between October and April. The revisions reflect the fevered pace of data center development across the U.S.  
其他机构也上调了预测。电力行业非营利组织EPRI将其2024年预估提高了一倍以上，而标普（S&P）的预测在去年10月至今年4月间增长了超过三分之一。这些修订反映了美国各地数据中心建设的狂热速度。

In the coming decade, BloombergNEF expects the majority of new data centers to hit electrical grids that are already strained. The PJM Interconnection, which spans Virginia to Illinois, will see 34% of its electricity go to data centers, while ERCOT, which covers most of Texas, will have to devote 22% of its generating capacity.  
未来十年，彭博新能源财经预计大多数新建数据中心将接入本已紧张的电网。覆盖弗吉尼亚至伊利诺伊州的PJM互联电网，其34%的电力将供给数据中心；而覆盖德克萨斯州大部分地区的ERCOT电网，则需将其22%的发电容量用于数据中心。

PJM, which already hosts a large number of the country’s data centers, has struggled to cope with connection requests from both large generators and large loads. It paused applications for new sources to connect to the grid for four years, putting it in a precarious position as demand continued to grow.  
已承载大量美国数据中心的PJM电网，一直难以应对大型发电商和大型用电负荷的并网申请。它曾暂停新电源并网申请长达四年，在需求持续增长的情况下使其陷入危险境地。

Though PJM reopened the queue to new generating sources in April, the situation has grown so dire that one utility, American Electric Power, has threatened to pull out of the interconnection. The supply-demand imbalance has pushed electricity prices up 76% over the past year.  
尽管PJM于今年4月重新开放了新电源并网申请队列，但情况已变得如此严峻，以至于美国电力公司（American Electric Power）威胁退出并网。供需失衡已推动电价在过去一年上涨76%。

Even with the congestion, data centers still want to connect to PJM — they represented 38% of charges in the grid manager’s most recent capacity auction.  
尽管存在拥堵，数据中心仍希望接入PJM——它们占用了该电网管理者最近一次容量拍卖中38%的配额。

Despite the U.S. claiming a majority of AI compute, data centers will continue to grow elsewhere. By 2033, if AI adoption continues along an aggressive trajectory, data centers will create 1,935 terawatt-hours of new electricity demand worldwide, nearly as much as India uses annually.  
尽管美国占据了大部分AI算力，但数据中心将在其他地区持续增长。到2033年，如果AI采用沿激进轨迹发展，全球数据中心将产生1,935太瓦时的新电力需求，几乎相当于印度全年的用电量。
