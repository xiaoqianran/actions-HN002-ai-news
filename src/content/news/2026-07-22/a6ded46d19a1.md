# Apple defeats liability for not scanning iCloud for CSAM  
苹果因未扫描iCloud中的CSAM内容而免于承担责任  

This case involves Apple’s handling of user-uploaded files hosted in private iCloud storage. Instead of adopting PhotoDNA to scan hosted files for CSAM, Apple created its own proprietary alternative, NeuralHash, which apparently wasn’t as good. So Apple U-turned on its efforts to scan for CSAM in its cloud storage. Instead, Apple implemented end-to-end encryption for iCloud files. Apple’s manuevers confused the public and seemed like an embarrassing unforced error for Apple. It also ensured pressure from governments and plaintiffs, including CSAM victims, who preferred Apple’s more interventionist approaches, which Apple had voluntarily demonstrated it was willing to do.  
本案涉及苹果对存储在私有iCloud中的用户上传文件的管理。苹果未采用PhotoDNA来扫描托管文件中的CSAM，而是创建了自己的专有替代方案NeuralHash，但显然效果不佳。因此，苹果在扫描iCloud云存储中的CSAM方面来了个180度大转弯。相反，苹果为iCloud文件实施了端到端加密。苹果的这一系列操作让公众感到困惑，似乎是一个令人尴尬的、本可避免的错误。这也确保了来自政府和原告（包括CSAM受害者）的压力，他们更倾向于苹果更主动干预的做法，而苹果曾自愿表示愿意这样做。  

This lawsuit represents a full-scale attack on Apple and Section 230. “Plaintiffs allege that Apple’s failure to implement any known CSAM detection is a design defect because Apple can safely implement readily available features to prevent the spread of known CSAM but has continuously failed to do so.” Prior blog post. The court dismisses the third amended complaint, which tees this case up for the Ninth Circuit, where (as usual) anything could happen.  
此诉讼是对苹果和第230条的全方位攻击。原告指控，苹果未能实施任何已知的CSAM检测功能属于设计缺陷，因为苹果本可以安全地实施现成的功能来防止已知CSAM的传播，但却持续未能做到。”之前的博客文章。法院驳回了第三次修订的起诉书，将此案提交第九巡回上诉法院，在那里（和往常一样）任何事情都可能发生。  

* * *  
The court reiterates that Section 230 applies to the plaintiffs’ claims: First, Plaintiffs’ claims treat Apple as a publisher or speaker of the CSAM content that animates Plaintiffs’ injuries. Fundamentally, Plaintiffs contend that Apple has elected to permit users to disseminate and share third-party CSAM content when it could have—and, in their view, should have—used readily available technology to prevent the distribution of child pornography depicting the Plaintiffs in this putative class. The duties Plaintiffs seek to invoke “spring[ ] from the defendant’s status as publisher,” and consequently, “immunity applies.”  
法院重申第230条适用于原告的索赔：首先，原告的索赔将苹果视为引发原告伤害的CSAM内容的发布者或发言者。从根本上说，原告认为，苹果选择允许用户传播和分享第三方CSAM内容，而本可以——在原告看来也应该——使用现成的技术来防止描绘该拟议集体中原告的儿童色情制品的传播。原告试图援引的义务“源于被告作为发布者的身份”，因此，“豁免适用”。  

Second, immunity also applies because “the means to avoid liability requires [Apple] to act as a publisher.” As a result, Apple is entitled to complete immunity under § 230. Citing Doe 1 v. Meta, the court says: Plaintiffs’ injuries are the direct result of the actions of third parties who used iCloud to share CSAM, a use Apple neither explicitly condones nor prevents (even assuming—as alleged in the TAC—that Apple was aware of the use of iCloud for this purpose)….though Plaintiffs allege that Apple knew that its tools were likely to be used to distribute child pornography (as confirmed by the internal Apple text messages at the center of this case), under the current state of the law, Apple is still entitled to immunity under § 230—irrespective of that general knowledge….  
其次，豁免也适用，因为“避免责任的手段要求[苹果]扮演发布者的角色”。因此，苹果有权根据第230条获得完全豁免。引用Doe 1诉Meta案，法院表示：原告的伤害是使用iCloud分享CSAM的第三方行为的直接结果，苹果既未明确纵容也未阻止这种使用（即使假设——如第三次修订起诉书所 allegation——苹果知晓iCloud被用于此目的）……尽管原告指控苹果明知其工具可能被用于传播儿童色情制品（如本案核心的苹果内部短信所证实），但根据现行法律，苹果仍有权根据第230条获得豁免——无论这种一般性知识如何……  

Plaintiffs cannot avoid the fact that a tool that detects CSAM must review CSAM to make such a determination. And while Apple could have taken steps to do so—as its competitors have done by using PhotoDNA—Grindr confirms that § 230 bars claims arising from the design decisions Apple could have taken where those claims relate to Apple’s role facilitating the communication and content of others (A reminder that the defendant’s scienter is irrelevant to Section 230).  
原告无法回避这样一个事实：检测CSAM的工具必须审查CSAM才能做出此类判定。虽然苹果本可以采取步骤这样做——如其竞争对手通过使用PhotoDNA所做的那样——但Grindr案确认，第230条禁止因苹果本可采取的设计决策而产生的索赔，只要这些索赔涉及苹果在促进他人通信和内容方面的角色（提醒：被告的主观意图与第230条无关）。  

The plaintiffs tried to fit into the new Section 230 exceptions created in Doe v. Twitter, but the court rebuffs the move: This case does not concern or even discuss Apple’s content reporting systems; it concerns Apple’s “failure to implement industry-standard safeguards” against the dissemination of CSAM. Though reporting systems and CSAM safeguards may both be described as “defects,” the latter requires the Court to treat Apple as a publisher. Twitter “could fulfill its purported duty to cure reporting infrastructure deficiencies without monitoring, removing, or in any way engaging with third-party content”; Apple cannot fulfill a duty to institute CSAM safeguards without deploying a tool like NeuralHash or PhotoDNA. Both NeuralHash and PhotoDNA were built to monitor and report violative images uploaded to company servers. Yet just the decision regarding whether to deploy either tool is a choice related to content moderation. Also, Apple didn’t fail to satisfy any duty to report items to NCMEC if it never identified CSAM in the first place.  
原告试图套入Doe诉Twitter案创建的第230条新例外，但法院驳回了这一举动：本案不涉及甚至未讨论苹果的内容报告系统；它涉及苹果“未能实施行业标准保障措施”以防止CSAM的传播。虽然报告系统和CSAM保障措施都可能被描述为“缺陷”，但后者要求法院将苹果视为发布者。Twitter“可以在不监控、删除或以任何方式参与第三方内容的情况下履行其所谓治愈报告基础设施缺陷的义务”；苹果若不部署像NeuralHash或PhotoDNA这样的工具，就无法履行建立CSAM保障措施的义务。NeuralHash和PhotoDNA都是为了监控和报告上传到公司服务器的违规图像而构建的。然而，是否部署任一工具的决定本身就是一个与内容审核相关的选择。此外，如果苹果从未识别出CSAM，它就没有未能履行向NCMEC报告项目的任何义务。  

The Lemmon v. Snap workaround fails: “all of Plaintiffs’ claims here are inexorably linked to third-party content; Plaintiffs do not allege that Apple created content like a Snapchat filter that caused them harm.” The Roommates.com workaround also fails: “Plaintiffs do not allege that Apple modified or augmented the CSAM on its servers in any way.”  
Lemmon诉Snap的变通方法失败：“原告在此的所有索赔都与第三方内容密不可分；原告未指控苹果创造了像Snapchat滤镜那样导致他们受害的内容。”Roommates.com的变通方法也失败：“原告未指控苹果以任何方式修改或增强了其服务器上的CSAM。”

The case reaches its inevitable denouement of a win for Apple. However, Judge Wise remains troubled about its implications.
