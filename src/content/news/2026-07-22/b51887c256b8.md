# Title: “No AI” Statements Are Much More Than Mere Statements  
# 标题：“无AI”声明远不止是声明  

---

**Content:**  
**内容：**

“No AI” Statements Are Much More Than Mere Statements 21 Jul, 2026 I came across orchids’ post sharing their conflicted feelings on whether or not “No AI” statements are genuinely necessary, helpful and/or useful. In their post, orchids argues that the onus is on the viewer/reader to distinguish human-created content from AI-generated one; it can be an indication of questionable quality of your work if it leads the viewer/reader to believe it was AI-generated, and as long as your voice and style are embedded in your works, it’s already enough (at least this is my interpretation of the final paragraph of their post). I strongly disagree with most of the post, and I think the post missed some important considerations. I believe that, now more than ever, it’s necessary and useful to label your human-made content as such because AI’s ability to replicate the final results of human work will only improve over time.  

“无AI”声明远不止是声明 2026年7月21日 我偶然看到用户orchids的帖子，分享了他们对“无AI”声明是否真正必要、有帮助或实用的矛盾感受。在帖子中，orchids认为区分人类创作内容与AI生成内容的责任在于观众/读者；如果作品让观众/读者误认为是AI生成的，这可能表明你的作品质量存疑，只要你的声音和风格融入作品，就已经足够（至少这是我对其帖子最后一段的解读）。我强烈反对帖中的大部分观点，我认为该帖子忽略了一些重要考量。我相信，如今比以往任何时候都更有必要且有用地标注你的人类创作内容，因为AI复制人类工作最终成果的能力只会随时间推移而提升。

---

“Made by human” statements have been around since before AI Fancy handmade kitchenware is labeled “Handmade in [country]”; mom-and-pop bakeries label their breads “Homemade”; indie games and animated series make explicit statements that all the art was hand-drawn. There’s a reason why the act of making such statements well predates the rise of AI as you simply cannot rely on the other side—be it the consumer, the viewer or the reader—to have the “skills” to tell what’s made by human vs machine (now AI too). Just as machines have been able to create physical products that rival or surpass handcrafted ones, AI can already create content—audio, video and text—that, in many aspects, rivals human-made one. You may argue that you can tell when the text was AI-generated, but that’s now; what about the future, as AI gets better and better? Even right now in 2026, I’m not convinced anyone can consistently distinguish what’s written by AI and what’s not. The line is even blurrier when the content is part-AI, part-human (often called “AI-assisted”). The r/isthisai subreddit has millions of weekly visitors because it’s harder and harder to spot AI-generated content. You’re never expected to be able to tell handmade ceramics from machine-made ones, so why should anyone be expected to be able to tell human-made content from AI-generated ones?  

“人类制作”声明在AI出现前就已存在 精美的手工厨具标有“手工制作于[国家]”；家庭面包店将其面包标为“自制”；独立游戏和动画系列明确声明所有艺术均为手绘。这类声明早于AI兴起就有其原因——你根本无法指望对方（无论是消费者、观众还是读者）具备区分人类与机器（如今也包括AI）作品的“技能”。正如机器能制造媲美甚至超越手工艺品的实体产品，AI已能创作在许多方面媲美人类作品的内容——音频、视频和文本。你或许争辩说你能分辨文本是否AI生成，但那是现在；未来AI越来越强时呢？即使在2026年的当下，我也不相信有人能持续一致地区分AI生成与非AI生成内容。当内容部分由AI、部分由人类完成（常称“AI辅助”）时，界限更加模糊。subreddit论坛r/isthisai每周有数百万访问者，因为 spotting AI生成内容正变得越来越难。你从不被期待能区分手工陶瓷与机制陶瓷，为何有人该被期待能区分人类创作与AI生成内容？

---

There was a time when people automatically assumed a body of text was AI-generated when they see the use of em dashes. As someone who loves using the em dash, that was an infuriating trend that simply led to many false positives and frustrated writers. I’ve had my comments on Reddit labeled as written by ChatGPT just because they were well-written and -formatted. I’ve put my writings in AI detectors before and it would come back saying it’s “80% written by AI.” I also recently came across this comment on a Reddit thread discussing some audio software: “at this point i just assume unless the creator is screaming from the rooftops "I HAND CODED THIS" its vibe coded.” u/Rude-Armadillo-6963 View original Reddit screenshot  

曾有一段时间，人们一看到长破折号（em dash）的使用就自动认定文本是AI生成的。作为热爱使用长破折号的人，这种趋势令人愤怒——它仅导致大量误判并挫伤写作者。我的Reddit评论曾只因“写得工整、格式规范”就被标记为ChatGPT所写。我曾将作品放入AI检测器，结果返回“80%由AI撰写”。我最近还在一个讨论音频软件的Reddit帖子中看到这条评论：“到现在我干脆认为，除非创作者在屋顶大喊‘我手写了这段代码’，否则都是氛围编码（vibe coded）。” 用户u/Rude-Armadillo-6963 查看原Reddit截图

---

It’s clear that viewers are terrible at detecting AI-generated content and some have even assumed a “generated by AI unless declared otherwise” position. I don’t blame them as the tell-tale signs of AI constantly change and dwindle—how can we expect our audience to keep up with that? It’s never about quality, voice and/or style  

显然，观众在检测AI生成内容方面表现糟糕，有些人甚至采取了“除非声明否则视为AI生成”的立场。我不怪他们，因为AI的识别特征不断变化且减少——我们怎能指望受众跟上这种变化？这从来与质量、声音或风格无关

---

This paragraph that orchids wrote in their post saddens me: If someone thinks my work is LLM-generated, then my work is probably shit and doesn't have a distinct voice. It's boring. And it might as well be AI because the difference doesn't really matter at the end of the day. I think it’s highly problematic to believe that one’s work “might as well be AI” if it’s of low quality (but how is “quality” even defined?), lacks a distinct voice and/or boring. Academic articles and dissertations can be dry and boring to most; a user guide of a piece of software can lack a distinct voice; and an essay written by a high school student may be low-quality—but I can appreciate them all the same when they were fully written by people, not AI. Not all works need to be interesting or to have a voice. Technical writing is an example where you often do not want those qualities, and it’s a field of study that some people go to school for to specifically develop skills to write in technical contexts.  

orchids帖子中的这段让我难过：如果有人觉得我的作品是大语言模型（LLM）生成的，那我的作品可能就是垃圾且没有独特声音。它很无聊。它可能干脆就是AI写的，因为最终区别并不重要。我认为这种信念非常有问题——如果作品质量低（但“质量”如何定义？）、缺乏独特声音或无聊，那它“可能干脆就是AI写的”。学术文章和论文对大多数人可能枯燥无聊；软件用户指南可能缺乏独特声音；高中生写的文章可能质量低——但当我得知它们完全由人类而非AI撰写时，我同样能欣赏它们。并非所有作品都需要有趣或有声音。技术写作就是一个例子，你通常不希望它具备这些特质，而这是有人专门去学校学习以培养技术语境写作技能的领域。

---

The truth is that people still value handmade items and homemade foods that can easily be machine-made not because they’re better (though sometimes they are), but because we know someone—or multiple people—put in the time, energy and effort to create it. I argue that it’s exactly the same reason why many people yearn for text written by human and “hand-coded” apps rather than vibe coded ones. A blog post fully written by a blogger may have typos, grammatical errors and incoherent paragraphs, but maybe that’s because the blogger isn’t good at writing and is therefore actively working on their writing; maybe the blogger is a language learner; or maybe the blogger is an extremely busy new parent who doesn’t have the time to proofread but still wants to write. Despite the “lower quality,” that blog post would still be valuable because it was the result of dedication, efforts and thoughts by a person.  

事实是，人们仍然珍视那些本可机器制造的手工制品和自制食品，并非因为它们更好（尽管有时确实如此），而是因为我们知道有人——或多人——投入了时间、精力和努力来创造它。我认为这正是许多人渴望人类撰写的文本和“手写代码”应用而非“氛围编码”应用的完全相同原因。博主完全撰写的博客文章可能有拼写错误、语法问题和 incoherent 段落，但也许是因为博主不擅长写作因而正在积极练习；也许博主是语言学习者；或者博主是极其忙碌的新手父母，没时间校对但仍想写作。尽管“质量较低”，那篇博客文章仍有价值，因为它是某人的投入、努力和思想的结果。

---

On a more practical level, attempting to use quality, voice or style as touchstones for human-made content is inherently futile as AI can generate any of those now—it’d a huge mistake to think that AI cannot generate text with a distinct voice or style. These days, I’ve even found myself having to remove quality to get around “AI detectors” on Reddit. I recently published a detailed, well-formatted post to a subreddit, and it got automatically removed because it was flagged as AI-generated. To get pass the AI detector, I had to reduce the level of detail, remove the formatting (bullet points, headings, italics etc.) and rewrite it like someone word-vomited it on a phone—I was furious by the end.  

从更实际的层面看，试图用质量、声音或风格作为人类创作内容的试金石本质上是徒劳的，因为AI如今已能生成所有这些——认为AI无法生成具有独特声音或风格的文本是巨大错误。如今，我甚至发现自己不得不降低质量以绕过Reddit上的“AI检测器”。我最近在一个subreddit发布了一篇详细、格式良好的帖子，却因被标记为AI生成而自动删除。为通过AI检测器，我不得不减少细节、移除格式（项目符号、标题、斜体等），并重写成像有人在手机上仓促打字的样子——最终我愤怒不已。
