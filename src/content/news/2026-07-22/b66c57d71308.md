# Title: Let Tom Hiddleston be your guide to Pompeii's final day  
**标题：让汤姆·希德勒斯顿带你探秘庞贝末日**

---

When Mount Vesuvius erupted in 79 CE, it spewed molten rock, pumice, and hot ash over the cities of Pompeii and Herculaneum, killing thousands of people. It’s one of the most famous natural disasters in human history, so naturally there have been countless documentaries about Pompeii (and at least one popular song). But only one features actor Tom Hiddleston taking on the role of a “time detective” to bring the people of Pompeii to vivid life. That would be National Geographic’s new three-part docuseries, Pompeii: Out of Time.  
公元79年，维苏威火山喷发，将熔岩、浮石和滚烫的火山灰倾泻在庞贝和赫库兰尼姆两座城市上，夺走了数千人的生命。这是人类历史上最著名的自然灾害之一，因此自然产生了无数关于庞贝的纪录片（至少还有一首流行歌曲）。但只有一部纪录片由演员汤姆·希德勒斯顿扮演“时间侦探”，让庞贝的居民栩栩如生地重现眼前。那就是国家地理频道的新三集纪录片《庞贝：出时记》。

---

The Marvel connection made Out of Time happen. Executive producer Kevin Wright was also a producer on the Disney+ series Loki, which included a scene where Hiddleston’s Loki travels through time to visit Pompeii. Hiddleston also studied classics at Cambridge University and had visited the Pompeii archaeological site as a teenager, which the Marvel star attributes to indirectly influencing his career path into acting.  
漫威的关联促成了《出时记》的诞生。执行制片人凯文·赖特也曾是Disney+剧集《洛基》的制片人，该剧包含一个场景，希德勒斯顿饰演的洛基穿越时空访问庞贝。希德勒斯顿还在剑桥大学学习过古典学，并在青少年时期参观过庞贝考古遗址，这位漫威明星认为这间接影响了他走向演艺事业的路径。

---

“There is just a passion to the way Tom tells stories that’s infectious,” Wright told Ars. “We knew that if we could capture that and put it into [the series], it might interest people who wouldn’t normally want to watch a documentary about Pompeii.”  
“汤姆讲故事的方式有一种极具感染力的热情，”赖特告诉Ars网站。“我们知道，如果我们能捕捉到这一点并融入[这部系列剧]，它可能会吸引那些通常不想看庞贝纪录片的人。”

---

The docuseries combines the usual historical and scientific facts with Hiddleston’s personal journey of discovery. He homes in on three people in particular and follows them over the city’s final 24 hours to discover whether or not they survived: a teenage boy, a local businesswoman, and a Praetorian guard.  
这部纪录片将通常的历史和科学事实与希德勒斯顿个人的探索旅程相结合。他特别聚焦于三个人，并追踪他们在城市最后24小时里的经历，以查明他们是否幸存：一名少年、一名当地女商人，和一名禁卫军士兵。

---

Stilted re-enactments are replaced with scripted dramatic scenes and high production values to make it all feel more cinematic. It’s an unusual approach, if only because we’re so conditioned to what a documentary “should” be. But the blended approach makes for a powerful experience.  
生硬的重演被剧本化的戏剧场景和高制作水准所取代，让整体更具电影感。这是一种不寻常的方法，仅仅因为我们太习惯于纪录片“应该”是什么样子。但这种融合的方法创造了强大的体验。

---

“We felt we could use the imagination to make a more human story. This isn’t just ancient history; these were real people, and their lives were not dramatically different than ours even though they lived 2,000 years ago,” said Wright. “One of the most freeing moments was when we came to this idea of ‘historia,’ the merging of imagination and historical fact, and that ‘storytelling’ and ‘history’ in Latin are the same word. It was Hiddleston who put us onto that. When we went to our historians, our experts, with that word, we were met with nothing but enthusiasm. There seems to be a real movement in archaeology of, ‘This is how you have to approach it now.’”  
“我们觉得我们可以利用想象力来讲述一个更人性化的故事。这不仅仅是古代历史；这些都是真实的人，即使他们生活在2000年前，他们的生活与我们的也没有天壤之别，”赖特说。“最解放的时刻之一是我们想到‘historia’（历史/故事）这个概念，即想象与历史事实的融合，而且拉丁语中‘storytelling’（讲故事）和‘history’（历史）是同一个词。是希德勒斯顿让我们想到这一点。当我们带着这个词去咨询我们的历史学家和专家时，我们得到的只有热情。考古学界似乎真的有一种运动，认为‘你现在必须这样去接近它。’”

Factual science does get its due. Geologist and science communicator Chris Jackson, for instance, is on hand to clear up any misconceptions the general public might have about the eruption and its aftermath.  
事实科学也确实得到了应有的地位。例如，地质学家和科学传播者克里斯·杰克逊随时准备澄清公众可能对喷发及其后果存在的任何误解。

---

“The most common misconception people have about Pompeii was the belief that people were killed by lava,” Jackson told Ars. “That’s understandable. In Pompeii and Herculaneum, around all of Naples, a lot of the buildings are made from these black hard igneous rocks, cooled lava. When you tell people that what led to the devastation in 79 AD was nothing to do with lava, their faces sort of drop. It’s a bit sad to hear that it was this more devastating thing called a pyroclastic flow.”  
“关于庞贝，人们最常见的误解是认为人们是被熔岩杀死的，”杰克逊告诉Ars网站。“这可以理解。在庞贝和赫库兰尼姆，在那不勒斯周围，许多建筑都是由这些黑色坚硬的火成岩——冷却的熔岩建造的。当你告诉人们，导致公元79年毁灭的原因与熔岩无关时，他们的表情会变得沮丧。听到毁灭的元凶是一种叫做火山碎屑流的东西，有点令人难过。”

---

Outrunning those flows would not have been an option. Based on reconstructions, Jackson estimates that the pyroclastic flows would be moving between 100 and 200 kilometers per hour (60–120 mph). “Imagine the speed you can drive on the freeway in the US and double that,” he said. “You can’t outrun a car. So there was no way you were going to outrun these things. That’s what makes these so devastating. And remember the depth perspective. It’d be traveling a few hundred kilometers an hour. But to you from 10 kilometers away, you’d be like, ‘That’s going pretty slowly.’ So it needs to be seen through the lens of the understanding of people 2,000 years ago.”  
想要跑赢那些碎屑流是不可能的。根据重建，杰克逊估计火山碎屑流的速度在每小时100到200公里之间（60-120英里/小时）。“想象一下你在美国高速公路上能开到的速度，然后翻倍，”他说。“你跑不过汽车。所以你不可能跑赢这些东西。这就是它们如此具有毁灭性的原因。还要记住距离的视角。它以几百公里每小时的速度移动。但如果你在10公里外看，你会觉得‘那移动得相当慢。’所以需要透过2000年前人们的理解视角来看待它。”

---

The eruption of Vesuvius dramatically altered the geography of the Bay of Naples, per Jackson. “These pyroclastic flows actually changed the shoreline shape,” he said. “Some went through Vesuvius and Pompeii, which at the time had beaches. They were next to the sea. Now there’s some distance from it because [the flows] added new land beyond the settlement and reshaped the coastline.”  
据杰克逊说，维苏威火山的喷发戏剧性地改变了那不勒斯湾的地理。“这些火山碎屑流实际上改变了海岸线的形状，”他说。“有些流经了维苏威和庞贝，当时那里有海滩。它们紧邻大海。现在那里离海有了一段距离，因为[碎屑流]在定居点之外增加了新的土地，并重塑了海岸线。”

---

The eruption also triggered a small tsunami. That said, “A lot of what’s causing some of the strange shape of the Bay of Naples is actually related to another volcano to the northwest of Vesuvius that nobody  
喷发还引发了一场小型海啸。话说回来，“那不勒斯湾一些奇怪形状的成因，实际上与维苏威西北部的另一座火山有关，而这座火山无人……”
