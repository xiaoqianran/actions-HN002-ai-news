**Title:** LLM Unlearning for Cyber Defense: A Survey on Methods, Challenges, and Emerging Threats  
**中文标题：** 大语言模型遗忘用于网络防御：方法、挑战与新兴威胁综述  

**Authors:** Ruppikha Sree Shankar, Abhishek Bhardwaj, Arnav Doshi, Anusri Nagarajan, Troy Paulus Asia, Saptarshi Sengupta  
**作者：** Ruppikha Sree Shankar, Abhishek Bhardwaj, Arnav Doshi, Anusri Nagarajan, Troy Paulus Asia, Saptarshi Sengupta  

---

**Abstract:**  
LLMs are increasingly deployed in security-critical systems across healthcare, finance, education, and decision support, yet their inability to forget creates serious cybersecurity, privacy, and safety risks. Sensitive personal information, copyrighted material, hazardous domain knowledge, and memorized training data remain encoded across billions of parameters long after deployment, leaving models vulnerable to extraction, jailbreak attacks, membership inference, and regulatory non-compliance. Real-world incidents, from chatbots regenerating private information to fabricated legal citations producing direct legal and financial cost, place the problem at the center of the emerging-threats landscape rather than the realm of speculation.  

大语言模型（LLM）正日益部署在医疗、金融、教育和决策支持等安全关键型系统中，然而其“无法遗忘”的特性引发了严重的网络安全、隐私和安全风险。敏感个人信息、受版权保护材料、危险领域知识以及记忆的训练数据在部署后仍长期编码在数十亿参数中，使模型易受数据提取、越狱攻击、成员推断和违规风险。现实世界中的事件——从聊天机器人重现私人信息到伪造法律引用导致直接的法律和财务成本——已将这一问题置于新兴威胁格局的核心，而非推测领域。  

---

Because retraining billion-parameter models on revised corpora is computationally infeasible, and because knowledge within an LLM is distributed and entangled across parameters rather than localized to identifiable units, LLM unlearning has emerged as the principal cyber defense response, aiming to remove or suppress targeted knowledge from a trained model without retraining and without eroding what the model should still know.  

由于在修订语料上重新训练十亿参数模型在计算上不可行，且 LLM 中的知识是分布式且纠缠于参数中而非集中于可识别的单元，LLM 遗忘已成为主要的网络防御应对策略，旨在无需重新训练且不损害模型应保留知识的前提下，移除或抑制训练模型中的目标知识。  

---

A central question, however, remains unresolved. Do current methods genuinely remove knowledge, or do they only stop the model from expressing it under ordinary prompting conditions? This survey examines LLM unlearning through the lens of security, robustness, and verifiable forgetting, with primary focus on gradient-based methods, which have come to dominate the field due to their compatibility with existing training pipelines and their scalability to billion-parameter models.  

然而，一个核心问题仍未解决：当前方法是否真正移除了知识，还是仅在常规提示条件下阻止模型表达它？本 survey 从安全性、鲁棒性和可验证遗忘的角度审视 LLM 遗忘，主要聚焦于基于梯度的方法——因其与现有训练流程的兼容性及对十亿参数模型的可扩展性，已主导该领域。
