**Title: Flock Credibility Lost as It Repeatedly Lies to City Councils, Police, & Public**  
**标题：Flock 信誉尽失：屡次对市议会、警方及公众撒谎**

---

The ACLU documents how an automatic license plate reader company has lied about its operations, signaling a need for reputable governments to avoid working with Flock Safety.  
美国公民自由联盟（ACLU）记录了一家自动车牌识别系统公司如何在运营中撒谎，这明确表明信誉良好的政府机构应避免与 Flock Safety 合作。

During a city council meeting in a suburb of Wisconsin in April, the city of Oshkosh considered whether it should approve a contract to use automatic license plate readers (ALPR) from Flock Safety, a prominent company that provides ALPRs to law enforcement agencies across the country.  
今年四月，在威斯康星州一个郊区的市议会会议上，奥什科什市审议是否应批准一份合同，使用来自 Flock Safety 的自动车牌识别系统（ALPR）。Flock Safety 是一家向全国执法机构提供 ALPR 的知名公司。

During the meeting, one city council member asked Flock if the company’s ALPR system created heat maps that could reveal where a particular vehicle had driven over a period of time. Flock’s chief information security officer, who was in attendance, told the council that Flock’s system did not “create a pattern or heat map of an individual’s movement” through the tracking of their vehicles.  
会议期间，一名市议员询问 Flock，其 ALPR 系统是否会生成热力图，以显示特定车辆在一段时间内的行驶位置。出席会议的 Flock 首席信息安全官告诉议会，Flock 的系统不会“通过跟踪车辆来创建个人移动的模式或热力图”。

At the end of that meeting, the Oshkosh City Council approved a contract with Flock. The very next morning, the city learned that Flock had lied.  
会议结束时，奥什科什市议会批准了与 Flock 的合同。第二天早上，该市得知 Flock 撒谎了。

Later that day, the city council reconvened to discuss what it had learned. Confronting Flock, Oshkosh Deputy Mayor Joe Stephenson said “I don't know how this body can govern if someone tells untruths, mistruths, exaggerated truths. I don't know how I can make a decision or discern what's right or what's wrong, or even the capabilities of this system if you lie to me.”  
当天晚些时候，市议会再次召开会议讨论此事。奥什科什市副市长乔·斯蒂芬森面对 Flock  representatives 说：“如果有人告诉不实之词、错误信息或夸大其词，我不知道这个机构如何治理。如果你对我撒谎，我不知道如何做出决定，或分辨对错，甚至不了解这个系统的真实能力。”

“Shame on them,” Oshkosh Mayor Matt Mugerauer added. “All I'm saying is if you give bad information, then I just don't want to work with you.”  
“真可耻，”奥什科什市长马特·穆格劳尔补充道。“我的意思是，如果你提供错误信息，那我就不想与你们合作。”

Ultimately, the Oshkosh City Council voted to immediately revoke its approval, thereby setting a record for the shortest time between a city approving and cancelling a Flock contract: one day.  
最终，奥什科什市议会投票决定立即撤销批准，创造了城市批准与取消 Flock 合同之间时间间隔最短的记录：一天。

Flock later admitted that its ALPR system does indeed produce a “heat map” that shows where “point-in-time images have been captured of a vehicle” for up to an entire month.  
Flock 后来承认，其 ALPR 系统确实会生成“热力图”，显示“车辆在特定时间点被拍摄的位置”，时间跨度可长达整整一个月。

However, the company chose to respond to the revocation of its contract by attacking the City of Oshkosh and its city council, complaining that Flock had “not [been] afforded the opportunity” to explain its lie after being caught.  
然而，该公司选择通过攻击奥什科什市及其市议会来回应合同被撤销，抱怨 Flock 在被抓包后“没有（被给予）机会”解释其谎言。

Flock also sought to trivialize its factually inaccurate statement by categorizing it as “one small misconception” and referring to the dispute over the system’s heat map tracking feature as “a minor nuance.”  
Flock 还试图将其与事实不符的陈述轻描淡写，将其归类为“一个小误解”，并将关于系统热力图跟踪功能的争议称为“一个细微差别”。

What happened in Oshkosh was not an isolated incident. Rather, it reflects a pattern of Flock regularly misleading or even lying about its business practices, safety record, commitment to privacy, and efforts to protect vulnerable populations.  
奥什科什发生的事件并非孤立事件。相反，它反映了 Flock 经常就其商业行为、安全记录、对隐私的承诺以及保护弱势群体的努力误导公众甚至撒谎的模式。

And as was the case in Oshkosh, Flock’s lies are not just directed at the general public; they often specifically target Flock’s potential government customers.  
与奥什科什的情况一样，Flock 的谎言不仅针对普通公众；它们还经常专门针对 Flock 潜在的政府客户。

The urgent takeaway for government officials and police departments is that they should be extremely hesitant to believe anything Flock tells them about its company, its products, or its commitment to safety and privacy.  
政府官员和警察部门亟需明白的是，他们应极度犹豫，不应轻信 Flock 关于其公司、产品或对安全和隐私承诺的任何说法。

**Flock's Pattern of Lies**  
**Flock 的谎言模式**

This is far from the first time Flock has misled the public and elected officials. The company has demonstrated a pattern of treating legitimate operational questions and concerns not as problems to be solved, but rather as mere public relations issues.  
这远非 Flock 首次误导公众和民选官员。该公司已展示出一种模式：将合理的运营问题和担忧视为公关问题，而非需要解决的问题。

In Colorado, Loveland Police Chief Tim Doran raised concern that federal agents were accessing the town’s ALPR data. Flock responded by telling the chief that federal agencies no longer had access to Loveland’s license plate readers, and had their CEO reiterate to the press that federal data sharing was a non-issue because Flock had no federal contracts.  
在科罗拉多州，洛弗兰德警察局长蒂姆·多兰曾担忧联邦探员正在访问该镇的 ALPR 数据。Flock 回应称，联邦机构已无法访问洛弗兰德的车牌识别器，并让其 CEO 向媒体重申，联邦数据共享不是问题，因为 Flock 没有联邦合同。

After contradictory information later came to light, the company was forced to admit that it did, in fact, have contracts with U.S. Customs and Border Protection (CBP) and Homeland Security (DHS) for pilot projects that gave those agencies direct access to license data.  
后来，矛盾的信息浮出水面，该公司被迫承认，它实际上与美国海关和边境保护局（CBP）及国土安全部（DHS）有试点项目合同，这些项目使这些机构能直接访问车牌数据。

“We clearly communicated poorly,” Flock’s CEO said, acknowledging that Flock’s “public statements inadvertently provided inaccurate information.”  
“我们的沟通显然很糟糕，”Flock 的 CEO 说，承认 Flock 的“公开声明无意中提供了不准确的信息”。

Last year, reports revealed that, even in the absence of federal contracts, cooperating police officers and departments were regularly sharing Flock ALPR data and search results with immigration agencies like U.S. Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) and CBP.  
去年，报道揭露，即使在没有联邦合同的情况下，合作的警察和部门也定期与美国移民和海关执法局（ICE）及 CBP 等移民机构分享 Flock ALPR 数据和搜索结果。

The company responded to the reports with a disingenuous blog entitled “Does Flock Share Data With ICE? No. Flock Does Not Work With ICE.”  
该公司以一篇虚伪的博客回应报道，标题为“Flock 是否与 ICE 共享数据？不。Flock 不与 ICE 合作。”

In its blog, the company pushed back by asserting that “ICE does not have direct access to Flock cameras, systems, or data” and that license data is “owned and controlled by the customer.”  
在博客中，该公司反驳道：“ICE 无法直接访问 Flock 的摄像头、系统或数据”，并且车牌数据“由客户拥有和控制”。

Flock knew that, despite not being a customer, ICE had indirect access to Flock’s data and system through the company’s state and local law enforcement customers.  
Flock 知道，尽管 ICE 不是其客户，但通过该公司州和地方执法客户，ICE 能间接访问 Flock 的数据和系统。
