# Anthropic’s $1.5B copyright settlement approved; only 350 authors opted out  
# Anthropic 15亿美元版权和解获批准；仅350名作者选择退出

---

On Monday, a judge approved a $1.5 billion settlement between Anthropic and authors, ending the largest copyright class-action ever certified and granting the largest copyright settlement ever reached.  
周一，一名法官批准了 Anthropic 与作者之间达成的 15 亿美元和解协议，结束了有史以来被认证的最大规模版权集体诉讼，并创下了史上最高金额的版权和解记录。

Back in May, some authors fought to block the settlement, which was proposed after the court ruled that Anthropic training AI on books was fair use; however, its piracy of works was likely not.  
早在五月，部分作者曾试图阻止这项和解。该和解方案是在法院裁定 Anthropic 使用书籍训练 AI 属于“合理使用”之后提出的；但法院同时认为，其对作品的“盗用”行为可能不构成合理使用。

Authors opposing the settlement argued that lawyers’ fees were too high and authors’ payouts were too low.  
反对和解的作者认为，律师费过高，而作者获得的赔偿过低。

Hoping to avoid accepting the estimated $3,000-per-work payout and file separate lawsuits to seek higher damages, a handful of authors tried to opt out past the deadline.  
为了避开每部作品约 3000 美元的预估赔偿，并提起单独诉讼以寻求更高赔偿，少数作者试图在截止日期后退出和解。

In her order, US District Judge Araceli Martínez-Olguín overruled objections to the settlement as lacking merit.  
美国地区法官 Araceli Martínez-Olguín 在命令中驳回了对和解协议缺乏依据的异议。

She emphasized that about 95 percent of the class received notifications and approximately 91 percent of authors and publishers impacted have already filed claims.  
她强调，约有 95% 的集体成员收到了通知，约 91% 受影响作者和出版商已提交索赔。

Only 350 class members opted out, while another 54 sought to object or filed late opt-out requests, Martínez-Olguín said.  
Martínez-Olguín 表示，仅有 350 名集体成员选择退出，另有 54 人提出异议或提交了逾期退出申请。

The high rate of participation suggested that most authors received timely notice of the distribution plan, agreed it was “fair,” and supported the settlement, Martínez-Olguín wrote.  
Martínez-Olguín 写道，如此高的参与率表明，大多数作者及时收到了分配计划通知，认为其“公平”，并支持该和解。

She also noted that the $3,000-per-work payouts were “four times the minimum statutory damages.”  
她还指出，每部作品 3000 美元的赔偿是“法定最低赔偿金的四倍”。

---

Although Martínez-Olguín approved the total settlement amount, she reduced both lawyers’ fees and the requested awards for the three authors who represented the class in the litigation.  
尽管 Martínez-Olguín 批准了和解总金额，但她削减了律师费以及代表集体参与诉讼的三名作者所要求的服务报酬。

Lawyers originally asked for 20 percent of the settlement in fees, requesting $300 million.  
律师最初要求获得和解金额 20% 的费用，即 3 亿美元。

Ahead of Martínez-Olguín’s ruling, that percentage was reduced to 12.5, with the total fees requested amounting to approximately $187 million.  
在 Martínez-Olguín 裁决前，该比例已降至 12.5%，请求的总费用约为 1.87 亿美元。

The judge ruled that 12.5 percent was too high, however, cutting fees down to less than 7 percent of the settlement fund, which is about $101 million.  
但法官裁定 12.5% 过高，将费用削减至和解基金不足 7%，即约 1.01 亿美元。

Some of those fees include future work that lawyers will do to distribute funds, and some authors argued that lawyers’ projections for time spent were alarmingly inflated.  
部分费用包括律师未来分配资金的工作，一些作者认为律师对工作时间的预估被严重夸大。

Acknowledging these concerns, Martínez-Olguín wrote in a footnote that the court decided to take “an additional step to protect the interests of the Class.”  
 acknowledging 这些担忧，Martínez-Olguín 在脚注中写道，法院决定采取“额外步骤以保护集体成员的利益”。

Once payouts have been finalized, lawyers will be required to file a post-distribution accounting, and if their fees are less, then the court “may reduce” them, Martínez-Olguín said.  
Martínez-Olguín 表示，一旦赔偿最终确定，律师将被要求提交分配后账目说明，如果实际费用更低，法院“可能减少”其费用。

Although the settlement likely relieves the three authors who spent years defending their works and 506,194 works from potential class members, the lead plaintiffs are likely disappointed by a decision to reduce their requested service awards from $50,000 to $15,000.  
尽管这项和解可能让花费数年捍卫作品的三名原告以及 506,194 部潜在集体成员的作品得以解脱，但将原告请求的 5 万美元服务报酬削减至 1.5 万美元的决定，很可能让首席原告感到失望。

But Martínez-Olguín said that the higher amount was “unreasonable,” despite authors spending substantial time and resources on both litigation and the settlement negotiation.  
但 Martínez-Olguín 表示，尽管作者在诉讼与和解谈判中投入了大量时间和资源，更高的金额仍是“不合理的”。

Without a sign that authors may face retaliation for filing the lawsuit, the judge ruled that lower awards were due.  
由于没有迹象表明作者可能因提起诉讼而面临报复，法官裁定应给予较低报酬。

---

In a statement to Reuters, lead plaintiffs said that the settlement “brings us one step closer to real accountability for Anthropic and puts all AI companies on notice they can’t shortcut the law or override creators’ rights.”  
在向路透社发表的声明中，首席原告表示，这项和解“让我们离让 Anthropic 真正负责更近了一步，并警示所有 AI 公司不能走法律捷径或凌驾于创作者权利之上”。

It’s unlikely, but still possible, that authors may receive more than $3,000 per work.  
作者每部作品获得超过 3000 美元赔偿的可能性不大，但依然存在。

“If any funds remain in the Settlement Fund after all Valid Claims are paid, the Parties anticipate a redistribution of the remaining funds to Settlement Class Members unless it is economically infeasible to do so,” Martínez-Olguín said.  
Martínez-Olguín 表示：“在所有有效索赔支付完毕后，如果和解基金仍有剩余，各方预计将向和解集体成员重新分配剩余资金，除非在经济上不可行。”

Authors’ lawyers celebrated the judge’s approval, writing in a press release that “rightsholders came together to call foul” on Anthropic’s piracy and describing support for the settlement among them as “overwhelming.”  
作者律师对法官的批准表示庆祝，他们在新闻稿中写道，“权利人团结起来，对 Anthropic 的盗用行为表示谴责”，并形容支持和解的比例“压倒性”。

Anthropic also seems happy to be done arguing about the settlement.  
Anthropic 似乎也对和解争论的结束感到高兴。

In a statement provided to Ars, Anthropic’s deputy general counsel, Aparna Sridhar, said that Anthropic is glad the case’s landmark ruling established that its AI training was fair use and is happy that most rightsholders have already filed claims.  
在向 Ars 提供的声明中，Anthropic 副法律总顾问 Aparna Sridhar 表示，Anthropic 很高兴该案具有里程碑意义的裁决确立了其 AI 训练属于合理使用，并欣慰于大多数权利人已提交索赔。

“We are pleased that more than 91 percent of authors and publishers covered by the settlement have claimed their share of the payment, and we’re looking forward to bringing this matter to a close,” Sridhar said.  
Sridhar 表示：“我们欣慰地看到，超过 91% 受和解覆盖的作者和出版商已申领其应得份额，我们期待此案最终结案。”

---

Anthropic blocked last-minute opt-outs  
Anthropic 阻止了最后时刻的退出申请

For every opt-out granted, Anthropic risks facing separate litigation on what Martínez-Olguín said remains a complex case for courts to assess.  
对于每一个获准的退出，Anthropic 都面临就 Martínez-Olguín 所称的“法院评估仍复杂的案件”提起单独诉讼的风险。

It makes sense then that Anthropic was motivated to block delayed requests to opt out.  
因此，Anthropic 有动机阻止延迟的退出申请，这很合理。

Some authors who opposed the settlement argued that they did not receive timely notices, the opt-out period was too short, and the settlement awards to authors were unacceptable, because the Copyright Act allowed for potentially higher statutory damages.  
一些反对和解的作者辩称，他们未及时收到通知，退出期过短，且和解给予作者的赔偿不可接受，因为《版权法》允许潜在的更高法定赔偿。

Martínez-Olguín confirmed that 350 class members successfully opted out of the settlement, but dozens of objections were denied, as were at least nine authors’ direct requests to opt out after the March 30 deadline.  
Martínez-Olguín 确认，350 名集体成员成功退出和解，但数十项异议被驳回，至少 9 名作者在 3 月 30 日截止日期后提出的直接退出请求也被拒绝。

Ultimately, Martínez-Olguín overruled most of the objections and denied most of the late opt-out requests.  
最终，Martínez-Olguín 驳回了大多数异议，并拒绝了大部分逾期退出请求。

The court only accepted two late opt-out requests from authors who showed “excusable neglect” in failing to meet the deadline, Martínez-Olguín said.  
Martínez-Olguín 表示，法院仅接受了两位作者因“可原谅的疏忽”而未能在截止日期前退出的逾期申请。

The two were co-authors who did not receive settlement notices.  
这两人是未收到和解通知的合著者。

One author filed an opt-out request a few days late, which was granted, while the other request was granted after a much longer delay, due to the author suffering a stroke.  
一位作者提交退出申请晚了几天，该申请被批准；另一份申请则在更长时间延迟后才被批准，因为该作者遭遇了中风。

That author also garnered the court’s sympathy by noting that she “lives in Mexico, speaks Spanish, was not able to understand the class notice, and asserts no Spanish translation was provided.”  
该作者还指出她“居住在墨西哥，讲西班牙语，无法理解集体通知，并声称未提供西班牙语翻译”，从而获得了法院的同情。
