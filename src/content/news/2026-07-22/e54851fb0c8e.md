# Some Large Language Models Exhibit Consistent Risk Attitudes  
# 部分大语言模型表现出一致的风险态度  

**Authors:** Bowen Sun, Rui Min, Yuxi Wang, Brian Odegaard, Qi Wang, Jing Du  
**作者：** 孙博文、闵睿、王宇曦、Brian Odegaard、王琪、杜婧  

**Abstract:**  
**摘要：**  

As artificial intelligence systems are deployed in open-ended, high-stakes settings, a critical dimension remains unmeasured: how perceived risk is translated into action.  
随着人工智能系统被部署于开放性的高风险场景中，一个关键维度仍未得到衡量：感知到的风险如何转化为具体行动。  

We test whether large language models (LLMs) exhibit systematic and consistent risk attitudes under uncertainty.  
我们测试了大语言模型（LLMs）在不确定性下是否表现出系统化且一致的风险态度。  

We introduce a cross-domain framework that decouples contextual risk belief from categorical decision, and apply it to six representative LLMs and 100 human participants across spatial navigation, clinical triage, and financial allocation tasks.  
我们引入了一个跨领域框架，将情境性风险信念与分类决策解耦，并将其应用于六个代表性LLMs和100名人类参与者，涵盖空间导航、临床分诊和财务分配任务。  

Using regression models, we extract each agent's belief-to-decision mapping and quantify risk sensitivity and risk attitude bias.  
通过回归模型，我们提取了每个主体的信念-决策映射，并量化了风险敏感性和风险态度偏差。  

We find that most tested LLMs exhibit (i) robust intra-task consistency, indicating stable mappings from contextual belief to risk decision within a fixed task domain;  
我们发现，大多数受测LLMs表现出：(i) 稳健的组内一致性，表明在固定任务领域内，从情境信念到风险决策的映射是稳定的；  

(ii) cross-domain rank-order stability, preserving relative risk posture across tasks; and  
(ii) 跨领域排序稳定性，在不同任务间保持相对风险姿态；  

(iii) a convergence toward a restricted risk-attitude distribution relative to the broader human baseline.  
(iii) 相对于更广泛的人类基线，其风险态度分布趋于收敛于一个更受限的范围。  

These results reveal risk attitude as a stable and previously uncharacterized dimension of LLM behavior, establishing a foundation for evaluating and aligning AI systems in open-ended decision-making and motivating further investigation into the origins of these intrinsic behavioral dispositions.  
这些结果揭示了风险态度是LLM行为中一个稳定且此前未被充分表征的维度，为评估和调整开放决策场景中AI系统奠定了基础，并推动了对这些内在行为倾向起源的进一步研究。
