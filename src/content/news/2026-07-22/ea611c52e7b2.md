# When your vehicle outlives its cloud: What happens next?

# 当您的汽车比它的云端更长寿：接下来会发生什么？

Do you use your vehicle’s app to pre‑cool the cabin on a scorching day, confirm the doors are locked from bed, or have the car automatically call for help when the airbags go off? Savor those connected, cloud‑based conveniences while you can—the clock is already ticking on their obsolescence. In a few short years, you may be driving today’s vehicle without many of the features you currently enjoy.

您是否使用汽车应用程序在酷热的日子里预先冷却车厢，躺在床上时确认车门已锁，或在安全气囊弹出时让汽车自动呼叫求助？趁现在尽情享受这些基于云端的连接便利吧——它们的过时倒计时已经开始。短短几年后，您可能驾驶着今天的汽车，却无法享受目前许多已有的功能。

How we got hooked on the cloud “Connected services”—cloud-based and networked features that let a vehicle communicate with external systems to deliver apps, data, and remote functions to drivers and passengers—have been around since the 1990s, when GM introduced OnStar as an option. Those early systems were mostly voice‑centric, but they still depended on centralized back‑end systems. Modern versions, which are fully data-driven and cloud-hosted, use much more sophisticated over-the-air data links to allow a considerably better feature set.

我们如何对云端产生依赖？“连接服务”——即基于云端和网络的、让车辆与外部系统通信以向驾驶员和乘客提供应用程序、数据和远程功能的功能——自20世纪90年代就已存在，当时通用汽车推出了OnStar作为选配。这些早期系统主要是以语音为中心，但它们仍然依赖于集中式的后端系统。现代版本完全以数据为驱动并托管在云端，使用更复杂的空中下载数据链接，以提供更强大的功能集。

Today, virtually every volume brand offers some form of cloud-based telematics or a connected services stack. They can be deeply embedded, and most are fully integrated as the core of modern vehicle connectivity, supporting over-the-air (OTA) updates for the vehicle and app-based services for owners. As GM’s OnStar celebrates more than 30 years of service, a dozen others have joined, with each automaker branding its own unique services. HondaLink, Lexus Enform, FordPass, BMW ConnectedDrive, Volvo On Call, and Porsche Connect are just a few.

如今，几乎所有大众品牌都提供某种形式的基于云端的远程信息处理或连接服务套件。它们可以深度嵌入，并且大多数已完全集成作为现代车辆连接的核心，支持车辆的空中下载（OTA）更新和为车主提供的基于应用程序的服务。随着通用汽车OnStar庆祝其超过30年的服务，已有十几个其他品牌加入，每家汽车制造商都打造了自己独特的服务品牌。HondaLink、Lexus Enform、FordPass、BMW ConnectedDrive、Volvo On Call和Porsche Connect只是其中几个例子。

Despite their catchy marketing names, the systems are all functionally similar—each offers a very comparable feature set. Basics include remote lock/start, vehicle status and maintenance updates, location and tracking, and emergency assistance. Additional premium features include in-vehicle Wi-Fi hotspots, cabin preconditioning, stolen-vehicle tracking services, and concierge owner services.

尽管营销名称很吸引人，但这些系统在功能上都很相似——每套系统都提供非常 comparable 的功能集。基本功能包括远程锁车/启动、车辆状态和维护更新、位置和跟踪以及紧急援助。额外的 premium 功能包括车内Wi-Fi热点、客舱预调节、被盗车辆跟踪服务和礼宾车主服务。

Plenty of consumers have embraced the remote conveniences and demonstrated to automakers that they are more than willing to pay for them. After a bundled “free trial” following new vehicle delivery, manufacturers charge monthly fees or subscriptions for connected services. GM said that 40 percent of OnStar customers choose to renew their subscriptions after the introductory period expired.

许多消费者已经接受了这些远程便利，并向汽车制造商表明他们非常愿意为此付费。在新车交付后的捆绑“免费试用期”结束后，制造商会对连接服务收取月费或订阅费。通用汽车表示，40%的OnStar客户在试用期结束后选择续订订阅。

Of course, the OEMs get something out of it, too. Cloud-based services allow manufacturers to perform OTA software updates, eliminating the need to pay dealers for labor-intensive warranty repairs such as software-related safety recalls, bug fixes, and powertrain calibration errors. Instead of repairing one vehicle at a time, automakers can push updates to thousands of vehicles simultaneously. Plus, they can offer “product improvements” to owners, including UX changes, ADAS refinements, and new software versions for infotainment apps long after the start of production.

当然，原始设备制造商（OEM）也能从中获益。基于云端的服务使制造商能够执行OTA软件更新，从而无需向经销商支付与软件相关的安全召回、错误修复和动力总成校准错误等劳动密集型保修维修费用。汽车制造商可以一次性向数千辆车推送更新，而不是一次维修一辆车。此外，他们还可以在投产很久之后向车主提供“产品改进”，包括用户体验（UX）更改、高级驾驶辅助系统（ADAS）的改进以及信息娱乐应用程序的新软件版本。

Vehicles require unique hardware for OTA and connected services, of course. The basic components of original equipment (OE) hardware in most passenger vehicles include a telematics box (TCU) with a 3G/4G/5G modem, a SIM/eSIM, and antennas—these comprise the “smartphone” component. There’s also a main onboard computer with flash memory and a boot system to install the new hardware (programmed to revert to early code if something goes wrong so the car doesn’t “brick” itself). The TCU/main computer is tasked with sending signals over the vehicle’s internal wiring (CAN/Ethernet) to various ECUs, which are engineered to accept updates. For everyone’s peace of mind, robust security measures must be put in place to ensure that only genuine factory software can be installed and that hackers are locked out.

当然，车辆需要独特的硬件来支持OTA和连接服务。大多数乘用车原厂设备（OE）硬件的基本组件包括一个带有3G/4G/5G调制解调器、SIM/eSIM和天线的远程信息处理盒（TCU）——这些构成了“智能手机”组件。还有一个主车载计算机，带有闪存和引导系统以安装新硬件（编程为在出错时恢复早期代码，以防汽车“变砖”）。TCU/主计算机的任务是通过车辆内部布线（CAN/以太网）向各种ECU发送信号，这些ECU经过设计可以接受更新。为了让大家安心，必须实施强大的安全措施，以确保只能安装正品工厂软件，并将黑客拒之门外。

Subscription costs aside, consumers are generally pleased with cloud-based services. Connectivity improves convenience, comfort, and safety, while OTA updates keep vehicles feeling fresh and up to date. Unfortunately, that honeymoon phase doesn’t last forever—over time, vehicle-cloud-based connected services are scaled back or, worse, shut off entirely.

撇开订阅成本不谈，消费者通常对基于云端的服务感到满意。连接性提高了便利性、舒适性和安全性，而OTA更新则让车辆保持新鲜感和时效性。不幸的是，这种蜜月期不会永远持续——随着时间的推移，基于车辆云端的连接服务会被削减，或者更糟的是，被完全关闭。

The day the signal dies There is no single event or element that triggers the end—it’s a combination of things. As vehicles age, OE on-board hardware becomes obsolete as cellular providers replace older technology and automakers stop investing in aging back-end stacks and features. Manufacturer contracts for connected services can also expire. Taken together, these factors mean that time ultimately kills cloud-based vehicle services.

信号消失的那一天 没有一个单一事件或因素会触发终结——这是一系列因素的结合。随着车辆老化，原始设备制造商（OE）的车载硬件会过时，因为蜂窝网络提供商会替换旧技术，汽车制造商也会停止对老化的后端堆栈和功能进行投资。汽车制造商关于连接服务的合同也可能到期。综合来看，这些因素意味着时间最终会扼杀基于云端的车辆服务。

In 2022, many US cellular carriers, including AT&T, T‑Mobile/Sprint, and Verizon, shut down their 3G networks to create system capacity for upcoming 4G and 5G rollouts. When the 3G signal finally ceased, vehicles with the technology lost their connected cloud-based services (Porsche, Nissan, and Volkswagen were just a few of the automakers affected).

2022年，包括AT&T、T-Mobile/Sprint和Verizon在内的许多美国蜂窝运营商关闭了它们的3G网络，以为即将推出的4G和5G部署创造系统容量。当3G信号最终消失时，使用该技术的车辆失去了它们的基于云端的连接服务（保时捷、日产和大众只是受影响的少数汽车制造商）。

Lexus owners felt this shift acutely. The company’s Enform connectivity, which relied on the 3G network and was fitted to nearly all 2010–2017 models, went dark overnight because the hardware simply wasn’t forward‑compatible. (Lexus refunded owners for paid time left, while opportunistic dealers suggested that frustrated owners upgrade to a new Lexus that supported current technology.)

雷克萨斯车主敏锐地感受到了这一转变。该公司依赖3G网络并安装于几乎所有2010-2017年款车型的Enform连接服务，因为硬件根本不具备向前兼容性而在一夜之间失效。（雷克萨斯为车主退还了剩余付费时间，而机会主义的经销商则建议沮丧的车主升级到一款支持当前技术的新雷克萨斯。）

The announcement itself was unremarkable, but many were stunned to learn that the technology was fitted to 2014–2022 models, including cars with current 4G hardware.
