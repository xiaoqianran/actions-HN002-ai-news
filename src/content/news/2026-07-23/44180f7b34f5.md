**Title: Social media addiction lawsuit against Meta is dropped**  
**标题：针对 Meta 的社交媒体成瘾诉讼已被撤销**

---

A day after Snap tentatively settled with the plaintiff in a social media addiction lawsuit, leaving Meta as the only remaining defendant, the case has been dropped.  
在 Snap 就一起社交媒体成瘾诉讼与原告达成初步和解（使 Meta 成为唯一 remaining 被告）仅一天后，该案已被撤销。

In a statement, Meta said the plaintiff chose to drop his case against Meta without receiving any payment.  
Meta 在声明中表示，原告选择撤销对 Meta 的诉讼，且未获得任何赔偿。

TikTok and Google’s YouTube had previously reached settlement agreements with the plaintiff.  
TikTok 和谷歌的 YouTube 此前已与原告达成和解协议。

(Snap on Tuesday confirmed a tentative agreement had been reached.)  
（Snap 于周二确认已达成初步协议。）

The bellwether jury trial had been set to begin next week in the Superior Court of California in Los Angeles.  
这场具有指标意义的陪审团审判原定于下周在加州洛杉矶高等法院开始。

The plaintiff, a Florida teenager known by the initials “R.K.C.,” had sued the social media companies for creating addictive platforms.  
原告是一名来自佛罗里达州的青少年，代号为“R.K.C.”，他起诉这些社交媒体公司创建了令人成瘾的平台。

It was one of thousands of similar lawsuits from teens, schools, and state attorneys general that had accused the big tech companies of knowingly creating addictive platforms.  
这是数千起类似诉讼之一，这些诉讼来自青少年、学校和各州总检察长，指控大型科技公司明知故犯地创建成瘾性平台。

The precedent that would have been set by this lawsuit and others could have impacted how the companies build their apps, known for features that keep people engaged, like the infinite scroll and their continual buzz of notifications.  
此案及其他类似诉讼可能设定的先例，本可能影响这些公司如何构建其应用——这些应用以让人持续投入的功能闻名，如无限滚动和持续不断的通知提示音。

The plaintiff’s decision to drop the case follows Meta’s loss in a New Mexico case earlier this year, which marked its first courtroom defeat over social media harms.  
原告撤诉的决定发生在 Meta 今年早些时候在新墨西哥州一案中败诉之后，那是 Meta 在社交媒体危害问题上首次在法庭上失利。

Meta was ordered to pay $375 million in penalties after the company was found to have misled consumers about the safety of its platforms and endangered children.  
法院裁定 Meta 因误导消费者关于其平台安全性并危害儿童，需支付 3.75 亿美元罚款。

In March, a Los Angeles jury also handed both Meta and Google another defeat, awarding the defendant in that case some $6 million in damages.  
今年 3 月，一个洛杉矶陪审团也让 Meta 和谷歌再次败诉，判决后者在该案中向被告支付约 600 万美元的赔偿金。

Meta had been prepared to argue that the plaintiff in this case had allegedly only used Facebook and Instagram accounts for minutes per day on average, and was planning to claim that most of his accounts had been created after hiring a lawyer.  
Meta 原本准备辩称，本案原告据称平均每天仅使用 Facebook 和 Instagram 账户几分钟，并计划主张其大部分账户是在聘请律师后才创建的。

In its statement, Meta said that, “this outcome makes clear that we will not back away from defending ourselves against baseless lawsuits.”  
Meta 在声明中表示：“这一结果清楚地表明，我们不会回避对无根据诉讼的辩护。”
