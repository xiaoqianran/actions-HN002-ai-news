# Recti-Q: Feature-Space Rectification for Out-of-Distribution-Robust Quantized Perception in Edge Robotics  
**Recti-Q：面向边缘机器人分布外鲁棒量化感知的特征空间校正**

**Authors:** Hamidreza Yaghoubi Araghi, Parastoo Pilevar, Ming C. Lin  
**作者：** 哈米德雷扎·亚古比·阿拉吉，帕拉斯图·皮莱瓦尔，林明

---

**Abstract:**  
Robotic perception pipelines increasingly rely on large vision backbones deployed on SWaP-constrained edge platforms, making post-training quantization (PTQ) attractive for real-time inference. However, while PTQ often preserves clean in-distribution accuracy, we show that it can substantially degrade reliability under deployment-relevant distribution shifts (e.g., sensor noise, severe weather, and novel operating environments), creating a Quantization-Induced Robustness Gap. Across foundational vision benchmarks (ImageNet-C and PACS), 4-bit PTQ models exhibit pronounced robustness degradation despite negligible ID accuracy loss.  

**摘要：**  
机器人感知流水线日益依赖部署在SWaP受限边缘平台上的大型视觉主干网络，这使得后训练量化（PTQ）成为实时推理的有吸引力的选择。然而，尽管PTQ通常能保持干净的分布内（ID）准确率，我们证明它在与部署相关的分布偏移（例如传感器噪声、恶劣天气和新操作环境）下会显著降低可靠性，从而产生“量化诱导的鲁棒性差距”。在基础视觉基准（ImageNet-C和PACS）上，4位PTQ模型尽管ID准确率损失可忽略，却表现出明显的鲁棒性下降。

---

To address this, we propose Recti-Q, a lightweight feature-space rectification framework that freezes the quantized backbone and trains a small classifier-head LoRA adapter using only source data. Recti-Q is architecture-agnostic across CNNs and Transformers, supports efficient teacher-free training, and recovers a significant portion of the lost robustness, in some cases matching or exceeding FP32 performance.  

为解决此问题，我们提出了Recti-Q，一个轻量级的特征空间校正框架，它冻结量化主干网络，并仅使用源数据训练一个小的分类头LoRA适配器。Recti-Q对CNN和Transformer架构均无依赖，支持高效的无需教师训练，并恢复了大部分损失的鲁棒性，在某些情况下甚至达到或超越FP32性能。

---

At less than 1% parameter overhead (as small as 6 KB), Recti-Q preserves over 99% of PTQ memory savings, adds negligible compute, and enables low-bandwidth Over-The-Air (OTA) resilience patching for deployed robotic fleets operating in unpredictable physical environments.  

Recti-Q的参数开销不到1%（小至6 KB），保留了超过99%的PTQ内存节省，增加的算力可忽略不计，并为在不可预测物理环境中运行的已部署机器人舰队实现了低带宽的空中（OTA）弹性补丁。
