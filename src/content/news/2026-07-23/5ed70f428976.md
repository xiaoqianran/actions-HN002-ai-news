**Title: It's a shame what's happened to radio**  
**标题：广播的现状令人遗憾**

---

I signed off the air for the last time in 1994, capping a nine-year side career on the radio. People still sometimes ask me if I miss being a disk jockey, and for a long time I always wistfully answered yes. But not anymore. It’s not that I would be rusty as heck after all these years – and boy, would I. It’s that radio has changed drastically, and it just wouldn’t be any fun for me today.  
我于1994年最后一次告别播音，结束了一段为期九年的兼职电台生涯。人们仍偶尔问我是否怀念做唱片骑师（DJ）的日子，长久以来我总是怅然若失地回答“是的”。但如今不再如此。并非因为这些年我早已生疏——天哪，确实生疏了。而是广播已发生巨变，今天对我来说绝不会再有乐趣。

---

I listened to a lot of radio when I was a teen. It was a companion when I was by myself doing homework or whatever. I called in requests and tried to win contests (but never did). I had a few favorite DJs, the ones who kept you listening because you wanted to know what they’d say next. The fun they were having made whatever I was doing more fun.  
我青少年时经常听广播。独自做作业或做其他事时，它是我的伴侣。我打电话点歌，尝试赢得比赛（但从未成功）。我有几位最爱的DJ，他们让你持续收听，只因你想知道他们接下来会说什么。他们的快乐让我的任何活动都变得更有趣。

---

So when I got to college and found out about the campus radio station, WMHD, I had visions of being the kind of entertaining on-air companion I had enjoyed. I asked for and was given a weekly two-hour shift, just like every other disk jockey at the station. We could play whatever music we wanted, but my musical tastes were pretty narrow and I had trouble filling my time without always playing the same handful of artists. And I found out that wit failed me when the mic was open; I was lucky just to announce the next song without tripping over my tongue. My early shows were really pretty bad! Fortunately our puny signal covered just a few square miles, so hardly anybody heard me.  
因此当我进入大学，得知校园电台WMHD时，我梦想成为我曾享受的那种有趣播音伴侣。我申请并获得了每周两小时的时段，就像 station 里其他唱片骑师一样。我们可以播放任何想听的音乐，但我的音乐品味相当狭窄，很难在不重复播放少数几位艺人的情况下填满时间。我还发现，麦克风一开，我的机智就荡然无存；能顺利报出下一首歌而不口吃，已属幸运。我早期的节目真的很糟糕！幸好我们微弱的信号只覆盖几平方英里，几乎没人听到我。

---

Here’s a brief clip from the oldest show I have on tape, from 1986. Needing to expand my repertoire, I had fun discovering classic and progressive rock of the ’60s and ’70s and even dabbling in heavy metal. I brought the music I found to my shift and learned how to match key and tempo to transition smoothly between songs. I also started to find my on-air voice, as you can hear in this 1988 clip.  
这是我磁带中最旧节目的简短片段，来自1986年。为扩充曲目库，我愉快地探索了60、70年代的经典与前卫摇滚，甚至涉猎重金属。我把发现的音乐带到节目中，学会了如何匹配调性和节奏以流畅过渡歌曲。我也开始找到自己的播音风格，如这个1988年片段所示。

---

When I got my first part-time professional radio gig at WBOW, I had fun building and honing my on-air skills. There was a lot more to pro radio than what I’d done in college and it took time and practice to be good at it. If you’re old enough, you’ll remember the station in your town that everybody turned to for news, community information, and inoffensive music; in Terre Haute, that was WBOW. I was supposed to provide some “personality” between songs. Here’s a clip from 1992; you be the judge of whether I succeeded!  
当我获得在WBOW的第一份兼职专业电台工作时，我愉快地建立并磨练播音技能。专业电台比大学电台复杂得多，需要时间和练习才能做好。如果你足够年长，会记得镇上那个 everybody 收听新闻、社区信息和温和音乐电台；在特雷霍特，那就是WBOW。我需要在歌曲间提供一些“个性”。这是1992年的片段；由你来判断我是否成功！

---

When I moved down the hall to the company’s rock station, WZZQ, I had fun connecting with listeners. I loved hearing from them when they called to make requests and play the contests. Over time, a handful of listeners came to know me on the air and called during my shifts to just say hello. I looked forward to their calls and meeting them at station events around town. It was great to know that I was providing the same kind of pleasure for them that radio gave me when I was young. It gave me the energy to do my best work, as you can hear in this clip from 1994.  
当我搬到公司旗下的摇滚电台WZZQ时，我愉快地与听众建立联系。我喜欢听他们打电话点歌、参与比赛。渐渐地，一些听众在节目中认识了我，会在我的时段打来只是问候。我期待他们的电话，并在镇上的电台活动中与他们见面。知道我为听众提供了与我年轻时广播给我的相同快乐，这种感觉很棒。它给了我能量去做到最好，如这个1994年片段所示。

---

After I left Terre Haute for Indianapolis I tried to get on part-time at a few stations. One polite rejection letter essentially said that I might have been fine in Terre Haute, but I wasn’t ready for the big time in Indianapolis. I decided to take the hint and went back to being just a listener, and now I’ve been out of radio more than three times as long as I was in it.  
离开特雷霍特前往印第安纳波利斯后，我尝试在几家电台获得兼职机会。一封礼貌的拒信 essentially 说，我在特雷霍特可能还行，但还没准备好进入印第安纳波利斯的大场面。我决定领会其意，重新变回普通听众，而如今我离开广播行业的时间已是在职时间的三倍多。

---

In the intervening years, a number of things have changed that have made radio less fun to listen to and, I’m sure, to work in. First, now that I’m in my 50s, advertisers don’t care about me anymore. Radio stations choose their formats to appeal to the groups that advertisers think spend the most money. Advertisers love thirtysomething moms, by the way, which is why there are so many country and adult-contemporary stations playing eleven hits in a row or forty minutes of uninterrupted music. No one radio station really reaches me.  
这些年来，许多变化让广播听起来（我确信也做起来）不再有趣。首先，如今我已年过五十，广告商不再关注我。电台选择节目格式以吸引广告商认为花钱最多的群体。顺便说一句，广告商钟爱三十多岁的妈妈们，这就是为什么有那么多乡村和成人当代电台连续播放十一首金曲或四十分钟不间断音乐。没有一家电台真正能吸引我。

---

Second, thanks to government deregulation radio is now big business. Owners have always been in it to make money, even when ownership was local or regional. But now very large corporations own so many stations and cost management seems to be more important than the quality of the on-air product. Live and local talent is all but gone, replaced by satellite-delivered formats and a form of prerecording called voicetracking. The evening jock on your favorite station probably recorded tonight’s shift this morning in a studio in Tampa or Minneapolis. Try calling the station you listen to in the evening or on the weekend. Nobody will answer, because nobody’s there. It’s cheaper that way.  
其次，得益于政府放松管制，广播现在成了大生意。所有者历来以盈利为目的，即使所有权是本地或区域性的。但现在大型企业拥有众多电台，成本管理似乎比播音产品质量更重要。现场和本地人才几乎消失，被卫星传送的格式和一种叫“语音跟踪”的预录形式取代。你喜爱电台的晚间主持人可能今早在坦帕或明尼阿波利斯的录音室录制了今晚的节目。试试在晚间或周末给你收听的电台打电话。没人会接，因为根本没人。这样更便宜。

---

Third, a change many years ago in the way radio ratings are measured has changed radio programming. As long as there have been ratings, radio stations have formatted themselves to maximize listening among the average, everyday people the ratings companies ask to track the stations they listen to. But the new way of measuring ratings, which uses a listening device called the Portable People Meter, showed a very different picture of actual listening from the older paper-diary method. It pinpointed exactly what caused listeners to change the station. This has led to stations framing programming in much shorter blocks with less human interaction with the audience. It’s why many stations have become anonymous appliances. Why listen to a station that doesn’t relate much with you when you can just listen to Spotify on shuffle instead?  
第三，多年前广播收视率测量方式的改变影响了节目编排。只要有收视率，电台就格式化自己以最大化吸引那些被收视率公司询问收听习惯的普通大众。但新的测量方式使用一种叫“便携式人员测量仪”的收听设备，显示出与旧纸质日记法截然不同的实际收听情况。它精准定位了导致听众换台的原因。这导致电台将节目编排成更短的区块，减少与听众的人机互动。这就是许多电台变得像匿名设备的原因。当你可以直接随机播放Spotify时，为何要听一个与你关联甚少的电台？
