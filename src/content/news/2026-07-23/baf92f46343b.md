# Title: ISPs' long nightmare of having to list all the fees they charge is finally over  
# 标题：互联网服务提供商长期噩梦——列出所有收费项目——终于结束  

The Federal Communications Commission today voted to eliminate a Biden-era rule that required Internet service providers to list all of their so-called “passthrough” fees on easily accessible broadband price labels.  
美国联邦通信委员会（FCC）今日投票废除了拜登政府时期的一项规定，该规定要求互联网服务提供商（ISP）在易于访问的宽带价格标签上列出所有所谓的“转嫁”费用。

Instead of advertising accurate prices, ISPs like to advertise artificially low prices and then add all sorts of fees to subscribers’ monthly bills.  
ISP们倾向于宣传人为的低价，然后在用户的月度账单上添加各种费用，而非宣传准确价格。

To prevent bill shock and help consumers compare prices, the FCC updated its broadband-label rules in 2023 to require that ISPs “itemize on the label all discretionary monthly fees that the provider passes through to the consumer.”  
为防止用户遭遇意外高额账单并帮助消费者比较价格，FCC在2023年更新了宽带标签规则，要求ISP“在标签上逐项列出所有提供商转嫁给消费者的可自由支配月度费用”。

ISPs complained about the burden and complexity of listing all the passthrough fees they had chosen to charge.  
ISP们抱怨列出所有他们选择收取的转嫁费用负担过重且过程复杂。

It would presumably be less burdensome for providers to advertise accurate prices, eliminating the need to charge additional fees and list them on the labels.  
 presumably，对提供商而言，宣传准确价格负担会更轻，这样就不需要收取额外费用并在标签上列出。

But a few weeks ago, FCC Chairman Brendan Carr helped ISPs out by proposing to let them display passthrough fees in an aggregate “up to” amount instead of listing them all individually.  
但几周前，FCC主席 Brendan Carr 帮助了ISP，提议允许他们以总计“最高”金额的方式显示转嫁费用，而非逐一列出。

With this change, ISPs can continue advertising inaccurate prices as long as they provide the total amount of passthrough fees on broadband price labels.  
随着这一改变，ISP可以继续宣传不准确的价格，只要他们在宽带价格标签上提供转嫁费用的总金额即可。

Separating these costs from the advertised price makes it harder for consumers to find out what they will pay each month, and gives ISPs another method of raising customer bills without announcing a price increase.  
将这些费用与宣传价格分开，使消费者更难查明每月实际支付金额，并为ISP提供了另一种在不宣布涨价的情况下提高客户账单的方法。

As we wrote previously, Carr also proposed letting ISPs provide links to the required price labels instead of displaying the full labels prominently on ordering pages and account portals.  
如我们先前所写，Carr还提议允许ISP提供指向所需价格标签的链接，而非在订购页面和账户门户上显著显示完整标签。

In addition to having less information on pricing, the new labels will be a little harder for customers to find.  
除了价格信息减少外，新标签对客户来说也将更难找到。

The proposal was approved today. It was changed a bit since it was released in draft form, but the final order is not yet public.  
该提案今日获得批准。自草案发布以来略有修改，但最终命令尚未公开。

The vote was 3-0, with Democrat Anna Gomez joining the Republican majority.  
投票结果为3票赞成、0票反对，民主党人Anna Gomez加入了共和党多数阵营。

Gomez described some changes made since the proposal was released in draft form.  
Gomez描述了自草案发布以来所做的一些修改。

Gomez said she would prefer to keep requiring full itemization of fees, but thanked the chairman’s office for working with her on displaying certain kinds of information in the labels.  
Gomez表示，她更倾向于继续要求费用的完全逐项列出，但感谢主席办公室就标签中显示某些类型信息与她合作。

Instead of listing every fee, she said the updated labels will provide a description of fees that are remitted to state and local governments and a description of fees remitted to third parties.  
她说，更新后的标签将不再列出每一项费用，而是提供转嫁给州和地方政府费用的说明，以及转嫁给第三方费用的说明。

“Though I would have preferred that the labels provide a complete itemization [of fees], consumers will have the option to click on a link below to learn more about each of the types of fees and what they are used for,” Gomez said at today’s FCC meeting.  
“尽管我更希望标签能提供费用的完整逐项列出，但消费者将可以选择点击下方链接，了解每种费用的类型及其用途，”Gomez在今日的FCC会议上表示。

Unless the scheduled implementation date has changed since the draft was released, today’s order will take effect 30 days after it is published in the Federal Register.  
除非自草案发布以来计划实施日期已更改，否则今日的命令将在《联邦公报》发布30天后生效。

Final version a compromise  
最终版本是一种妥协

A spokesperson for Gomez told Ars that under the labels’ aggregate or “up to” amount for passthrough fees, there will be one subcategory for fees remitted to state and local governments and another subcategory for fees remitted to third parties.  
Gomez的发言人告诉Ars，根据标签中转嫁费用的总计或“最高”金额，将有一个子类别用于转嫁给州和地方政府的费用，另一个子类别用于转嫁给第三方的费用。

The two subcategories will have lists of the types of passthrough fees and “a link to a provider-managed webpage that describes what each of these fees (e.g., E911 or pole attachment fees) means and what it is used for,” Gomez’s office said.  
Gomez办公室表示，这两个子类别将包含转嫁费用类型的列表，以及“一个由提供商管理的网页链接，该网页描述每种费用（例如E911或杆件附着费）的含义及用途”。

These requirements were not in the publicly released draft order but were added in a compromise, Gomez’s office told us.  
Gomez办公室告诉我们，这些要求不在公开发布的草案命令中，而是在妥协中加入的。

Carr’s draft order released a few weeks ago would have let providers display only a single number to indicate the maximum amount of fees that might be charged.  
Carr几周前发布的草案命令本将允许提供商仅显示一个数字，以表明可能收取的最高费用。

At today’s meeting, Gomez discussed how the new rules will let ISPs provide links to the labels instead of displaying them prominently.  
在今日会议上，Gomez讨论了新规则将如何允许ISP提供指向标签的链接，而非显著显示它们。

The final order “retains the requirement that providers include broadband labels in customers’ account portals,” she said.  
她说，最终命令“保留了提供商在客户账户门户中包含宽带标签的要求”。

“However, we narrow that requirement to providing a hyperlink or icon rather than requiring the full label to be displayed. While my preference would have been to display the full label, my hope is that the commission, our industry partners, and consumer groups will work together to promote and advertise the concept of broadband labels such that consumers seek out this resource and use it regularly.”  
“然而，我们将该要求缩小为提供超链接或图标，而非要求显示完整标签。虽然我的偏好是显示完整标签，但我希望委员会、我们的行业合作伙伴和消费者团体能共同努力推广和宣传宽带标签的概念，使消费者主动寻找并定期使用这一资源。”

Passthrough fees are not taxes, but vary by location and are “imposed by a government entity or third-party infrastructure owner rather than set by the provider itself,” the FCC said in the draft order.  
FCC在草案命令中表示，转嫁费用不是税，但因地而异，且“由政府实体或第三方基础设施所有者征收，而非由提供商本身设定”。

The fees include “state and local right-of-way fees, pole attachment fees imposed by third-party pole owners, and similar charges,” the FCC said.  
FCC表示，这些费用包括“州和本地通行权费、第三方杆件所有者征收的杆件附着费以及类似收费”。

In the FCC’s words, these fees “represent costs the provider chooses to pass through to consumers rather than rolling them into the base monthly price.”  
用FCC的话说，这些费用“代表提供商选择转嫁给消费者的成本，而非将其纳入基础月费中”。

Ultimately, the fees are part of an ISP’s total cost of doing business and could be reflected in the overall advertised price.  
最终，这些费用是ISP总经营成本的一部分，本可以体现在整体宣传价格中。

But ISPs choose to treat them as separate charges for the purpose of billing consumers.  
但ISP选择将其作为单独收费项目，以便向消费者收费。

The burden of listing fees  
列出费用的负担

While ISPs seem to have little difficulty charging the fees when it’s time to issue monthly bills, they complain about the burden of listing all the fees on the broadband price labels.  
虽然ISP在开具月度账单时收取这些费用似乎毫无困难，但他们抱怨在宽带价格标签上列出所有费用的负担。

Broadband lobby group USTelecom complained to the FCC that “providers must create and update hundreds of different labels to account for geographic variability and to ensure that their systems properly queue the label specific to the proper location when the customer inputs their address.”  
宽带游说团体USTelecom向FCC投诉称，“提供商必须创建和更新数百个不同的标签，以考虑地理差异性，并确保当客户输入地址时，其系统能正确排列特定于正确位置的标签。”

Previous FCC leadership implemented broadband labels in response to a directive from Congress.  
前任FCC领导层为响应国会指令而实施了宽带标签。

“Over the last few years, we have learned that when labels become cluttered with unnecessary details, they stop being the quick, easy-to-use shopping tool Congress envisioned,” Carr said at today’s meeting.  
“过去几年，我们了解到，当标签因不必要细节而变得杂乱时，它们就不再是国会设想的快速、易用的购物工具，”Carr在今日会议上表示。
