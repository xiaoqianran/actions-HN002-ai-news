# Next Space Force chief throws cold water on the idea of space privateers  
# 下一任太空军司令对“太空私掠”想法泼冷水

In the early years of the United States, when the nascent US Navy was still getting its sea legs, several presidents used privateers to capture or destroy enemy warships when armed naval vessels were unable to do so.  
在美国建国初期，当 nascent 美国海军尚在摸索阶段时，数位总统曾动用私掠船，在武装海军舰艇无法行动时，俘获或摧毁敌方战舰。

President John Adams was one of the most vigorous proponents of commissioning private vessels for national ends. His administration issued letters of marque and reprisal during the so-called “Quasi-War” with France in the final years of the 18th century.  
约翰·亚当斯总统是最积极主张为国家目的征用私掠船的人之一。其政府在18世纪末与法国的所谓“准战争”期间，签发了私掠许可证。

These letters created the legal distinction between privateering and piracy. One of the letters signed by Adams, dated November 1799, authorized the use of a merchant ship to “subdue, seize, and take any armed French vessel” found near US coastal waters of “elsewhere on the high seas.”  
这些许可证确立了私掠与海盗行为之间的法律区别。亚当斯于1799年11月签署的一份许可证，授权使用一艘商船“制服、扣押并俘获”在美国沿海水域或“公海任何其他地方”发现的“任何武装法国船只”。

France also routinely used privateers against US shipping at the time. The Continental Navy also relied on privateers for military purposes during the American Revolution, and President James Madison approved the use of merchant ships to fight against the much larger British Royal Navy in the War of 1812.  
当时，法国也 routinely 使用私掠船袭击美国商船。大陆海军在美国独立战争期间同样依赖私掠船执行军事任务，而詹姆斯·麦迪逊总统在1812年战争中批准使用商船对抗规模庞大的英国皇家海军。

America’s use of privateers declined as the government expanded the size of the US Navy. The Constitution explicitly gives the government the power to use privateers. But the practice has become more controversial over the last 250 years.  
随着政府扩大美国海军规模，美国对私掠船的使用逐渐减少。宪法明确赋予政府使用私掠船的权力。但这一做法在过去250年里变得越来越有争议。

Many leaders in the late 18th century already considered privateering a morally ambiguous practice, according to Michael Crawford, a former senior historian at the Naval History and Heritage Command.  
据海军历史与遗产司令部前高级历史学家迈克尔·克劳福德称，18世纪末的许多领导人已经认为私掠是一种道德上模糊的做法。

The use of private vessels in warfare is largely moot in today’s era of US naval dominance.  
在美国海军主导当今世界的时代，在战争中使用私掠船 largely 已无实际意义。

A letter of marque signed by President James Madison and Secretary of State James Monroe in 1813. Credit: Naval History and Heritage Command  
1813年由詹姆斯·麦迪逊总统和国务卿詹姆斯·门罗签署的私掠许可证。来源：海军历史与遗产司令部

Benjamin Franklin wrote essays in the 1780s objecting to the use of privateers, despite his backing of privateering during the War of Independence a few years before.  
本杰明·富兰克林在1780年代撰写文章，反对使用私掠船，尽管几年前在独立战争期间他曾支持私掠。

With the war over, he argued on humanitarian and economic grounds that privateering should be banned by all sides in any future conflict.  
战争结束后，他基于人道和经济理由主张，在任何未来的冲突中，各方都应禁止私掠。

“This will be a happy improvement of the law of nations,” Franklin wrote in a 1785 letter. “The humane and the just cannot but wish general success to the proposition.”  
“这将是国际法的一个令人欣喜的改进，”富兰克林在1785年的一封信中写道。“人道和正义之士无不希望这一提议获得普遍成功。”

Franklin wasn’t the only Founding Father opposed to privateering. William Whipple, a delegate from New Hampshire of the Declaration of Independence, was more pointed in his criticism:  
富兰克林并非唯一反对私掠的开国元勋。《独立宣言》新罕布什尔州代表威廉·惠普尔的批评更为尖锐：

“Those who are actively in it soon lose every idea of right and wrong, and for want of an opportunity of gratifying their insatiable avarice with the properties of the enemies of their country, will without the least compunction, seize properties of their friends.”  
“积极参与其中的人很快会丧失是非观念，由于没有机会用敌国财产满足其贪婪的欲望，他们会毫不犹豫地掠夺朋友的财产。”

**Privateering in the 21st century**  
**21世纪的私掠**

Fast forward to today. Privateering is now prohibited under international law, and although it remains enshrined in the Constitution, the US government hasn’t used the practice since the War of 1812 (the Confederacy commissioned privateers against the Union Navy in the Civil War).  
快进到今天。私掠现已被国际法禁止，尽管它仍载于宪法，但美国政府自1812年战争后（南方邦联在内战期间曾委托私掠船对抗联邦海军）便未再使用这一做法。

Still, some present-day lawmakers want to bring it back. Sen. Mike Lee (R-Utah) and Rep. Tim Burchett (R-Tenn.) authored a bill last year seeking to allow privateering in the fight against drug cartels.  
然而，一些当代立法者希望将其恢复。犹他州共和党参议员迈克·李和田纳西州共和党众议员蒂姆·伯切特去年起草了一项法案，寻求允许在打击毒品卡特尔中使用私掠。

Lee and Burchett introduced a separate bill earlier this month calling for the government to use letters of marque and reprisal for cyber warfare.  
李和伯切特本月早些时候又提出了一项单独法案，呼吁政府将私掠许可证用于网络战。

In a statement, Lee said the legislation “allows American digital privateers to raid cartels, cybercriminals, and foreign adversaries” and seize their assets.  
李在一份声明中表示，该法案“允许美国数字私掠船袭击卡特尔、网络罪犯和外国对手”并扣押其资产。

“This is the ultimate in white-hat hacking: stealing from America’s enemies at the direction of the president and splitting the profits with the federal government.”  
“这是白帽黑客行为的极致：在总统指挥下从美国敌人那里窃取，并与联邦政府分享利润。”

Suddenly, it feels like it’s 1812 again. So why is a space reporter writing about this arcane corner of American history?  
突然间，感觉仿佛回到了1812年。那么，一位太空记者为何要撰写关于美国历史这一晦涩角落的文章？

With the possible exception of cyber, the lines between commercial and military operations in space are blurrier than in any other domain in modern warfare.  
除了网络领域可能例外，太空领域商业与军事行动之间的界限，比现代战争任何其他领域都更加模糊。

The rules of engagement in space are still unrefined, if they exist at all. Global debates have weighed the legitimacy of targeting commercial satellites in军事行动, especially as communications networks like Starlink and numerous commercial surveillance satellites underpin nearly everything on the terrestrial battlefield.  
太空交战规则即便存在，也仍未完善。全球辩论一直在权衡在军事行动中 targeting 商业卫星的合法性，尤其是因为像星链这样的通信网络和众多商业监视卫星支撑着陆地战场的几乎一切。

The tentative verdict seems to be that, yes, these privately owned assets could be legitimate military targets. If that’s true, what is stopping a private operator from going on the offense in space? Would that be space piracy?  
初步结论似乎是，是的，这些私有资产可能成为合法的军事目标。如果这是真的，是什么阻止私营运营商在太空中采取进攻行动？那会是太空海盗行为吗？

Not if that operator holds a letter of marque or reprisal.  
如果该运营商持有私掠许可证，那就不是。

Modern military operations are interconnected across domains, from land, air, and sea to space and cyber. Credit: Lockheed Martin  
现代军事行动跨域互联，从陆地、空中、海洋到太空和网络。来源：洛克希德·马丁公司

The US Space Force is already using privately owned satellites in military exercises, simulating offensive and defensive maneuvers to respond to threat scenarios in low-Earth orbit.  
美国太空军已在军事演习中使用私有卫星，模拟进攻和防御行动，以应对近地轨道的威胁场景。

Those satellites don’t actually have weapons onboard, unless you consider the option of ramming an adversary’s spacecraft. There are good reasons not to do that.  
这些卫星实际上没有 onboard 武器，除非你考虑撞击对手航天器的选项。有充分理由不这样做。

But space-based weapons will soon be part of the US arsenal, if they’re not already. US military officials believe Russia is already operationalizing an anti-satellite (ASAT) weapon system in orbit to go along with ground-based ASAT missiles.  
但天基武器很快将成为美国武库的一部分（如果还不是的话）。美国军方官员认为，俄罗斯已经在轨 operationalizing 反卫星武器系统，以配合地基反卫星导弹。

The Pentagon’s planned Golden Dome missile defense shield is designed to include space-based missile interceptors in low-Earth orbit, which could, in theory, be used to target other spacecraft.  
五角大楼计划中的“金穹”导弹防御盾设计包括近地轨道上的天基导弹拦截器，理论上可用于 targeting 其他航天器。
