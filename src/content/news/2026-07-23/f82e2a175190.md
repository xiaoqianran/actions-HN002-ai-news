**Title: Meta won’t have to face the next planned social media addiction trial / Meta将无需面对下一场计划中的社交媒体成瘾审判**

**Content:**

Less than a week before Meta’s lawyers were set to return to a Los Angeles courtroom, the plaintiff accusing the platform of inflicting harm dropped the case.  
就在Meta的律师准备重返洛杉矶法庭不到一周前，指控该平台造成伤害的原告撤回了案件。

Brought by 15-year-old Florida plaintiff going by initials R.K.C., the case was set to be the second in a set of bellwether trials meant to test legal arguments that social media giants allegedly broke the law by creating features that hooked and harmed teens.  
此案由一名来自佛罗里达州、年仅15岁、以姓名首字母R.K.C.为代称的原告提起，原定作为一系列标志性审判中的第二场，旨在测试社交媒体巨头被指控通过设计令青少年上瘾并伤害他们的功能而违法的法律论点。

TikTok, Snap, and YouTube previously settled claims brought by R.K.C. for undisclosed amounts.  
TikTok、Snap和YouTube此前已与R.K.C.达成和解，赔偿金额未公开。

“In light of the overall successful result of the litigation and his concerns about enduring a grueling weekslong trial, he has elected to withdraw his claims against Meta,” R.K.C.’s lawyers Emily Jeffcott and Rahul Ravipudi said in a statement. “He’s ready to close this chapter and focus on his recovery and engage in therapy as he aspires to have a normal life.”  
“鉴于诉讼整体取得圆满结果，以及他对于承受数周漫长审判的担忧，他已选择撤回对Meta的指控，”R.K.C.的律师Emily Jeffcott和Rahul Ravipudi在一份声明中表示。“他已准备好结束这一章，专注于康复和接受治疗，因为他渴望拥有正常的生活。”

That means Meta got out of this case without having to pay a settlement.  
这意味着Meta在此案中得以脱身，无需支付任何和解金。

“The claims never held up, and this outcome makes clear that we will not back away from defending ourselves against baseless lawsuits,” Meta spokesperson Andy Stone said in a statement.  
“这些指控从未站得住脚，这一结果清楚地表明，我们不会回避对无根据诉讼的辩护，”Meta发言人Andy Stone在一份声明中表示。

Multiple cases have already begun to test claims about social media companies allegedly misleading users about the safety of their products and inflicting harm.  
多起案件已开始测试关于社交媒体公司被指控误导用户其产品安全性并造成伤害的说法。

Meta and Google’s YouTube lost the first bellwether case tried at the courthouse, with a jury finding them to be negligent and ordering them to pay a total of $6 million in compensatory and punitive damages to the single plaintiff.  
Meta和谷歌的YouTube在首场标志性审判中败诉，陪审团认定其存在疏忽，并命令它们向该单一原告支付总计600万美元的补偿性及惩罚性赔偿。

Separately, a New Mexico jury ordered the company to pay a $375 million penalty, and the state is preparing for the next phase of trial seeking structural business changes.  
此外，新墨西哥州的一个陪审团命令该公司支付3.75亿美元的罚款，该州正准备下一阶段审判，寻求结构性业务变更。

There’s still seven more bellwether cases on deck in the California state courthouse in LA, and a separate set of cases pending in a federal court in Oakland.  
在洛杉矶的加州州立法院，仍有另外七起标志性案件待审，而在奥克兰联邦法院还有一组独立的案件悬而未决。

Meta, TikTok, Snap, and YouTube settled with a school district that was set to be the first in that separate set of bellwether cases seeking money to cover mental health-related costs to students allegedly caused by their platforms.  
Meta、TikTok、Snap和YouTube与一个学区达成和解，该学区原定作为那组独立标志性案件中的首个原告，寻求资金覆盖其学生因这些平台而 alleged 产生的心理健康相关费用。

Meta is still set to face claims brought by state attorneys general in federal court next month, alleging that it illegally misled the public about harmful and allegedly addictive features.  
下个月，Meta仍将面临由各州总检察长在联邦法院提起的指控，指控其非法误导公众关于其产品有害且 alleged 具有成瘾性的功能。
