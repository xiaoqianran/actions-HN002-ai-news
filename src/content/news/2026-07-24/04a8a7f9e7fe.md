**Title:** A woman got a UTI. Two years later, the bacteria had evolved, invaded her brain.  
**标题：** 一名女性患上尿路感染。两年后，细菌发生进化，侵入她的大脑。

---

A 63-year-old woman arrived at a hospital in pain. She had abruptly lost vision in her right eye.  
一名63岁女性因疼痛前往医院。她突然丧失了右眼视力。

As doctors got to work, the series of findings that followed revealed not just bad news for her, but an alarming revelation of how infections can evolve within a patient.  
随着医生开始工作，随后的一系列发现不仅揭示了她糟糕的病情，还揭示了一个令人震惊的发现：感染如何在患者体内进化。

The woman’s remarkable case was reported this week in the New England Journal of Medicine.  
这位女性的 remarkable 病例本周发表于《新英格兰医学杂志》。

According to her doctors, initial magnetic resonance imaging scans of her brain quickly identified the key problem.  
据她的医生称，初步脑部磁共振成像扫描迅速确定了关键问题。

The imaging indicated inflammation in her right eye, but also picked up lesions in her brain.  
影像显示她的右眼有炎症，但也发现脑部有病变。

Specifically, the imaging suggested problems in her parietal lobe, which is a hub for processing sensory information.  
具体而言，影像提示顶叶存在问题，该区域是处理感觉信息的中心。

The doctors suspected she had a brain abscess.  
医生怀疑她患有脑脓肿。

Laboratory results of her blood and urine samples suggested an infection as well as kidney problems.  
血液和尿液样本的实验室结果提示感染以及肾脏问题。

Doctors decided to open up her skull for a brain biopsy.  
医生决定进行开颅脑活检。

Inside, the surgical team was met with pus.  
手术团队在脑内发现了脓液。

Meanwhile, doctors worked to identify the bacteria found in two urine samples.  
与此同时，医生致力于鉴定两份尿液样本中发现的细菌。

In all, the two urine samples and the brain sample all grew the same bacteria: *Klebsiella pneumoniae*.  
总之，两份尿液样本和脑部样本均培养出同一种细菌：肺炎克雷伯菌（*Klebsiella pneumoniae*）。

This is a bacterium well known to doctors. It often dwells in the gastrointestinal tract, but can lurk in healthcare settings and spark infections, particularly pneumonia and urinary tract infections.  
这是医生们熟知的细菌。它通常存在于胃肠道，但可在医疗环境中潜伏并引发感染，特别是肺炎和尿路感染。

It’s also known for having a variant that can go metastatic, traveling via the bloodstream to invade other organs, particularly the liver, but also the lungs, eyes, and brain.  
它还以具有一种可转移的变体而闻名，该变体通过血液传播，侵入其他器官，特别是肝脏，以及肺、眼睛和大脑。

In a standard case report, this might be the end of the story. Doctors would diagnose a disseminated *K. pneumoniae* infection and treat with antibiotics.  
在标准的病例报告中，故事可能到此为止。医生会诊断为播散性肺炎克雷伯菌感染并用抗生素治疗。

But there was more.  
但还有更多发现。

Doctors and researchers brought in to help with the case noticed that although the three samples all grew *K. pneumoniae*, the bacteria were different.  
参与会诊的医生和研究人员注意到，尽管三个样本均培养出肺炎克雷伯菌，但细菌存在差异。

Bacteria in the first urine sample looked like normal *K. pneumoniae*.  
第一份尿液样本中的细菌看起来像正常的肺炎克雷伯菌。

But the bacteria from the second urine sample and the brain were gloopy and sticky.  
但第二份尿液样本和脑部样本中的细菌呈黏稠、胶冻状。

Under an electron microscope, the doctors could see that the *K. pneumoniae* in these two samples had thick capsules—they were seemingly encased in goop.  
在电子显微镜下，医生可以看到这两个样本中的肺炎克雷伯菌具有厚厚的荚膜——它们似乎被黏液包裹。

Further tests showed that the thick sticky coating made these bacteria more virulent than the normal-looking bacteria from the first urine sample.  
进一步测试表明，这种厚而黏稠的涂层使这些细菌比第一份尿液样本中外观正常的细菌更具致病性。

With the coating, protective immune cells called macrophages couldn’t gobble up the bacteria as they could with the normal *K. pneumoniae*.  
有了这层涂层，保护性免疫细胞（巨噬细胞）就无法像吞噬正常肺炎克雷伯菌那样吞噬这些细菌。

In mice, the snotty bacteria were lethal, while the normal one wasn’t.  
在小鼠体内，这种黏稠的细菌是致命的，而正常细菌则不是。

In all, the two sticky samples met the definition of hypervirulent *K. pneumoniae*—the kind known for going metastatic.  
总之，两份黏稠样本符合高毒力肺炎克雷伯菌的定义——即那种以转移性侵袭著称的菌株。

But being hypervirulent wasn’t all fun and brain-eating games for these bacteria; it had drawbacks, too, the researchers found.  
但研究人员发现，成为高毒力菌株对这些细菌而言并非全是“好处”；它也有缺点。

The hypervirulent bacteria couldn’t grow nearly as quickly.  
高毒力细菌的生长速度远不如正常菌株。

They also couldn’t infect cells in the bladder as well.  
它们感染膀胱细胞的能力也较差。

Genome sequencing of the three bacterial samples showed that they were all highly related.  
三个细菌样本的基因组测序表明，它们高度相关。

And, interestingly, they all lacked the hallmark genes associated with hypervirulence in *K. pneumoniae*.  
而且，有趣的是，它们都缺乏与肺炎克雷伯菌高毒力相关的标志性基因。

The bacteria in the two urine samples differed by 66 small mutations, while the two hypervirulent bacterial samples differed by just four small mutations.  
两份尿液样本中的细菌有66处微小突变差异，而两份高毒力细菌样本之间仅有4处微小突变差异。

These two hypervirulent bacterial samples had key mutations in genes that produce the bacterium’s capsule, explaining their gloopiness.  
这两份高毒力细菌样本在产生细菌荚膜的基因中存在关键突变，这解释了它们的黏稠特性。

(The researchers confirmed this by genetically engineering normal *K. pneumoniae* to contain these specific mutations, which reproduced the goopiness.)  
（研究人员通过基因工程改造正常肺炎克雷伯菌，使其携带这些特定突变，从而重现了黏稠特性，证实了这一点。）

With the genome sequencing, the researchers estimated that the bacteria in the two urine samples diverged from a common ancestor one to two years before the woman’s current state.  
通过基因组测序，研究人员估计，两份尿液样本中的细菌在该女性目前发病状态的一到两年前从一个共同祖先分化而来。

Then, more recently, the hypervirulent urine bacteria disseminated, spreading to the woman’s brain.  
随后，更近一些时候，高毒力的尿液细菌发生播散，蔓延至该女性的大脑。

The woman’s doctors noted that two years before her brain abscess, she was hospitalized with a urinary tract infection.  
该女性的医生指出，在脑脓肿发生两年前，她曾因尿路感染住院。

**Heterovirulence**  
**异毒力**

Bacterial evolution within a single patient is not particularly new. Infecting bacteria often adapt to have a fitness advantage in their victim.  
单一患者体内的细菌进化并非全新现象。感染性细菌常会适应，以在宿主体内获得生存优势。

But in most cases, the variant with the biggest edge overtakes the population.  
但在大多数情况下，最具优势的变体会取代整个菌群。

In less common cases, the population splits.  
在较少见的情况下，菌群会分裂。

This has been seen when bacteria are trying to survive antibiotic treatments.  
这在细菌试图在抗生素治疗中存活时已被观察到。

In this case, some variants become more resistant to the treatment, but the resistance comes at a cost—slower growth or less virulence, for example.  
在此例中，某些变体对治疗产生更强耐药性，但耐药性伴随代价——例如生长更慢或毒力降低。

This keeps them from dominating. Instead, they remain as a subpopulation.  
这使它们无法占据主导地位。相反，它们作为亚群存在。

This scenario is called ‘heteroresistance,’ which is linked to treatment failure.  
这种情况被称为“异质性耐药”，与治疗失败相关。

In the woman’s case, all three isolates were susceptible to antibiotics—some good news.  
在该女性的病例中，所有三个分离株均对抗生素敏感——这算是个好消息。

She was able to be treated with antibiotics to clear the infection.  
她得以用抗生素治疗以清除感染。

Further, most patients with *K. pneumoniae* brain abscesses recover, though there were no follow-up details of how her neurologic or vision recovery went.  
此外，大多数肺炎克雷伯菌脑脓肿患者可以康复，尽管没有关于她神经或视力恢复的后续细节。

While her infection didn’t have heteroresistance, it had something new: heterovirulence.  
虽然她的感染没有异质性耐药，但出现了新情况：异毒力。

The hypervirulent subpopulation had a fitness disadvantage in the urinary tract—the origin of the infection.  
高毒力亚群在尿路（感染源头）中存在适应性劣势。

But it had an advantage for dissemination and virulence, spreading to the brain and eye.  
但它在播散和毒力方面具有优势，蔓延至大脑和眼睛。

Overall, the researchers and doctors report that “this case shows the ability of *K. pneumoniae* to disseminate within a patient from a clinical (urinary) reservoir to distant body sites and highlights the potential for the emergence.”  
总体而言，研究人员和医生报告称，“本病例展示了肺炎克雷伯菌能够从临床（泌尿道）储存库在患者体内播散至远处部位，并凸显了其出现的可能性。”
