**Protecting our FLOSS commons from LLMs**  
**保护我们的自由开源软件公共资源免受大语言模型影响**

Bastian Greshake Tzovaras, Otto Richter, William Zijl  
Thu 23 July 2026  
Category: Contrib  

In Brief: Two motions regarding "artificial intelligence" and Large Language Models (LLMs) were voted on among Codeberg e. V. members and passed. We are promising to not use any of your data to train LLM and explain what the planned Terms of Use change mean for 'vibe-coded' projects. We believe that LLMs endanger the free/libre software ecosystem as a whole.  

摘要：Codeberg e. V. 成员就“人工智能”和大语言模型（LLMs）相关两项动议进行了投票并均通过。我们承诺不会使用您的任何数据训练LLM，并解释 planned Terms of Use 变更对“氛围编码项目”的影响。我们认为LLMs 危及整个自由/开源软件生态系统。

The Codeberg e. V. annual assembly is the meeting that puts power into the hand of our active members. Proposals are discussed live, and later voted on asynchronously. Since Large Language Models (LLMs) are an emerging but controversial technology, it is not surprising that two of the votes were concerned with Codeberg's position about this technology. The 14-day voting period ended yesterday and both proposals were accepted.  

Codeberg e. V. 年度大会是将权力交予活跃成员的关键会议。提案现场讨论，随后异步投票。由于大语言模型（LLMs）是新兴但有争议的技术，两项投票涉及 Codeberg 对此技术的立场并不意外。为期14天的投票期于昨日结束，两项提案均获通过。

The first vote was a statement about Codeberg e. V.'s stance on using your data to train LLMs. As stated in our privacy policy, "We do not want to need your data", and this also holds for the use of our user and project data for using or training generative "AI": The Codeberg forge and its associated services are not and will not use the code or data of projects and users to train "Artificial Intelligence" tools such as Large Language Models, whose purpose is to create output modelled after their training input. As an association, we believe that these technologies are incompatible with responsibly creating and maintaining free & open source software.  

首次投票是关于 Codeberg e. V. 对使用用户数据训练 LLMs 的立场声明。如隐私政策所述：“我们不需要您的数据”，这也适用于将用户和项目数据用于生成式“AI”的使用或训练：Codeberg 及其关联服务现在和将来都不会使用项目或用户的代码和数据来训练大语言模型等“人工智能”工具——这些工具的目的是创建模仿其训练输入的输出。作为协会，我们认为这些技术与负责任地创建和维护自由开源软件不兼容。

The second vote was more controversial, but was also accepted with 358 agreements vs 144 disagreements (and 14 abstentions), with a high voter turn-out of around 50% of active members. It implies a change to our terms of use to prohibit 'vibe-coded projects'. We'll share thoughts about the practical impact at the end of the article.  

第二次投票争议更大，但仍以358票赞成、144票反对（14票弃权）通过，活跃成员投票率约50%。这意味着我们将修改服务条款以禁止“氛围编码项目”。我们将在文章末尾分享对实际影响的看法。

We all pay for hungry LLMs  
LLMs are a very costly technology, and those costs keep rising as the companies providing them have to start recouping their investments. They are not only costly for those who use and explicitly subscribe to these services. The costs are not only hidden in 'normal' cloud and service subscriptions that cross-finance the 'innovative new features' you never asked for. LLMs are so costly that companies externalize the costs on a massive scale - on those who don't use them and society at large. Increased hardware prices, energy use and environmental damage - we all pay for it!  

我们都在为贪婪的 LLMs 买单  
LLMs 是成本极高的技术，随着提供服务的公司开始收回投资，成本持续上升。这些成本不仅由明确订阅服务的用户承担。成本不仅隐藏在“正常”云和服务订阅中——这些订阅交叉资助了您从未要求的“创新新功能”。LLMs 成本如此之高，以至于公司大规模将成本外部化——转嫁给未使用它们的人和整个社会。硬件价格上涨、能源消耗和环境污染——我们都在为此买单！

Strained servers due to nonsensical crawling  
In past posts we have already outlined how our infrastructure at Codeberg is regularly put under heavy load from webcrawlers of those companies who plan to ingest all of the code that is hosted on Codeberg for training their LLMs. At Codeberg, we are happy to provide free and open access to code. Just run git clone and enjoy. Unfortunately, these crawlers instead try to read every single page from Codeberg, no matter if it makes sense. This includes all the different issue filter variants, Git history, as well as the actual files at any point in Git history - even if they are still equal. These needless accesses create expensive database queries that diminish the service quality for all of us, requires substantial amounts of work from our system administrators, and force us to spend time building defensive mechanisms instead of cool new stuff. Mechanisms that also affect new and existing legitimate users, as we're having to impose limits or outright blocks on their desired workflow; leaving them a worse experience with Codeberg.  

因无意义爬取导致的服务器压力  
我们之前的文章已概述 Codeberg 基础设施如何因计划抓取 Codeberg 上所有代码以训练 LLMs 的公司网络爬虫而定期承受重压。在 Codeberg，我们乐于提供免费开放的代码访问。只需运行 git clone 即可享受。不幸的是，这些爬虫反而试图读取 Codeberg 的每一个页面，无论是否有意义。这包括所有不同的问题筛选变体、Git 历史以及 Git 历史中任何时间点的实际文件——即使它们仍然相同。这些不必要的访问会产生昂贵的数据库查询，降低我们所有人的服务质量，需要系统管理员大量工作，并迫使我们花费时间构建防御机制而非开发新功能。这些机制也影响新老合法用户，因为我们不得不对其期望的工作流施加限制或完全阻止；导致他们在 Codeberg 的体验变差。

The development team of none  
Using LLMs to work with your code gives you a kick of adrenaline. You can develop at a rapid pace, build things as if you had a large team. Only that you have none. In fact, you are (often) alone, working with a statistical machine that turns energy into code. It seems like many ‘vibe coders’ don't realize that they don't actually have a community around them. They build projects as if they had, and spend resources accordingly. We see projects having a lot of code activity, heavy CI/CD testing, frequent and large release binaries. Sometimes, it feels like the amount of supported platforms exceeds the amount of actual users. To us, it seems ridiculous to see projects with a single developer and virtually no users consuming as much or even more resources than some of the largest community projects on Codeberg, which operate frugal with CI/CD and storage resources. We do not believe it is reasonable for Codeberg to invest our precious donation money into hosting of large ghost projects.  

不存在的开发团队  
使用 LLMs 处理代码会带来肾上腺素激增。您可以快速开发，仿佛拥有大型团队。只是您实际上没有。事实上，您（通常）独自一人，与将能量转化为代码的统计机器合作。似乎许多“氛围编码者”并未意识到他们周围并没有真正的社区。他们像拥有社区一样构建项目，并相应投入资源。我们看到项目有大量代码活动、繁重的 CI/CD 测试、频繁且庞大的发布二进制文件。有时，支持的平台数量似乎超过了实际用户数量。在我们看来，看到单个开发者且几乎没有用户的项目消耗与 Codeberg 上一些最大社区项目相当甚至更多的资源是荒谬的——这些大型项目在 CI/CD 和存储资源上非常节俭。我们认为 Codeberg 将宝贵的捐款用于托管大型“幽灵项目”是不合理的。

Hardware sourcing is becoming an headache  
The training and deployment of LLMs has drastically raised the cost of buying hardware, in particular for SSDs and memory. To give you an example: The type of drive we sourced for € 700 only some years ago has risen to € 3.700 now - and is often out of stock. As a consequence, hosting code on Codeberg is becoming more expensive. While we are owning our hardware, and are thus not directly impacted by inflating 'cloud' rental costs, it means that replacing or expanding our hardware is now substantially more costly than it used (and needs) to be. While we might be able to afford paying those inflated hardware prices, it is money that we can not spend elsewhere to improve our service and foster the mission of Codeberg.  

硬件采购正成为难题  
LLMs 的训练和部署大幅提高了硬件采购成本，尤其是 SSD 和内存。举例说明：几年前我们采购的700欧元硬盘现已涨至3700欧元——且常缺货。因此，在 Codeberg 托管代码正变得更昂贵。虽然我们拥有自己的硬件，因此未直接受“云”租赁成本上涨影响，但更换或扩展硬件现在比以往（和需要）成本高得多。尽管我们可能负担得起这些 inflated 硬件价格，但这笔钱无法用于其他地方以改善服务和推进 Codeberg 的使命。

A growing digital divide  
These price hikes also lead to a growing digital divide: Small and even large operators are endangered by rising costs, while only the largest cloud companies have reliable agreements for hardware. Increasing costs for services like website hosting, storage or compute can be challenging to a lot of small NGOs, local coops, research projects and other usage of digital tools that we considered for granted until recently.
