**Title: Google hit with $1 billion in fines as EU braces for Trump battle**  
**标题：谷歌被罚10亿美元 欧盟备战与特朗普的博弈**

---

On Thursday, Google became the third tech giant slapped with massive Digital Markets Act (DMA) fines, ordered to pay more than $1 billion for two serious violations that the European Commission (EC) found went unchecked for way too long.  
周四，谷歌成为第三家因违反《数字市场法案》（DMA）而被处以巨额罚款的科技巨头，被责令支付超过10亿美元，以解决欧洲委员会（EC）认定的两项长期未受制止的严重违规行为。

In a press release, the EC explained that the fines were due to Google “self-preferencing its own services on Google Search” ($522 million) and for anti-steering practices, like charging fees or restricting app developers from directing consumers to cheaper purchase options outside of Google Play ($488 million).  
欧盟委员会在新闻稿中解释，罚款原因包括谷歌“在谷歌搜索中优先展示自身服务”（5.22亿美元），以及实施“反引导”行为，例如向应用开发者收费或限制其引导用户至谷歌以外的低价购买渠道（4.88亿美元）。

Google has 60 days to make changes to its services, or else risk even more daily fines. To comply with the DMA, the EC has asked Google to “treat third-party services that feature on Google’s search results in a fair and non-discriminatory manner” compared to its own services in categories like shopping, hotels, and flights.  
谷歌有60天时间修改其服务，否则将面临更高的每日罚款。为遵守DMA，欧盟委员会要求谷歌“以公平、非歧视的方式对待出现在谷歌搜索结果中的第三方服务”，在购物、酒店和航班等类别中与自身服务一视同仁。

And Google must also allow app developers, “both technically and contractually,” to freely sign up users and promote offers outside of Google Play.  
同时，谷歌必须在技术和合同层面允许应用开发者自由引导用户注册并推广谷歌以外的优惠。

Google can appeal the decision, and if it does, it may get backing from the Trump administration.  
谷歌可对裁决提起上诉，若如此，可能获得特朗普政府的支持。

Ahead of the EC’s decision, 25 Republican lawmakers urged Donald Trump in a letter to retaliate against the anticipated Google fine by launching trade investigations.  
在欧盟委员会裁决前，25名共和党议员致信唐纳德·特朗普，敦促其通过启动贸易调查来报复预期的谷歌罚款。

Such a move could threaten the European Union with tariffs or restrict the EU from accessing US tech, if any resulting probes confirm lawmakers’ fears that the EU plans to use the DMA as a “tool of economic extraction and regulatory coercion against American firms,” Reuters reported.  
路透社报道称，若后续调查证实议员们的担忧——即欧盟计划将DMA作为“针对美国企业的经济掠夺和监管胁迫工具”——此举可能对欧盟加征关税，或限制其获取美国技术。

The letter may embolden Trump, who’s been threatening to impose more aggressive trade restrictions against the EU since last spring—when Apple and Meta together were first to get hit with more than $700 million in DMA fines.  
这封信可能助长特朗普的气焰，自去年春季苹果和Meta首次被处以超过7亿美元的DMA罚款以来，他一直在威胁对欧盟实施更激进的贸易限制。

Lawmakers don’t even think that Apple and Meta should be labeled as gatekeepers under the DMA since popular Chinese firms like Temu and AliExpress aren’t bound by the same rules, the letter said.  
信中指出，议员们甚至认为苹果和Meta不应被认定为DMA下的“守门人”，因为 Temu 和 AliExpress 等热门中国企业并未受相同规则约束。

“It is important to make clear that the EU’s access to the US market is not guaranteed and can be limited should the EU continue to pursue discriminatory acts, policies, and practices in the digital sector,” the letter said.  
信中写道：“必须明确，欧盟进入美国市场的权利并非 guaranteed，若欧盟继续在数字领域推行歧视性行为、政策和做法，其市场准入可能受到限制。”

In response to the letter, the EC’s spokesperson Thomas Regnier told Reuters that “when it comes to our rules, the EU has the sovereign right to regulate economic activities on its territory.”  
对此，欧盟委员会发言人托马斯·雷尼耶向路透社表示：“关于我们的规则，欧盟拥有在其境内监管经济活动的的主权权利。”

“This of course also applies to our digital regulation, where we will keep enforcing our rules in a fair and non-discriminatory manner—as we have always done,” Regnier said.  
雷尼耶说：“这当然也适用于我们的数字监管，我们将始终以公平、非歧视的方式执行规则——一如往常。”

Seemingly, the EU is still hopeful that a diplomatic path will emerge allowing it to enforce the DMA without clashing with the Trump administration.  
显然，欧盟仍希望找到一条外交途径，使其能在不与特朗普政府冲突的情况下执行DMA。

“We believe there is significant potential to deepen cooperation across a broad set of digital issues, including those identified in the EU-US Joint Statement, while respecting our regulatory autonomy,” Regnier said.  
雷尼耶表示：“我们相信，在尊重监管自主权的同时，有巨大潜力深化在广泛数字议题上的合作，包括《欧盟-美国联合声明》中提及的议题。”

---

Google rushes to make changes  
谷歌急于做出改变

Risking even higher fines for delaying updates, Google does not appear to be depending on Trump quickly stepping in to block the fines.  
尽管延迟更新可能招致更高罚款，谷歌似乎并未指望特朗普迅速介入阻止罚款。

The EC confirmed that Google has already “rolled out changes related to Google’s steering terms,” which the Commission said showed “good progress towards compliance.”  
欧盟委员会确认，谷歌已“实施与谷歌引导条款相关的变更”，委员会称这显示了“合规的良好进展”。

Additionally, Google has conducted testing on “changes to how it presents its own services on Google Search for免费服务 such as shopping, hotels and flights,” as well as “changes to how it presents shopping ads and content related services, such as sports.”  
此外，谷歌已测试“在谷歌搜索中展示购物、酒店和航班等免费服务自身服务方式的变更”，以及“展示购物广告和体育等内容相关服务方式的变更”。

The search giant has also said that it will voluntarily make changes to AI Overviews and AI Mode to similarly align with the EC’s expectations and avoid self-preferencing or anti-steering practices.  
这家搜索巨头还表示，将自愿修改AI概览（AI Overviews）和AI模式（AI Mode），以同样符合欧盟委员会的期望，避免自我优先或反引导行为。

Ars could not immediately reach Google for comment, but its President of Global Affairs, Kent Walker, told Reuters that Google disagreed with the decision and is considering an appeal.  
Ars Technica未能立即联系到谷歌置评，但其全球事务总裁肯特·沃克向路透社表示，谷歌不同意该裁决，正在考虑上诉。

---

Yelp applauds Google DMA fine  
Yelp为谷歌DMA罚款叫好

Although Google is cooperating with the EC, the company is clearly upset about required changes that it claims degrade its popular products just to satisfy “a small group of self-serving complainants.”  
尽管谷歌正在与欧盟委员会合作，该公司显然对必须做出的变更感到不满，声称这些变更是为了满足“一小群自私的投诉者”而降低其热门产品的质量。

“To comply, we are having to strip away real-time Search features Europeans love—like instant pricing and direct availability for hotels, flights, ‌and restaurants—and dismantle safety protections on Google Play,” Walker said.  
沃克说：“为遵守规定，我们不得不移除欧洲用户喜爱的实时搜索功能——如酒店、航班和餐厅的即时价格和直接可订状态——并拆除谷歌应用商店的安全保护。”

“Regulation should improve products, not make them worse,” he added.  
他补充道：“监管应改善产品，而非使其变差。”

Since last September, Google has advocated for a “reset” of the DMA, warning that US tech giants face “considerable uncertainty and unpredictability” trying to comply.  
自去年9月以来，谷歌一直倡导“重置”DMA，警告美国科技巨头在遵守该法案时面临“相当大的不确定性和不可预测性”。

Google has maintained that the DMA currently favors “commercial interests of a small set of intermediary sites” and complying has already delayed some of Google’s AI features “by up to a year.”  
谷歌坚称，DMA目前偏袒“一小部分中介网站的商业利益”，且合规已导致谷歌部分AI功能“延迟长达一年”。

However, the EC said the fines were “proportionate and appropriate,” following a “thorough investigation, including feedback from market participants, and extensive dialogue with Google.”  
然而，欧盟委员会表示，罚款是“相称且适当的”，此前进行了“彻底调查，包括收集市场参与者反馈，并与谷歌广泛对话”。

Among Google rivals celebrating the DMA ruling was Yelp.  
在庆祝DMA裁决的谷歌竞争对手中，Yelp是其中之一。

In a statement provided to Ars, Yelp’s vice president of public policy, David Segal, “applauded” the decision as finally ending Google’s enduring abuses, while accusing Google of degrading its own user experience by promoting its own products above others.  
Yelp公共政策副总裁大卫·西格尔向Ars Technica发表声明，“ applauded ”该裁决，称其终于终结了谷歌长期的滥用行为，同时指责谷歌通过优先推广自身产品来降低用户体验。

“When told to give equal footing to rivals in line with the DMA, a solution that fosters competition and benefits consumers, Google has instead consistently chosen to unilaterally degrade its own user experience by removing functionality, which is the telltale mark of a monopolist,” Segal said.  
西格尔说：“当被告知需按DMA给予竞争对手平等地位——这一促进竞争、惠及消费者的解决方案——谷歌却 consistently 选择单方面通过移除功能来降低自身用户体验，这正是垄断者的典型特征。”

“European consumers and businesses will reap the rewards of a more fair and open Internet as a result of meaningful enforcement of the DMA, and we are hopeful that the Commission will work to ensure Google comes into compliance and implements lasting remedies,” he added.  
他补充道：“由于DMA得到有意义执行，欧洲消费者和企业将收获更公平、更开放互联网的回报，我们 hopeful 委员会将努力确保谷歌合规并实施持久补救措施。”
