**Title: Google just had its first negative cash flow quarter due to massive AI spending**  
**标题：谷歌因巨额AI支出首次出现负现金流季度**

---

Google has reported its financial results for the second quarter of 2026 (PDF), and as usual, the search giant raked in an unfathomable amount of money. Google saw total revenue of $119.8 billion, beating analyst expectations by a comfortable margin. Despite that, the company’s stock has taken a hit.  
谷歌已公布2026年第二季度财务报告（PDF），与往常一样，这家搜索巨头赚取了难以想象的大量资金。谷歌总营收达1198亿美元，轻松超出分析师预期。尽管如此，公司股价仍出现下跌。

Along with all that revenue, Google has announced a further increase in its AI-fueled capital expenditures (or capex). The company is actually spending so much on AI infrastructure that it has negative cash flow for the first time.  
伴随着高额营收，谷歌还宣布进一步增加由AI驱动的资本支出。该公司在AI基础设施上的投入如此之大，以至于首次出现负现金流。

Search was the largest chunk of Google’s income, accounting for $63.3 billion. Google Cloud pulled in $24.8 billion, a significant 23.8 percent increase from the first quarter. This shows there is massive demand for Google’s AI services.  
搜索业务是谷歌收入的最大来源，达633亿美元。谷歌云业务收入为248亿美元，较第一季度显著增长23.8%。这表明市场对谷歌AI服务存在巨大需求。

Google also earned $12.9 billion from its subscriptions, platforms, and devices portfolio, as well as $11.1 billion from YouTube ads. The company managed to goose that last one by more than 12 percent since last quarter as it made YouTube ads even longer.  
谷歌还从其订阅、平台和设备组合中获得129亿美元收入，YouTube广告收入为111亿美元。由于进一步延长了YouTube广告时长，该部分收入较上季度增长超过12%。

A significant chunk of Google’s revenue comes from investments. When you subtract those non-cash earnings, Google’s operating cash flow for Q2 2026 was about $39.1 billion. That’s not the most the company has ever seen, but it’s a healthy 40 percent increase from Q2 2025.  
谷歌收入的相当一部分来自投资。扣除这些非现金收益后，谷歌2026年第二季度的运营现金流约为391亿美元。这并非公司历史最高水平，但较2025年第二季度健康增长了40%。

The problem is that Google’s spending has also gone up—a lot. Before this latest round of financial updates, Google told investors it was expecting $180 billion to $190 billion in capital expenditures for 2026. Like other AI-obsessed tech behemoths, Google is burning cash on building and running the data centers powering its AI models.  
问题在于，谷歌的支出也大幅增长。在此轮财务更新前，谷歌曾告知投资者，预计2026年资本支出在1800亿至1900亿美元之间。与其他痴迷AI的科技巨头一样，谷歌正在为建设和运营驱动其AI模型的数据中心大举烧钱。

These numbers were already well above the $91 billion Google spent in 2025. The company now says it’s planning to spend as much as $205 billion on infrastructure in 2026.  
这些数字已远高于谷歌2025年支出的910亿美元。公司现表示，计划在2026年投入高达2050亿美元用于基础设施。

As a result of its increasing AI demands, Google reports it spent $44.9 billion expanding its AI footprint in the second quarter, and you don’t need an accounting degree to know which number is larger. With $39.1 billion in cash income, this spending left Google with -$5.8 billion free cash flow.  
由于AI需求不断增长，谷歌报告称第二季度投入449亿美元扩大AI布局，无需会计学位也能看出哪个数字更大。以391亿美元的现金收入计算，这笔支出使谷歌的自由现金流为负58亿美元。

To be clear, Google is still profitable—wildly so. It’s also sitting on a war chest of more than $100 billion. But free cash flow is an important metric that goes to the overall health of a business. This is the actual money a company takes in to fund its operations without selling investments or taking out new loans.  
需要明确的是，谷歌依然盈利——而且利润极高。它还坐拥超过1000亿美元的现金储备。但自由现金流是衡量企业整体健康状况的重要指标。这是公司在不出售投资或新增贷款的情况下，为运营提供资金的实际现金流入。

So it’s notable that Google’s free cash flow has dipped into negative territory for the first time since going public. Google’s stock price took a hit overnight on the news, dropping about 4.5 percent. It has continued to trend downward today.  
因此，值得注意的是，自上市以来，谷歌的自由现金流首次跌入负值区间。消息公布后，谷歌股价隔夜下跌约4.5%，今日延续下行趋势。

Onward and upward  
前路何方？

Google’s leadership is signaling to投资者 that this state of affairs is the new normal. The company points out with much fanfare that its capex spending currently is about six times higher than the $22 billion it spent in 2022 before the AI boom. Google expects spending to be even higher next year, too.  
谷歌管理层向投资者表明，这种状况将成为新常态。公司大张旗鼓地指出，其当前资本支出约为2022年AI热潮前220亿美元的六倍。谷歌预计明年支出还将进一步增加。

Investors have started to question the scale of AI spending, which is expected to top $700 billion industry-wide this year. Tech firms used to be reliable stocks, reporting high profits and juicy margins, but the race to build AI data centers and train new models has changed the landscape.  
投资者已开始质疑AI支出的规模，预计今年整个行业将超过7000亿美元。科技公司曾是可靠的股票，报告高利润和高利润率，但竞相建设AI数据中心和训练新模型的竞赛已改变了格局。

Google is in a better position than most, with a robust ad business and cloud services that pull in plenty of cash. It just wasn’t quite enough cash to offset its spending this time. It also designs its own AI chips, and the latest Tensor 8i and 8t are supposed to be more efficient for AI data centers.  
谷歌处境优于大多数同行，其强劲的广告业务和云服务带来了大量现金。但这次这些现金不足以抵消其支出。它还自主设计AI芯片，最新的Tensor 8i和8t据称在AI数据中心能效上更优。

Where things go from here is uncertain. People are clearly more sensitive to the high cost and lack of profit in AI, so Google’s stock price may be in for a minor correction. Things will probably even out for the company if it can continue to compete with other major AI players.  
未来走向尚不确定。人们显然对AI的高成本和缺乏盈利更加敏感，因此谷歌股价可能面临小幅回调。如果谷歌能继续与其他主要AI参与者竞争，情况可能会逐渐稳定。

Google recently delayed the release of its flagship Gemini 3.5 Pro model, which it says is still in testing with a small number of partners. Reports have suggested that Google is not seeing the kind of gains it needs to remain competitive with the likes of GPT 5.6 and Claude Mythos.  
谷歌最近推迟了旗舰模型Gemini 3.5 Pro的发布，称其仍在与少数合作伙伴进行测试。有报道指出，谷歌并未获得维持与GPT 5.6和Claude Mythos等模型竞争所需的进展。

Google has also been hit with a wave of resignations among its top AI researchers. The next few months may be pivotal for Google’s AI efforts.  
谷歌还遭遇了一波顶尖AI研究人员的离职潮。未来几个月可能对谷歌的AI efforts至关重要。
