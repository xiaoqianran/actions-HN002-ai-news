# America’s Hot Hybrid Summer  
# 美国的混合动力热潮之夏  

---

Save this storyIn Europe, the rise in gas prices due to the Iran war has car buyers thinking about electrics more than ever before.  
在欧洲，因伊朗战争导致的油价上涨，使购车者比以往任何时候都更关注电动汽车。  

Battery-electric vehicle (BEV) sales were up more than 33 percent in the first half of 2026 compared to the same time last year, according to data published by European consultancies and nongovernmental organizations this month.  
根据本月欧洲咨询机构和非政府组织发布的数据，2026年上半年纯电动汽车（BEV）销量同比增长超过33%。  

The same has not happened in the US. Electric sales are up by nearly 15 percent in the second quarter of the year compared to the first. But the overall number of EVs sold is still small.  
美国的情况则不同。今年第二季度电动汽车销量较第一季度增长近15%，但总体销量仍然很小。  

They're lagging well behind the optimistic projections made before the Trump administration reversed federal support for the tech, which included generous tax credits of up to $7,500, and before automakers responded by dialing back their own electric plans.  
它们远落后于此前乐观的预测——当时特朗普政府尚未撤销对这项技术的联邦支持（包括高达7500美元的慷慨税收抵免），汽车制造商也尚未因此缩减各自的电动化计划。  

Instead, the more noticeable sales boom has been in cars that use traditional fuel, but more efficiently: hybrids.  
相反，更显著的销售热潮出现在那些仍使用传统燃料、但效率更高的车型上：混合动力汽车。  

Sales of cars that sip gas rather than glug are projected to rise 9 percent this year, according to Kelley Blue Book, while sales of cars overall are slated to dip by more than 2 percent.  
据凯利蓝皮书（Kelley Blue Book）预测，今年“省油”而非“耗油”的汽车销量将增长9%，而汽车总销量预计将下降超过2%。

## Fuel Efficient  
## 燃油效率  

The price of oil has fluctuated following the continuing conflict in the Strait of Hormuz, and so have gas prices, which are now up 30 percent in the US compared to this time last year.  
由于霍尔木兹海峡持续冲突，油价持续波动，美国汽油价格目前较去年同期上涨30%。  

That alone has Americans thinking about alternatives to the traditional gas-powered car.  
仅此一点就足以让美国人开始考虑传统汽油车的替代方案。  

Hybrids are more fuel-efficient because they combine internal combustion engines with electric motors.  
混合动力车因结合了内燃机与电动机而更省油。  

Some plug in and use smaller gas tanks to move once their electric charge has run out; most don't, depending on technology that’s decades older.  
部分车型可插电，在电量耗尽后使用较小的油箱继续行驶；大多数则不可插电，依赖已存在数十年的技术。  

But gas prices aren’t totally responsible for the US’s hot hybrid summer, analysts say.  
但分析师表示，汽油价格并非美国今年混合动力车热销的全部原因。  

Hybrids are more available than they used to be. They are, on average, more expensive than gas-powered cars, but not by much.  
混合动力车如今比过去更容易买到。平均而言，它们比汽油车贵，但贵得不多。  

And they don’t give Americans range anxiety in the way that pure battery-powered cars do.  
而且它们不会像纯电动汽车那样让美国人产生里程焦虑。  

---

Now the vehicles—once viewed as a bridge between gas and electric—will likely stick around for the long haul.  
如今，这些曾被视为汽油车与电动车之间“过渡桥梁”的车型，很可能长期存在下去。  

“A hybrid doesn't feel like an electric vehicle—these are very different animals,” says Stephanie Brinley, the associate director of AutoIntelligence at Mobility Global.  
“混合动力车感觉不像电动汽车——它们是完全不同的两类车，”Mobility Global旗下AutoIntelligence的副总监Stephanie Brinley表示。  

“People are looking to save; they have range anxiety. The hybrid is the perfect solution," says Karl Brauer, an executive analyst at iSeeCars.com.  
“人们想省钱，又有里程焦虑。混合动力车是完美的解决方案，”iSeeCars.com的执行分析师Karl Brauer说道。  

“Hybrids still have a long life ahead of them.”  
“混合动力车仍有很长的生命周期。”  

Unlike electrics, which tend to confuse Americans right now, hybrids aren’t as alien.  
与目前让美国人感到困惑的电动汽车不同，混合动力车没那么陌生。  

More buyers thinking about hybrids isn’t necessarily bad for EV adoption, Brinley argues.  
Brinley认为，更多买家考虑混合动力车，对电动汽车的普及未必是坏事。  

“When buyers say, ‘I’ll look at this hybrid,’ that’s opening up a willingness to consider something that’s not traditional. It’s a little crack in the armor that can make a difference when they come back to the market,” she says.  
“当买家说‘我会看看这款混合动力车’时，这打开了考虑非传统车型的意愿。这是盔甲上的一道小裂缝，当他们再次回到市场时，这可能产生影响，”她表示。  

The American fondness for hybrids is another way US cars are looking really different from their global counterparts.  
美国人对混合动力车的青睐，是美国汽车与全球同行显著不同的又一表现。  

---

## Toyotathon  
## 丰田时刻  

This year’s jump in US hybrid sales has a lot to do with one automaker: Toyota.  
今年美国混合动力车销量的跃升与一家车企密切相关：丰田。  

The Japanese automaker made its initial bets in hybrid tech back in the late 1990s, with the birth of the Prius.  
这家日本车企早在1990年代末就首次押注混合动力技术，普锐斯（Prius）由此诞生。  

By the early 2000s, its hybrid system had made its way into other mass-market cars, like the Camry.  
到2000年代初，其混合动力系统已应用于凯美瑞（Camry）等其他大众市场车型。  

And then, critically, Toyota stuck with hybrids as other automakers went all-in on electrics in the 2010s and early 2020s.  
关键在于，当其他车企在2010年代和2020年代初全力投入电动化时，丰田坚持发展混合动力。  

Now, US customers can buy a hybrid version of nearly every car in its lineup.  
如今，美国消费者几乎可以买到丰田任何车型的混合动力版本。  

What’s more, many of the automaker’s most popular vehicles, including the Camry and the RAV4, are now hybrid-only.  
此外，包括凯美瑞和RAV4在内的许多热门车型，如今仅提供混合动力版本。  

So maybe it’s unsurprising: This quarter, Toyota reported its hybrid sales were up by nearly 20 percent.  
因此，或许并不意外：本季度丰田报告其混合动力车销量增长近20%。  

“You have people who have been buying RAV4s for 20 years, and they literally don’t know what a hybrid is, but they go into a dealership and buy a new one,” Brauer says.  
“有些买了20年RAV4的人，根本不知道什么是混合动力车，但他们走进 dealership 就买了一辆新的，”Brauer表示。  

It helps hybrids that, even for vehicles with pure gas-powered alternatives, the mixed powertrain now adds only a few thousand dollars or so to the sticker price.  
对混合动力车有利的是，即使与纯汽油版车型相比，混合动力系统如今仅使标价增加几千美元。  

For those who can afford today’s eye-watering car prices, that’s not much.  
对于能承受如今令人咋舌的汽车价格的人来说，这不算什么。  

Honda, another automaker with recent success in hybrid models, reported last month that its popular CR-V SUV with hybrid trims accounted for 51 percent of model sales.  
另一家在混合动力车型上近期取得成功的车企本田（Honda）上月报告称，其热门CR-V SUV的混合动力版本占该车型销量的51%。  

Honda said last week that it would discontinue its Prologue EV in the US for the 2027 model year, leaving it with zero BEVs in its North American lineup.  
本田上周表示，将于2027款车型在美国停产Prologue EV，使其在北美的产品线中不再有纯电动汽车。  

Hybrid sales from Hyundai and Kia were also up this quarter.  
现代（Hyundai）和起亚（Kia）本季度的混合动力车销量也有所增长。  

Even US automakers, which devoted much of the last half-decade to a pivot to electrics, are talking bigger games about hybrids.  
即使是过去五年主要致力于转向电动化的美国车企，如今也在更大力度地宣传混合动力车。  

Ford’s smaller Maverick truck has seen serious success with its hybrid trim, and it offers a handful of other hybrids.  
福特（Ford）较小的Maverick皮卡凭借混合动力版本取得了显著成功，并提供其他几款混合动力车型。  

Stellantis has backed away from plug-in hybrids, but it has publicly floated the possibility of more electrified powertrains.  
Stellantis已退出插电式混合动力领域，但公开表示可能推出更多电动化动力系统。  

Even GM, long a hybrid holdout, now says it will lean on the tech to help it get through the messy middle of the electric transition.  
甚至长期抵制混合动力的通用汽车（GM）如今也表示，将依赖该技术帮助其度过电动转型的“混乱中期”。  

Edmunds reported this month that even Tesla owners are getting in on the hybrid act.  
Edmunds本月报告称，甚至特斯拉车主也开始购买混合动力车。  

More than 20 percent of those trading in the electric cars in the second quarter of this year purchased hybrids.  
今年第二季度，超过20%置换电动汽车的买家购买了混合动力车。  

(The figure doesn't account for direct-to-consumer brands like Rivian, Lucid, and of course, Tesla.)  
（该数据未计入Rivian、Lucid以及特斯拉等直接面向消费者的品牌。）  

---

Oddly, the slow-moving success of hybrid technology has Brinley optimistic about the future of EVs.  
奇怪的是，混合动力技术缓慢而成功的进展，却让Brinley对电动汽车的未来感到乐观。  

“Hybrids showed up more than 25 years ago, and now they’re reaching 15 percent of the market,” she says.  
“混合动力车在25多年前就已出现，如今正达到市场占有率的15%，”她表示。  

“We expect EVs to be 12 percent of sales in 2030, and that’s a growth pace that’s even faster than hybrids. It takes a long time for people to change.”  
“我们预计到2030年电动汽车将占销量的12%，这一增长速度甚至快于混合动力车。人们需要很长时间才能改变。”
