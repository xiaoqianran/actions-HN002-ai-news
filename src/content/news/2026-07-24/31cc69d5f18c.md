# The power line that could reshape New York’s grid is hitting snags  
# 可能重塑纽约电网的输电线路遭遇挫折  

On July 3, as a heat wave swept the region, New York State’s grid imported 52 gigawatt-hours of electricity from Canada—enough to meet about 9% of its total electricity demand that day. Some of that power shuttled in on a 339-mile power line stretching from Quebec to Queens called the Champlain Hudson Power Express (CHPE). It opened in May and is officially the longest underground transmission line in North America.  
7月3日，当热浪席卷该地区时，纽约州电网从加拿大进口了52吉瓦时的电力——足以满足当天约9%的总电力需求。其中部分电力通过一条从魁北克延伸至皇后区、长达339英里的输电线路输送，这条线路名为尚普兰-哈德逊电力快线（CHPE）。它于5月投运，正式成为北美最长的地下输电线路。  

An underground power line might not sound all that exciting, but this could be a big deal for the state’s grid planning, and for emissions. It could provide up to 20% of New York City’s electricity demand, largely with abundant hydropower from Quebec.  
地下输电线路听起来或许并不激动人心，但这可能对该州的电网规划和减排意义重大。它最多可满足纽约市20%的电力需求，主要依靠魁北克丰富的水电。  

One wrinkle: The line has been down for most of this month, and some experts are concerned about how drought will affect the power supply feeding it.  
一个小插曲是：该线路本月大部分时间处于停运状态，一些专家担心干旱将如何影响为其供电的水源。  

Let’s look at how the CHPE transmission line could help shape the future of our grid, and what barriers it needs to overcome to make a difference.  
让我们看看CHPE输电线路如何助力塑造未来电网，以及它需要克服哪些障碍才能发挥作用。  

Planning for the CHPE (which is charmingly pronounced “chippy”) started 15 years ago, with the permitting process formally beginning in March 2010. The vision was to build infrastructure to better connect Quebec and southern New York.  
CHPE（发音可爱地类似“chippy”）的规划始于15年前，审批流程于2010年3月正式启动。愿景是建设基础设施，以更好地连接魁北克与纽约南部。  

The Champlain Hudson Power Express stretches 339 miles from Quebec to Queens. It is officially the longest underground transmission line in North America.  
尚普兰-哈德逊电力快线从魁北克延伸至皇后区，全长339英里。它正式成为北美最长的地下输电线路。  

Over 99% of Quebec’s electricity comes from renewable sources; most demand is met with hydropower, though the province’s wind capacity is growing quickly.  
魁北克超过99%的电力来自可再生能源；大部分需求由水电满足，尽管该省的风电容量正在快速增长。  

New York has some hydropower of its own, as well as nuclear and wind, but the state still relies on fossil fuels for most of its energy generation.  
纽约自身也有一些水电、核电和风电，但该州大部分能源发电仍依赖化石燃料。  

Transmission Developers, a company owned by the alternative asset management firm Blackstone, and Hydro-Québec, the province’s manager of generation and transmission, partnered to build CHPE. Construction began in late 2022 and wrapped up earlier this year. The total cost for the privately funded project turned out to be $6 billion.  
由另类资产管理公司黑石集团拥有的输电开发商公司（Transmission Developers）与负责发电和输电的魁北克水电局（Hydro-Québec）合作建设了CHPE。工程于2022年底动工，今年早些时候完工。这个私人资助项目的总成本最终达到60亿美元。  

The construction of this line was a feat. It’s made up of a bundle of two high-voltage direct-current power cables, each measuring roughly five inches across. Developers buried the bundle underground or underwater across the length of New York State. Much of the line was laid at the bottom of the Hudson River, requiring special boats that shot water jets deep into the sediment to create trenches for the cable.  
这条线路的建设是一项壮举。它由两根高压直流电力电缆组成，每根直径约五英寸。开发商将电缆束埋入纽约州全境的地下或水下。大部分线路铺设于哈德逊河河床，需要特种船只将高压水射流深入沉积物中，为电缆开挖沟槽。  

Connecting grids together can help accelerate the transition away from fossil fuels. The ability to move electricity to where it’s needed could also help limit the amount of new capacity we need to build. Research has shown that interconnection can help cut emissions and lower system costs.  
电网互联有助于加速摆脱化石燃料的转型。将电力输送到所需之处的能力还能帮助我们限制需要新建的发电容量。研究表明，互联互通有助于减少排放并降低系统成本。  

But CHPE is off to a slow start and has seen two outages so far. The first, on July 1, was reportedly caused by a trip at a converter on the Canadian side of the border. The second outage began on July 4, and the power line is still down as of the morning of July 22.  
但CHPE开局缓慢，迄今已发生两次停电。第一次发生在7月1日，据称是边境加拿大一侧的换流器跳闸所致。第二次停电始于7月4日，截至7月22日上午，该线路仍处于停运状态。  

Some experts say this isn’t unusual for a new infrastructure project. Other power lines have seen similar startup challenges, and the equipment hasn’t really been fully tested until it’s in operation, Normand Mousseau, a physics professor at Université de Montréal, told the Gazette.  
一些专家表示，这对新基础设施项目而言并不罕见。蒙特利尔大学物理学教授诺曼德·穆索告诉《公报》：“其他输电线路也曾面临类似的启动挑战，设备只有在实际运行中才能得到真正全面的测试。”  

Officials traced the issue to a damaged section of cable on the US side of the border, and the company that manufactured the line sent experts to investigate the cause, according to reporting from RTO Insider, a trade publication. The damaged portion of the cable has been removed and replaced, says Lynn St-Laurent, a spokesperson for Hydro-Québec. “It is currently estimated that the remaining work, including necessary post-repair testing, will be completed by the weekend.”  
据行业刊物《RTO Insider》报道，官员们将问题追溯到边境美国一侧一段损坏的电缆，线路制造商已派遣专家调查原因。魁北克水电局发言人林恩·圣洛朗表示：“损坏的电缆部分已被移除并更换。目前估计，包括必要修复后测试在内的剩余工作将在本周末前完成。”  

Similar woes have afflicted the New England Clean Energy Connect line, which opened in January, stretching 145 miles from Quebec to Maine. That project has also seen outages, and very little additional energy has flowed into the Northeast.  
类似的问题也困扰着1月投运的新英格兰清洁能源连接线，该线路从魁北克延伸至缅因州，全长145英里。该项目也遭遇了停电，几乎没有额外电力流入美国东北部。  

The good news for New York is that the grid wasn’t relying on CHPE yet. “Our planning studies did not assume CHPE would be available this summer, and that was one reason the grid performed reliably during the heat wave earlier this month,” Kevin Lanahan, a spokesperson for the New York Independent System Operator, the state’s grid management company, said in a statement. “A core principle of reliability planning is not relying on any single project.”  
对纽约而言，好消息是电网尚未依赖CHPE。纽约独立系统运营商（州电网管理公司）发言人凯文·拉纳汉在一份声明中表示：“我们的规划研究并未假设CHPE今年夏天会可用，这也是本月早些时候热浪期间电网运行可靠的原因之一。可靠性规划的核心原则是不依赖任何单一项目。”  

The idea is that eventually, states and regions will be able to rely—at least in part—on these projects, so there is pressure to get them working smoothly: Building massive transmission lines is a major long-term investment.  
最终，各州和地区将能够（至少部分）依赖这些项目，因此存在让它们顺利运行的压力：建设大规模输电线路是一项重大长期投资。  

In future years, as the equipment gets stress-tested and utilities begin to feel more confident in the projects’ reliability, they could play a bigger role on the grid.  
在未来几年，随着设备经受压力测试，公用事业公司开始对项目可靠性更有信心，它们可能在电网中发挥更大作用。  

One thing to keep an eye on moving forward is the condition of Quebec’s hydropower fleet: The region has seen intense drought for the past three years, eating into the water reserves used to generate electricity. That could mean there won’t always be abundant hydropower to ship across the border—even if the transmission lines are able to carry it.  
未来需要关注的一点是魁北克水电 fleet 的状况：该地区过去三年遭遇严重干旱，侵蚀了用于发电的水库储备。这可能意味着即使输电线路能够输送，也不总有丰富的水电可供跨境运输。
