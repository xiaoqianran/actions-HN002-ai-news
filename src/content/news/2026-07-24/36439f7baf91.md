# Meta drops out of a major clean energy pact as its natural gas buildout accelerates
# 随着天然气发电规模加速扩张，Meta退出了一项重大清洁能源协议

---

Over the past year, Meta has funded the construction of at least a dozen natural gas power plants, including one project that alone will burn enough natural gas to generate as much electricity as the entire state of South Dakota uses.
在过去一年里，Meta资助建设了至少十几座天然气发电厂，其中仅一个项目燃烧的天然气所产生的电力，就相当于整个南达科他州的用电量。

Now Meta is no longer part of the RE100, a corporate renewable energy initiative, after a decade of membership, the company confirmed to TechCrunch today.
Meta今日向TechCrunch证实，在加入该组织十年后，公司已不再是企业可再生能源倡议组织RE100的成员。

The breakup was mutual, according to a Meta spokesperson.
据Meta发言人表示，此次退出是双方共同的决定。

The exit caps months of Meta expanding its bet on化石燃料 to power its AI data centers and begs the obvious question: What does “clean energy” actually mean to a company that keeps building gas plants while still calling itself renewable?
此次退出标志着Meta数月来扩大对化石燃料的投入以支持其AI数据中心的行动达到顶峰，并引出了一个显而易见的问题：对于一个不断建设天然气发电厂却仍自称使用可再生能源的公司而言，“清洁能源”究竟意味着什么？

RE100 is a project of the Climate Group, a U.K.-headquartered nonprofit co-founded by former prime minister Tony Blair.
RE100是由总部位于英国的非营利组织“气候组织”（The Climate Group）发起的项目，该组织由前首相托尼·布莱尔共同创立。

The initiative provides policy and technical support to corporations seeking to transition to 100% renewable energy.
该倡议为寻求过渡到100%可再生能源的企业提供政策和技术支持。

Meta competitors Apple, Google, and Microsoft remain among the group’s 444 members.
Meta的竞争对手苹果、谷歌和微软仍是该组织444名成员中的一员。

Recharge News was first to report Meta’s departure.
Recharge News最先报道了Meta的退出。

While Meta wouldn’t comment on the reasons behind the departure —and the Climate Group did not reply to TechCrunch’s inquiry — the nonprofit recently updated its guidance for companies, enforcing more rigorous reporting on progress toward renewable energy goals.
尽管Meta拒绝对退出原因置评，且气候组织未回复TechCrunch的询问，但该非营利组织最近更新了企业指南，要求更严格地报告可再生能源目标的进展。

Previously, Meta told RE100 that it would “run its entire operations on renewable electricity by 2020.”
此前，Meta曾告诉RE100，它将在2020年前“实现全部运营使用可再生能源电力”。

Like many tech companies, Meta’s embrace of AI has pushed it to secure large amounts of power for its data centers, and while the company continues to procure renewable energy, it has embraced natural gas like few others.
与许多科技公司一样，Meta对AI的拥抱推动其为数据中心获取大量电力，尽管公司继续采购可再生能源，但它对天然气的依赖却鲜有公司能及。

Meta’s toe in the water was a 200-megawatt behind-the-meter gas power plant in Ohio, announced in June of last year, that will power one of its data centers.
Meta的初步尝试是去年6月宣布在俄亥俄州建设一座200兆瓦的“表后”天然气发电厂，将为其中一个数据中心供电。

Two months later, Meta said it would build three large natural gas power plants in Louisiana to supply electricity to its Hyperion data center.
两个月后，Meta表示将在路易斯安那州建设三座大型天然气发电厂，为其Hyperion数据中心供电。

Then in April, the company announced that it would fund seven more natural gas power plants for the same project.
随后在4月，公司宣布将为同一项目再资助七座天然气发电厂。

Combined, the 10 power plants will generate 7.5 gigawatts, enough electricity to power South Dakota and then some.
这十座发电厂合计将产生7.5吉瓦的电力，足以供应南达科他州并有余。

Meta, through a spokesperson, told TechCrunch that it remained committed to matching its data center electricity usage “with 100% clean and renewable energy.”
Meta通过发言人告诉TechCrunch，公司仍致力于“以100%清洁和可再生能源”匹配其数据中心的电力使用。

That’s a lot to promise.
这是一个很大的承诺。

While natural gas burns more cleanly than coal, it still produces significant amounts of pollution.
虽然天然气比煤炭燃烧更清洁，但它仍会产生大量污染物。

A single 1-gigawatt data center running 24/7, powered exclusively by natural gas, will release 438 metric tons of nitrogen oxides, 149 metric tons of fine particulate matter, 61 metric tons of sulfur oxides, and 298 metric tons of carbon monoxide.
一个仅由天然气供电、24/7运行的1吉瓦数据中心，将排放438公吨氮氧化物、149公吨细颗粒物、61公吨硫氧化物和298公吨一氧化碳。

Those pollutants contribute to a range of diseases, including asthma, cancer, cardiovascular disease, and dementia, among many others.
这些污染物会导致多种疾病，包括哮喘、癌症、心血管疾病和痴呆症等。

Meta can still claim to be 100% renewable by purchasing environmental attribute certificates.
Meta仍可通过购买环境属性证书（Environmental Attribute Certificates）声称实现100%可再生能源。

These allow companies to invest in a solar farm in Arizona, for example, while building a data center in Ohio.
例如，这些证书允许公司在亚利桑那州投资太阳能农场，同时在俄亥俄州建设数据中心。

As long as the solar farm makes enough energy in one year to offset the data center’s use, Meta counts that as 100% renewable.
只要太阳能农场在一年内产生的能源足以抵消数据中心的用量，Meta就将其视为100%可再生能源。

Most companies have tackled their renewable power goals using annual matching, but some, including Microsoft, are striving to match their electricity use on an hourly basis.
大多数公司通过年度匹配来实现其可再生能源目标，但包括微软在内的一些公司正努力实现按小时匹配其用电量。

This more stringent approach would bring power production more in line with how data centers use electricity.
这种更严格的方法将使电力生产更符合数据中心的用电方式。

It also encourages companies to invest in projects that pair renewables with batteries, like Google did earlier this year in Minnesota, rather than polluting ones like Meta’s Hyperion power plants.
它还鼓励公司投资将可再生能源与电池配对的项目（如谷歌今年早些时候在明尼苏达州的做法），而不是像Meta的Hyperion发电厂那样的污染项目。

Meta isn’t alone in pursuing natural gas — both Google and Microsoft have recently invested in large fossil fuel projects — but it has placed the biggest bet.
并非只有Meta追求天然气——谷歌和微软最近也投资了大型化石燃料项目——但Meta下的赌注最大。

Withdrawal or removal from a voluntary industry group isn’t always big news, but the timing, amid Meta’s fossil-fuel buildout, makes the change hard to ignore.
退出自愿性行业组织并不总是大新闻，但考虑到Meta正在扩张化石燃料业务，这一变化难以忽视。
