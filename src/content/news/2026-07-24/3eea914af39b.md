# Title: The EU Fines Google $1 Billion for Prioritizing Its Own Services in Search  
标题：欧盟因搜索引擎偏袒自家服务对谷歌罚款10亿美元  

---

The European Commission has levied a $1 billion penalty against Google over alleged competition law violations.  
欧盟委员会已对谷歌处以10亿美元罚款，指控其违反竞争法。  

An EC investigation found that Google had abused its dominance in the European Union’s search and app store markets to funnel people toward its own apps and services, in violation of the EU’s Digital Markets Act.  
欧盟调查发现，谷歌滥用其在欧盟搜索引擎和应用商店市场的支配地位，引导用户使用其自有应用和服务，违反了欧盟《数字市场法案》。  

The body has ordered Google to refrain from giving preferential treatment to its own services—such as shopping, accommodations, transport, and flights—in search rankings.  
该机构命令谷歌在搜索排名中不得偏袒其自有服务，如购物、住宿、交通和航班等。  

Google must also allow app developers to communicate and transact with users outside the Play Store, where it takes a commission on sales.  
谷歌还必须允许应用开发者通过Play商店以外的渠道与用户沟通并进行交易，而Play商店目前对销售收取佣金。  

“The best products should succeed because they’re better, not because they’re owned by the company running the search engine,” says Teresa Ribera, an executive vice president at the EC.  
欧盟执行副主席特蕾莎·里贝拉表示：“最好的产品应该因为更好而成功，而不是因为属于运营搜索引擎的公司。”  

“European consumers have a right to be told by app developers where to sign up to the best offers, even when the app store owner does not get a cut.”  
“欧洲消费者有权从应用开发者处获知哪里能获得最佳优惠，即使应用商店所有者无法从中抽成。”  

In a statement to WIRED, Google said it would consider appealing the penalty.  
谷歌在致《连线》杂志的声明中表示，将考虑对罚款提起上诉。  

“This isn't fair competition; it’s product degradation driven by a small group of self-serving complainants, with European businesses and consumers taking the hit,” says Kent Walker, president of global affairs at Google.  
谷歌全球事务总裁肯特·沃克表示：“这不是公平竞争；而是由一小撮自私的投诉者驱动的产品降级，最终由欧洲企业和消费者买单。”  

Tech industry trade associations argue that heavy-handed enforcement of the Digital Markets Act is self-defeating.  
科技行业贸易协会认为，对《数字市场法案》的过度执法会适得其反。  

“Reducing the quality of what Europeans have access to is not a positive outcome,” Daniel Friedlaender, senior vice president at trade organization CCIA Europe, tells WIRED.  
贸易组织欧洲CCIA高级副总裁丹尼尔·弗里德兰德告诉《连线》：“降低欧洲人所能接触到的服务质量并非积极结果。”  

The EU has brought numerous multi-billion-dollar fines against Google in the last decade over a medley of antitrust violations.  
过去十年，欧盟因一系列反垄断违规行为对谷歌处以多项数十亿美元罚款。  

In early July, a European court upheld a record $4.1 billion fine brought against Google in 2018 over agreements that required phone makers to install Google Search and the company’s Chrome web browser on their devices.  
7月初，一家欧洲法院维持了2018年对谷歌创纪录的41亿美元罚款，该罚款源于谷歌要求手机制造商在其设备上预装谷歌搜索和Chrome浏览器的协议。  

“Certainly, the stakes are really high for companies. How they are ranked affects their businesses a great deal,” says Kathryn McMahon, an associate professor of law at the University of Warwick.  
华威大学法学副教授凯瑟琳·麦克马洪表示：“当然，企业的风险真的很高。它们的排名方式对其业务影响巨大。”  

“The way EU competition law looks at it, firms in a dominant position—like Google—have a special responsibility not to distort competition.”  
“根据欧盟竞争法，处于主导地位的公司——如谷歌——有特殊责任不去扭曲竞争。”  

To address the latest complaints, Google has proposed alterations to the way it administers the Play Store and presents its products in search rankings, which the EC has characterized as "progress towards compliance.”  
为应对最新投诉，谷歌已提议调整其管理Play商店以及在搜索排名中展示产品的方式，欧盟委员会称此为“合规进展”。  

Recently, US president Donald Trump vowed to impose steep new tariffs on European countries that seek to restrict American technology companies.  
近日，美国总统唐纳德·特朗普誓言对试图限制美国科技公司的欧洲国家征收高额新关税。  

The White House did not respond to a request for comment.  
白宫未回应置评请求。  

The latest penalty is “quite a strong response, in the context of the transatlantic complaints—the way that Trump can leverage fines,” says McMahon.  
麦克马洪表示：“在跨大西洋抱怨的背景下——特朗普利用罚款的方式——最新罚款是‘相当强烈的回应’。”  

“It shows the commission is willing to be tough.”  
“这表明委员会愿意采取强硬态度。”
