**Title: I Put Real Planes in the Sky With AR. The 3D Was the Easy Part.**  
**标题：我用AR把真实飞机送上天空。3D部分反而是最简单的。**

---

A few years ago I would have told you AR was a graphics problem. You get a 3D model, you get a camera feed, you draw one on top of the other. Ship it.  
几年前，我会告诉你AR是个图形问题。你拿到一个3D模型，拿到一个摄像头画面，把一个画在另一个上面。发布就行。

Then I built location-based AR for real, and the graphics turned out to be the least interesting part of the whole thing.  
但当我真正构建基于位置的AR时，发现图形反而是整件事中最无趣的部分。

The build that taught me this was Planes XR, one of the location-based AR experiences I worked on for ARCortex.  
让我明白这一点的是Planes XR，这是我为ARCortex开发的基于位置的AR体验之一。

The pitch is simple to say and brutal to deliver: point your phone at the sky, and the actual aircraft flying overhead show up, positioned where they really are. Not a canned demo of a plane. The plane that is genuinely up there right now, pulled from live flight data, rendered in the correct patch of sky.  
它的理念说起来简单，实现起来却极其困难：将手机对准天空，头顶上真实飞行的飞机会出现在它们实际所在的位置。不是预录的飞机演示。而是此刻真正在天空中、从实时飞行数据中提取、渲染在正确天空区域的飞机。

If you have only ever done marker-based or surface AR, that one requirement changes almost every engineering decision you make.  
如果你只做过基于标记或表面的AR，这一要求会改变你几乎所有的工程决策。

So I want to walk through why, because the lesson generalizes well past AR.  
所以我想一步步解释原因，因为这个教训的适用范围远不止AR。

---

The three flavors of AR are not the same job  
AR的三种类型不是同一份工作

It is worth being precise, because "AR" gets used for three genuinely different builds:  
值得精确区分，因为“AR”被用于三种截然不同的构建：

Marker-based AR shows content when the camera recognizes a specific image or QR code. Reliable, but something physical has to be in frame.  
基于标记的AR：当摄像头识别到特定图像或二维码时显示内容。可靠，但画面中必须有实体物体。

Surface AR drops content onto a detected flat surface near you, a floor or a table. This is your furniture-in-your-living-room app.  
表面AR：将内容放置在附近检测到的平面上，如地板或桌子。这就是你客厅里的家具应用。

Location-based AR anchors content to real-world coordinates, so it works across a whole site, a city, or the open sky, with no marker at all.  
基于位置的AR：将内容锚定到真实世界坐标，使其在整个场所、城市或广阔天空中生效，完全不需要标记。

Marker and surface AR are, engineering-wise, mostly solved problems with good SDK support.  
从工程角度看，基于标记和表面的AR大多是已解决的问题，有良好的SDK支持。

The moment your experience has to span something bigger than a tabletop, you cross into location-based territory, and the difficulty steps up hard.  
一旦你的体验需要覆盖桌面以外的范围，你就进入了基于位置的领域，难度会急剧上升。

Location-based AR is really three systems pretending to be one  
基于位置的AR实际上是三个系统伪装成一个

A location-based AR app has to know three things at the same instant: where the user is, which way they are facing, and where the content is supposed to live in the world.  
基于位置的AR应用必须同时知道三件事：用户在哪里、面朝哪个方向、内容在世界上应该存在的位置。

Get all three aligned and a digital object sits convincingly in a real street or a real sky.  
三者对齐，数字物体就能令人信服地停留在真实街道或天空中。

Get any one wrong and the illusion collapses instantly, because humans are shockingly good at noticing when something floats in the wrong spot.  
任何一项出错，幻觉都会瞬间崩塌，因为人类极其擅长察觉物体悬浮在错误位置。

That splits into three subsystems, and each one is a place the whole thing can quietly fall apart.  
这分解为三个子系统，每一个都可能导致整个系统悄然崩溃。

---

**Positioning**  
**定位**

The app fuses several signals to place you in the world: GPS for a rough fix, the compass and motion sensors for heading, and the camera itself for fine correction.  
应用融合多个信号来定位用户：GPS提供粗略定位，指南针和运动传感器提供朝向，摄像头本身进行精细校正。

The thing nobody tells you up front is that raw GPS is only accurate to several meters.  
没人提前告诉你的是，原始GPS的精度只有几米。

That is completely fine for "you are near this landmark" and completely useless for "this arrow points at that specific doorway."  
这对“你在这座地标附近”完全足够，但对“这个箭头指向那个具体的门”则完全无用。

Closing that gap, from good-enough-for-a-map to good-enough-to-overlay, is one of the core engineering problems in the entire category.  
弥合这一差距——从“地图够用”到“叠加够用”——是整个领域的核心工程问题之一。

You do not solve it with a better model. You solve it with sensor fusion.  
你无法通过更好的模型解决它。你通过传感器融合来解决。

---

**Anchoring**  
**锚定**

Once you know where the user is, you have to lock the content to the world and hold it there as they walk, turn, and pan around.  
一旦知道用户位置，就必须将内容锁定到世界，并在用户行走、转身、环顾时保持其位置。

If the anchor drifts even slightly, the object slides off its spot and the experience reads as broken.  
如果锚点哪怕轻微漂移，物体就会滑离原位，体验就会显得破损。

Stable anchoring is the whole difference between an overlay that feels physically present and one that visibly wobbles, and it gets harder outdoors, at distance, and across large areas, which is to say exactly the conditions location-based AR lives in.  
稳定的锚定是让叠加层感觉真实存在还是明显晃动的全部区别，而且在户外、远距离和大面积区域下会更难——而这正是基于位置的AR所处的环境。

---

**Data**  
**数据**

Here is the part that ate the most of my time.  
这部分吞噬了我最多时间。

The interesting location-based experiences are almost never showing a static model.  
有趣的基于位置的体验几乎从不展示静态模型。

They are showing something tied to what is happening right now at that place, which means a live-data pipeline feeding the immersive layer.  
它们展示的是与当下该地发生之事相关的内容，这意味着一个为沉浸层提供数据的实时数据管道。

For Planes XR that meant pulling live aircraft positions from OpenSky, keeping them current, and rendering each plane where it actually is in the sky relative to where the user is standing and pointing.  
对于Planes XR，这意味着从OpenSky获取实时飞机位置，保持数据最新，并根据用户站立和指向的位置，将每架飞机渲染在天空中的实际位置。

That pipeline, fetching the data, cleaning it, keeping it fresh, and handling the moments it is slow or simply unavailable, was as much work as the AR rendering itself.  
这个管道——获取数据、清洗、保持新鲜、处理延迟或不可用时刻——其工作量与AR渲染本身相当。

The overlay is the part users see. The data plumbing is the part that decides whether they trust it.  
叠加层是用户看到的部分。数据管道是决定用户是否信任它的部分。

---

The failure modes live in the seams, not the scene  
失败模式存在于接缝处，而非场景中

That sentence is the thing I actually took away from the build, and it is why I keep bringing it up in other work.  
这句话才是我从这次开发中真正带走的收获，也是我为什么总在其他工作中提起它。

Every part of Planes XR that was hard to get right lived in a seam between two systems, not inside any one of them.  
Planes XR中所有难以做对的部分都存在于两个系统之间的接缝处，而非任何一个系统内部。

The 3D plane model rendered fine on day one.  
3D飞机模型第一天就渲染得很好。

What broke was the plane rendering in the correct spot only when positioning, heading, and the data feed all agreed at the same moment.  
出问题的是：只有当定位、朝向和数据流在同一时刻达成一致时，飞机才会渲染在正确位置。

When a GPS reading was stale, or the compass drifted a few degrees, or the OpenSky feed lagged, no single component was "wrong," but the plane ended up in the wrong piece of sky and the whole thing felt fake.  
当GPS读数过时，或指南针漂移几度，或OpenSky数据流延迟时，没有任何单个组件是“错的”，但飞机最终出现在错误的天空区域，整个体验就显得虚假。

You debug those problems by staring at the boundaries between subsystems, not by staring at any subsystem alone.  
你调试这些问题时要盯着子系统之间的边界，而不是单独盯着任何一个子系统。

That is a very different debugging mindset from a normal app, where a bug usually lives in one function you can put a breakpoint in.  
这与普通应用的调试思维截然不同——在普通应用中，bug通常存在于某个你可以设置断点的函数里。

---

How I scope one now, so it does not become a science project  
我现在如何规划项目，以免它变成科研项目

The most expensive mistake in this category is commissioning a broad, city-scale, multi-feature experience before proving the core alignment even feels right.  
此类项目中最昂贵的错误，是在证明核心对齐感觉正确之前，就委托一个广泛的、城市规模的、多功能的体验。

So the way I approach it now is deliberately narrow at the start:  
所以我现在的做法是：一开始就故意收窄范围：

Prototype the hardest alignment in week one.  
第一周就原型化最难的对齐。

If the whole thing hinges on content landing accurately at a real place, build exactly that first, at one single location, and then go physically stand in the real spot with a real device.  
如果整个体验依赖于内容准确落在真实地点，那就先精确构建这个，只在一个地点，然后带着真实设备 physically 站在那个真实位置。

Everything else is comparatively predictable once positioning and anchoring feel right.  
一旦定位和锚定感觉正确，其他一切都会相对可预测。

Prove one location before you build a hundred.  
在建造一百个之前，先证明一个地点。

Nail a single site, or a single sky, measure whether the overlay holds and the data stays trustworthy, then expand.  
搞定一个地点，或一片天空，测量叠加层是否成立、数据是否可信，然后再扩展。

Coverage is a multiplier, not a starting point.  
覆盖范围是乘数，而非起点。

将数据管道视为独立项目。

如果实时数据是价值的一部分，就将其作为一流工作流进行预算，包括那些不光彩的部分：数据新鲜度、错误处理、降级方案……（原文未完成）
