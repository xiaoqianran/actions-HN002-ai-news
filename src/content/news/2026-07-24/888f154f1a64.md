**DS Airlines Selling Extra Tickets**  
**DS 航空公司超售机票**

I’d like to call this company DS Airlines. They sell 304 seats for a flight with a capacity of 300 seats. The probability of a passenger showing up for a flight is 95% based on historical flight data. We will try to answer the following questions:  
我暂且称这家公司为 DS 航空公司。他们为一架容量为 300 个座位的航班售出了 304 张机票。根据历史航班数据，乘客登机的概率为 95%。我们将尝试回答以下问题：

What is the probability that this flight is overbooked? It happens if at least 301 passengers show up for the flight.  
这趟航班超售的概率是多少？如果至少有 301 名乘客登机，就会发生超售。

What is the expected value of the number of overbooked passengers?  
超售乘客数量的期望值是多少？

What is the airline risking when selling more tickets than available seats?  
当航空公司售出的机票超过可用座位时，它在冒什么风险？

---

We start by making the assumption that passengers are independent from each other. One passenger’s choice of showing up for the flight has zero impact on other passengers’ choices. Then, we can say that the probability of any passenger showing up is 0.95 (i.e. 95%). This assumption does not always hold because there might be families or groups flying together. If one person in a family shows up, then other members of the family are much more likely to show up than 95%. However, when we get into such details and conditions, the math becomes a nightmare with complex conditional probabilities. Besides, we may not always know who are flying together (e.g. members of a group can purchase tickets separately). With the assumption of independent passengers, we can say that the number of passengers who show up for the flight follows binomial distribution.  
我们首先假设乘客之间是相互独立的。一名乘客是否登机的选择对其他乘客没有影响。因此，任何乘客登机的概率都是 0.95（即 95%）。这个假设并不总是成立，因为可能存在一起旅行的家庭或团体。如果家庭中的一人登机，那么其他家庭成员登机的可能性远高于 95%。然而，当我们深入这些细节和条件时，数学计算会因复杂的条件概率而变得棘手。此外，我们并不总是知道谁是一起旅行的（例如，团体成员可能分开购票）。在假设乘客独立的条件下，我们可以说登机乘客的数量服从二项分布。

---

**Binomial Distribution**  
**二项分布**

Binomial distribution is a probability model used for counting successes in a series of repeated, identical, and independent events. The outcome of these events must be binary (i.e. 1 or 0, show up or do not show up for the flight). Hence, 304 passengers showing up for the flight can be modeled using a binomial distribution of a series of 304 independent events where the probability of an event is 95%.  
二项分布是一种概率模型，用于计算一系列重复、相同且独立事件中的成功次数。这些事件的结果必须是二元的（即 1 或 0，登机或不登机）。因此，304 名乘客登机的情况可以用一系列 304 个独立事件的二项分布来建模，其中每个事件发生的概率为 95%。

For a scenario to be qualified for a binomial distribution, it needs to satisfy the following conditions:  
一个场景要符合二项分布，需要满足以下条件：

- **Binary outcome**: Success (show up for the flight) or failure (does not show up).  
  **二元结果**：成功（登机）或失败（不登机）。
- **Independent events**: A person showing up for the flight has zero impact on other passengers.  
  **独立事件**：一人登机对其他乘客没有影响。
- **Same probability of success**: It’s 95% for all the passengers.  
  **相同的成功概率**：所有乘客都是 95%。
- **Fixed number of events**: This is the number of tickets sold for this flight, which is 304.  
  **固定的事件数量**：即本次航班售出的机票数，为 304。

Binomial distribution helps us answer the following question: If I repeat an experiment multiple times, what is the probability of getting exactly k successful outcomes?  
二项分布帮助我们回答以下问题：如果我重复实验多次，恰好得到 k 次成功的概率是多少？

---

**Probability of Overbooking**  
**超售概率**

Our flight will be overbooked if more than 300 passengers show up so we need to calculate the probability of k > 300 successful outcomes. With binomial distribution, the probability of k number of successful outcomes is:  
如果超过 300 名乘客登机，我们的航班就会超售，因此我们需要计算 k > 300 次成功的概率。根据二项分布，k 次成功的概率为：

\[
P(X = k) = \binom{n}{k} p^k (1-p)^{n-k}
\]

n is the total number of events, which is 304  
n 是事件总数，即 304

k is the number of successes  
k 是成功次数

p is the probability of success event, which is 0.95. (1-p) is the probability of failure event (not showing up), which is 1 – 0.95 = 0.05.  
p 是成功事件的概率，即 0.95。(1-p) 是失败事件（不登机）的概率，即 1 – 0.95 = 0.05。

This formula relies on the independence of events. In probability theory, if A and B are independent, the probability of both events happening is their individual probabilities multiplied together:  
这个公式依赖于事件的独立性。在概率论中，如果 A 和 B 独立，则两个事件同时发生的概率是它们各自概率的乘积：

\[
P(A \text{ and } B) = P(A) \times P(B)
\]

To calculate the probability of k=301 passengers showing up, we multiply \(301 \times 0.95\) and \(5 \times 0.05\) together. Then, we multiply it with the combinator at the beginning. It reads as “n choose k”, which counts for how many different ways we select 301 people out of 304.  
要计算恰好 301 名乘客登机的概率，我们将 \(301 \times 0.95\) 和 \(5 \times 0.05\) 相乘。然后，再乘以开头的组合数。它读作“n 选 k”，计算的是从 304 人中选出 301 人的不同方式数。

The following drawing makes it a little easier to grasp the logic behind the formula of the probability with binomial distribution. To simplify things, it illustrates the probability of 4 passengers showing to a flight with 5 tickets sold:  
下面的图示有助于理解二项分布概率公式背后的逻辑。为简化说明，它展示了 5 张机票的航班中有 4 人登机的概率：

The first row assumes passenger 5 does not show up, the second row assumes passenger 2 does not show up. Since these events are independent and identical, the probability of these events are the same. There are 5 combinations of 1 passenger not showing up (i.e. 5 chooses 4 is 5). Hence, the probability of 4 passengers showing up for the flight is 5 times the probability of 1 passenger not showing up.  
第一行假设乘客 5 不登机，第二行假设乘客 2 不登机。由于这些事件是独立且相同的，这些事件的概率相同。有 5 种 1 人不登机的组合（即 5 选 4 等于 5）。因此，4 人登机的概率是 1 人不登机概率的 5 倍。

So the answer to our first question (What is the probability that this flight is overbooked?) can be calculated as P(X > 300) which is the sum of P(X=301) + P(X=302) + P(X=303) + P(X=304):  
因此，我们第一个问题（航班超售的概率是多少？）的答案可以计算为 P(X > 300)，即 P(X=301) + P(X=302) + P(X=303) + P(X=304) 的总和：

\[
P(X > 300) = \binom{304}{301} (0.95)^{301} (0.05)^{3} + \binom{304}{302} (0.95)^{302} (0.05)^{2} + \binom{304}{303} (0.95)^{303} (0.05)^{1} + \binom{304}{304} (0.95)^{304} (0.05)^{0}
\]

The numbers at the beginning come from combinations: We can select 301 passengers from a group of 304 passengers in 4,636,304 different ways. So the final result is:  
开头的数字来自组合数：从 304 名乘客中选出 301 人有 4,636,304 种不同方式。因此最终结果是：

\[
P(X > 300) \approx 0.000139
\]

The exact probability that the flight will be overbooked is 0.000139, which translates to roughly 0.014% (or about a 1-in-7,200 chance).  
航班超售的确切概率为 0.000139，约等于 0.014%（或大约 7200 分之一的机会）。

We’ve found the probability that this flight will be overbooked. As we can see in the calculations above, the flight is overbooked if 301, 302, 303, or 304 passengers show up (i.e. 1, 2, 3, or 4 overbooked passengers). This brings us to the second question: What is the expected value of the number of overbooked passengers?  
我们已经求出了航班超售的概率。如上计算所示，如果 301、302、303 或 304 名乘客登机（即超售 1、2、3 或 4 人），航班就会超售。这引出了第二个问题：超售乘客数量的期望值是多少？
