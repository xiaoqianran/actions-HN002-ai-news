# Title: Astronomers may have found the first exomoon  
# 标题：天文学家或已发现首个系外卫星  

European Southern Observatory Press Release New ‘exomoon’ detection challenges cosmic labels 22 July 2026 Artist’s impression of CD-35 2722, a system with a moon-like object (Credit: ESO/M. Kornmesser)  
欧洲南方天文台新闻稿 新“系外卫星”探测挑战宇宙分类标准 2026年7月22日 CD-35 2722系统（含类月天体）的艺术想象图（来源：ESO/M. Kornmesser）  

Observations made with the European Southern Observatory’s Very Large Telescope (ESO’s VLT) have revealed evidence for a moon-like object in the CD-35 2722 system.  
使用欧洲南方天文台甚大望远镜（ESO的VLT）进行的观测，在CD-35 2722系统中发现了类月天体的证据。  

Unlike moons in our Solar System, the newly found object does not orbit a planet, raising questions about what to name it.  
与太阳系中的卫星不同，新发现的天体并不围绕行星运行，这引发了对其命名的疑问。  

Instead, it circles a brown dwarf, an object larger than a planet, that orbits the CD-35 2722 star.  
相反，它围绕一颗褐矮星运行——褐矮星是比行星更大的天体，并围绕CD-35 2722恒星公转。  

If confirmed, this could be the first ‘moon’ discovered outside our Solar System.  
若获证实，这可能是太阳系外发现的首个“卫星”。  

Kevin Hoy, an ESO student in Chile and lead author of the study published today in Nature, describes the system he spent months analysing as “super weird” compared to our own.  
该研究今日发表于《自然》杂志，主要作者、在智利的ESO学生凯文·霍伊（Kevin Hoy）将耗费数月分析的这一系统描述为与我们太阳系相比“超级怪异”。  

The biggest and most massive object in this young system is the star CD-35 2722, which has about half the mass of the Sun.  
这个年轻系统中最大最重的天体是恒星CD-35 2722，其质量约为太阳的一半。  

The star is being orbited by a brown dwarf, an object too massive to be a planet but too small to be a star.  
一颗褐矮星围绕该恒星运行——其质量过大无法称为行星，但又过小无法称为恒星。  

The newly discovered object orbits this brown dwarf.  
新发现的天体围绕这颗褐矮星运行。  

“This system is somewhat hard to define using Solar-System-based words like ‘planet’ and ‘moon,’” states Hoy, who is also affiliated with the Universidad Diego Portales and the Millennium Nucleus of Young Exoplanets and their Moons (YEMS) in Chile.  
“用太阳系为基础的词汇如‘行星’和‘卫星’来定义这个系统有些困难，”霍伊说道。他同时隶属于智利迭戈·波塔莱斯大学（Universidad Diego Portales）及年轻系外行星及其卫星千禧年 nucleus（YEMS）。  

The new object, which the team call an exosatellite, is at least as massive as Jupiter while the brown dwarf has more than 30 times the mass of Jupiter.  
研究团队将这一新天体称为“系外卫星”，其质量至少与木星相当，而褐矮星的质量超过木星的30倍。  

“The exosatellite is clearly massive enough to be a planet, but it does not orbit a star, though it orbits an object that orbits a star,” says Hoy.  
“这个系外卫星的质量显然足以成为行星，但它不围绕恒星运行，尽管它围绕一个本身围绕恒星运行的天体运行，”霍伊说。  

“Being the third wheel in this system makes us want to call it a moon, even if it is nothing like the small, rocky moons we have in our system.”  
“作为这个系统中的‘第三者’，让我们想称它为卫星，即使它与我们系统中那些小型岩石卫星毫无相似之处。”  

This exosatellite or ‘exomoon,’ a natural satellite outside our Solar System [1], is difficult to label, given the differences in this system compared to our own.  
鉴于该系统与我们太阳系的差异，这个系外卫星或“系外卫星”（太阳系外的天然卫星[1]）很难被归类。  

Alice Zurlo, YEMS Director and collaborator on the study explains: “The satellite we report is a giant gaseous body orbiting a highly massive companion, itself several times the mass of Jupiter.”  
YEMS主任、本研究合作者爱丽丝·祖尔洛（Alice Zurlo）解释道：“我们报告的卫星是一个巨大的气态天体，围绕一个质量极高的伴星运行，而该伴星本身的质量就是木星的数倍。”  

“We have a clear delineation between the planets and the Sun in the Solar System, so defining things like moons is simple.  
“在太阳系中，行星与太阳之间有清晰的界限，因此定义卫星之类的事物很简单。  

In the CD-35 2722 system, where we are blurring the lines between stars, planets, and moons, the whole thing becomes more complicated to describe,” adds Zurlo, who is also an astrophysicist at Universidad Diego Portales.  
在CD-35 2722系统中，恒星、行星和卫星之间的界限变得模糊，整体描述就更为复杂，”祖尔洛补充道。她同时也是迭戈·波塔莱斯大学的天体物理学家。  

Regardless of what to call this object, astronomers have been trying to detect satellites outside our Solar System for years, but none has yet been confidently detected.  
无论这个天体如何命名，天文学家多年来一直试图探测太阳系外的卫星，但至今尚无任何一次被确凿探测到。  

Therefore, despite the over 6000 exoplanets discovered to date, only a few exomoon candidates have been spotted and the evidence to support them is limited.  
因此，尽管迄今已发现超过6000颗系外行星，但仅发现了少数几个系外卫星候选体，且支持它们的证据有限。  

Just a few months ago, a team led by Quentin Kral reported on observations with ESO’s Very Large Telescope Interferometer in the HD 206893 star system, which revealed hints of a satellite, but no firm detection.  
就在几个月前，由昆廷·克拉尔（Quentin Kral）领导的一个团队报告了使用ESO甚大望远镜干涉仪在HD 206893恒星系统进行的观测，该观测揭示了卫星存在的迹象，但未获确凿发现。  

For the CD-35 2722 observations, Hoy, Zurlo and their team used the CRIRES+ instrument on ESO’s VLT, employing the method that was used to find the first exoplanet around a Sun-like star.  
对于CD-35 2722系统的观测，霍伊、祖尔洛及其团队使用了ESO的VLT上的CRIRES+仪器，采用了用于发现首颗围绕类太阳恒星的系外行星的方法。  

They applied this radial velocity method to detect small wobbles on the brown dwarf caused by the object orbiting it, finding what the team believe to be strong evidence for this ‘moon.’  
他们应用这种径向速度法，探测到褐矮星因该天体绕其运行而产生的微小摆动，研究团队认为这是该“卫星”存在的有力证据。  

“As exotic as it is, this system is truly unique and represents a breakthrough: the first plausible detection of an exosatellite,” says Zurlo.  
“尽管这个系统非常奇特，但它确实是独一无二的，并代表了一项突破：首次 plausibly 探测到系外卫星，”祖尔洛说。  

Beyond the excitement of discovering new types of objects, detecting satellites in other planetary systems can help us understand how diverse their formation and evolution might be.  
除了发现新类型天体带来的兴奋感外，探测其他行星系统中的卫星还能帮助我们理解它们的形成和演化可能有多么多样。  

With its 39-metre mirror and advanced instrumentation, ESO’s upcoming Extremely Large Telescope (ELT) will allow astronomers to detect smaller exomoons.  
凭借其39米口径镜面和先进仪器，ESO即将建成的极大望远镜（ELT）将使天文学家能够探测更小的系外卫星。  

Discoveries with the ELT will make us further reconsider how we label planetary objects from systems different from our own.  
通过ELT的发现，我们将进一步重新思考如何为与我们太阳系不同的系统中的行星天体命名。

**注释**  

[1] A satellite is an object that orbits another object and it can be natural (like our own moon) or artificial (like a spacecraft). An exosatellite is a satellite outside our Solar System. An exomoon is generally considered to be a natural satellite orbiting a planet or another object outside the Solar System, though there is no officially accepted definition for exomoon.  
[1] 卫星是围绕另一个天体运行的天体，可以是天然的（如我们的月球）或人造的（如航天器）。系外卫星是太阳系外的卫星。系外卫星通常被认为是围绕系外行星或其他天体运行的天然卫星，尽管对“系外卫星”尚无官方认可的定义。
