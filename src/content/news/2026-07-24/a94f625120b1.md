# EU fines Google €890M for competition breaches over search and apps

欧盟因搜索和应用商店违规行为对谷歌罚款8.9亿欧元

Google has been fined a total of €890m (£760m) by the EU for breaches of online competition laws by its search and app store services.
欧盟因搜索和应用商店服务违反在线竞争法，对谷歌处以总计8.9亿欧元（7.6亿英镑）的罚款。

The European Commission, the EU’s executive arm, said Google had broken the Digital Markets Act by giving priority to its own services, such as shopping and hotel deals, in search results over those of its rivals.
欧盟执行机构欧盟委员会表示，谷歌违反了《数字市场法案》，在搜索结果中优先展示其自身的服务（如购物和酒店优惠），而非竞争对手的服务。

It also infringed the DMA by preventing app developers from steering consumers towards cheaper offers, including for subscriptions, on websites or alternative app stores.
此外，谷歌还通过阻止应用开发者引导消费者转向网站或其他应用商店上更便宜的优惠（包括订阅服务）来违反《数字市场法案》。

Google has been fined €460m for the search-related breach and €430m for the app store violation. The commission has ordered the company to treat third-party services that appear in its search results in a “fair and non-discriminatory manner” and allow app developers to make offers outside Google’s app store.
谷歌因搜索相关违规被罚款4.6亿欧元，因应用商店违规被罚款4.3亿欧元。欧盟委员会已命令该公司以“公平且非歧视性的方式”对待出现在其搜索结果中的第三方服务，并允许应用开发者在谷歌应用商店之外提供优惠。

It noted that Google had already started testing changes to how it displayed search results featuring its own services. It said those changes represent “substantial progress towards compliance”.
欧盟委员会指出，谷歌已开始测试如何展示包含其自身服务的搜索结果的变化。并表示这些变化代表了“在合规方面取得了实质性进展”。

Consumers would be direct beneficiaries of the decision by the EU, a senior official said. “Search results will be different in Europe. They will have to adapt their search engine going forward,” they said.
一位高级官员表示，消费者将是欧盟此项决定的直接受益者。“搜索结果在欧洲将会有所不同。他们未来将不得不调整其搜索引擎，”该官员说。

Max von Thun, director of the Open Markets Institute Europe thinktank, said the fines were the “bare minimum” for a company that made revenues of just over $400bn last year.
开放市场研究所欧洲智库主任马克斯·冯·图恩表示，对于一家去年收入略高于4000亿美元的公司来说，这些罚款只是“最低限度”。

“Having finally established Google’s non-compliance, the commission must now move quickly to force Google to end its anti-competitive practices once and for all. Europe’s startups and innovators cannot wait much longer,” he said.
“在最终确定谷歌不合规后，委员会必须迅速采取行动，迫使谷歌一劳永逸地结束其反竞争行为。欧洲的初创企业和创新者已等待不了多久，”他说。

The decision to impose the fine risks the ire of Donald Trump, only hours before a series of temporary global tariffs against about 60 countries expires.
在针对约60个国家的一系列临时全球关税到期前几小时，这一罚款决定可能引发唐纳德·特朗普的愤怒。

A senior official for the EU said they had no knowledge of how Trump was likely to react, insisting that the bloc had the “sovereign right” to regulate US tech companies in its own jurisdiction and that the timing of the fine was not connected to tariffs.
一位欧盟高级官员表示，他们不知道特朗普可能如何反应，并坚称欧盟拥有“主权权利”在其管辖区内监管美国科技公司，且罚款时间与关税无关。

Last year Apple and Mark Zuckerberg’s Meta were fined under the DMA. Apple was told to pay €500m for anti-competitive practices at its app store and Meta was told to pay €200m in a ruling on its ad-free “consent or pay” proposal for Facebook and Instagram.
去年，苹果公司和马克·扎克伯格的Meta公司根据《数字市场法案》被罚款。苹果公司被命令为其应用商店的反竞争行为支付5亿欧元，Meta公司则因针对Facebook和Instagram的无广告“同意或付费”方案被罚款2亿欧元。

Google can appeal against the decision and ask for interim measures, including a request to suspend the measure. The search company’s president of global affairs, Kent Walker, described the fine as “product degradation driven by a small group of self-serving complainants” that will have a negative impact on European businesses and consumers.
谷歌可以对该决定提出上诉，并请求采取临时措施，包括要求暂停该措施。这家搜索公司的全球事务总裁肯特·沃克将罚款描述为“由一小撮自私的投诉者驱动的产品降级”，这将对欧洲企业和消费者产生负面影响。

He argued that the DMA forces Google “to strip away real-time search features Europeans love – like instant pricing and direct availability for hotels, flights, and restaurants – and dismantle safety protections on Google Play”.
他认为，《数字市场法案》迫使谷歌“剥离欧洲人喜爱的实时搜索功能——如酒店、航班和餐厅的即时定价和直接可用性——并拆除Google Play上的安全保护措施”。
