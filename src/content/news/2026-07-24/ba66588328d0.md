# Supercooled kidneys have been transplanted into pigs in a “landmark achievement”  
# 猪移植“过冷”肾脏 实现“里程碑式成就”

---

**EXECUTIVE SUMMARY** When it comes to organ donation, time is everything. As soon as an organ has been carefully removed from a donor’s body, it starts to deteriorate. Surgeons have a matter of hours to get it into a recipient. Leave it too long and the organ will become unusable. In most cases, organs will be kept on ice during that time, at around 4 °C (39 °F). They cannot be frozen—in previous attempts, ice has formed, causing all kinds of damage. Matthew Powell Palm at Texas A&M University and his colleagues have an alternative solution—a device that allows organs to be cooled to -4 °C (25 °F) without forming any ice. Now, in new research with pig organs, his team has shown that kidneys, at least, can be supercooled and preserved in the device for days. Once rewarmed, the organs have been successfully transplanted into animals, and they seem to do better than organs kept on ice. The work represents “a landmark achievement,” says Kevin Myer, president and CEO of LifeGift, an organ procurement organization based in Texas, who was not involved in the research.

**内容摘要** 在器官捐献中，时间就是一切。一旦器官从捐献者体内被小心取出，它就开始退化。外科医生只有几个小时的时间将其植入受体。时间过长，器官就会变得无法使用。在大多数情况下，器官在此期间会被保存在冰上，温度约为4°C（39°F）。器官不能被冷冻——在之前的尝试中，冰晶形成，造成了各种损伤。德克萨斯A&M大学的马修·鲍威尔·帕尔姆（Matthew Powell Palm）及其同事提出了一种替代方案——一种能让器官冷却至-4°C（25°F）而不形成任何冰晶的装置。现在，在与猪器官进行的新研究中，他的团队表明，至少肾脏可以在该装置中过冷并保存数天。一旦重新升温，这些器官已成功移植到动物体内，且表现似乎优于冰上保存的器官。未参与该研究的德克萨斯州器官获取组织LifeGift的总裁兼首席执行官凯文·迈耶（Kevin Myer）称，这项工作代表了“一项里程碑式的成就”。

---

**Cooling organs** Powell Palm hopes this approach could ultimately help ease the organ shortage crisis. Today, there are more than 104,000 people waiting for a kidney transplant in the US alone. It is estimated that 17 people die every day in the US while waiting for a transplant. That’s partly due to a lack of donated kidneys, but it’s also because many of those that are available never make it to a recipient. In some years, around one in three donated kidneys end up being discarded, often because they end up too degraded to use by the time they reach a recipient. Kidneys can be stored on ice for around 24 hours or placed in devices that aim to mimic the conditions of the body, also for up to around 24 hours. That’s not always long enough to find a suitable recipient and transport the organ, says Myer.

**冷却器官** 帕尔姆希望这种方法最终能有助于缓解器官短缺危机。仅在美国，目前就有超过104,000人在等待肾脏移植。据估计，美国每天有17人在等待移植期间死亡。这部分是由于缺乏捐献的肾脏，但也是因为许多可用的肾脏从未送达受体。在某些年份，大约三分之一的捐献肾脏最终被丢弃，通常是因为它们送达受体时已经退化到无法使用。肾脏可以在冰上保存约24小时，或放入旨在模拟人体条件的设备中，保存时间也约为24小时。迈耶表示，这并不总是有足够的时间找到合适的受体并运输器官。

---

Scientists around the world have been working on ways to store organs for longer by cooling them to even chillier temperatures. Cooling an organ slows its metabolism—the colder you go, the greater the effect, and the longer you can store it. We’ve long been able to successfully cryopreserve eggs, sperm, and embryos, but it’s much harder to freeze large organs. Teams have been exploring various temperatures and cryoprotectants (chemicals that essentially work like antifreeze), but so far no one has been able to freeze human organs for transplantation. As a thermodynamicist, Powell Palm explored another approach. By keeping an organ submerged at a constant pressure, it should be possible to prevent the formation of ice at temperatures a little below 0 °C, without the need for cryoprotectants (which might have side effects and would need to be approved before being used in human transplants). To test this theory, Powell Palm and his colleagues have created a device that does just that. The device itself is essentially a hermetically sealed chamber with a transparent lid. At its base is a device that monitors the organ’s temperature and checks for the formation of ice. Organs are submerged in a solution that is already commonly used to preserve them for transplant. “I always describe this as low-tech high science,” says Powell Palm. “A lot of work has gone into understanding the … kinetics at play in this system, but ultimately … it’s quite simple.”

全球科学家一直致力于通过将器官冷却到更低的温度来延长其储存时间。冷却器官会减缓其新陈代谢——温度越低，效果越显著，储存时间也越长。我们长期以来一直能够成功冷冻卵子、精子和胚胎，但冷冻大型器官要困难得多。各个团队一直在探索各种温度和冷冻保护剂（本质上像防冻剂一样的化学物质），但迄今为止，还没有人能够冷冻人类器官以供移植。作为一名热力学家，帕尔姆探索了另一种方法。通过将器官在恒定压力下保持浸没状态，应该有可能在略低于0°C的温度下防止冰晶形成，而无需使用冷冻保护剂（冷冻保护剂可能有副作用，用于人体移植前需要获得批准）。为了验证这一理论，帕尔姆和他的同事创造了一个实现这一点的装置。该装置本质上是一个带有透明盖子的密封腔室。其底部有一个监测器官温度并检查冰晶形成的设备。器官浸没在一种已常用于移植保存的溶液中。帕尔姆说：“我总是把这描述为‘低技术含量的高科技’。”“我们投入了大量工作来理解这个系统中起作用的……动力学，但最终……它相当简单。”

---

**Supercooled kidneys** To test their device, Powell Palm and his colleagues first removed single kidneys from pigs. The organs were flushed with the same commonly used solution to remove the blood, just as transplant organs are. The team then kept some kidneys on ice for either two hours or 24 hours, to mimic standard conditions used in human transplantation. They also put some of the removed kidneys in their device for 24, 48, or 72 hours. The stored kidneys were then each transplanted back into the original donor pigs. Each pig’s second kidney was removed in the same procedure, leaving each animal with only the kidney that had been stored, and reimplanted. Once the 24-hour supercooled kidneys were transplanted, they immediately began producing urine—a key indication that they were working. The team members also measured other markers of kidney function and found that the organs appeared to be working normally within about 10 days of being transplanted. A kidney that was supercooled for 72 hours recovers once it is transplanted back into a pig.

**过冷肾脏** 为了测试他们的装置，帕尔姆和他的同事首先从猪身上取出单个肾脏。这些器官用同样的常用溶液冲洗以去除血液，就像处理移植器官一样。然后，研究团队将一些肾脏在冰上保存两小时或24小时，以模拟人类移植中使用的标准条件。他们还将一些取出的肾脏放入装置中24、48或72小时。之后，这些储存的肾脏被各自移植回原来的供体猪体内。每只猪的第二颗肾脏在同一手术中被移除，使每只动物只剩下那颗被储存过的肾脏，并重新植入。一旦24小时过冷的肾脏被移植，它们立即开始产生尿液——这是它们正在工作的关键迹象。团队成员还测量了其他肾脏功能指标，发现这些器官在移植后约10天内似乎已恢复正常运作。一颗被过冷72小时的肾脏在移植回猪体内后恢复了功能。

---

That’s slower than kidneys stored on ice for two hours but much quicker than kidneys kept on ice for 24 hours, says Powell Palm. The organs that were kept supercooled for 48 and 72 hours performed similarly, he says. “Even at three days—triple the clinical standard—we’re getting recovery that is faster than … [what has been] the gold standard for the last three decades,” he says. “So we’re really, really pumped about this.” “It is impressive,” says Heidi Yeh, a transplant surgeon at Mass General Brigham for Children, who also researches organ preservation technologies. “Often kidneys that have been stored for 48 hours [in other studies] take a week or two before they start working again.”

帕尔姆表示，这比冰上保存两小时的肾脏恢复得慢，但比冰上保存24小时的肾脏快得多。他说，被过冷48和72小时的器官表现相似。“即使是在三天——三倍于临床标准——我们得到的恢复速度也快于……过去三十年的黄金标准，”他说。“所以我们真的、真的为此感到兴奋。”也研究器官保存技术的马萨诸塞州总医院儿童分院移植外科医生海蒂·叶（Heidi Yeh）表示：“这令人印象深刻。在其他研究中，储存了48小时的肾脏通常需要一周或两周才能重新开始工作。”

---

**Organs that grow** The supercooled organs seem to work well in the long term, too. Over a 30-day period, the pigs grew by around 30%—and the kidneys grew with them, almost doubling in size to compensate for both the pigs’ growth and the lack of a second kidney. The team monitored one of the pigs for 200 days before removing and analyzing its kidney. Even at that point the organ looked healthy, says Powell Palm. He and his colleagues presented the findings at the American Transplant Congress in Boston last month. Earlier this year, researchers in Canada showed they could also cool pig kidneys to below-zero temperatures and transplant them into pigs. The team’s protocol included the use of a cryoprotectant, and organs were stored for up to 48 hours before being transplanted into pigs. Those organs survived for a week.

**生长的器官** 过冷器官的长期表现似乎也很好。在30天的时间里，猪增长了约30%——而肾脏也随之生长，体积几乎翻倍，以补偿猪的生长和缺少第二颗肾脏的情况。团队监测其中一头猪长达200天，之后才取出并分析其肾脏。帕尔姆表示，即使在那个时间点，器官看起来仍然健康。他和同事上个月在波士顿举行的美国移植大会上展示了这些发现。今年早些时候，加拿大的研究人员表明，他们也能将猪肾脏冷却到零下温度并移植到猪体内。该团队的方案包括使用冷冻保护剂，器官在移植前被储存了长达48小时。那些器官存活了一周。
