# AI Companies Are Trying to Hide a Staggering Amount of Debt  
# AI公司正试图隐瞒一笔惊人的债务

---

AI companies are pouring untold billions of dollars into enormous data centers in their efforts to sustain increasingly complex and resource-intensive AI models. It’s an extremely costly undertaking built on seemingly bottomless hype — and a mountain of debt.  
AI公司正将数十亿美元投入庞大的数据中心，以维持日益复杂且资源密集的AI模型。这是一项建立在看似无底洞般的炒作之上的极端昂贵的事业——以及一座债务高山。

---

As Japanese financial newspaper Nikkei Asia found in a recent investigation, just five US tech giants — Alphabet, Microsoft, Amazon, Meta, and Oracle — are hiding an estimated $1.65 trillion in debt that doesn’t appear on balance sheets. That’s even more than the $1.35 trillion in debt the five companies officially reported in their financial data for the most recent quarter.  
据日本金融报纸《日经亚洲》近期调查发现，仅五家美国科技巨头—— Alphabet、微软、亚马逊、Meta和甲骨文——就隐瞒了估计达1.65万亿美元、未出现在资产负债表上的债务。这甚至高于这五家公司最近一个季度财务数据中 officially 报告的1.35万亿美元债务。

---

Meta alone has amassed around $420 billion in off-balance-sheet debt, according to Nikkei, highlighting how precarious the AI industry’s steep investment in AI has become, and inspiring comparisons to energy company Enron, which collapsed in spectacular fashion in 2001 because of similar debts hidden behind shell companies.  
据《日经》报道，仅Meta一家就积累了约4200亿美元的资产负债表外债务，这凸显了AI行业对AI的巨额投资已变得多么不稳定，并引发了与能源公司安然（Enron）的比较。安然在2001年因通过空壳公司隐藏类似债务而戏剧性崩溃。

---

Like Enron, they’re using special purpose vehicles, or off-balance sheet arrangements such as legally distinct subsidiaries, as a way to make their financial reporting look healthier than it actually is — often a glaring sign that something is deeply amiss behind the scenes.  
与安然类似，它们正在使用特殊目的实体（SPV）或资产负债表外安排（如法律上独立的子公司），以使财务报告看起来比实际情况更健康——这通常是一个明显的信号，表明幕后存在严重问题。

---

“The accounting treatment itself is in fashion,” technical accounting consultant Tom Selling told Bloomberg. “But what if one of these companies was a house of cards and was propping itself up with this accounting treatment? To me, that’s the risk.”  
“这种会计处理本身正流行，”技术会计顾问汤姆·塞林（Tom Selling）告诉彭博社。“但如果其中一家公司是纸牌屋，并靠这种会计处理支撑自己呢？在我看来，那就是风险。”

---

Experts continue to warn of an AI bubble, noting the enormous and widening gulf between公司估值 and their comparatively measly profits. The latest news will do little to quiet critics who say the situation is more dire than the companies’ official balance sheets suggest.  
专家们继续警告AI泡沫的存在，指出公司估值与其相对微薄的利润之间存在巨大且不断扩大的差距。最新消息无助于平息批评者的声音，他们称实际情况比公司官方资产负债表显示的更为严峻。

---

To keep up with the ongoing AI race, tech giants are committing vast sums to build out large-scale data center projects, a long-term bet that may — or may not — pay off. They’re also selling new shares to raise new funds, as Nikkei reports, which could lead to equity dilution and a drop in investor confidence. That could make them even more vulnerable if the AI bubble does pop, or the industry fails to generate enough demand to justify the data center construction frenzy.  
为了跟上持续的AI竞赛，科技巨头们正投入巨资建设大规模数据中心项目，这是一场可能——也可能不会——获得回报的长期赌博。据《日经》报道，它们还在出售新股以筹集新资金，这可能导致股权稀释和投资者信心下降。如果AI泡沫真的破裂，或该行业未能产生足够需求来证明数据中心建设狂潮的合理性，这可能使它们更加脆弱。

---

The pressure is on: four of the five companies Nikkei analyzed are set to report second quarter earnings in the coming days and weeks. We’ll be watching.  
压力正大：《日经》分析的五家公司中有四家将在未来几天和几周内发布第二季度收益报告。我们将持续关注。
