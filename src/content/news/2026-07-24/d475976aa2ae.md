# The arguments against open source AI are bad  
# 反对开源人工智能的论点站不住脚  

The release of Kimi K3 has opened a fresh round of angst and confused discourse. There's a loud cohort of journalists, business leaders, and politicians arguing that open source AI is a dangerous threat. OpenAI's Dean Ball: One probable outcome of an open-weight-model-dominant world is full AI communism... rather than a market product, AI is a "public good" Freely available AI for anyone? The horror!  
Kimi K3的发布引发了一轮新的焦虑与混乱讨论。一群记者、商业领袖和政治家大声疾呼，称开源人工智能是危险的威胁。OpenAI的迪恩·鲍尔：在一个开源权重模型占主导的世界里，一个可能的结果是完全的“人工智能共产主义”……人工智能不是市场产品，而是一种“公共品”。任何人都能免费获取人工智能？太可怕了！  

Frontier labs' case against open source AI is essentially: Open source models are dangerous (and un-American!). We should open the AI Pandora's Box, but only with responsible gatekeepers (toll collectors, preferably us!). Only trusted users (our most profitable customers) should be able to use it.  
前沿实验室反对开源人工智能的论点本质上是：开源模型是危险的（且不美国！）。我们应该打开人工智能的潘多拉魔盒，但只能由负责任的守门人（ preferably 是我们这些收费者！）把关。只有受信任的用户（我们最有利可图的客户）才能使用它。  

I want to address some bad arguments against open source AI, but some corrections on how the argument is being framed are in order: Open source software is the foundation for commercial software.  
我想针对反对开源人工智能的一些糟糕论点进行回应，但首先需要纠正一下论点的框架：开源软件是商业软件的基础。  

Ball's framing strolls past the fact that open source software is the foundation of all proprietary software. This includes frontier models, which at the end of the day are software products. Open source software is counterintuitive to people outside of the software industry. Why work hard on a product, and give it away for free?  
鲍尔的论述忽略了开源软件是所有专有软件基础这一事实。这包括前沿模型，它们归根结底也是软件产品。对于软件行业之外的人来说，开源软件是违反直觉的。为什么要努力开发一个产品，然后免费送出去？  

A software program is a stack of programs, with each layer built on top of another. To build Uber, you need programming language frameworks, software to send and receive web traffic, data analysis tools, and countless other components. Most of these are not differentiators for a commercial enterprise, so it serves commercial actors to cooperate on lower components in the stack and compete on the higher level pieces that actually differentiate their products.  
一个软件程序是程序的堆栈，每一层都建立在另一层之上。要构建Uber，你需要编程语言框架、收发网络流量的软件、数据分析工具和无数其他组件。其中大多数对企业来说并非差异化因素，因此商业参与者合作开发堆栈中较低层的组件，并在实际区分其产品的高层部分展开竞争，是符合其利益的。  

Frontier labs would very much like AI models to not fall into the category of "so commonplace that it doesn't make sense to compete on". Whether that happens remains to be seen.  
前沿实验室非常希望人工智能模型不属于“如此普遍以至于不值得竞争”的类别。这种情况是否会实现，还有待观察。  

Open source software is very difficult to suppress. In reality, the argument about suppressing open source models is mostly beside the point. History tells us that suppression of open source software is extremely difficult, and attempting to do so only serves to weaken companies against international competitors.  
开源软件极难压制。实际上，关于压制开源模型的论点大多离题了。历史告诉我们，压制开源软件极其困难，试图这样做只会削弱公司面对国际竞争对手时的实力。  

A brief history of encryption is illustrative: Today, PGP is a commonplace tool anyone can use, and most devs are at least familiar with. But when Phil Zimmermann invented it in 1991, the U.S. government considered encryption to be military technology. A criminal investigation was opened against Zimmermann. When Netscape created SSL, the U.S. government allowed it to only release a weakened version of it internationally. These controls backfired: it was much easier to acquire the weakened, "international" version, so even many Americans used it.  
加密的简短历史很有说明性：如今，PGP是一个任何人都能使用的普通工具，大多数开发者至少有所了解。但1991年菲尔·齐默曼发明它时，美国政府将加密视为军事技术。对齐默曼提起了刑事调查。当网景公司创建SSL时，美国政府只允许其发布一个弱化的国际版本。这些管制适得其反：获取弱化的“国际”版本要容易得多，以至于许多美国人也使用了它。  

Export controls did not succeed in limiting encryption as the government wished. SSL, PGP, and similar tools were readily available throughout the world, and the controls disadvantaged Americans. Eventually, courts ruled that releasing encryption source code is protected speech, and the U.S. government relaxed encryption export controls.  
出口管制并未如政府所愿限制加密。SSL、PGP和类似工具在世界各地 readily available，这些管制使美国人处于不利地位。最终，法院裁定发布加密源代码属于受保护的言论，美国政府放松了加密出口管制。  

Narrowing suppression to "Chinese" models won't make things easier. What, exactly, makes an AI model Chinese? Is it Chinese if, as frontier models allege, it was distilled from American models? What about if an American fine-tunes a Chinese model? At best, regulating AI in this way will (temporarily) encumber Americans with red tape and diminished AI access relative to the rest of the world.  
将压制范围缩小到“中国”模型不会让事情变得更简单。究竟什么使一个AI模型成为“中国”模型？如果像前沿模型所声称的那样，它是从美国模型蒸馏而来，它算中国模型吗？如果一个美国人微调了一个中国模型呢？充其量，以这种方式监管人工智能只会（暂时）让美国人被繁文缛节所累，并相对于世界其他地区减少人工智能的获取。  

Open source AI is not just a Chinese phenomenon. There's an assumption baked into the open source AI debate that open source models are something that only the Chinese government has an incentive to develop. In reality there are many commercial actors with ample incentive to develop open source AI:  
开源人工智能不只是中国现象。开源人工智能的辩论中隐含着一个假设：只有中国政府才有动力开发开源模型。实际上，许多商业参与者都有充足的动力开发开源人工智能：  

Chip makers: Nvidia CEO Jensen Huang has described what Nvidia is building as “token factories”. Nvidia doesn't care if its chips are used to run frontier models or cheap open source models - it just wants to produce and generate demand for as many tokens as possible. And indeed Nvidia has itself released a suite of open source models.  
芯片制造商：英伟达CEO黄仁勋将英伟达正在构建的东西描述为“代币工厂”。英伟达不在乎其芯片是用于运行前沿模型还是廉价开源模型——它只是想生产并创造尽可能多的代币需求。事实上，英伟达自己也发布了一系列开源模型。  

American Startups: Thinking Machines Lab recently released a powerful open source model. They and others are betting that models will be commoditized, and a defensible moat can be built around auxiliary services that complement or customize models.  
美国初创公司：Thinking Machines Lab最近发布了一个强大的开源模型。他们和其他公司都赌模型将商品化，并且可以通过补充或定制模型的辅助服务建立可防御的护城河。  

Enterprise AI users: Frontier model customers aren't currently all that active in open source AI development, but they will be. They will want lower-cost models for low-complexity tasks, and more fine-grained control over customer-facing features.  
企业AI用户：前沿模型客户目前并未积极参与开源AI开发，但他们将会参与。他们将需要用于低复杂度任务的低成本模型，以及对面向客户功能的更精细控制。  

BigCos: You can be sure that Google and Meta are watching OpenAI's new ad product closely. Should frontier model ad products gain traction, it would be well worth it for these behemoths to commoditize ad-free, open source models to squash ad competition.  
大公司：你可以肯定谷歌和Meta正在密切关注OpenAI的新广告产品。如果前沿模型广告产品获得 traction，这些巨头 commoditize 无广告的开源模型以扼杀广告竞争将是值得的。  

The "AI race" is... what, exactly? Much of the angst around China's models centers on "losing the AI race". But what's the goal of this race? Is it to develop the best model? To sell the most tokens? To destroy humanity first? Talking about an "AI Race" doesn't make more sense than talking about an "Internet Race". We're not competing to be the first to send a rocket to the moon, we're reacting to a new, transformational technology.  
“人工智能竞赛”……究竟是什么？围绕中国模型的许多焦虑集中在“输掉人工智能竞赛”上。但这场竞赛的目标是什么？是开发最好的模型？卖出最多的代币？还是先毁灭人类？谈论“人工智能竞赛”并不比谈论“互联网竞赛”更有意义。我们不是在竞争第一个发送火箭到月球，而是在应对一种新的、变革性技术。  

To the extent there's a race between nations, it's to absorb this transition and grow economies. In this framing, free AI models are a boon, not a threat.  
就国家之间存在竞赛而言，是为了吸收这一转型并增长经济。在这个框架下，免费的人工智能模型是福音，而非威胁。  

Bad arguments to fear Chinese AI models. China is "AI dumping!" Scott Galloway has argued that free Chinese AI is an attempt to eliminate competitors in the long run: This is what China did to solar panels, steel, EVs, and batteries. First, they match Western quality, or they don't even match it. 89%. Close. Actually, match it with cars, they've matched it, but go ahead. Then they cut the price by two thirds, then they own the market.  
害怕中国AI模型的糟糕论点。中国正在进行“人工智能倾销！”斯科特·加洛韦认为，免费的中国人工智能是长期消灭竞争对手的企图：这就是中国对太阳能电池板、钢铁、电动汽车和电池所做的事。首先，它们匹配西方质量，或者甚至不匹配。89%。接近。实际上，在汽车方面，它们已经匹配了，但请继续。然后它们将价格削减三分之二，然后它们就拥有了市场。  

But apart from chips, AI isn't a physical good. Solar panels and steel require physical supply chains.  
但除了芯片，人工智能不是实体商品。太阳能电池板和钢铁需要实体供应链。
