# Title: John C. Dvorak has died / 约翰·C·德沃夏克去世

---

**Wednesday, July 22, 2026 John C. Dvorak has died**  
2026年7月22日，星期三 约翰·C·德沃夏克去世

Reports coming in of the death of John C. Dvorak, apparently passed away on Monday (July 20th) from complications of heart bypass surgery.  
据最新消息，约翰·C·德沃夏克已于周一（7月20日）因心脏搭桥手术并发症去世。

Dvorak started in wine and moved to silicon as an early columnist at InfoWorld, one of the pillar references for this particular blog because of its studious regular reporting, and most notably for PC Magazine where he had no less than two columns since at least 1986.  
德沃夏克最初涉足葡萄酒行业，后转入科技领域，成为《InfoWorld》的早期专栏作家。该杂志因其严谨定期的报道，成为本博客的重要参考来源之一。他最知名的身份是《PC Magazine》的专栏作者，自1986年起至少同时主持两个专栏。

In addition to this and other tech journalism credits, he was on the flagship CNET Central cable TV program in the mid 1990's with his "Buy It, Try It, Skip It" reviews, plus other shows hosted on NPR and then-ZDTV.  
除了上述及其他科技新闻工作外，他在1990年代中期参与了旗舰有线电视节目CNET Central，以“买它、试它、跳过它”的评测栏目闻名。他还曾主持NPR和当时的ZDTV电视台的节目。

He was also a regular guest on Leo LaPorte's syndicated radio program and podcasts, and joined former MTV veejay Adam Curry (well known to us in Gopherspace for MTV Networks v. Curry, and an early user of TTYtter before Twitter started to suck) in 2007 as co-host of the No Agenda podcast, a role he maintained until his death.  
他同时也是利奥·拉波特 syndicated 广播节目和播客的常客。2007年，他与前MTV音乐主持人亚当·库里（在Gopherspace社区因“MTV Networks诉库里案”而知名，且在Twitter变质前就是TTYtter的早期用户）共同主持《No Agenda》播客，并在此职位上工作直至去世。

Dvorak hailed from a different generation of tech journalists, far more personal and yet far more technical, and even his detractors (given his political views, which we won't talk about here, there are many) would admit he was at least entertaining.  
德沃夏克属于科技记者中与众不同的一代——既更富个人风格，又更具技术深度。即便是他的批评者（鉴于他的政治观点，这里我们不展开，但确实有很多）也不得不承认，他至少是极具娱乐性的。

He was 80. Rest in peace.  
他享年80岁。愿他安息。
