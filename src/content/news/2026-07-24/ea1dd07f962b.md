## Namecheap Gave My Account to an Unverified Third Party Just Because They Asked  
## Namecheap 仅因他人请求便将我的账户交给未经验证的第三方

I’ve been a NameCheap customer for 13 years. I’ve also helped out an old college club paying for a .com they use (that is registered to me under my name, address, and phone number). During a recent leadership transition, the incoming club lead wanted to make changes to the DNS and didn’t know to contact me. They figured out the domain name was parked at NameCheap, so they initiated a password reset using the domain name. I got a password reset email and immediately filed a NameCheap support ticket saying “I did not initiate this”. They called me to verify I was the one who filed the ticket, and then followed up with a canned email with tips like check your anti-virus.  
我是 NameCheap 13 年的老客户。我还曾帮一个大学旧社团支付他们使用的 .com 域名费用（该域名以我的姓名、地址和电话号码注册）。最近一次社团领导层换届时，新负责人想修改 DNS 设置，但不知道要联系我。他们查到这个域名托管在 NameCheap，于是直接用域名发起了密码重置。我收到重置邮件后，立刻提交了 NameCheap 工单说明“这不是我发起的”。他们打电话确认了我确实是工单提交者，随后发了一封模板邮件，建议诸如检查杀毒软件之类。

The incoming club leader was persistent though, and called NameCheap support. He convinced them the domain registered in my name and address really belonged to his club, and with no verification or validation whatsoever, NameCheap changed my password, and changed the email address associated with my account. All because someone simply asked nicely on a phone call.  
但新社团负责人很执着，他打电话给 NameCheap 客服，说服对方这个以我姓名地址注册的域名实际上属于他的社团。在没有进行任何核实或验证的情况下，NameCheap 修改了我的密码，并更改了关联账户的邮箱地址。仅仅因为有人在电话里“礼貌请求”。

Meanwhile in the background, someone advised the new club leader who I was and we were able to connect and get things transferred over. Ultimately I was happy to give them access or even ownership if they wanted (student club turnover being what it is, it’s likely a domain doesn’t get renewed and gets gobbled up by a squatter, which is why I was keeping it current for them).  
与此同时，有人告知了新负责人我的身份，我们得以联系并完成了转移。最终我很乐意给予他们访问权甚至所有权（考虑到学生社团换届频繁，域名很可能因未续费而被抢注，这也是我一直帮他们保持续费的原因）。

But NameCheap had no way of knowing any of this. As far as NameCheap was aware, this was a personal account of mine. They demonstrated they were perfectly able to pick up a phone and call me (to verify my initial support ticket) but when someone calls them and says “but I really want access to that account” they don’t bother?  
但 NameCheap 对此毫不知情。在他们看来，这只是我的个人账户。他们明明能打电话给我（验证我最初的工单），可当有人打电话说“但我真的很想访问那个账户”时，他们却不再核实？

I’d hesitate to even call this social engineering. It’s clearly a massive vulnerability. I’ve already moved a dozen of my most critical domains out of NameCheap after seeing just how easy it is for a third party to completely take over a NameCheap account: just ask nicely.  
我甚至不愿将此称为社会工程学攻击。这显然是一个巨大的安全漏洞。看到第三方仅凭“礼貌请求”就能完全接管 NameCheap 账户后，我已经将十几个最关键域名迁出了 NameCheap。

Namecheap has been owned by a private equity firm for several months now. It would be nice to have a nonprofit registrar so jumping every few years isn't necessary.  
Namecheap 已被私募股权公司持有数月。要是有非营利性注册商就好了，就不用每隔几年就换一次。

...seriously? Where do I jump ship to now?  
…… seriously？我现在该跳槽到哪家？

Porkbun. Yes, Cloudflare Domains exists but let's support the small guys.  
Porkbun。Cloudflare Domains 确实存在，但咱们还是支持小公司吧。

Given Cloudflare's reputation for shakedowns once you pass their undisclosed thresholds I wouldn't trust them with my domains. To be clear I'm not expecting something for nothing, happy to stay within the limits or even have no free tier at all. I just don't want the fear of waking up to a sales email demanding I suddenly fork out for an enterprise plan because I've exceeded their undisclosed thresholds.  
鉴于 Cloudflare 一旦超过其未公开阈值就勒索的名声，我不会把我的域名托付给他们。明确说，我不是想占便宜，乐意遵守限制甚至没有免费层也行。我只是不想某天醒来收到销售邮件，要求我突然为超出未公开阈值而购买企业方案。

Enshittification and PE sellouts are great for DNS providers because migrating it can be a real pain sometimes and carry a high risk if something goes wrong. It's why so many of them are chains of "Buy through Company N! We used to work for Company N-1 before they sold out!"  
“劣化”和私募股权出售对 DNS 提供商有利，因为迁移可能很麻烦，出错风险也高。这就是为什么那么多提供商是“通过 N 公司购买！我们曾在 N-1 公司出售前工作！”的链条。

I've been a long, long term customer of Namecheap as well. Domain privacy protection is a feature that Namecheap provides for all valid domains. Its included by default at no extra cost. The poster didn't indicate if they had this feature enabled or not for the domain. It would have prevented the college club person from even seeing their email address to initiate a password reset. This clearly isn't an answer for NC's customer support personnel and company policies. But I've been a happy customer for many years and I discourage others from immediately reading other comments and rushing to jump to other registrars without doing your due dilligence. Remember that in any situation, the people most likely to leave negative comments and reviews are the people that have had genuine bad experiences or feel like they've been slighted, even if unwarranted.  
我也是 Namecheap 的长期客户。域名隐私保护是 Namecheap 为所有有效域名提供的功能，默认免费包含。发帖人没说明是否启用了此功能。若启用，本可防止社团负责人看到邮箱发起重置。这显然不能为 NC 客服和公司政策开脱。但我多年满意，劝大家别急着看评论就换注册商，务必做好尽职调查。记住，任何情况下，最可能留负面评论的是真有糟糕经历或感觉被怠慢的人，哪怕理由不充分。

How is domain privacy relevant here? That only hides your email from public records. What if the attacker already knows it (as they did in this case)? Email address is quite literally something you are meant to share publicly. It is not a password.  
域名隐私在这里有何关联？那只是隐藏公共记录中的邮箱。如果攻击者已知邮箱（如此案）呢？邮箱本就是公开分享的，不是密码。

Registration info usually also includes a physical address and names.  
注册信息通常还包括实际地址和姓名。

I did have domain privacy enabled. NC allows people to initiate a password reset via username, email address, or domain name. I was a happy customer right up until this incident. And I certainly agree that due diligence is a must for something as critical as a registrar.  
我确实启用了域名隐私。但 NC 允许通过用户名、邮箱或域名发起重置。我直到此事发生前都是满意客户。我也同意，对注册商这么关键的事，尽职调查必不可少。
