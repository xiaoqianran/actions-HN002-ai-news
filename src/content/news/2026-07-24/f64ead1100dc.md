# What happened to TheNumbers.com  
# TheNumbers.com 发生了什么事？

What just happened to TheNumbers.com should worry us all. The inside story of how one of film data's most trusted sites vanished overnight, and why every website you rely on is more fragile than you think.  
TheNumbers.com 的遭遇应当引起我们所有人的警惕。这家最受信赖的电影数据网站之一一夜之间消失的内幕，以及为什么你所依赖的每一个网站都比想象中更脆弱。

If you work in or around the film industry, there is a decent chance you have used the work of The Numbers this month, whether you realise it or not. Its hand-researched data is the highest quality, tracking box office grosses, budgets, home video and streaming across more than 78,000 films and 236,000 people. It gets north of eight million visitors a year, and is treated as THE definitive authority by journalists, academics, filmmakers, prediction markets, and even Guinness World Records.  
如果你在电影行业工作或与之相关，这个月你很可能（无论是否意识到）使用过 The Numbers 的数据。其人工研究的数据质量最高，追踪超过 78,000 部电影和 236,000 人的票房总收入、预算、家庭影音和流媒体数据。它每年吸引超过 800 万访客，被记者、学者、电影人、预测市场甚至吉尼斯世界纪录视为**终极权威**。

And it was this GOAT status which caused the catastrophic events of March this year.  
正是这种“史上最佳”（GOAT）的地位引发了今年三月的灾难性事件。

On the 5th March 2026, TheNumbers.com website vanished. The site was down for over a week, without explanation. A week later, it resurfaced at a fraction of its former size. Gone were the historical charts, the individual movie pages, and even the much-loved Report Builder.  
2026 年 3 月 5 日，TheNumbers.com 网站突然消失。网站在没有解释的情况下宕机超过一周。一周后，它以原来规模的很小一部分重新上线。历史图表、单部电影页面，甚至深受喜爱的“报告生成器”（Report Builder）都已不见踪影。

With only a generic “we’re rebuilding, please bear with us” message to go on, the internet responded as it always does - with confusion, anger, and conspiracy theories. One Reddit theory even suggested it was a deliberate rug pull designed to cripple the free site to push people towards paid products.  
面对只有一句通用的“我们正在重建，请耐心等待”的公告，互联网的反应一如既往：困惑、愤怒和各种阴谋论。Reddit 上甚至有一种理论认为，这是一次蓄意的“卷款跑路”（rug pull），旨在瘫痪免费网站，迫使用户转向付费产品。

Three months on, I spoke at length with Bruce Nash, founder and CEO of The Numbers, about what happened. He describes quite an unpleasant and eventful experience:  
三个月后，我与 The Numbers 的创始人兼 CEO Bruce Nash 进行了长谈，了解事情的来龙去脉。他描述了一段相当不愉快且波折的经历：

> We got a lot of angry emails from people who are like, 'Where's this page that you used to have and you don't have anymore?'  
> “我们收到了很多愤怒的邮件，人们会说：‘以前有的那个页面怎么没了？’”

Within his tale are a number of things that should worry anyone who runs, relies on, or simply appreciates the internet.  
他的故事中包含了许多让任何运营、依赖或仅仅欣赏互联网的人担忧的因素。

First, some background  
首先，一些背景

On Friday 17 October 1997, mathematician and former IBM software developer Bruce Nash launched a Geocities site that tracked 300 films.  
1997 年 10 月 17 日星期五，数学家、前 IBM 软件开发人员 Bruce Nash  launch 了一个追踪 300 部电影的 Geocities 网站。

Bruce described the launch in a 20th anniversary essay (which now survives only in the Internet Archive, for reasons that will become clear):  
Bruce 在一篇 20 周年纪念文章中描述了这次 launch（该文如今仅在互联网档案馆中留存，原因即将明了）：

> I hit a button in an Access database, uploaded some HTML pages to Geocities, and made a brief announcement on the Hollywood Stock Exchange message boards to let people know that I was starting to analyze box office for films to help them pick MovieStocks to trade on HSX.  
> “我在 Access 数据库中按下一个按钮，将一些 HTML 页面上传到 Geocities，并在好莱坞证券交易所（HSX）的留言板上做了简短宣布，告诉大家我开始分析电影票房，以帮助他们挑选用于交易的 MovieStocks。”

From those humble beginnings, Bruce and the team he built around the site turned The Numbers into the film industry's most reliable financial source. At the start of 2026, the database tracked 78,396 movies, 178,375 theatrical release records, and 236,176 people.  
从这些 humble 的开端，Bruce 和他组建的团队将 The Numbers 打造成了电影行业最可靠的财务数据源。到 2026 年初，该数据库追踪了 78,396 部电影、178,375 条影院发行记录和 236,176 人。

The robots arrive  
机器人来了

During its lifetime, the challenges The Numbers has faced have changed immensely. For its first quarter century or so, the traffic was manageable and mostly polite. As Bruce puts it:  
在其运营期间，The Numbers 面临的挑战发生了巨大变化。在最初的约四分之一个世纪里，流量是可控的，且大多“有礼貌”。正如 Bruce 所说：

> Pre-AI, we got human traffic, mostly well-behaved search engine crawlers, and a few people crawling the site for personal projects. If someone got too greedy, we could spot them and block them.  
> “在人工智能（AI）出现前，我们收到的是人类流量，主要是行为良好的搜索引擎爬虫，以及少数为个人项目爬取网站的人。如果有人太贪婪，我们可以发现并阻止他们。”

Over the past couple of years, website owners the world over have seen their web traffic change. What was initially only people browsing gave way to an ever-increasing number of bots. By 2024, automated traffic had surpassed human traffic, and just last month, Cloudflare announced that bots had reached 57.5% of web page requests.  
过去几年，全球网站所有者都见证了其网络流量的变化。最初只有人类浏览，如今已让位于不断增长的机器人流量。到 2024 年，自动化流量已超过人类流量，就在上月，Cloudflare 宣布机器人已占网页请求的 57.5%。

The Numbers felt this shift in two distinct waves. The first started around 2024:  
The Numbers 在两个 distinct 的浪潮中感受到了这种转变。第一次浪潮始于 2024 年左右：

> We saw a big increase in crawls as AI training joined the search engine crawlers. The AI crawlers are generally less well-behaved than the search engines, which increased the management tasks for us to keep the site running smoothly.  
> “随着 AI 训练加入搜索引擎爬虫，我们看到爬取量大幅增加。AI 爬虫通常不如搜索引擎‘有礼貌’，这增加了我们保持网站平稳运行的管理任务。”

And the second wave was stronger and more damaging:  
而第二次浪潮更加强劲且破坏性更大：

> Around December 2025, we saw another big spike in traffic which I attribute to agentic AI: a combination of AI agents that scrape sites in response to prompts, and people being able to write agents that scrape sites. Like every data-rich site, by early 2026 The Numbers was being hammered hard by AI bots scraping its pages over and over at an industrial scale.  
> “大约在 2025 年 12 月，我们看到了另一次流量大高峰，我将其归因于‘智能体 AI’（agentic AI）：响应提示而爬取网站的 AI 智能体，以及能够编写爬取网站智能体的人的结合。像每个数据丰富的网站一样，到 2026 年初，The Numbers 正被 AI 机器人以工业规模反复爬取页面而猛烈冲击。”

Bruce says that only 10% of their traffic is from humans browsing the site, with the rest coming from AI bots and automated traffic.  
Bruce 表示，他们的流量中只有 10% 来自人类浏览，其余均来自 AI 机器人和自动化流量。

Websites try to adapt to the new robots  
网站试图适应新机器人

This put enormous strain on the site, but Bruce and his team were able to take measures to mitigate the worst of it. One of the cleverest was talking to the robots in their own language:  
这给网站带来了巨大压力，但 Bruce 和他的团队能够采取措施减轻最坏的影响。最聪明的措施之一是用机器人的“语言”与它们对话：

> There’s stuff on the site which is designed for an LLM to read, so that it can tell somebody ‘here’s how you licence the data’ rather than ‘here’s how you scrape the website’. It’s had a huge effect. We’re now getting probably ten times the volume of licensing enquiries.  
> “网站上有一些内容是为大语言模型（LLM）阅读而设计的，这样它就可以告诉某人‘这是如何许可数据’而不是‘这是如何爬取网站’。这产生了巨大影响。我们现在收到的许可咨询量可能是以前的十倍。”

But mitigation is not the same as escape. From December through early March, the team struggled to keep the site alive under the load. Bruce estimates that:  
但缓解不等于逃脱。从 12 月到 3 月初，团队在负载下艰难维持网站运行。Bruce 估计：

> Around 90% of our time was spent keeping the existing site running while we spent our spare moments working on a new and improved system.  
> “我们大约 90% 的时间都花在维持现有网站运行上，同时利用空闲时间开发一个新的、改进的系统。”

The problem was compounded by the site’s age: thirty years old, with approximately 160,000 source files serving around 2 million pages.  
问题因网站年龄而加剧：它已有三十年历史，拥有约 160,000 个源文件，服务着约 200 万个页面。

Then, in the early hours of Thursday 5 March, the servers collapsed.  
然后，在 3 月 5 日星期四的凌晨，服务器崩溃了。

The team scrambled to understand what had happened, initially assuming it was the sheer weight of AI traffic. It seems AI was to blame... but possibly not only in the way they first thought.  
团队急忙弄清楚发生了什么，最初以为是 AI 流量的 sheer 重量所致。看来 AI 确实是罪魁祸首……但可能不仅仅是以他们最初设想的方式。

Buried in the flood of agentic traffic, the site’s logs showed something more pointed than scraping. As Bruce describes it:  
在智能体流量的洪流中，网站日志显示出比爬取更针对性的东西。正如 Bruce 所描述：

> Some of these used the site using legitimate URLs, others were looking for back doors, most likely so they could get to the data before it appeared on the site, or to manipulate the data presented to users.  
> “其中一些使用合法 URL 访问网站，另一些则在寻找后门，很可能是为了在数据出现在网站上之前获取数据，或操纵呈现给用户的数据。”

On the advice of a friend who works in cybersecurity, the old server stayed off. For good. Restoring the backups and nursing the thirty-year-old site back online would have meant defending 160,000 legacy files against attackers who had spent months probing them.  
在一位从事网络安全的朋友的建议下，旧服务器保持关闭状态。永久性地。恢复备份并让这个有三十年历史的网站重新上线，意味着要防御 160,000 个遗留文件，而攻击者已经花了数月时间探测它们。
