# Agentic Coding is Burning Me Out
# 智能体编程正在让我精疲力竭

The familiar pacing of software development has been completely compressed by agentic coding because you no longer have those routine stretches of just wiring things together to catch your breath. Writing code by hand, or trad coding as Twitter likes to call it, forced me to respect the natural ebb and flow of the problem solving process and get into a much more steady, sustainable rhythm. Doing tasks manually naturally builds up the context required for the decisions involved later because you have time to process everything along the way and construct your mental model of the project's structure.

智能体编程（Agentic coding）彻底压缩了软件开发原本熟悉的节奏，因为你不再拥有那些通过编写常规代码来喘息的间隙。手动编写代码——也就是 Twitter 上所说的“传统编程”——迫使我尊重解决问题过程中自然的起伏，并进入一种更稳定、可持续的节奏。手动完成任务自然会积累后续决策所需的上下文，因为你有时间在过程中处理一切，并构建起对项目结构的心理模型。

Working with LLMs means you cold start more often than not since the code just appears in front of you (like relying on the tattoos from Memento to understand what's going on). The process of managing agentic workflows turns programming into a string of variable psychological rewards (dare I say, gacha or slot machine mechanics?) followed by cognitive fatigue instead of the deep, focused intellectual labor that initially brought a lot of developers into this industry.

使用大语言模型（LLM）意味着你经常处于“冷启动”状态，因为代码直接出现在你面前（就像电影《记忆碎片》中依靠纹身来理解发生了什么一样）。管理智能体工作流的过程将编程变成了一连串不确定的心理奖励（我敢说，这就像抽卡游戏或老虎机机制？），随之而来的是认知疲劳，而不是那种最初吸引许多开发者进入这个行业的、深度专注的智力劳动。

### Changing How We Write Code
### 改变我们编写代码的方式

The LLMs are currently generating so many orders of magnitude more code than any single human could ever have the chance to properly debug or reason through. You find yourself signing off on an overwhelming amount of raw code just to keep up with the output that's expected these days. You get there, inevitably and reluctantly, by ceding operational control to the model. While not wrong, we have to be honest with ourselves in admitting we're mostly just trusting the raw power of the tool to get the job done. It is honestly surprising how well this functions most of the time, until you hit those edge cases where it completely falls apart and leaves you in this strange limbo where you are forced to keep using it for the productivity boost, but you can never actually trust it to run fully unsupervised.

目前，大语言模型生成的代码量比任何单个人类能够进行适当调试或推理的代码量都要多出几个数量级。为了跟上当今预期的产出，你发现自己不得不对海量的原始代码进行确认。你不可避免且不情愿地通过将操作控制权让渡给模型来达到这一目标。虽然这并没有错，但我们必须诚实地承认，我们大多只是在依赖工具的原始能力来完成工作。令人惊讶的是，它在大多数时候运行得非常好，直到你遇到那些完全崩溃的边缘情况，这让你陷入了一种奇怪的困境：为了生产力提升，你被迫继续使用它，但你永远无法真正信任它在完全无人监管的情况下运行。

### The Illusion of Infinite Scale
### 无限扩展的幻觉

The fatigue really scales up once you find yourself juggling several agents at once, simultaneously checking their work, and fixing missteps just to keep the whole development loop moving forward. The fundamental problem here is that the work drains you through constant judgment and relentless oversight. This process requires more attention and context switching, along with way more decisions per hour. Making constant architectural, big-picture decisions while overseeing the work of a cracked junior dev is fundamentally harder than executing standard programming tasks yourself. All of these continuous choices require a lot more mental endurance, since pausing to review generated output and decide on the next step takes time and energy and completely breaks the natural rhythm and momentum you build when doing the actual implementation yourself.

当你发现自己同时操控多个智能体、检查它们的工作并修复错误以保持整个开发循环向前推进时，疲劳感会成倍增加。这里的根本问题在于，这种工作通过不断的判断和无情的监督消耗着你的精力。这个过程需要更多的注意力、更频繁的上下文切换，以及每小时更多的决策。在监督一个“疯狂的初级开发者”工作的同时，还要不断做出架构层面的大局决策，这从根本上比你自己执行标准的编程任务要困难得多。所有这些持续的选择都需要更强的心理耐力，因为停下来审查生成的输出并决定下一步行动需要时间和精力，这会彻底打断你自己进行实际实现时所建立的自然节奏和动力。

### Navigating Decision Fatigue
### 应对决策疲劳

You quickly bump into exactly how much a single human can feasibly keep track of with modern LLMs. Decision fatigue is, in my opinion, the next invisible friction point for developers. I assume there will always be a pretty hard upper bound on what I can achieve as long as I have to stay in the loop and verify things until LLMs are completely better than humans in all cases, including review and verification. You might only get four or five extremely intense hours before your brain is fully cooked, compared to managing eight to ten normal productive hours. Some of my friends are already completely burnt out from this cycle. They rarely say it out loud, but I can clearly tell by watching the way they approach their current projects.

你很快就会触碰到单个人类在现代大语言模型辅助下所能追踪的极限。在我看来，决策疲劳是开发者面临的下一个隐形摩擦点。我认为，只要我必须保持在循环中并进行验证，直到大语言模型在所有情况下（包括审查和验证）都完全优于人类之前，我所能取得的成就总会有一个相当硬性的上限。你可能在经历四五个小时的高强度工作后大脑就彻底“烧干”了，而相比之下，正常工作状态下你可以管理八到十个小时的生产力。我的一些朋友已经因为这种循环而彻底精疲力竭。他们很少大声说出来，但我通过观察他们处理当前项目的方式就能清楚地看出来。

### Throttling the Agentic Loop
### 节流智能体循环

A lot of ambitious engineers think they just need to spin up more agents and ship more code so they can outwork everyone else, but MOAR is probably not the answer. We have to acknowledge that while these automated systems can comfortably operate around the clock, the human supervisors can't sustain the cognitive load. Trying to is an almost guaranteed path to burnout. Better review and verification loops sound like the obvious answer, but you have to figure out who is actually going to build them, you or the LLM. You have to ask yourself if you would really trust a verification system built by an LLM if you already do not completely trust the original code that it just generated for you. That immediately leads to the next issue of how you actually verify that the verification system itself even works and how you guarantee that it is targeting the correct parts of your system and actually testing them correctly. I honestly don't know the answer to any of that, but I am just thankful a lot of very smart people are working on this.

许多雄心勃勃的工程师认为，他们只需要启动更多的智能体并交付更多的代码，就能在工作量上胜过其他人，但“更多”可能并不是答案。我们必须承认，虽然这些自动化系统可以舒适地全天候运行，但人类监督者无法承受这种认知负荷。试图这样做几乎注定会导致精疲力竭。更好的审查和验证循环听起来是显而易见的答案，但你必须弄清楚到底是谁来构建它们——是你还是大语言模型？你必须问自己，如果你已经不完全信任大语言模型刚刚为你生成的原始代码，你真的会信任它构建的验证系统吗？这立即引出了下一个问题：你如何验证验证系统本身是否有效？你如何保证它针对的是系统的正确部分，并且确实在正确地测试它们？老实说，我不知道这些问题的答案，但我很庆幸有许多非常聪明的人正在致力于解决这些问题。
